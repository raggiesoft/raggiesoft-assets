# Book 4: Summer in the Basement

## Chapter 1: The Summer Sanctuary

### Part 1: A View of the Future

The convoy pulled up to the house on Geneva Street, and the Group of 8 piled out of the cars, stretching after the ride from campus. The group began the familiar process of moving their belongings into the Brooks' basement. As they had done many times before, Caleb and Owen expertly carried Jordan down the internal stairs, while Lauren followed carefully behind them with his wheelchair.

Once they were settled, Caleb wheeled Jordan toward the basement's sliding glass door. "Hey Jordan, come check this out," he said, calling for the rest of the group to join them. "Dad has a surprise for you."

He led them into the backyard. The group stopped and stared in awe. The series of small survey stakes from the beginning of the year was gone. In its place stood a massive, winding wooden ramp that snaked its way down the steep hill, connecting the front yard of the Brooks' house on Geneva Street to the future back patio of their own home at the bottom of the hill. The ramp was for the most part complete; the structure was fully in place and just needed its code-compliant handrails and a few cosmetic touches like weather-sealing and staining. It would be ready soon.

### Part 2: The Summer of Blueprints

The Group of 8 moved their mountain of belongings back into the familiar, cozy fortress of the Brooks' family basement. The four queen-sized portable beds were set up again, and a comfortable, chaotic domesticity quickly filled the space. They all knew this was not just for a few weeks; this was their home for the next year while their new, permanent home was being built.

The summer settled into a new rhythm. While Jordan, Aubrie, and Madison focused on decompressing after the immense stress of the semester, the others found minimum-wage jobs around town to earn some money. Mark, with his tech skills, easily got a job at the Blue Tech store in Spradlin Farms. Hannah started working at a department store in the New River Mall Christiansburg mall, with Zoe taking a job at a fast-food place in the food court and Lauren working at a clothing retailer in the same mall. Sarah, putting her communication skills to good use, landed a customer service position at the main Blue Ridge Transit office.

Their days were a mix of quiet afternoons spent in the Brooks' backyard and evenings filled with excited planning. Instead of watching construction next door, their focus was now on the massive roll of architectural blueprints that lived on the coffee table.

In the evenings, Laura would often bring down new photos from the construction site on U.S. 11, and the group would huddle around her mPhone, their dreams taking shape in the grainy images of cleared land and poured concrete foundations.

"Look!" Aubrie would say, pointing to a photo of the massive, sprawling foundation. "That's the basement. That's where our rooms are going to be!"

"And look at the size of that suite," Madison would add, tracing a section with her finger. "That's where the Alaskan King bed is going. It's going to be huge!"

"That must be our room, Mark," Hannah would say, pointing to another framed-out section. "And Bob and Emily's over there. And look, you can see where they've stubbed out the plumbing for the big kitchen on the first floor."

They were happy. They were safe. And miles away, their forever home was rising from the dust, a promise of the incredible future they were all building together.
