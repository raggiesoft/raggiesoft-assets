**LORE NOTE:**
The title track of the album serves as the emotional crux of the duo's new identity. The lyric *"There was a time we had a name"* is a direct, mournful reference to the Manning Family Orchestra, the sprawling six-piece 1970s band that existed before the tragedy. 

This track also marks the first time we hear the "Safe Harbor" leitmotif played on Gwen's salvaged wooden flute. While Claire is credited with the flute parts, her performance here is intentionally sparse. The emotional weight of playing her deceased cousin's instrument was so paralyzing that she could only bring herself to play the haunting melody at the beginning, before pushing through the grief to weave it into the thundering stadium-rock climax.
