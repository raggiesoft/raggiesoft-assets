**LORE NOTE:**
"Mirrors of Yesterday" represents a significant emotional breakthrough for Claire. After the paralyzing grief that limited her performance on the title track, she finally finds the strength to play her late cousin Gwen’s salvaged wooden flute throughout an entire song. 

The upbeat, melodic progressive pop production masks a profound desperation to cope with their loss. Most notably, the track features a sweeping, virtuoso flute solo in the bridge. Because the tragic loss of their cousin Simon severed the band's connection to traditional rock and roll, Claire and Rhys were forced to let the woodwinds and synthesizers carry the soaring emotional weight that a guitar solo would have traditionally held.
