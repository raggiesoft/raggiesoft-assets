# I-64 (The Gauntlet)

**LORE NOTE:**
This track explores the visceral, geographic nature of trauma. In an attempt to conquer his demons and see the Blue Ridge Parkway at Rockfish Gap, Jordan agrees to a road trip. However, reaching the gap requires driving on Interstate 64 East. To Jordan, I-64 is not just a highway; it is a psychological "rootkit virus," intrinsically tied to the route back to his abuser, Aubrie Sinclair, in Hampton Roads. The tension mounts during the agonizing 30-mile stretch starting in Lexington, where I-81 and I-64 merge. Despite his desire to be brave, the moment Mark actually takes Exit 221 to continue onto I-64 East in Staunton, Jordan's system catastrophically crashes. The sheer terror of being on the road heading toward his mother induces a severe meltdown, forcing Mark to immediately abort the mission and bail out at the very next ramp—Exit 91 (SR-285 towards Fishersville)—to break the virus's hold.
