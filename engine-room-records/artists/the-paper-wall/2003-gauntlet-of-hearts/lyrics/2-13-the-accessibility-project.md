# The Accessibility Project

**LORE NOTE:**
Now firmly in his recovery phase and back to his coursework, Jordan and his chosen family tackle a massive class assignment: mapping CPI's accessibility features. The group realistically acknowledges that they cannot physically fix the campus—they don't have the budget to pour concrete ramps or install new elevators. However, they realize that *information* is just as powerful as infrastructure. They set out to create comprehensive maps detailing every automatic door, hidden ramp, and accessible path across the rugged terrain. A major victory in their research is mapping the "Hillside Pass-Throughs." They document the university's policy allowing disabled non-residents to get their student IDs specially encoded by the Office of Educational Accessibility. This grants 24/7 access to the 1st and 2nd-floor lobbies and the elevator of buildings built on inclines—like Jordan's old dorm, "The Old Lady on the Hill" (Eleanor Bennett Hall)—allowing students to safely bypass steep hills regardless of standard guest hours. Musically, this track is an upbeat, focused, and triumphant "heist" song, turning data collection into a grand adventure.
