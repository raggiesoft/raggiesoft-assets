### **1-01-the-stardust-engine-live-in-chicago.md**
***

**LORE NOTE:**
This track is the "Smoking Gun" that proves Julian Vance (the executive behind the *Friction* disaster) never did his homework. In the intro, Ryan O'Connell—who, like his sister, is autistic and values absolute clarity—explicitly introduces Cassidy as "my sister," publicly disproving the "married couple" assumption years before it destroyed their contract.

Musically, this recording is a source of fury for the band. It was the only concession Apex made—allowing them to play their self-titled anthem as the opener of their strict 30-minute support slot. However, the mix is "sabotaged": Cassidy's iconic "Stardust" synth leitmotif—the musical heart of the song—is buried so deep in the mix it is practically inaudible, leaving only the "Engine" rock elements.

### **1-04-if-you-walked-away-live-in-chicago.md**
***

**LORE NOTE:**
For Cassidy, who is autistic, the lyrics of this song were not a performance—they were a terrifying "what-if" scenario that she experienced viscerally every time she sang it. While the studio marketed it as a romantic breakup ballad, Cassidy sang it as a desperate plea to her brother. **The fear in her voice on this recording is real.**

Ryan, who is also autistic, understood her need for absolute certainty better than anyone. He famously had to reassure her after *every single concert*—often using the exact same phrase, **"Family sticks together"**—to ground her back in reality and confirm that he was never going to leave her.

**Note: After this specific performance, Cassidy broke down in tears backstage. Ryan had to hold her in a deep pressure hug for twenty minutes to help her reset and remind her that he was right there.**
