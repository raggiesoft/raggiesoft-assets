**LORE NOTE:**
Inserted right after their breakout hit *Electric Color*, this track is a monument to corporate miscalculation. *Light Up the Night* was the song Apex Records poured *all* of their money into. The executives were convinced that this highly polished, aggressively safe synth-pop track would be "The Big One" that cemented The Stardust Engine as global pop idols. 

It wasn't. Despite a massive marketing campaign, the sterile, manufactured sound failed to connect with audiences. Looking back from 1997, the band included it here on *Essential Tracks* as a point of pride: it proved to the label that money couldn't buy authenticity, and that the audience actually craved the heavier rock elements the studio was desperately trying to suppress.
