**LORE NOTE:**
Ten years ago, Apex Records executives deliberately buried this track at the very end of the band's 1987 debut album, terrified of its heavier, uncompromised rock elements. Today, on this 1997 retrospective compilation, it stands as the undeniable blueprint for the band's entire independent era. 

It is the only studio track from their debut that perfectly blended Cassidy's "Stardust" analog synths with Ryan's driving "Engine" rock guitar. Music historians universally agree: had the label trusted the band and released this as the lead single in '87, it would have spent 11 straight weeks at #1, dethroning the existing record holder for the decade. Placed right in the heart of *Essential Tracks*, the band is finally giving the original studio recording of their true anthem the massive spotlight it always deserved.
