**LORE NOTE:**
Serving as the dark, aggressive counterpart to Cassidy's *Moon 1 (Tidal Lock)*, this track features Ryan’s only vocal performance on Disc 2. Where Cassidy’s track used the concept of a tidal lock to explore stable, unbreakable loyalty, Ryan uses the astrophysical "Roche limit"—the point at which a moon is literally torn apart by the gravity of the planet it orbits—to describe a toxic, fatal attraction. Sonically, it shatters the ethereal calm of the album's second half, introducing heavy, distorted electric guitars and a blistering rock tempo to the zero-gravity environment. The Twin Moons stand as a brilliant, contrasting thesis on the dual nature of gravity.
