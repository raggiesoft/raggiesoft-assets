**LORE NOTE:**
The mandatory title track of their 1989 sophomore album. While *Electric Color* was a genuine, if overly polished, attempt at finding their footing in the industry, *Neon Hearts* represents the exact moment the band realized they were trapped in a corporate formula. 

Apex Records was terrified of losing the massive pop audience they had cultivated, so they demanded pure brand consistency for the second album. They forced Cassidy to write a direct lyrical callback to their first hit ("It's an electric-colored feeling") and mandated that this "safe," high-energy synth track open the record. For the band, who had just spent two years secretly writing hard rock riffs on the road, flying to Los Angeles to record this sterile pop anthem was a miserable experience. It stands on this compilation as a monument to the suffocating creative control of the "Cold War" era.
