**LORE NOTE:**
The absolute peak of the Apex Records synth-pop machine. If *Electric Color* introduced their studio-mandated sound, *Dancing in the Neon* cemented their status as pop idols—a label the band deeply resented. 

Apex pushed this high-energy, undeniably catchy single heavily, prioritizing its polished, radio-friendly production over the band's actual rock roots. To achieve the "perfect" sound, Evan and Tyler's driving rhythm section was largely buried under layers of electronic drum machines and sequencing. Looking back from 1997, the band acknowledges the track as an undeniable earworm that kept the lights on, but it remains a prime example of the "plastic" sound they eventually had to violently reject to survive.
