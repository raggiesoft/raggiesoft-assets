**LORE NOTE:**
The first true crack in the armor. Closing out the "Apex Years" section of this compilation, *Not Your Doll* is the sound of Cassidy O'Connell finally breaking. 

During the recording of the 1989 *Neon Hearts* album, the studio desperately tried to maintain her "sweet, wholesome Stardust" persona. This track was Cassidy’s fierce, arena-rock rejection of that box. While Apex executives hated it—viewing it as an unmarketable deviation from their pop formula—they begrudgingly allowed it on the B-Side to appease the band. From the perspective of 1997, this track isn't just an outlier; it is the definitive turning point. It proved to the world (and to the label) that Cassidy wasn't just a pop singer; she was the co-pilot of the Engine.
