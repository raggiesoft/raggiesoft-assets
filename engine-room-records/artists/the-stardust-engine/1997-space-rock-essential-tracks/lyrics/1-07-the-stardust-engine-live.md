**LORE NOTE:**
Closing out the "Live Rebellion" act of this compilation, this track is included for one reason only: evidence. Recorded during the 1989 *Neon Hearts Tour* and released on 1990's *Live in Chicago*, this is the "sabotaged mix" that the band absolutely despises. 

During their strict 30-minute support slot, Apex Records made a single concession by allowing them to play their self-titled anthem. However, the studio manipulated the final live mix, deliberately burying Cassidy's iconic "Stardust" synth leitmotif so deep it is practically inaudible. By silencing her half of the arrangement, the label tried to artificially separate the siblings and suppress their unified sound. 

This recording is also the historical "Smoking Gun." In the opening seconds, Ryan explicitly introduces Cassidy as "my sister," publicly disproving the "married couple" assumption years before executive Julian Vance used it to destroy their contract. The band included this butchered version on *Essential Tracks* to document exactly what they were fighting against, with a firm promise to the fans: this is the last time this compromised mix will *ever* be pressed to a compilation.
