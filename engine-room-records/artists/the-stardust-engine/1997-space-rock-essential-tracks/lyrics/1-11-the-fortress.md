**LORE NOTE:**
Serving as the ultimate bridge between the desperation of "The Wilderness" and their fully funded "Comeback," *The Fortress* is the band's raw, arena-rock love letter to the fans who kept them alive. 

During the lean years of 1995, well before the lottery windfall that secured their permanent independence, The Stardust Engine survived entirely on the grassroots support of their underground fanbase. The "Ironheads" effectively crowdfunded the band's legal defense and studio rent through the "Forger Nation War Chest." 

When Ryan and Cassidy recorded this in 1995, "The Fortress" was a literal description of their freezing, concrete warehouse studio ("This isn't L.A., this isn't a dream / This is a warehouse, this is a scream"). Placed here on the 1997 compilation, the lyrics take on a much grander scale. It stands as an eternal monument to the fans: the "Fortress" is the impenetrable wall the audience built around the band, giving them the safety they needed to finally start over.
