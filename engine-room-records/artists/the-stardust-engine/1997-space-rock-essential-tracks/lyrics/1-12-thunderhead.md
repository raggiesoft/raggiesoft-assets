**LORE NOTE:**
Kicking off Act IV of the compilation ("The Comeback"), *Thunderhead* represents the fully actualized, uncompromised Stardust Engine. If the "Wilderness" years were about surviving the storm, this track is the sound of the band *becoming* the storm.

Sourced from their triumphant 1997 double album, *Hard Reset*, this track trades the frantic, desperate energy of their earlier rebellion for pure, slow-stomping sonic intimidation. Channeling the sludgy, heavy arena-rock anthems of the 1970s, the band deliberately drops the tempo to create maximum pressure. Lyrically, it is a direct, heavy-handed attack on the pristine, drum-machine-laden production Apex Records forced upon them for years ("You muted the kick / You silenced the crash"). 

Here on *Essential Tracks*, it stands as an unbothered victory lap. It is a heavy, undeniable declaration that the pop-star uniforms are dead, the label's control is broken, and the heavy weather has finally arrived.
