**LORE NOTE:**
Because the title track, *Midnight Drivers*, was the only song actually written during the rejected 1987 sessions, the band had to build an entire album around it from scratch in 2001. *Neon Pulse* serves as the retro-engineered prologue to that project. 

Written by Cassidy and Ryan in 2000, they deliberately designed the track to sound exactly like the pristine, high-speed synth-pop smash hits of the late 1980s. It represents the "before" picture—the vibrant, unburdened energy of the band driving into the city lights, completely unaware of the darkness that would follow. By opening the 2001 album with such a massive, era-accurate burst of energy, The Stardust Engine perfectly sets the stage for the sun to set, allowing the original 1987 Darkwave title track to hit with maximum impact on Side B.
