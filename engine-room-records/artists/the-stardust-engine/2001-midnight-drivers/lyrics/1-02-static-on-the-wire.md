**LORE NOTE:**
Continuing the retro-engineered illusion of Side A, *Static on the Wire* was crafted in 2001 to perfectly mimic the sterilized, radio-friendly synth-pop that Apex Records demanded in 1987. Cassidy and Ryan deliberately wrote the track to sound like a manufactured commercial hit—complete with a bright, sequenced synth hook and a driving dance beat. 

However, the detached, spoken-word bridge reveals the band's true identity bleeding through the pop facade. In the narrative context of the 2001 album, this track represents the band desperately "searching for a frequency" to connect with their true artistic voice amidst the corporate noise. It is a brilliant, catchy pop song that secretly acts as a critique of the 1980s music industry.
