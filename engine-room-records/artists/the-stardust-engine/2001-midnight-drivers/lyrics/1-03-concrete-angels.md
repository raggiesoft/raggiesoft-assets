**LORE NOTE:**
As the third track on Side A, *Concrete Angels* represents the massive, stadium-ready rock anthems that dominated the late 1980s. Written in 2001 specifically for the *Midnight Drivers* project, Ryan and Cassidy deliberately engineered this track to sound like a pristine, arena-shaking blockbuster that Apex Records would have eagerly pushed to rock radio in 1987. 

With Ryan taking over lead vocals, the song leans heavily into the band's raw rock roots while maintaining the polished, synth-heavy production of the era. To achieve this, the band relied heavily on Tyler's "Human Metronome" timing, anchoring the track with an explosive, quintessential 80s gated-reverb snare drum. Lyrically, while it masquerades as a standard driving anthem, it acts as a subtle nod to the band's survival—finding a sanctuary made of "stone and steel" and keeping the engine running on the inside, despite the heavy pressure of the corporate machine.
