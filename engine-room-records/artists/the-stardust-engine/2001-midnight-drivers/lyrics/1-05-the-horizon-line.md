**LORE NOTE:**
Serving as the grand finale to Side A, *The Horizon Line* was engineered in 2001 to act as the exact moment the "sun sets" on the band's bright, 1980s corporate pop era. It is the narrative bridge that connects the high-energy city lights of the first half to the isolating Darkwave reality of Side B.

To capture the emotional weight of this transition, Ryan and Cassidy utilized a rare, sweeping sibling duet. Their harmonies—honed over a lifetime of non-verbal communication—anchor a massive, hopeful crescendo of analog synth brass. However, rather than resolving the chord progression, the band intentionally cuts the track off abruptly. This jarring, unresolved silence was designed to physically force the listener into the quiet tension of flipping the vinyl record over, perfectly setting the stage for the original 1987 title track waiting in the dark.
