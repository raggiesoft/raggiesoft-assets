**LORE NOTE:**
This is the artifact. *Midnight Drivers* is the singular, original track actually written and recorded during the fraught 1987 studio sessions. Apex Records executives famously halted production upon hearing it, deeming the track far too dark, moody, and atmospheric for their manufactured pop stars, ultimately shelving the entire album. 

When the band finally won their financial and creative independence from Apex in 1992, this track sat in the vault. It wasn't until the monumental success of the 1997 *Hard Reset* double-album that The Stardust Engine finally had the bandwidth to revisit it. In 2001, they built an entire retro-engineered 1980s New Wave album from scratch just to give this one song a conceptual home. Crucially, because this was recorded in 1987—three years prior to the crash that caused Ryan's T-10 paralysis—the band explicitly chose not to rework or re-record any of the stems. The 2001 release uses the raw, untouched 1987 master, preserving the exact darkwave energy of that era. It opens Side B, pulling the listener out of the city lights and into the isolating sanctuary of the night.
