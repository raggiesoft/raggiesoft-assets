**LORE NOTE:**
Following the dark, pulsing anchor of the 1987 title track, *Shatterproof* plunges the listener deeper into the isolating atmosphere of Side B. Written and recorded in 2001, the band designed this track to emulate the cold, European industrial Darkwave sound that was gaining underground traction in the late 1980s—the exact sound Apex Records actively feared.

Ryan steps up to the mic, but completely strips away his roaring, stadium-rock "Engine" persona. Instead, he delivers a detached, almost robotic vocal performance over a bed of icy keyboard pads and a sparse, mechanical drum machine. The result is a chilling, claustrophobic track about emotional lockdown and absolute isolation, perfectly sustaining the tension of the record's "Night Shift."
