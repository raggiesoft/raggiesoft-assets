**LORE NOTE:**
*Terminal Velvet* is the atmospheric centerpiece of Side B, and in the meta-narrative of the *Midnight Drivers* project, it is a fascinating chronological paradox. 

When recording the album in 2001, Cassidy O'Connell fully deployed her signature audio architecture: **The Cosmic Tidal Lock Sound™**. Because the album is presented as a "lost" 1987 record, Cassidy effectively used her 2001 engineering mastery to create a fictional "prototype" of the sound that wouldn't actually be perfected in reality until 1997's *Moon 1 (Tidal Lock)*. 

The result is a brooding, zero-gravity space-rock ballad that completely abandons the driving drum machines of Side A. Featuring her crystal-clear vocals suspended in a massive, mathematically precise web of lush spatial reverb, the track perfectly captures the sensation of drifting untethered into the endless black. (And while the in-universe lore proudly states Holly legally weaponized and trademarked this sonic architecture in 1997, the true magic simply lies in Cassidy's meticulous layering).
