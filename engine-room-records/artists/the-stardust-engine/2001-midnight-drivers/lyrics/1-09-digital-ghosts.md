**LORE NOTE:**
As the penultimate track of the core album, *Digital Ghosts* strips The Stardust Engine down to its absolute bare minimum. Engineered in 2001 to simulate the sterile, early digital recording environments of the late 1980s, the band completely removes Evan's driving bass guitar and reduces Tyler's massive drum kit to a solitary, echoing click track. 

The result is a haunting, ethereal duet between Ryan and Cassidy. Lyrically, it explores the existential dread of being erased by the corporate machine—becoming nothing more than "ghosts in the mainframe" of a label like Apex Records. The minimalist analog synthesizers and heavy spatial reverb create a vast, empty soundscape where the siblings' harmonies drift like lost signals, perfectly capturing the isolation of Side B's "Night Shift" before the sun finally rises.
