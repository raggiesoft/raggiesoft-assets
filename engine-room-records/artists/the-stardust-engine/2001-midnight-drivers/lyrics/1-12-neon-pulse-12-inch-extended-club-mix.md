**LORE NOTE:**
To fully sell the illusion that *Midnight Drivers* was a complete, commercially prepared 1987 release before being shelved, Engine Room Records included this "rare" 12-inch extended club mix on the 2001 CD reissue. 

In the 1980s, labels like Apex Records would regularly press these extended, 5-minute remixes onto heavy 12-inch vinyl and distribute them exclusively to underground club DJs in New York and London. The wider vinyl grooves allowed for massive, bone-rattling bass response. Engineered in 2001 to perfectly replicate this era, the track deconstructs the tight, radio-friendly structure of the original *Neon Pulse*. It stretches the arrangement into a hypnotic, driving groove, replacing the traditional guitar solo with a heavy, four-on-the-floor instrumental dance break designed entirely to keep the dance floor moving.
