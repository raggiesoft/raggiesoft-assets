# Part 1: Monday – The Shared Mountain

The first Monday of the semester arrived with a nervous, electric energy. The six suitemates, having agreed to walk to their first classes together for moral support, moved as a single, cohesive unit through the bustling campus walkways. While the others peeled off towards buildings for their own classes, Aubrie and Jordan headed for the science complex. Jordan’s anxiety was a low, thrumming hum, but Aubrie’s presence at his side was a steady, calming force.

Their 9:00 AM Astronomy 101 class was in a large, tiered lecture hall already buzzing with the chatter of a hundred other freshmen. As they had planned, they found two seats together in the accessible row near the front.

Jordan stared at the massive projection screen and the models of planets hanging from the ceiling, his hands gripping the armrests of his chair. This was it. His mother’s weapon. The insurmountable mountain she had chosen for him, designed to make him fail.

"This is... a lot," he whispered, his voice tight with anxiety.

"It's okay," Aubrie beamed back, her own excitement a shield against his fear. "It's the real deal. No more high school physics". She saw his panic and gently, discreetly, took his hand under the table. Her grip was firm and certain. "We're in this together, remember? I've got you. I'm not going to let you fail".

Jordan looked at her, at this girl who had climbed onto the mountain just to keep him company. He took a shaky breath and squeezed her hand. With her by his side, he felt like he *could* get through this.

When their professor, Dr. Alistair, began the lecture, a comfortable silence fell between them. For Aubrie, this was her sanctuary, a place where the universe operated on clear, understandable laws. For Jordan, it was a terrifying, new language.

All he knew of the stars before this moment was the small comfort of the glow-in-the-dark plastic stars his mother had stuck on his ceiling as a child. They were a flat, pale imitation. His only other connection was the quiet, lonely act of looking out his bedroom window in Hampton Roads, wondering what those pinpricks of light in the night sky really were.

Now, Dr. Alistair was speaking about stellar nurseries and hydrostatic equilibrium, and Jordan’s anxiety began to spike. He didn't understand. He couldn't follow. This was it. He was going to fail.

He felt a sudden, gentle pressure. Aubrie, sensing his rising panic, discreetly laced her fingers with his. Her hand was warm, her grip firm and steady. She was a grounding presence, a silent, physical promise that he was not alone.

He took a shaky breath and forced himself to listen, not to the complex words, but to the *tone* of the lecture. Dr. Alistair wasn't trying to trick anyone; he was just excited. And beside him, Aubrie was fascinated, hanging on every word.

Jordan looked at their joined hands. He looked at Aubrie's rapt, happy expression. She was his safe person, and she *loved* this. She seemed to really like him, and she had promised to help him. Slowly, the terror in his chest began to recede. His mother’s weapon, this impossible, terrifying subject, suddenly felt just a little less scary.

When Dr. Alistair dismissed the class, the spell was broken. The lecture hall erupted back into noise as students began packing their bags. Jordan let out a breath he felt like he'd been holding for fifty minutes. He had survived. He hadn't understood everything, not even close, but he hadn't panicked.

He looked at Aubrie, who was finally letting go of his hand to gather her notebook. "See?" she whispered, her smile bright. "Not so bad."

He managed a small, genuine smile back. "Not so bad," he agreed.

For the first time, he was actually looking forward to the Friday lab section, even knowing it wouldn't start until the second week of class. His mother's insurmountable mountain suddenly seemed... climbable. He had someone to climb the mountain with.

As they moved out into the hallway, the crowd of students jostled past them. Jordan tensed, but Aubrie, seeing his unease, stepped in front of him, acting as a small buffer. He felt relieved.

"I guess we have to go our separate ways for our other classes," she said, her own expression a little sad at the thought.

"Yeah," Jordan confirmed, his heart sinking a little.

"Well," she said, and then, right there in the crowded hallway, she offered him another hug. This one was different from the first. It wasn't a desperate, grounding act in the middle of a meltdown. It was a warm, reassuring promise. *I'll be back.*

He held on for a moment, absorbing her steadying presence. As she pulled back, she looked at him, not wanting the connection to break.

"Are you free for lunch?" she asked.

He looked at his schedule, his heart giving a small, happy jolt. "Uh, yeah. I am. At noon.".

"Perfect," she beamed. "Meet me at the dining hall? Newman Commons?".

"Yeah. Okay. Lunch," he said, a real smile spreading across his face.

"It's a date," she said, then blushed. "I mean... not a... you know. I'll just see you then."

As she turned and walked away, Jordan watched her go. He still had to face the rest of his morning classes alone, a prospect that still terrified him. But as he maneuvered his wheelchair toward his next building, his mind was at ease.

He had a schedule. He had a *system*.

He knew he would see her Monday, Wednesday, and Friday mornings for class, and on Fridays for their lab. He knew he would see her in the dorm with him tonight. And now, he knew he would see her in just a couple of hours, for lunch.

His mother's weapon had been defused.
