# Part 3: The First Choice

The hug in the middle of the dining hall had grounded him. Jordan let out a shaky breath as Aubrie pulled back, though she kept a firm, reassuring hand on his shoulder. "Come on," she said, her voice bright. "Let's get lunch. Together."

She pushed his wheelchair toward the long, winding serving lines. The noise and smell of the food was overwhelming. "Hey," Aubrie said, noticing him trying to figure out how to navigate the line with a tray. "It's a pain to try and balance a tray in your lap. Let me just grab yours for you. What do you want?"

Jordan looked up at the massive, illuminated menus above the stations. Pizza. Pasta. A deli. A grill. He froze. He was completely, utterly overwhelmed by the choices. His entire life, food had been a simple, non-negotiable command. His mother made something, and he ate it. If he didn't like what Aubrie Sinclair was serving, he didn't eat. He was never *asked* what he wanted.

"Jordan?" Aubrie asked gently, seeing him staring blankly. "You okay?"

He spotted it. At the grill station, a student was flipping burgers on a flat top. A basket of fresh, golden french fries was right next to it. His mother *never* made burgers, and fries were a rare, frozen-bag-in-the-oven affair. He pointed, his voice small. "Can I... can I have that? A cheeseburger? And... fries?".

"A burger? Heck yeah. Good choice," Aubrie said, not blinking an eye. She had no idea she had just approved two items his mother would have denied him. "What do you want on it?"

He was stunned. "Uh... everything?"

"You got it. One cheeseburger with everything, and a side of fries," she called to the worker. "What about a drink?".

He pointed to the soda fountains, another battleground he'd always lost. "A... a soda?".

"Perfect." She grabbed a cup, filled it with soda, and placed it on the tray next to the burger and fries. She then quickly grabbed her own lunch—a large salad and a water.

They arrived at the register. Jordan, still reeling from the sheer ease of his choices, fumbled in his pocket for his new student ID, nervous about paying.

"Don't worry about it," Aubrie said, already handing her own ID to the cashier. "Put us both on my plan, please".

The cashier swiped, and it was done. Aubrie balanced the two trays and began steering them toward a table. Jordan was speechless. She had just bought him lunch—a meal *he* chose—without a second thought, without a lecture on cost, without a single sigh of inconvenience. She just... *did* it. She was showing this scared boy in a wheelchair that someone *did* care about him, and that he might, just maybe, have found a safe harbor.
