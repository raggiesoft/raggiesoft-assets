# Part 5: An Unconventional Study Session

That Tuesday evening, after their first full day of both classes, the group was relaxing in the suite. The health class, however, was still on Jordan's mind. He wheeled himself over to where the girls were gathered in Room 214, holding the "Dimensions of Human Sexuality" syllabus like a mysterious map.

"That health class today," he began, his voice hesitant but earnest. "All those chapter titles... about the human body... I've never learned about any of that. My mother forcibly opted me out of those classes every year."

A deep, collective empathy filled the room. They understood this wasn't just about academics; it was about a fundamental part of his education that had been denied to him. It was Aubrie who, looking at the confusing diagrams in the open textbook on her desk, formulated a plan rooted in the unique trust their family had built.

"The diagrams in the book are just flat drawings, Jordan. It's hard to learn from that," she said matter-of-factly. "We could... help you. It would be easier to remember if you could connect the clinical terms to physical reality."

The offer was radical, generous, and born of pure, practical kindness. Zoe and Madison nodded in immediate agreement.

Hannah and Lauren, out of respect for their boyfriends, lovingly opted out. "That is an incredibly sweet and amazing thing to do for him," Hannah said. "Just knock on the connecting door when you guys are done." With that, the two of them retreated to Room 216, closing the door behind them.

Jordan looked at the three remaining women, a mix of nervousness and profound gratitude on his face. Aubrie gave him a reassuring smile. "Ready for your first real health class?"

Aubrie moved first, her demeanor calm and entirely devoid of the charged tension that usually accompanied such vulnerability. With practiced efficiency, she unbuttoned her shirt and stepped out of her jeans, leaving them neatly folded on the edge of the bed. Madison and Zoe followed suit, their expressions reflecting only supportive patience.

Jordan sat frozen in his chair, his hands gripping the armrests. He had never seen a woman unclothed, and the sheer reality of it, standing right in front of him, was overwhelming. But there was no seduction in their posture; they stood before him with the objective openness of a medical demonstration.

"It's okay to look, Jordan," Aubrie said softly, sitting on the edge of the mattress, directly across from his chair. She picked up the heavy textbook from the desk and laid it open on her lap, pointing to the anatomical diagram of the female reproductive system.

"The first thing to understand," Madison began, stepping beside Aubrie and adopting the steady, informative tone of a seasoned clinical lecturer, "is that the external female genitalia is collectively referred to as the vulva. The drawings in your book separate everything with distinct lines and bright colors, but in reality, the epithelial and mucosal tissues flow continuously into one another."

Jordan leaned forward slightly, his eyes moving between the diagram on the glossy page and the physical reality in front of him.

Madison gently guided his hand. "These outer folds of adipose tissue and skin," she explained, her touch clinical and precise, "are the labia majora. They contain sebaceous glands and protect the more delicate inner structures."

Jordan traced the line in the book, then looked back, his expression one of intense, respectful concentration. The paralyzing mystery that had been enforced upon him for years was slowly unraveling into simple, biological truth.

"And medial to those," Zoe added, leaning over to point at the diagram, "are the labia minora. These are the inner, hairless mucosal folds. The textbook makes them look perfectly symmetrical, but as you can visually verify, biological morphology is highly varied in size and shape. Anteriorly, they merge to form the prepuce, and they serve to enclose and protect the urethral and vaginal openings."

Aubrie guided Jordan's index finger, placing it gently at the very top apex of the structure. "This," she said calmly, "is the clitoral hood, or prepuce, which protects the glans clitoris. The glans is the external, visible portion of a much larger internal erectile network. While the textbook focuses heavily on the reproductive cycle, this specific structure's primary biological function is somatosensory."

Madison took over the instruction, her voice steady as she directed his attention downward. "The space enclosed by the labia minora is called the vulval vestibule. Directly below the clitoris is the external urethral orifice, or meatus. It is the terminus of the urinary tract, used exclusively for the expulsion of urine."

She pointed just below it. "And posterior to the urethra is the vaginal opening, known medically as the introitus. This is the entrance to the vaginal canal, a fibromuscular tube connecting the vulva to the internal cervix and uterus. It serves as the outflow tract for menstruation and the birth canal. Finally, the area of tissue separating the introitus from the anus is the perineum."

Jordan exhaled a long breath, a look of profound understanding washing over his features. The terrifying, forbidden unknown his mother had weaponized against him was suddenly just... anatomy. Tissue, function, physiology, and biology. There was no arousal, no crossing of boundaries—only an intense, grounding focus.

"It all connects," he murmured, looking at the three women who trusted him enough to demystify the world. "The diagram makes it look like separate, disparate mechanical parts, but it's a single, continuous, integrated physiological system."

"Exactly," Aubrie said with a warm, encouraging smile.

An hour later, the lesson—one conducted with more gentle patience and clinical respect than any university professor could have managed—was over. The atmosphere in Room 214 was calm and studious. The textbook was closed on the desk, its mysteries now significantly clearer.

Jordan let out a long breath, a look of genuine understanding on his face. "Thank you," he said, his voice thick with an emotion that went far beyond academics. "That makes so much more sense than the drawings."

"Any time," Madison said with a cheerful smile as the women began to pull their clothes back on. "That's what we're here for."

Aubrie walked to the connecting door, knocking gently. "We're done!" she called out.

A moment later, the door opened and Hannah and Lauren rejoined the group, their expressions open and friendly, with no hint of awkwardness. The family was whole again.

"Well," Zoe said, glancing at the clock on the wall. "After that successful study session, I believe my stomach is requesting dinner."

A chorus of agreement went through the room, and the six of them headed out together towards the cafeteria, their bond of trust now deeper and more unique than ever before.
