# Part 6: Diverging Paths

The group of six exited the dormitory together, a wave of cheerful chatter washing over them as they walked out into the cool evening air. The decision to get dinner had been unanimous, and the mood was light.

"Alright, guys," Hannah said, giving a happy wave as they reached the main campus path. "Mark is waiting for me by the library. I'll catch up with you all later tonight!" She broke off from the group, heading down a branching brick walkway.

"Tom and I are meeting up, too," Lauren added, feeling happy and looking forward to seeing him. "I'll see you back at the suite!" She gave a wave of her own and headed in the opposite direction, toward the main quad.

That left the remaining four—Jordan, Aubrie, Madison, and Zoe—to continue their journey toward the distant, welcoming lights of the cafeteria.

Lauren made her way to the specific bench on the quad where she and Tom always met. It was unoccupied. "He's probably just running a few minutes late," she murmured to herself, pulling out her phone to scroll through social media as she waited. Five minutes passed, then ten. The steady stream of students walking by began to thin as people settled in for dinner.

A small frown touched her lips. This wasn't like him. She sent a quick text: Hey, I'm at our bench. Where are you?

She waited another five minutes, her eyes scanning the quad. No reply. A small pang of disappointment hit her. She tried not to feel annoyed; maybe he got held up by a professor, or his phone died. Shrugging it off with a sigh, she decided she wasn't going to wait around all night. Her friends were waiting.

Back in the bustling cafeteria, Jordan, Aubrie, Madison, and Zoe were just sitting down with their trays when Lauren walked up to their table.

"Hey, I thought you were eating with Tom?" Madison asked, looking up in surprise.

"Yeah, he must have gotten held up or something," Lauren said, trying to sound more casual than she felt as she set her bag down. "He wasn't there and he's not answering his phone. Oh well," she finished with a slightly forced smile, "his loss! That means I don't have to share my fries."

The group welcomed her back without a second thought, Aubrie giving her a comforting smile as they all began to eat. To them, it was just a minor change of plans. As they settled into their meal, the disappointment Lauren felt began to fade, replaced by the easy camaraderie of her new family. There was no sign of Tom anywhere in the crowded dining hall, but she pushed the thought aside and dove into the conversation.

"So, first two days in the books," Madison said through a mouthful of salad. "Verdict?"

"My Advanced Calculus professor is sufficiently challenging," Zoe reported. "The syllabus is well-structured."

"Dr. Alistair is even better than I hoped," Aubrie said, her eyes lighting up as she looked at Jordan. "He actually explained the Schwarzschild radius in a way that made perfect sense."

"The lab is going to be amazing," Jordan agreed. "I saw the equipment list."

"My art history class seems cool," Lauren offered, genuinely enjoying herself now. "Lots of reading, but the paintings are incredible."

As they talked and laughed, Lauren, who was sitting next to Jordan, leaned her head on his shoulder for a moment as Madison told a funny story. It felt nice. Comfortable. A small, sad part of her wished it was Tom's shoulder she was leaning on, that he was here sharing this moment. But he was probably just stuck in a meeting with an instructor, his phone dead. He'd have a good reason. She was sure of it. For now, this was more than okay. This was good. She gave Jordan's arm a friendly, appreciative squeeze, and he smiled back, the five of them a happy little island in the noisy room.
