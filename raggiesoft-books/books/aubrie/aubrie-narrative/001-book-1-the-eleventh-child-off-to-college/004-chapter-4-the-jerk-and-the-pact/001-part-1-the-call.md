# Part 1: The Call

The first official day of classes, a Monday, had been a whirlwind. That evening, the six suitemates were scattered throughout the suite, a comfortable, domestic energy settling over them as they worked on assignments. In Room 216, Lauren was on a video call with her boyfriend, Tom, happy to finally connect after a busy day.

"...and my last lecture finished late, so I had to sprint to the dining hall before it closed," Lauren finished, laughing. "It was a crazy day. How was yours?"

"Boring without you," Tom said, smiling. "So, how are the roommates? Not driving you crazy yet?"

"Never! They're the best," Lauren said, her affection for her new friends genuine. "Actually, the most unbelievable thing happened. You're not going to believe it. One of our suitemates... is a guy."

She said it with a laugh, trying to convey the sheer strangeness of the situation. But on the screen, Tom’s smile tightened. "A guy? What are you talking about, Lauren?" The warmth in his voice vanished, replaced by a sharp, suspicious edge.

Lauren’s stomach dropped, but she pushed forward, determined to make him understand the truth. "Okay, just listen, because it's a wild story. Housing made a huge mistake with his name—it's Aubrey, but he goes by Jordan—and they accidentally assigned him to our suite."

"So they moved him, right?" Tom interrupted, his tone demanding.

"Well, no," Lauren said, trying to keep her voice calm. "He's in a wheelchair, and it's the only accessible room on the floor. And honestly, Tom... something amazing happened. He met my roommate, Aubrie. And they have just... completely fallen for each other. It was like... instant. I've never seen anything like it. They're perfect together. So, we all just decided to make it work. It's not weird, it's actually really, really sweet."

She looked at him earnestly, hoping he would see the beauty in the strange situation. Instead, he stared at her, his face twisting into a mask of disbelief.

"Fallen for each other?" he scoffed. "You've been there for what, three days? You expect me to believe in some fairytale love-at-first-sight story? You think I'm an idiot?"

"It's not a fairytale, Tom, it's the truth! It happened!" Lauren insisted, her voice rising with frantic energy. "If you could see them together, you'd understand!"

"Oh, I think I understand perfectly," he sneered, his voice dripping with a venom that shocked her. "It sounds like a convenient story you all cooked up to make me sound like the bad guy for questioning it. What’s really going on over there, Lauren? Is this guy just making his way through the whole suite? Is that it?"

"What? NO!" Lauren cried, horrified by the accusation. "How could you even say that? I love YOU!"

"It doesn't sound like it," Tom spat, his face a mask of cold fury. "It sounds like you're in some weird cult, and I'm not sticking around to find out what happens next. We're done."

And the screen went black. Lauren stared at the blank phone, her breath catching in a choked sob. A moment later, the screen lit up again, not with a call, but with a text message from Tom, stark and cruel against the dark background: Meet me in the lobby. I’m giving you your stuff back. NOW.
