# Part 2: The Humiliation

Lauren walked into the main dorm lobby like a zombie, her heart pounding. Tom was standing there by the vending machines, holding a small box of her things. Several students were milling about, chatting and studying. Many of them were Bennett Hall residents who knew the "worst-kept secret" of the dorm: the story about the clerical error in Suite 214/216 was completely true.

When Tom saw her, he didn't lower his voice. He raised it.

"Here's your stuff," he said loudly, shoving the box into her chest so hard she stumbled back. "I don't want to be part of your weird little 'sweet' fairytale anymore."

A few heads turned. Lauren flinched, clutching the box. "Tom, stop it. Please, don't do this here".

"What, you don't want people to hear the truth?" he shouted, his voice dripping with malicious glee as he saw he was gathering an audience. "You want me to believe that story? That some guy just shows up and instantly falls in love with your roommate? How dumb do you think I am, Lauren?"

"It's the truth!" she whispered, her plea lost in the cavernous room.

Before she could say more, two male students who had been waiting by the elevator stepped forward. "Hey, man, she *is* telling the truth," one of them said, trying to de-escalate. "We live here. We all know the story. It's legit".

Tom just shoved him aside. "Mind your own business!" he snapped. He turned his fury back to Lauren.

"It's a load of crap!" he bellowed. Now everyone was looking, their conversations stopping. "Tell me, what's really going on? Is your whole suite just passing him around? Is that the deal you've got going on in your little love shack?"

The accusation, so vile and baseless, struck her with the force of a physical blow. A collective gasp seemed to ripple through the lobby.

"You're a jerk!" a female student yelled from a nearby couch, standing up. "She's not lying! We all know them, and they're good people!"

Tom just sneered at her. "Get lost! This doesn't concern you!". The female student, flushing with anger at being dismissed, sat back down, immediately pulling out her phone to text her roommate: *'You will NOT believe the scene Tom is making right now. He's being a total pig to that girl from 214...'*.

He wasn't just breaking up with her; he was trying to ruin her, to paint her as something ugly and cheap, all to soothe his own pathetic, insecure ego.

He saw the utter devastation on her face and sneered, delivering the final blow. "Have fun with that. I'm sure the whole campus will be interested to hear all about it".

Lauren couldn't breathe. The weight of all the staring eyes, the poison in his words, the shattering of her heart—it was too much. Without another word, she turned and fled, not even waiting for the elevator, bursting through the stairwell door as the sound of his cruel, triumphant laughter echoed behind her.
