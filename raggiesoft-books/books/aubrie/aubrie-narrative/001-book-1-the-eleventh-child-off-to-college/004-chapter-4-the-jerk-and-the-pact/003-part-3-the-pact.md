# Part 3: The Pact

Instantly, all four of her suitemates were there, surrounding her. Madison and Zoe sat on either side of her bed, rubbing her back, while Hannah stood nearby, her expression full of helpless empathy. "He's a jerk, Lauren. An absolute pig," Madison said fiercely. "He's not worth a single one of your tears," Zoe added, her usual pragmatism replaced by a protective anger. "You can and will find someone so much better."

Jordan had heard the commotion from his room. He wheeled himself through the connecting doorway and stopped, his heart aching at the sight of Lauren’s pain. This, he understood. This feeling of being worthless, of being hurt by someone who was supposed to care. He watched as the girls offered their comfort, and then, he acted.

He moved his chair to the side of her bed. "Lauren?" he said softly. She looked up, her face tear-streaked and blotchy. He stretched out his arms. "Can I… can I give you a hug?"

With a fresh sob, she launched herself off the bed and into his embrace, burying her face in his shoulder. He held her tightly, just letting her cry. After a moment, he gently guided her to sit on the edge of his bed in Room 214, pulling her back into the hug. He didn't offer words; he just offered his presence, a solid, unwavering anchor in her storm.

The other four women had followed, now gathered in the doorway of his room, watching the scene with gentle expressions. After Lauren’s sobs subsided into sniffles, it was Aubrie who spoke, her voice thoughtful. "Zoe's right," she began, looking around the room. "You deserve someone so much better. Someone kind and gentle, who would never, ever treat you like that." Her eyes landed on Jordan, who was still holding Lauren’s hand. A radical idea, born from the unique, unconditional love of their suite family, sparked in her mind. "Why not Jordan?" she asked simply.

The room went silent. Lauren looked up, confused. Madison and Zoe stared at Aubrie, shocked. Aubrie continued, her belief in her own strange logic absolute. "I know. It sounds crazy. But think about it." She looked at Madison and Zoe directly. "I see how you look at him. I know you both really like him. I… I have a huge crush on him." Her confession was open, honest, and completely without shame. "He is the kindest person any of us have ever met. Why should only one of us get to be with him? Why should we be jealous of each other?"

She turned her gaze to Lauren. "You're hurting. You need someone good. He's right here." Then, she looked at Jordan, her eyes full of a love so profound it was almost startling. "I wouldn't care if you made out with Lauren, or Zoe, or Madison. I would still want to make out with you, too."

The air crackled with the sheer audacity of her proposal. It defied every conventional rule of dating. Yet, in the context of their strange, fiercely loyal family, it made a bizarre kind of sense.

Madison was the first to break the silence. "You'd really be okay with that?" Aubrie nodded. "I want everyone I care about to be happy. I don't see why we have to choose." Zoe, the pragmatist, was stunned, but a slow smile spread across her face. The idea of not having to compete, of simply being able to like him without conflict… it was brilliant. Lauren looked from Aubrie, to Madison, to Zoe, and finally to Jordan. The kindness radiating from him was a physical force. The idea of being with someone like that, after Tom’s cruelty, was a lifeline. Hannah, ever the supportive friend, smiled. "Well, I still have my boyfriend, so I'll just be his best friend and official hug-giver, but honestly? It sounds perfect for you guys."

And so, in a dorm room, surrounded by five women who had become his entire world, a pact was made. It was agreed. Jordan was allowed to be romantic with all the single women of the suite: with Aubrie, whose crush was a fairytale in the making; with Lauren, who needed kindness to heal her broken heart; with Madison, his affectionate best friend; and with Zoe, who really, really liked him. It wasn't about cheating or possession. It was about care, about healing, and about sharing a love that was big enough for all of them.
