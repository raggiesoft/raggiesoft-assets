# Part 3: The Good Faith Offer

A few days later, after the first chaotic energy of move-in had settled, a knock came at the door of Room 214. The six suitemates, who had been awkwardly trying to study around each other, looked up.

It was Kelsey, the Hall Director, but her expression was much less weary than it had been on Saturday.

"Hi everyone," she said with a genuine smile. "Do you all have a minute?"

The group gathered, curious.

"I went to my boss, the Director of Housing," Kelsey began, "and I explained the... unique situation here. And I explained how incredibly cooperative and mature you all have been in handling a clerical error that was 100% our fault."

She continued, "First, as a formal apology and a thank you from the university, we have added two hundred and fifty dollars in meal plan credits to each of your student accounts".

A collective gasp of surprise and excitement went through the room.

"Are you serious?" Madison said, her eyes wide. "That's... a lot of food."

"We felt it was the least we could do for the inconvenience," Kelsey said. "But second, and this is the bigger part, my director has authorized me to make you a formal offer."

She looked at the six of them. "We know that with the housing crisis, the lottery for sophomore year is going to be incredibly stressful. In light of what happened, we are offering all six of you a guaranteed housing contract for next year. You will get to bypass the freshman housing lottery entirely when selection opens in February".

This was the real prize. The relief in the room was palpable. Their single biggest stressor for next year was suddenly, completely gone.

"So we're all guaranteed a spot?" Zoe asked, her analytical mind immediately seeing the value. "We can all stay on campus?"

"You are all guaranteed a contract," Kelsey confirmed. "It's the best we can do to thank you for making this situation work".

They all immediately, and gratefully, accepted.

Kelsey smiled, relieved. "Excellent. I'll have the official letters sent to your student email accounts. Thank you again, all of you."

She left, and the moment the door clicked shut, a small cheer went up. They had survived their first week, gotten a massive food bonus, and now, their biggest worry for next year was already solved. They were set.
