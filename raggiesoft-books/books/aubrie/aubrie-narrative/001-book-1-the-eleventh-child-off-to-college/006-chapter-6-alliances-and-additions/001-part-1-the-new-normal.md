# Part 1: The New Normal

A few days passed, and a new, gentle rhythm established itself in the suite. The pact, born from trauma, was now a quiet, living thing. It was evident in the small moments. Lauren, slowly healing, would rest her head on Jordan’s shoulder while they watched a movie. Madison would bring him a hot chocolate just the way he liked it and give him an affectionate kiss on the cheek. Zoe, in moments of uncharacteristic boldness, would hold his hand while they all talked. And through it all, Aubrie would watch, her love for Jordan so deep and secure that it manifested as joy, not jealousy, at seeing him so cherished.

Mark, Hannah’s boyfriend, had become a welcome and regular fixture. He fit seamlessly into their strange dynamic, his easy-going nature a perfect complement to the group. He’d bring pizza, help Jordan with a hard-to-reach book, and talk sports with whoever was listening, his steady, normal relationship with Hannah serving as a happy, grounding force for everyone. He thought Tom was a world-class loser and was fiercely glad Lauren had found a soft place to land.
