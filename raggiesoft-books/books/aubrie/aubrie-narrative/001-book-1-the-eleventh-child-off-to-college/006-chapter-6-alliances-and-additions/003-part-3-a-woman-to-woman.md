# Part 3: A Woman-to-Woman

The two of them sat on a bench in the quiet campus quad, the autumn leaves rustling around them. Sarah took a shaky breath, her hands clasped tightly in her lap.

"I broke up with him," she said, her voice trembling slightly.

Lauren looked at her, confused. "You... what?"

"Tom," Sarah clarified. "I broke up with him this morning. I... I found out last night. While it was happening."

"What do you mean, *while* it was happening?"

"My roommate, Jessica, was in the lobby when you... when Tom met you". Sarah’s voice was low, laced with a shared humiliation. "She knows Tom is my boyfriend. She texted me. She... she sent me everything, Lauren. Every horrible thing he said to you".

"Oh my god," Lauren whispered, her hand flying to her mouth.

"When Jessica came back up to our room, she was pale," Sarah continued, her voice hardening. "She told me everything she saw. How he screamed at you. How he called you 'obsessed' just to cover his tracks, and how he was trying to publicly humiliate you to cover up the fact that he was cheating on me".

"He tried to lie, of course," she let out a short, humorless laugh. "I confronted him this morning at The Daily Grind. He's a bad liar. He tried to spin this whole story about *you* making moves on *him*. I told him I knew the truth."

She turned to face Lauren, her eyes shining with unshed tears. "I just... I needed to find you," she said, her voice cracking. "I needed to look you in the eye and say I'm so sorry. He played us both. He made us both his victims, and that's not your fault. It's his".

Tears streamed down Lauren’s face, but for the first time, they were tears of validation and relief, not just pain. "He told me..." she choked out, "he told me I was the affair".

"You weren't," Sarah said fiercely, her voice full of conviction. "You were a real person he had real feelings for, and he was too much of a coward to be honest with his long-term girlfriend. We were both his real relationship, and we were both his lie. That's on him".

They talked for over an hour, comparing the stories, the excuses, the lies. They found a powerful, unexpected bond in their shared experience of being deceived by the same man. When they stood up to leave, they weren't adversaries; they were friends.
