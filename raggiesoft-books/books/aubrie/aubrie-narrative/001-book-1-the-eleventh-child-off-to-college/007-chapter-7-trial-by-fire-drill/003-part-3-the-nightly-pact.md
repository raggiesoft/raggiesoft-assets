# Part 3: The Nightly Pact

Back in the relative quiet of the suite, the adrenaline from the fire drill began to fade, leaving behind a shared sense of exhaustion and camaraderie. The six of them were huddled in Room 214, wrapped in blankets, sipping from mugs of hot chocolate Madison had insisted on making.

"I still can't believe they do that in college," Lauren said, shaking her head. "It's barbaric."

"What matters is that we got through it," Zoe stated, her gaze resting on Jordan and Aubrie. "Your reactions were understandable, but your ability to trust us to help was key."

"It's good you have each other," Madison added, looking at the group as a whole. "That you have someone to... you know. After."

The unspoken thought hung in the air, referring to the nights Aubrie and Lauren had spent being comforted by Jordan. It was Aubrie, as always, who gave voice to the idea that was forming in everyone's mind.

"It's not just for a crisis," she said softly, looking at Jordan with a loving smile before turning to the other girls. "That feeling... of being safe, of not being alone in the dark... We all deserve that. And Jordan deserves it, too."

Lauren nodded emphatically. "That night... after everything with Tom was revealed... sleeping next to him was the only reason I got through it. Just knowing someone kind was there."

"So, we should make it official," Zoe said, her pragmatic mind seeking clarity and order. "A defined arrangement ensures fairness and prevents any potential for miscommunication."

"It doesn't have to be a strict, laminated schedule," Madison laughed. "But yeah. We should all agree. No jealousy, no weirdness. If one of us needs comfort, or just wants to be close to him, they can. It's an open invitation."

"It is," Jordan said, his voice quiet but firm, making it clear this was his decision as much as theirs. "I... I like it. It makes me feel safe, too. Knowing someone is there. I'm okay with... with all of you."

Hannah smiled, taking a sip of her cocoa. "You all know I think this is the most beautiful, amazing thing. My hugs are available 24/7," she said warmly. "But you also know that when Mark spends the night, he and I have our own space. That's our thing."

"Of course," Aubrie said, reaching out to squeeze Hannah's hand. "And that's perfect. But for the rest of us..." Her eyes scanned the room. "And for Sarah, too. When she's here and needs a safe place to land, the offer extends to her."

There it was. The pact, now fully formed and articulated. An open invitation for Aubrie, Madison, Zoe, Lauren, and their new friend Sarah to share their lives—and their nights—with Jordan, based not on a rigid schedule, but on a fluid, intuitive understanding of who needed comfort most. Mark and Hannah had their own solid, loving partnership. The rest of them had their own unique constellation, with Jordan at its warm, steady center.

The group understood the university's overnight guest policy would be respected, for the most part. Mark and Sarah would sign in when they could, but the suitemates' complete trust in them meant the "undocumented guest" loophole was always an option. Their family had its own rules, and trust was the most important one.

Zoe nodded, a look of peaceful resolution on her face. "The parameters are clear and mutually agreed upon."

Madison grinned. "Best. Suite. Ever."
