# Part 4: The First Night of the Pact

As the last of the hot chocolate was finished, a comfortable, sleepy silence fell over the group. The adrenaline from the fire drill had been replaced by a deep exhaustion.

"Alright," Zoe said, standing up and stretching. "As much as this has been a bonding experience, my bed is calling."

"So..." Madison began, her voice soft as she looked first at Jordan, then around at the others. "Who's turn is it tonight?"

The question hung in the air, simple and kind. It was the first official invocation of their new pact. Aubrie, from her spot on her own lower bunk, just smiled and looked at Madison. The message was clear: *it's your turn if you want it.*

"I'll take tonight," Madison said, her usual cheerfulness tempered by a gentle sincerity. "If that's okay with you, Jordan?"

Jordan looked at her, then at Aubrie, who gave him a reassuring nod. The trust in the room was a palpable, living thing. "Okay," he whispered.

With that, the suite began its nightly wind-down routine. Hannah and Lauren headed back to Room 216. In Room 214, Aubrie curled up in her own assigned bed, pulling the covers up with a contented sigh. There was no jealousy, no hint of possessiveness; only peace. This was her idea, after all, and seeing it in practice was its own reward.

Jordan transferred from his chair into his bed. A moment later, Madison, now in her pajamas, padded over. The Twin XL bed was a narrow space, but it was their space.

"Scooch," she whispered playfully.

He smiled and shifted over, making room. Madison slid in behind him, the warmth of her body a welcome comfort. She settled in as the big spoon, wrapping an arm securely around his waist and pulling him close against her, her chin resting near his shoulder. It was a mirror of the way Aubrie had first held him—an embrace that was purely about safety, warmth, and the simple, profound statement that he was not alone. It was the kind of closeness Mark and Hannah shared in their own bed, built on a foundation of absolute trust.

Lying there, spooned close with one of his best friends, with his girlfriend sleeping peacefully just a few feet away, Jordan felt a sense of calm he had never thought possible. This was his new reality. After a lifetime of nightmares and loneliness, his nights were now filled with the promise of a loving, trusting presence, a rotating guard of friends and girlfriends ensuring he never had to feel scared in the dark again. He closed his eyes and, for the first time since the alarm had sounded, felt truly safe.
