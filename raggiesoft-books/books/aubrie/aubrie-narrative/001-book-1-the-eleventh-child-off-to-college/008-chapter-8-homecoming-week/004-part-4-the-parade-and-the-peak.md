# Part 4: The Parade and the Peak

Friday evening, September 26th, was the main Homecoming Parade, and the "hype" was at its absolute peak. The Group of 8 didn't even need a bus. The parade route ran right along the edge of campus, just a short walk from Bennett Hall. They found a perfect, accessible spot on the sidewalk of Business US-460 (South Main Street), giving Jordan a front-row seat.

This was yet another first for Jordan. He had never seen a real parade. The sheer, unbridled joy of the community was infectious. He watched, wide-eyed, as the massive, elaborate floats built by different student organizations drifted by. The CPI Marching Forgers were a thunderous wall of maroon and orange sound, and the football team, riding on a flatbed truck, looked like invincible heroes.

The procession had started at The Crucible, CPI's massive football stadium. It moved down Beamer Way, took a right onto Washington Street, and then made the big left onto Main Street, passing right in front of them. The entire group was cheering, Mark and Madison the loudest, as the parade finally made the left turn onto Alumni Mall. It passed in front of the student union (where Newman Hall was) and headed for its end point at The Commonwealth Green.

As the last float passed, the sky went dark. The end of the parade was the signal for the main event: the fireworks.

Jordan watched the explosions of color burst high over the campus, their light reflecting in his eyes. He was surrounded by his family, his "safe people," in the heart of his new home. He was completely bought in. He was a CPI Forger, and he was proud. Tomorrow, he would watch his team—the \#3 team in the nation—defend that pride against "Norfolk." He couldn't wait.
