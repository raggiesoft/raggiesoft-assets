# Part 6: The Pre-Game

The Group of 8 moved with the massive, pulsing sea of maroon and orange toward the stadium gates. The roar of the crowd and the distant sound of the Marching Forgers' drumline was a physical vibration. Jordan, wheeling himself, and Aubrie, at his side, both wore their noise-canceling headphones, which muffled the sensory assault into a manageable, exciting rumble.

They found their accessible seating area, a perfect, clear view of the field. The energy in The Crucible was unlike anything Jordan had ever experienced. It was the peak of his Homecoming Week, his full initiation into the CPI Forger family.

"Alright, first things first," Mark announced, his eyes gleaming. "We can't watch a football game without stadium food. Who wants what?"

This was another first for Jordan. He had never had "concession stand food". "What... do they have?" he asked, looking at the massive, chaotic lines.

"They have *everything*," Madison grinned. She, Mark, and Hannah volunteered for the food run. They returned minutes later, their arms loaded. They handed Jordan a hot dog with everything on it and a giant souvenir cup of soda. He took a bite, and a look of pure, uncomplicated joy spread across his face.

As they ate, the stadium's massive PA system crackled to life.

"GOOD AFTERNOON, FORGER NATION!" the announcer's voice boomed, echoing across the stadium. "AND WELCOME TO YOUR 2014 HOMECOMING!"

The stadium *erupted*.

Jordan, anchored by his family, felt a surge of adrenaline. The Marching Forgers took the field, a massive, precise wave of sound and color. Mark, the group's sports fanatic, was explaining everything to Jordan, pointing out the players as they warmed up, his voice full of absolute confidence.

"See that quarterback, J? He's a Heisman candidate. The 'Norfolk' defense hasn't seen anything like him. This isn't going to be a game; it's going to be target practice."

Jordan nodded, feeding off Mark's energy. He looked at the scoreboard, which flashed CPI: \#3 next to VDU: Unranked. He looked at Aubrie, who was on her feet cheering, her loyalty to her new home absolute.

This was it. The stands were full. The pre-game hype was at its peak. The announcer was listing CPI's accomplishments. Jordan gripped the armrests of his chair, his heart full. He was a soldier, and his new, invincible army was about to take the field.
