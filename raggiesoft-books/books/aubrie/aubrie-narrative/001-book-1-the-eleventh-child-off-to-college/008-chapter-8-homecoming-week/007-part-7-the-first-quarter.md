# Part 7: The First Quarter

The captains met at midfield for the coin toss. The Crucible was a deafening sea of maroon and orange. CPI won the toss and, to a roar of approval, elected to receive.

"Here we go!" Mark yelled over the noise, clapping Jordan on the shoulder. "We're gonna score on the first drive!"

The opening kickoff sailed through the air, and the "Sports Rivalry War" was officially underway. CPI's Heisman-candidate quarterback trotted onto the field, the crowd chanting his name. The hype was real.

The first few plays were exactly what everyone expected. A 15-yard pass. A 10-yard run. The \#3-ranked Forgers were moving the ball with ease, marching right down the field. Jordan was on his feet (figuratively), his heart hammering with vicarious triumph.

Then, the drive stalled. The unranked VDU defense, which had looked so overwhelmed, suddenly held firm at their own 30-yard line. A 3rd-down pass was batted down at the line.

"It's fine!" Mark shouted. "This is an easy field goal! We'll take the points!"

The crowd quieted as CPI's kicker lined up for the "chip shot" field goal. The snap was good, the hold was good, but the kick... went wide left.

A collective, agonizing groan rippled through The Crucible.

"It's just a fluke! Bad wind!" Mark said, waving it off, but his confident smile was gone.

The VDU Monarchs ("Norfolk") took the field. They didn't look flashy. They just started to run the ball. 4 yards. 5 yards. 3 yards. It was a long, methodical, grinding drive that ate up the clock. The CPI defense, built for speed, looked confused by the slow, brute-force attack.

As the final seconds of the first quarter ticked away, the VDU running back punched the ball into the end zone. Touchdown.

The quarter ended. Jordan stared, horrified, at the massive scoreboard.

HOME: 0 VISITOR: 7

The VDU fan section was roaring. The rest of the stadium was in a state of stunned, angry silence. Jordan's "thrilled" feeling was gone, replaced by a cold, sharp knot of anxiety. This wasn't the plan. This wasn't how it was supposed to go. His mother's world was winning.
