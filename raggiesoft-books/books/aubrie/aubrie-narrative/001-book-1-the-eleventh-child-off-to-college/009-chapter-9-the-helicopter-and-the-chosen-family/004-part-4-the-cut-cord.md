# Part 4: The Cut Cord

An hour after his mother had stormed out of the lobby, a fragile peace had settled over the suite. The air was still thick with the residue of the confrontation, but it was overshadowed by a profound sense of relief. Jordan, emotionally drained but resolute, was surrounded by his friends, who were chattering quietly, trying to restore a sense of normalcy.

"Okay, I'm declaring this an official holiday," Madison announced cheerfully. "Jordan's Independence Day. And it requires celebratory pizza. A lot of it."

"I'll call it in," Jordan said, a small, tired smile on his face. He picked up his phone from his desk to dial the number for the local pizza place. He hit 'send.'

The call immediately failed, a "Call Ended" message flashing on the screen.

"Huh," he said, frowning. He tried again. The same result. He looked at the top of his screen and saw the cellular bars were completely empty, replaced by a "No Service" message. "That's weird. My phone says I have no service. I can't make a call."

"Try restarting it," Lauren suggested. "Sometimes mine does that."

Jordan restarted the phone, but when it powered back on, the result was the same. No service.

"That doesn't make any sense," Zoe said, pulling out her own phone. "We have the same provider, Jordan. Let me check." She glanced at her screen. "No, my service is fine. I have three bars, which is normal for in here."

She had just eliminated every logical possibility except one. The room went silent as the implication of her statement landed like a stone. Zoe looked at Jordan, her expression serious as she asked the question that was now on everyone's mind. "Jordan, who pays your cell phone bill?"

The answer was whispered, but it echoed in the quiet room. "My mother."

"That spiteful witch," Madison breathed, her face a mask of disbelief and fury. "She couldn't take you with her, so she cut you off. She actually cut off your phone."

The reality of the situation sank in. It wasn't a technical glitch; it was a calculated act of punishment. His mother's final attempt to control him by isolating him, by cutting his cord to the outside world.

"This is a significant safety issue," Zoe said immediately, her brain moving past anger and into problem-solving mode. "The building's WiFi is reliable, but when you are between buildings or anywhere on campus without a signal, you now have no means of communication. That's not acceptable."

"So what do we do?" Lauren asked, looking worried. "We can get him on a new plan, but that'll take a day or two."

"Then until we do, he can't be alone," Aubrie said, her voice filled with a fierce, protective certainty. "Ever. One of us has to be with him at all times when he's out of the suite."

"The buddy system," Madison agreed, nodding as the plan formed. "And we'll need a group chat. Whoever is with him just needs to send a quick text. 'Got Jordan, heading to the library,' or 'With J at the student union.' That way, if any of us need to reach him, we know exactly who to call." She pulled out her phone and immediately started creating a new group message, adding all the girls.

"I'll text Mark and Sarah and add them," Hannah said. "They'll be all in, you know they will."

They all looked at Jordan, who was staring at his now-neutered phone, a look of awe on his face. It was a pain. It was complicated and inconvenient. But it was also the most incredible display of love he had ever witnessed. His mother had tried to sever his connection to the world to punish him for choosing his new family. In doing so, she had only forced that family to wrap itself around him even more tightly, creating a web of communication and care so strong that nothing, not even her, could ever break through it.
