# Part 3: The Christiansburg Thanksgiving Plan

The Monday of Fall Break, the group of eight was gathered in the dorm lobby for their weekly Wrestling Night tradition when the email from Jordan's mother landed like an ultimatum. The cheerful atmosphere evaporated as Zoe confirmed the university's policy: all residence halls would close for Thanksgiving break, from Wednesday at 1:00 PM to Sunday at 4:00 PM. The deadline was absolute, and Jordan had nowhere to go.

"Okay," Mark said, leaning forward, his voice serious. "So, he's not going home. Where can we all go?"

Zoe, ever the pragmatist, immediately took charge, her mind processing a simple, all-encompassing solution. "My parents live in Christiansburg," she stated calmly. "Their house is built on a hillside, and we have a large, finished basement. It has its own bathroom and a sliding glass door that opens directly to the backyard, so there are no stairs for Jordan to worry about."

She looked around at the entire group, her offer extending to everyone. "There's a pull-out sofa, and my parents have plenty of inflatable mattresses and sleeping bags. You can all come stay with us. All of you."

The offer was direct, simple, and incredibly generous. A wave of relief washed over the group.

Sarah, whose family lived in Radford, grinned. "My house is right there, but crashing in a basement with you guys sounds way more fun! I'm in!"

"Perfect!" Hannah said, beaming. "Mark and I can just hop on the bus to get to our parents' houses here in Blacksburg, and then we can get rides over. We're there."

With a single phone call, Zoe confirmed the plan. "My parents are in," she announced after hanging up. "My mom is actually excited. She says, and I quote, 'A full house for Thanksgiving! Wonderful!' She just needs a headcount for pies."

"Okay," Madison said, thinking through the practicalities. "So for helping Jordan with things like showering, it'll just be us girls, right?"

Mark laughed out loud, a warm, genuine sound. "Absolutely. Hannah, you guys do whatever you need to do to take care of your friend." He shook his head, a grin on his face. "Honestly, the fact that a guy like Tom would freak out about something so basic just proves what a loser he is. I trust you. I trust all of you." Hannah looked at him, her expression full of love and pride.

The plan was now solid. The entire suite family would decamp to Zoe's finished basement in Christiansburg. It would be a massive, chaotic, week-long slumber party. The pact of care would continue, with the women of the group taking turns ensuring Jordan was supported, and Mark being the perfect, trusting ally.

They quickly agreed to the final detail: they would all vacate the dorm on Tuesday night, a full day before his mother was scheduled to arrive. When she showed up on Wednesday morning to collect her son, she would be met with nothing but an empty, silent room. Her power had been completely and utterly neutralized by the unbreakable bond of his chosen family.
