# Part 1: Setting the Board

In the dorm lobby, the group huddled together, their successful Fall Break relay already feeling like a distant memory in the face of the new, urgent Thanksgiving deadline. After they had solidified the "Christiansburg Plan," it was Zoe who brought up the next logical step.

"Just leaving isn't a complete plan," she said, her tone serious. "Your mother is manipulative. When she finds you gone, her first move will be to use the university or the police as a tool. We need to control the narrative *before* she has a chance to create one."

She was right. It wasn't enough to run; they had to build a wall behind them.

That night, back in the quiet of the suite, Jordan, with Aubrie and Zoe looking over his shoulder, drafted a formal email.

To: Kelsey, Hall Director, Bennett Hall; Office of Housing and Residence Life\
From: Jordan Fuller\
Subject: Thanksgiving Break Plan - Jordan Fuller, Room 214

Dear Hall Director Kelsey and the Office of Housing,

This email is to formally notify you that I, Jordan Fuller (Student ID#...), will be spending the upcoming Thanksgiving holiday (Wednesday through Sunday) off-campus with friends. I am safe and will be well cared for.

I feel it is necessary to inform you that my mother, Aubrie Sinclair, may attempt to contact you or visit Bennett Hall under the incorrect assumption that I will be going home with her. She may refer to me by my legal first name, Aubrey. Please be advised that I do not wish to have contact with her at this time. She does not have my permission to enter my residence hall or be granted access to any of my student information.

Thank you for your understanding and for helping to protect my privacy.

He clicked send, the first brick in his wall of independence firmly set in place.

The next morning, he took the next step. With Mark and Hannah as his supportive escort, he went to the Campus Police station. He spoke calmly and clearly to the officer at the desk.

"My name is Jordan Fuller," he began. "I am a resident of Bennett Hall, and I need to make a voluntary statement. I am going to be staying with my friends' family in Christiansburg for the Thanksgiving break. However, I have reason to believe my mother, Aubrie Sinclair, may file a false missing persons report for me, likely using the name 'Aubrey Fuller.' I want it officially on record that I am not missing, I am safe, and I have voluntarily chosen not to have contact with her. My new emergency contacts are Daniel and Laura Brooks at this address..."

He provided all the necessary information. The officer listened patiently, nodding. This was not the first time they had dealt with a difficult family situation involving a student. "Thank you for being proactive, Jordan," the officer said kindly. "We'll put a note on file right now and we'll share it with the Blacksburg and Christiansburg police, as well as the county sheriff. You have a safe holiday."

Leaving the station, Jordan felt a weight lift from his shoulders. He hadn't just run. He had prepared. He had used his voice and his rights as an adult. Now, whatever his mother tried, she would find only closed doors.
