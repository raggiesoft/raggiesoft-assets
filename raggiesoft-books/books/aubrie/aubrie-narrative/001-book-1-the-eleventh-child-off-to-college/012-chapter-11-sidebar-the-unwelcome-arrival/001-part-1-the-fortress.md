# Part 1: The Fortress

Kelsey, the Hall Director of the notoriously old Eleanor Bennett Commonwealth Hall—affectionately known to generations of students as "The Old Lady on the Hill"—had been on alert since her meeting with Jordan. Understanding the potential for a confrontation, she had taken preemptive measures. On Monday, a building-wide email was sent to all residents reminding them that holding a secure door for any non-resident was a serious security violation.

Furthermore, on Wednesday morning, two uniformed campus police officers were stationed discreetly in the main lobby, ostensibly for a "pre-break safety presence," but in reality, they were there at Kelsey's request. The entire on-duty staff knew the protocol: be polite, be firm, and do not acknowledge the name "Aubrey Fuller."

It was into this carefully prepared environment that Aubrie Sinclair swept just before 10:00 AM, radiating a confidence that was about to be thoroughly dismantled. She marched directly to the front desk on the lower level.

"Good morning," she said. "I'm here to pick up my son, Aubrey Fuller, from Room 214."

The student worker gave a polite, practiced smile. "I'm sorry, ma'am, I don't have a resident listed by that name in our system."

Furious at being stonewalled, Aubrie spotted her opportunity. A student was approaching the secure doors to the residential hallway. As the student swiped his card and the lock clicked, she rushed forward. "Oh, excuse me, dear, could you hold that?"

The student looked at her, then at the large, prominent sign on the door that read "DO NOT HOLD DOOR FOR OTHERS." He remembered the hall-wide email from two days ago. He gave her an apologetic look.

"I'm sorry, ma'am. We're not allowed to," he said, and let the heavy door swing shut, the lock clicking firmly into place.

Aubrie stood there, stunned and humiliated. Her manipulation had failed.

"Ma'am." The voice was calm and professional. She turned to see Kelsey standing there, with the two campus police officers now positioned a few feet behind her, their presence casual but unmissable.

"This is outrageous!" Aubrie sputtered. "I am being denied access to my son!"

"As my staff informed you," Kelsey said, her tone leaving no room for argument, "we are legally prohibited by FERPA from discussing any student's information. And as you've just seen, our residents take security in The Old Lady very seriously. We look out for each other here."

Kelsey gestured subtly towards the officers. "These officers are here to ensure a safe and orderly closing of the hall for the Thanksgiving break. I am sure you don't want a disturbance that would require their direct intervention."

The message was crystal clear. Every path was blocked. Faced with the choice of leaving on her own free will or being escorted out by campus police, Aubrie Sinclair made the only decision she could. With a look of pure venom at the staff who had so thoroughly defeated her, she spun on her heel and stormed out of the building.
