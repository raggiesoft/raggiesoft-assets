# Part 2: The Drive

The three-car convoy pulled out onto Geneva Street in a smooth, coordinated line. Inside Daniel Brooks's minivan, the atmosphere was thick with tension. Laura, in the front passenger seat, kept her eyes fixed on the side mirror. In the back, Madison, Zoe, and Lauren were uncharacteristically quiet, their gazes flicking to the windows.

Daniel reached the end of the road, where he could either turn right to get on the U.S. 460 highway for a direct, five-minute shot to the shopping center, or he could go straight and take the longer, winding route of Business U.S. 460 through town. He didn't hesitate. He went straight. He would sacrifice speed for unpredictability, hoping to shake off anyone who might be following.

He chose the longer, thirteen-minute local route, staying on U.S. 11 to wind his way through the heart of Christiansburg on Business U.S. 460. It was slower, with more stoplights and town traffic, but it was also far less predictable. It offered dozens of turns and opportunities to spot a tail, a trade-off he was more than willing to make for a greater sense of security.

In the second-row captain's chair, Jordan's heart was pounding against his ribs. Every passing sedan, every dark-colored SUV, was a potential threat in his mind. He was on high alert, scanning the traffic behind them for the gray car, for a spy who was, at that moment, likely reading the morning paper over a cup of coffee miles away, the Brooks family already a forgotten case file.

But Jordan didn't know that. All he knew was a deep, primal fear. He felt a desperate, overwhelming need for comfort, a craving he couldn't explain in words but felt in every nerve ending. In his mind, the name "Aubrie" had split into two opposing forces: one was the source of this terror, the woman he had fled. The other was the antidote.

There was only one Aubrie he wanted right now. And her name was Aubrie Jean Kelly.

His hand trembled as he reached across the small space between their chairs. She met him instantly, her hand taking his, her grip warm and firm. That was all the invitation he needed. He leaned into her, resting his head against her shoulder. She responded by wrapping her arm around him, pulling him into a tight, secure hug right there in the seat. He closed his eyes, focusing on the steady rhythm of her breathing, and let the feeling of safety she provided wash over him.

A few minutes later, the convoy signaled and turned into the chaotic parking lot of the Spradlin Farms shopping center. Daniel found a spot and put the van in park, but no one moved. He picked up his phone.

"Caleb, Mark, report. Anything?" he said into the "Jordan Comms" chat.

Caleb's voice came back instantly through the car's speakers. "Negative, Dad. We're clear. Had a clear view the whole way. No sign of anything."

Mark's voice followed. "All clear back here, too. We're good."

A collective sigh of relief went through the minivan. Daniel's evasive route had worked. Unbeknownst to him, it had worked because there was no one to evade. He turned around in his seat, a look of grim satisfaction on his face.

"Okay, team," he said. "We're clear. Let's go get a phone."
