# Part 4: The Big Box Gauntlet

The convoy arrived at the even more chaotic Blue Tech parking lot. The "outer perimeter"—Caleb, Owen, and Mark—did a thorough sweep before giving the all-clear. As Jordan transferred into his wheelchair, Hannah stepped forward.

"My turn," she said, giving him a firm, grounding hug before taking the handles of his chair. Mark looked on, a look of immense pride on his face. He was proud of his girlfriend for being so compassionate and proud of his friend for trusting her. Across the way, Aubrie watched the same exchange and felt an identical sense of peaceful certainty.

The moment they entered the store, a wall of noise and light hit them. Blue Tech was a sensory nightmare. Jordan tensed immediately, and even Aubrie grew pale. Zoe's sister, Megan, acted fast, helping Jordan get his new mPods Pro into his ears to muffle the cacophony. Seeing Aubrie was also struggling, Lauren smoothly took over pushing the wheelchair from Hannah, allowing Hannah to drop back and offer her own quiet support to Aubrie.

Daniel guided the group through the thickest crowds. "Laptops are in the back-left corner. Let's make a beeline," he commanded. He was scanning constantly, his head on a swivel. He briefly tensed when he saw a man in a different colored shirt seemingly watching them, only to relax when the man turned, revealing the "Blue Tech Repair Squad" logo on his back. Daniel let out a quiet breath, chiding himself for being jumpy over a threat that, unbeknownst to him, wasn't even there.

They reached the laptop section, and Mark took the lead. "Alright, Jordan, let's find you a beast," he said, his eyes scanning the displays. He pointed to a sleek, powerful machine. "This one. The new Pinnacle Precisionist 5000. Great processor, dedicated graphics card... this will handle anything the university throws at you." He looked at the spec sheet. "Ah, crap."

"What is it?" Jordan asked.

"It comes with Quantum OS 8.1 Home," Mark explained. "For what you're doing, you really need the Pro version. It has much better security features and remote desktop capabilities. The school's IT department can help you way easier if you have Pro."

Just as Jordan's face fell slightly, Mark's eyes lit up. "Wait here." He darted over to a nearby aisle filled with software and gift cards. He returned a moment later, holding a small cardboard card. "And here we go. The Quantum OS Home to Pro upgrade pack."

They took both items to the checkout, where Daniel was ready to pay. As the cashier rang up the laptop, Mark stepped forward and placed the small upgrade card on the counter.

"I've got this part, Mr. Brooks," he said firmly. He then turned to Jordan with a warm smile. "Consider it a welcome-to-the-family gift. I want you to have the best, most secure setup possible."

It was more than just a hundred-dollar piece of software. It was a clear, tangible statement. It was Mark's way of saying, I am so secure in my relationship with Hannah, and so comfortable with her friendship with you, that I am personally investing in your well-being. It was the ultimate anti-Tom gesture, an act of pure, trusting friendship.

Daniel, understanding the weight of the gesture, simply nodded at Mark with deep respect. After they paid, he was all business. "Okay, we have what we came for. Let's get Jordan back to the van."

Safely back in the minivan, the doors slid shut, and the rotation continued. "My turn," Zoe said softly, sliding into the captain's chair next to Jordan. Seeing he looked drained, she reached into the snack bag. "You must be hungry," she said in her practical way. "It's not quite lunchtime yet, but here."

She handed him a granola bar, and he took it gratefully. The mission was a success. He was safe, and he was cared for, surrounded by a family that thought of everything.
