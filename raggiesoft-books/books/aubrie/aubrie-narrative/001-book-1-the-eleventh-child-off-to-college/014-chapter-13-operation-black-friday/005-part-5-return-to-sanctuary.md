# Part 5: Return to Sanctuary

As the three-car convoy pulled out of the Spradlin Farms parking lot, Jordan saw the large, illuminated sign for the New River Mall Christiansburg mall. A part of him, the curious part that had been sheltered his whole life, felt a pang of desire to see the inside of a real shopping mall for the first time. But it was a small voice, easily drowned out by the overwhelming exhaustion and sensory residue from their trip to Blue Tech. The frantic energy of the crowds, the cacophony of noise and light—it had been too much. More than anything, he wanted to be back in the quiet safety of his dorm room in "The Old Lady," but he knew that wasn't an option. The next best thing was the basement on Geneva Street.

Having accomplished their main objectives, Daniel’s priority shifted from evasion to speed. He chose the direct five-minute highway route that connected with I-81. His knuckles were white on the steering wheel the whole time, his eyes constantly scanning the rearview mirror for a tail that wasn't there.

They pulled onto Geneva Street, the quiet cul-de-sac a welcome sight. But as they approached the house, Caleb’s voice came over the group chat. *"Unfamiliar vehicle parked in front of the Miller's house. Silver SUV."*

Instantly, the group’s training kicked in. As they got out of the cars, they formed a tight, protective bubble around Jordan, shielding him from view as they moved quickly toward the side of the house and the sloping path to the backyard. Just as they reached the basement door, Laura Brooks sent a text to the group. Laura B: *False alarm, everyone. I just texted Susan Miller. It's their new car. They got a Black Friday deal!*

A collective, nervous laugh went through the group. They were safe.

Inside the basement, the adrenaline from the day finally began to drain from Jordan, leaving him shaky and overstimulated. He needed to decompress. He looked across the room and saw Hannah cuddled up comfortably with Mark on one of the air mattresses, a picture of quiet contentment. His eyes then found Lauren. He didn't have to say a word.

She saw the look on his face and immediately came over, kneeling in front of his wheelchair and pulling him into a firm, grounding hug. "It's okay," she whispered, her voice a soothing balm. "You're safe now. We're home. That was a lot, but you did it." This was Tom's ultimate loss, she thought, and Jordan's immeasurable gain. To be cared for so completely, and to be able to care for someone so deeply, was a gift Tom would never understand.

After a few minutes, once Jordan's breathing had steadied, the excitement returned. It was time to set up the new laptop.

Mark took charge, unboxing the new Pinnacle Precisionist 5000 with the reverence of a true tech enthusiast. "Alright," he said, rubbing his hands together. "Let's get this beast running."

He spent the next hour with Jordan, meticulously setting up the machine to his exact preferences, customizing the layout, and installing the programs he'd need for his classes. Most importantly, he went through and uninstalled all the useless "bloatware" that came pre-packaged on the machine.

"You don't need this third-party antivirus trial," he explained, pointing at the screen. "The built-in Quantum Defender on OS 8.1 Pro is all you need, as long as you don't do stupid things like clicking on rogue links that claim to be from your bank."

By the time he was done, the laptop was a clean, powerful, and efficient tool, perfectly tailored to Jordan. The Black Friday mission was a complete success. Jordan sat before his new machine, a gift from his family, in the heart of their sanctuary, completely safe and utterly cherished.
