# Part 3: Spring Semester Planning

One evening in mid-November, an email from the university registrar arrived in all of their inboxes: "Spring Semester 2015 Course Registration is now open."

The suite immediately transformed into a command center. Laptops were opened, and Zoe, the group's natural logistician, took the lead.

"Okay, people," she announced. "Operation Spring Schedule is a go. Let's optimize."

Her first goal was to find common ground. "Based on a review of everyone's degree requirements," she stated, "all eight of us still need a second-semester English and Math class to complete the core sequence. Furthermore, none of us have satisfied the three-credit History requirement."

She quickly found the solution. "There is a large lecture section of HISTORY 115, 'History of Ancient Rome,' that has plenty of open seats and fulfills the entire requirement. I propose we all enroll." One by one, they all registered. They did the same for ENGLISH 102 and MATH 112, successfully building a shared academic core for the entire Group of 8.

With that settled, the focus shifted to Jordan and Aubrie. Jordan stared at the course catalog, a knot of anxiety tightening in his stomach. The relief of the upcoming Winter Break was tempered by the knowledge of the looming legal battles his mother was planning.

"I don't know how I'm going to focus next semester," he admitted quietly to Aubrie, who sat beside him. "Worrying about... everything she might try to do."

Aubrie looked at him, her expression firm and full of love. "You're not doing it alone," she said, her voice leaving no room for argument. She pulled her laptop next to his. "We're taking all the same classes. All of them."

It was more than a suggestion; it was a declaration. Together, they mapped out an identical schedule. They registered for the same section of PHYSICS 102, the next course in their Astrophysics major. They found a literature class that fulfilled a mutual requirement and a geology elective that sounded interesting. They clicked "register" on the exact same courses at the exact same time.

Jordan looked at their two screens, displaying identical schedules. It was a blueprint for a semester where he would never have to walk into a classroom alone, never have to wait in a hallway by himself, never have to face his fear without her by his side. It was a fortress built of lecture halls and lab sections, with Aubrie as his constant, unwavering shield.
