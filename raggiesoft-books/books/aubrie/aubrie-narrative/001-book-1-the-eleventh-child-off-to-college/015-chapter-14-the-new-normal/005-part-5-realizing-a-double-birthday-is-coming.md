# Part 5: Realizing a Double Birthday is Coming

It was a quiet Tuesday evening in early December. The pressure of final exams was still a week away, leaving a rare, quiet lull in the academic calendar. In Suite 214, most of the group was scattered around the room, focused on their own tasks. Jordan and Aubrie were at their desks, heads bent together over a shared astronomy textbook, their quiet, excited chatter a familiar and comforting background hum.

Madison, lying on her top bunk and scrolling through her mPhone, suddenly sat bolt upright.

"No. Way," she said, her voice full of stunned surprise. "No freaking way."

Hannah looked up from the book she was reading. "What is it, Mads?"

"Okay, this is either the world's biggest coincidence, or you two are actual soulmates," Madison declared, pointing her phone dramatically at Jordan and Aubrie. "I was just putting everyone's birthdays in my calendar so we don't forget, and... you two have the same birthday. December 21st. Both of you."

A quiet, stunned silence fell over the room as everyone processed the information. Jordan and Aubrie looked at each other, their own eyes wide with a shared, surprised delight. They had talked about everything—stars, physics, their shared anxieties—but the simple topic of their birthdays had never come up.

"Okay, that's it," Madison said, her mind already racing. "We have to do something. A party. A real, proper, surprise party. He's never had one," she added, her voice softening as she looked at Jordan. "He deserves one. They both do."

The idea was met with immediate, unanimous enthusiasm. Sarah and Mark, who had been visiting, were instantly on board.

"This is going to require next-level planning," Zoe said, her logistical brain already firing. "A cake, presents, a meal... we can't pull this off alone, and we definitely can't do it in the dorm without them knowing."

"You're right," Hannah agreed, her expression thoughtful. "We're going to need adult help for this. We need to loop in your parents, Zoe."

Zoe nodded, already pulling out her phone. The "Jordan Comms" group chat, their lifeline and central nervous system, sprang to life.

Jordan Comms: Zoe: \[LOGISTICS\] Urgent, non-emergency planning matter. Need to coordinate with Daniel & Laura. Mark and Sarah are here with us in the suite. Is now a good time for a quick video call?

A moment later, Daniel's reply came through.

Daniel B: Go for it. We're here.

The six of them—Madison, Zoe, Hannah, Mark, Lauren, and Sarah—huddled together in Room 216, their voices low and excited as they initiated a video call with the Brooks parents, leaving the two birthday kids blissfully unaware in the other room.

"Okay," Madison whispered into the phone, "so we just found out that Jordan and Aubrie have the exact same birthday. December 21st. And we want to throw them a surprise party during the winter break at your house. A real one. Jordan's never had one."

On the screen, Laura Brooks's face lit up with a brilliant, teary smile. "Oh, that is the most wonderful idea I have ever heard," she said. "Of course, we'll help. We can do a special birthday lunch, so the house is full of light for him. And I will order the most amazing cake."

"It should be astronomy-themed!" Lauren chimed in. "They'd love that."

"Perfect," Daniel said, his voice full of a father's pride. "Leave it to us. You all just focus on getting them there. We'll handle the rest."

The call ended, and the six co-conspirators looked at each other, a shared, secret excitement buzzing between them. The plan was in motion. Jordan and Aubrie were about to have the first real birthday party of their lives, and their entire found family was going to make it perfect.
