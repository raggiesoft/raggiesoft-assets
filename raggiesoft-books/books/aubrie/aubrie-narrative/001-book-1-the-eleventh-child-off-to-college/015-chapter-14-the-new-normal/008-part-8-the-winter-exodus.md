# Part 8: The Winter Exodus

On the last day of the semester, after the final exam was turned in, the mood in the suite was pure, unadulterated joy. The group was packing again, but this time for a much longer stay, the excitement for Winter Break palpable in the air.

Just before they were ready to leave, Jordan had one last stop to make.

Jordan Comms: \[LOGISTICS\] Aubrie signing on. One last stop at Student Health, then we are officially done with the semester!

At the Student Health Center, Jordan sat in a chair, his sleeve rolled up. The nurse was kind and efficient. "This is the first dose of the MMR series," she explained. "We'll schedule you for the next one after the break."

He barely felt the small prick of the needle. What he did feel was a profound sense of empowerment. It was the first vaccine he had ever consciously received, his first active step in reclaiming his own health. The nurse handed him a printed schedule detailing the long road ahead—a full calendar of appointments to get him caught up on everything he had missed. He folded it carefully and put it in his pocket.

Back at Bennett Hall at 4:00 PM, a final message went out to the "Jordan Comms" chat.

Zoe: \[LOGISTICS\] All finals complete. Grades are in fate's hands now. Operation Winter Break is a GO. Mr. Brooks' ETA for pickup is 15 minutes.

They hauled their bags out to the curb just as Daniel Brooks pulled up in his minivan, with Caleb following in his SUV. As the group loaded their luggage, Jordan wheeled himself over to Daniel, holding out the paper from Student Health.

"Mr. Brooks, look," he said, his voice full of pride. "This is my catch-up schedule. I got my first one today."

Daniel took the paper and looked over the long list of vaccinations and appointments that stretched over the next year. His expression was one of immense pride. He placed a firm, fatherly hand on Jordan's shoulder.

"Jordan, I am so incredibly proud of you for doing this," he said sincerely. He then glanced over at the other members of the Group of 8. "And I promise you," he continued, his voice full of resolve, "we will make sure you don't miss a single one of these appointments. We're all on your team now. You'll get it done."

With that vow made, they got into the cars. They were heading back to their other home, their sanctuary in Christiansburg. Their first, impossibly eventful semester was over. They had survived it all—a housing error, multiple autistic meltdowns, a fire drill, two vicious ex-boyfriends, and a controlling, abusive mother.

They hadn't just survived. They had won.
