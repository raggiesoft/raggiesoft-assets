# Part 3: A Brother's Trust

Later that evening, the chaotic energy of the mall trip had faded into a comfortable, sleepy quiet in the Brooks' basement. The group was sprawled out on the air mattresses, some reading, some quietly chatting. Hannah was sitting with Mark on one of the mattresses, her head resting on his shoulder.

"Hey," she said softly, just for him to hear. "I just wanted to tell you... thank you. For what you did for Jordan today at the mall." She looked up at him, her eyes shining with admiration. "It really meant a lot to me. Seeing you just... step up like that, without making it weird. It's one of the things I love most about you."

Mark just smiled and squeezed her hand, about to reply when a quiet voice interrupted them.

"Mark?"

They both looked up. Jordan had wheeled himself over, his expression nervous but determined.

"I... I just wanted to say thank you, too," Jordan said, looking Mark directly in the eye. "For today. Thank you."

Mark stood up from the mattress to face him properly, his expression warm and sincere. "Hey, man, don't even worry about it," he said. "He's my friend," he thought, "the man I consider my brother." To him, helping Jordan was simply the right thing to do.

Jordan hesitated for just a second, then did something he had never done before. He held his arms out slightly, offering a hug to another guy.

Mark's smile widened. He understood the significance of the gesture immediately. He knelt in front of Jordan's chair, meeting him at his level, and leaned in, wrapping Jordan in a firm, solid, brotherly hug.

As Mark's arms closed around him, an overwhelming wave of emotion hit Jordan so hard it brought tears to his eyes. It wasn't just gratitude. It was the sudden, staggering realization of what this meant. For his entire life, his vulnerability in male-only spaces—public restrooms, locker rooms, the communal men's shower down the hall back at The Old Lady—had been a source of constant, gnawing anxiety. But in this one simple, powerful embrace, that entire category of fear dissolved. He finally had a man in his life he could trust completely. A brother. A protector who could go where his sisters couldn't.

When they pulled back, Jordan quickly wiped a tear from his eye.

Hannah, who had watched the entire exchange with tears in her own eyes, looked at her boyfriend with immense pride. "That's my guy," she said softly. Then she smiled at Jordan, her eyes shining. "And that's our brother."

And just like that, the rules evolved once again. The grounding hug ritual, a cornerstone of his safety and comfort, had officially expanded. From that day forward, Mark was in. The fortress around Jordan now had a guard who could stand on the other side of the men's room door, and its walls felt stronger than ever.
