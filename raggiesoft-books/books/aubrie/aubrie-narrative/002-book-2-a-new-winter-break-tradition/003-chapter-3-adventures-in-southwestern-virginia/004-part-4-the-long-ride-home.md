# Part 4: The Long Ride Home

After a quiet and thoughtful dinner at Luigi's, the Group of 8 said their goodbyes to Peter Black and walked back to the McConnell Transit Center. The sun had set, and a chill had settled in the air. They checked the schedule; the Radford Connector was due to pick them up at 5:45 PM.

The bus finally arrived at 5:50 PM, running a few minutes late.

Jordan Comms: \[LOGISTICS\] All 8 on the Radford Connector. Running a little behind schedule. Heading to CPI.

The thirty-minute ride was quiet, the group tired from their long day. Traffic heading into Blacksburg was heavier than expected, and they arrived at the bustling CPI Transit Center at 6:30 PM, fifteen minutes later than planned. Their hopes of catching the 6:15 PM Maroon Express home evaporated as they saw its taillights disappearing down the road.

With a collective sigh, they found a place to wait for the next available bus to Falling Branch: the 6:45 PM RVT Roanoke Express. The energy from their exciting day was beginning to wane, replaced by a weary desire to just be home. The RVT bus, also caught in traffic, arrived late at 6:50 PM.

Jordan Comms: \[LOGISTICS\] Boarding the RVT Express at CPI. ETA to Falling Branch is approx. 7:15 PM.

But the delays continued. They finally pulled into the Falling Branch Park & Ride at 7:25 PM. They were exhausted. It had been a long day of riding buses all around the New River Valley.

As they made the final, short walk to the house on Geneva Street, the warm glow from the Brooks' windows was the most welcoming sight in the world. When they stepped inside, they didn't even stop on the main floor. The goal was the basement.

Without needing to be asked, Owen and Caleb immediately moved to help Jordan. They expertly and gently carried him down the internal flight of stairs, while Zoe followed right behind them, carrying his folded wheelchair.

One by one, the rest of the group came down and collapsed onto the nearest soft surface—the sofa, the large portable beds. They dropped their bags, kicked off their shoes, and let out a collective, exhausted sigh. They were tired, and they just wanted to catch their breaths. They were finally home.
