# Part 7: The Aroma of Home

As the afternoon wore on and the board games were packed away, a new, incredible aroma began to fill the Brooks' house. It was a symphony of holiday scents—the rich, savory perfume of a turkey roasting, the sweet tang of simmering cranberry sauce, and the unmistakable, comforting warmth of sage and onion for the dressing. In the kitchen, Laura and the Brooks sisters, with Zoe serving as their hyper-efficient sous chef, were in full command, preparing the Christmas feast.

Meanwhile, the rest of the massive family—the Group of 8 boys and the Brooks men—piled into the main living room for the next holiday tradition: the Christmas movie marathon.

First up was the perennial classic, "Holiday House-Sitter." The entire room roared with laughter at the slapstick antics of a clever kid outsmarting two bumbling burglars with a series of elaborate, painful-looking booby traps. Jordan, nestled on the sofa between Aubrie and Madison, found himself laughing so hard his sides ached. He had never seen these movies, and the shared, uncomplicated joy of the experience was intoxicating.

"Oh, here it comes!" Mark shouted gleefully as the main burglar slipped on a patch of ice. "Classic!"

Chuckling with the end credits of the first movie rolling, they put on the second movie. The group settled back in, surrounded by the twinkling lights of the Christmas tree, the sounds of laughter from the screen, and the ever-growing, delicious promise of the magnificent feast that was soon to come.
