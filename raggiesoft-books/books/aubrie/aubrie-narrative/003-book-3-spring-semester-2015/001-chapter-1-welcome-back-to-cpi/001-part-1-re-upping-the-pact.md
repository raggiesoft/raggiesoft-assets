# Part 1: Re-Upping the Pact

The "Group of 8" returned to Suite 214/216 after Winter Break, filling the rooms of "The Old Lady" with the happy, chaotic energy of a family coming home. On the afternoon before Spring semester classes began, Henry, their RA, knocked on the door and asked the six suitemates to pop down to Kelsey's office for a quick meeting.

"Welcome back, everyone," Kelsey began with a warm smile as they gathered in her office. "I hope you all had a wonderful break. Now that we're starting a new semester, the Office of Housing requires me to formally check in and confirm the living arrangements for this term." She looked around at each of them. "I need to ask if all six of you willingly consent to continue with the current co-ed suite arrangement for the Spring 2015 semester."

There was no hesitation. They all exchanged knowing, happy smiles.

"Yes," Aubrie said, speaking for the group but with everyone nodding in firm agreement. "Absolutely. We all want to stay together."

A pleased look crossed Kelsey's face. "I thought you might say that," she said, pulling a fresh document from a folder. "But for the university's records, we need it in writing."

She handed the "Suite Arrangement Consent Form" and a pen to Jordan first. He took the clipboard, and his smile instantly vanished. He stared down at the paper, his body going rigid.

Printed Name: Aubrey Jordan Fuller (Bed A)

He flinched as if he'd been struck. His hand, reaching for the pen, began to tremble. Aubrie, sitting beside him, immediately noticed. "Jordan? What's wrong?" she asked softly.

The others leaned in, their expressions shifting from easy confidence to concern. Jordan didn't look up. He just pointed a slightly shaking finger at the paper.

"That name," he said, his voice tight with a pain they had never heard before. "I hate that name. It's not me."

In that moment, they all understood. This wasn't about a simple preference or a nickname anymore. The name "Aubrey" was a trigger, a direct link to a past he was desperately trying to escape.

Kelsey’s professional demeanor softened instantly into one of deep compassion. "Jordan, I understand," she said gently. "And I am so sorry. Please know that from this point forward, in all my communications and with my staff, we will only ever use the name 'Jordan'."

She leaned forward, her voice kind and steady. "Unfortunately, for a formal university document like this—one that has to be filed with the central office and could have legal standing—I am required to use your full legal name from your birth certificate. It's a bureaucratic rule, but it's one we have to follow to protect everyone involved, including you."

She gave him a reassuring look. "Signing next to that name doesn't change who you are. It's just a piece of paper we have to get through so you can continue to live safely with your family. We see you, Jordan."

He took a deep, shuddering breath, looking at Aubrie, who gave his hand a firm squeeze of support. He picked up the pen, his hand still unsteady, and signed his name. As the clipboard was passed around the room, the mood was no longer celebratory, but one of quiet, fierce solidarity.

They handed the completed form back to Kelsey.

"Excellent," she said, her voice soft. "Your housing is secure for the rest of the year. Welcome home, all of you."

They left her office and headed back upstairs, armed with a profound new understanding of the battle their friend was fighting every single day just to be himself.
