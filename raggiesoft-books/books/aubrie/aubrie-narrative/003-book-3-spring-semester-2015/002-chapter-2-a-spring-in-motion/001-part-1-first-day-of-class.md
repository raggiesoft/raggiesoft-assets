# Part 1: First Day of Class

Monday morning, the first day of the Spring 2015 semester, arrived with a nervous, excited energy. As the Group of 8 got ready for their classes, the conversation was dominated by their future home.

"You know," Madison said thoughtfully to Jordan and Aubrie, "sharing that queen bed at the Brooks' house was great, but it was *really* cozy with three of us. If we're going to make our 'Room 214' arrangement permanent in the new house, we should definitely plan for a King-sized bed."

"I was thinking the same thing," Aubrie agreed, already on her laptop. "I wonder..." Her fingers flew across the keyboard as she typed a simple search: "bed bigger than a king". Her eyes went wide.

"Whoa. Guys, look at this," she said, turning her screen for the others to see. "There's something called an Alaskan King. It's nine feet by nine feet."

A huge, brilliant smile spread across her face as the full implication of her discovery hit her. She looked from Madison to Zoe, Lauren, and Sarah. "Forget just the three of us," she declared. "With a bed that big... we could *all* fit. The whole pact. A real puppy pile." The idea, born in that moment, was met with surprised, happy laughter.

Later that morning, the plan went into effect. True to her word, Aubrie was a constant, reassuring presence at Jordan's side. The entire Group of 8 filed into the large lecture hall for HISTORY 115, "History of Ancient Rome," taking up a whole section of a row. They were a single, supportive unit, ready to face the semester together.

When the morning class ended, the eight of them proceeded to the dining hall for lunch. The easy camaraderie and shared jokes continued, a bubble of warmth and safety in the bustling cafeteria.

After lunch, their paths diverged. Mark, Hannah, and the others peeled off toward their own major-specific classes. Jordan and Aubrie, their schedules now perfectly duplicated, headed for the science complex. They settled into their seats for their PHYS 102 (Astronomy 2) lecture, which was followed immediately by the lab component.

As they worked together on their first lab assignment of the semester, their heads bent close over a star chart, they were a perfect team. The fortress they had built out of friendship and trust was now being reinforced by shared lecture halls and late-night study sessions, with Aubrie as Jordan's constant, unwavering shield.
