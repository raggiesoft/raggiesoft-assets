# Part 3: The Summons

The first week of the Spring semester was a happy adjustment back into their routine. One afternoon, an official-looking email landed in Jordan's inbox with the subject line "Urgent: Meeting Required." It was from Kelsey, asking him to come to her office at 3:00 PM and, ominously, to please come alone.

A cold knot of anxiety formed in his stomach. "Alone?" The request went against every safety protocol they had built. He showed the email to Aubrie, who was his buddy for the day. Her face paled slightly.

"It's okay," she said, though her voice lacked its usual confidence. "Kelsey is on our side. I'll wait right outside her office door. I'll be right there."

At 3:00 PM, Jordan wheeled himself into Kelsey's office. She was there, her expression unusually somber. And she was not alone. Standing quietly by her desk was a uniformed Montgomery County Sheriff's Deputy. Jordan's blood ran cold.

"Jordan, thank you for coming," Kelsey said, her voice professional but her eyes full of compassion. "This is Deputy Miller. He is here on official business."

The deputy stepped forward, holding a thick manila envelope. "Sir, I need to confirm your identity before I can proceed. Are you Aubrey Jordan Fuller?"

The name, spoken by a man in a uniform, hit him like a physical blow. The careful walls around his new identity, the safety he had so painstakingly built, shattered. It wasn't a choice. It wasn't a thought. It was a system failure.

A low, keening hum of pure distress escaped his throat. His body began to rock violently back and forth in his chair, his hands flying up to press against his skull as if trying to hold himself together. The world dissolved into a blur of overwhelming light and sound. The monster had found him.

The deputy froze, the papers hovering in his hand, his expression shifting from professional duty to alarm and confusion.

Kelsey immediately took command of the room. "Deputy, please give us a moment," she said, her voice sharp but controlled. "He is having a severe autistic meltdown. The use of his legal first name is a known trauma trigger due to a history of familial abuse."

She then knelt in front of Jordan's chair, her focus entirely on him. Her voice became a low, calm anchor. "Jordan. It's Kelsey. You are safe. You are in my office in Bennett Hall. The deputy is just here to drop off a letter. That is all. You are safe here."

She continued speaking in that calm, steady rhythm for several minutes. Slowly, the hurricane inside him began to subside. The rocking became less frantic, his hands lowered, and his breathing, while still ragged, began to even out. He was pale, shaking, and utterly exhausted.

Kelsey gave him a moment before nodding to the deputy. Understanding now, the officer gently placed the envelope on the desk in front of Jordan, rather than trying to hand it to him. "Mr. Fuller," he said softly. "I am required by law to give you this." His duty done, he gave Kelsey a respectful nod and let himself out of the office.

The moment the door clicked shut, Kelsey opened it again. Aubrie was standing right there, her face a mask of pure terror after seeing the uniformed officer leave. She rushed in.

"Jordan!" she cried, immediately dropping to her knees beside his wheelchair, her hands covering his, her eyes scanning him for any sign of harm. "Are you okay? What happened? What did he want?"

Jordan, still unable to speak, just trembled.

"He's been served," Kelsey said quietly, her voice full of a weary anger. She picked up the envelope and showed Aubrie the formal title on the front. "It's a summons. His mother has filed a petition for guardianship. She's trying to have him declared legally incompetent."

Aubrie's face hardened with a fierce, protective rage. She wrapped her arms around Jordan, holding him tightly.

"I'm calling Daniel Brooks right now," Kelsey said, her phone already in her hand. "You are not going through one second of this alone."
