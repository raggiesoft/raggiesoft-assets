# Part 4: The Call to Arms

Aubrie knelt beside Jordan's wheelchair, her hands covering his, whispering reassurances he couldn't yet process. He was still non-verbal, the meltdown having rendered his voice useless for now, his body trembling with the aftershocks of the emotional storm. Kelsey, seeing the raw terror on both their faces, picked up her office phone, her expression a mask of calm, professional fury. She dialed Daniel Brooks.

"Kelsey? Is everything alright?" Daniel's voice answered, immediately sensing the gravity of an unscheduled call from the Hall Director.

"Daniel, I have Jordan and Aubrie here in my office," Kelsey said, pressing the speakerphone button on her end. "I'm putting you on speaker."

"You're on speaker here, too, Kelsey," Daniel's voice replied, now joined by the concerned tone of his wife, Laura. "We're both here. What's wrong?"

Kelsey took a steadying breath. "A sheriff's deputy was just here. Jordan has been served with a summons."

There was a moment of shocked silence on the other end of the line, then Daniel's voice returned, cold and hard as steel. "A summons for what?"

"His mother, Aubrie Sinclair, has filed a petition in Norfolk Circuit Court for an emergency guardianship and conservatorship," Kelsey explained. "She's alleging he's incapacitated and incapable of making his own decisions."

Laura's voice cut through the speaker, sharp with protective rage. "That woman... After everything he's done to prove himself... How dare she?"

"How are they?" Daniel asked, his focus immediately shifting to the kids. "Is Jordan okay?"

Aubrie, hearing the question, found her own voice, though it was a struggle. It came out as a shaky, terrified whisper. "He... he had a meltdown, Mr. Brooks. A bad one. The deputy... he used his other name. He's not talking right now."

The raw fear in Aubrie's voice was all Daniel needed to hear. His tone shifted from anger to absolute command. "Okay. Kelsey, thank you for making this call immediately. You did the right thing." He then spoke directly to Aubrie. "Aubrie, you listen to me. You are both safe. We are handling this now. Laura and I are on our way to campus *right now*. We will be there in twenty minutes. Do not leave Kelsey's office. Just stay with him. We're coming."

"We'll be right here," Kelsey affirmed.

The call ended. Aubrie squeezed Jordan's hand tighter, leaning close to his ear to relay the message. "They're coming, Jordan," she whispered, her own fear easing slightly in the face of Daniel's certainty. "They're on their way."

The first call to arms had been answered. The army was mobilizing.
