# Part 5: The War Council

Twenty minutes later, the door to Kelsey's office opened again. Daniel and Laura Brooks swept in, their faces a mixture of grim determination and deep, parental concern.

Jordan looked up. The sight of them—the two adults he trusted as his real parents, here in his sanctuary to protect him—was too much. The fragile composure he had just regained shattered completely. It was the overwhelming relief of seeing them that finally broke the dam. A fresh wave of meltdown crying, a raw, shuddering release of eighteen years of fear, tore through him. It was a panic attack, his breathing coming in short, terrified gasps. His system, flooded with both terror at the summons and profound relief at their presence, had crashed.

Aubrie, who was still kneeling beside him, didn't hesitate. She tightened her embrace, wrapping her arms around his shoulders and burying her face in his neck, refusing to let go. She became his physical anchor in the storm, whispering reassurances only he could hear.

While Aubrie focused entirely on Jordan's immediate comfort, the three adults formed a tight, quiet huddle nearby. Their voices were low but intense. This was a war council.

"Guardianship," Laura hissed, her voice trembling with a cold fury. "The absolute nerve of that woman. After he has done so well for himself."

"She has no legal standing with us," Kelsey stated firmly. "We have documented her harassing behavior and Jordan's formal request for no contact. The university will provide whatever testimony is needed to support his autonomy."

Daniel nodded, his mind already mapping out the strategy. "Okay. First things first," he said, taking command. "We need a lawyer. Not just a good one, a shark. Someone here in Blacksburg who specializes in family law and isn't afraid to go on the attack."

He looked at Kelsey. "Second, we start compiling evidence now. Kelsey, can you get us a formal, written statement from your office detailing the events from Fall Break?"

"I'll have it ready for you tomorrow morning," she promised without hesitation.

Daniel then turned to his wife. "Laura, we need copies of everything. The email Jordan sent to Housing, the police report number, his Dean's List transcript." He looked back at Jordan, who was still trembling in Aubrie's arms. "Third, and this is the most important... he can't stay here. Not with this hanging over his head. The dorm isn't secure enough."

Laura's expression was one of absolute agreement. She looked at Jordan with a fierce, maternal love. "He's coming home with us," she declared. "Tonight. Operation Winter Break is officially back in effect."

The battle plan was set.

Daniel walked over and placed a firm, steady hand on his son's shoulder. Jordan's crying had started to subside, anchored by Aubrie's constant, unwavering embrace. "It's going to be okay, Jordan," Daniel said, his voice a solid promise. "We've got you. The fight starts now."

With the immediate decision to fight made, Daniel Brooks's mind was already five steps ahead, planning the logistics of their new reality.

"Okay," he said, his voice a calm, authoritative presence in the tense office. "New rule. No more bus rides for now. The routes are too predictable. Me, your mom, Caleb, or Owen—one of us will drive the Group of 8 to and from campus every day. We are taking absolutely no chances."

Laura nodded in firm agreement. "And effective immediately, you are all welcome back in the basement. The beds are still there. We keep the family together."

Aubrie, who had not let go of Jordan's hand, looked up, her face pale with fear. She struggled to form the words, her voice a terrified whisper. "I... I want to come. I'm going with him."

Watching the raw fear on the young woman's face, a cold, terrible realization dawned on Laura. She looked at her husband, her eyes wide with alarm. "Daniel," she said, her voice sharp and quiet. "Wait. The PI. The car parked on our street. We have to assume she knows our address."

Daniel froze. His wife was right. Their sanctuary was compromised.

"You're right," he said, the entire plan shifting in his mind in an instant. "Okay. New plan. We're not going back to the house." He looked at Jordan and Aubrie. "We're putting you in the Hotel at Poly. It's right next to campus, it's secure, and we can control access."

He then looked directly at Aubrie. "Aubrie, you are going with him. Jordan will never forgive us if we separate the two of you right now."

Aubrie nodded, relief mixing with her fear. Then another thought occurred to her. "Madison," she said, her voice a little stronger. "Can Madison come too? We're all in the same room. We help each other."

All eyes turned to Jordan. He was still in the grip of his meltdown's aftermath, silent tears still tracing paths down his cheeks, completely overwhelmed by both the terror of his mother's attack and the fierce, swift generosity of his new family. He couldn't speak, but he gave a firm, clear nod.

Daniel's face set with grim determination. "Done," he confirmed. "Jordan, Aubrie, and Madison—the residents of Room 214—will stay at the Hotel at Poly. And one of us," he said, glancing from his own determined face to the memory of his strong sons, "me, Caleb, or Owen, will be in the room with you every single night on a rotating basis. Just in case. No arguments."
