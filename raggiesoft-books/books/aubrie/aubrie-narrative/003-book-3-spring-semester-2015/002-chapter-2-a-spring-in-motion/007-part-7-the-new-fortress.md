# Part 7: The New Fortress

With the plan set, the group returned to Bennett Hall, their mood grim and purposeful. While Zoe, Lauren, and Hannah helped them pack, Mark and Sarah stood guard in the hallway. They weren't moving out; they were evacuating. They packed only the essentials: clothes, toiletries, and their textbooks and laptops for the next day's classes.

Daniel drove the trio—Jordan, Aubrie, and Madison—the short distance to the Hotel at Poly, the large hotel adjacent to the CPI campus. Waiting for them in the lobby was Owen Brooks, his presence calm and solid. As Daniel had planned, the room was reserved under Owen's name. Since Owen no longer lived at the Geneva Street house, it was one less thread his mother's PI could potentially pull—a small but clever piece of counter-surveillance.

Owen handled the check-in, and soon they were all heading up to a wheelchair-accessible suite. At the door, Daniel held up a hand. "Wait here." He entered the room first, quickly and methodically checking the closet, the bathroom, and under the beds. After a moment, he nodded. "Okay. It's clear."

Aubrie wheeled the still-silent, terrified Jordan into the room. It was a clean, impersonal extended-stay suite with two queen beds and a small kitchenette. It was safe, but it wasn't home.

"Okay," Daniel said, laying out the rules for the night. "Owen and I will take this bed. Jordan, you, Aubrie, and Madison take the other one."

The three of them moved to the designated bed. Jordan, still unable to speak, sat on the edge, his body trembling. Aubrie and Madison immediately flanked him, sitting so close their shoulders touched his. From that night forward, this would be their new normal: Jordan would always sleep in the middle, cocooned in the safety and security of the two women he trusted most.

Daniel looked at his new, small, protected flock. "No one opens this door for anyone but one of us," he said, his voice a low command. "My cell, your mother's cell, Owen's cell. That's it. We're on watch now."
