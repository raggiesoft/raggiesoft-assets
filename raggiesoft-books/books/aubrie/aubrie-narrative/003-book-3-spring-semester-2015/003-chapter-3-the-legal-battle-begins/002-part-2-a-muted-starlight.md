# Part 2: A Muted Starlight

The meeting with the Guardian ad Litem left Jordan completely shattered. Aubrie wheeled him out of the library, her arm resting protectively on his shoulder, and they made their way in silence toward the science complex for their afternoon class. Astronomy was Jordan's favorite subject, a sanctuary of logic and wonder, but today, he dreaded it. His mind, still reeling from the meltdown, was a maelstrom of fear and static.

He was still non-verbal. The system crash had been so severe that his ability to speak was temporarily gone, locked away behind a wall of overwhelming emotion.

They took their usual seats in the accessible row of the large lecture hall for Astronomy 2. Aubrie, true to her promise, stayed right at his side. She could feel the tremors of anxiety running through him. His biggest fear now was being called on by Dr. Alistair to answer a question. He wouldn't be able to speak, and the shame of that, the public exposure of his shutdown, felt like an unbearable possibility.

As Dr. Alistair began the lecture on stellar evolution, Jordan stared down at his notebook, his pen frozen in his hand. He could hear the words, but they wouldn't connect. The concepts that usually filled him with awe felt distant and cold. His grades for the semester were already desperately hanging in there, the constant stress of the looming court case making it nearly impossible to focus. This new, official assault on his life threatened to be the final blow that would make him fail, proving his mother right.

He kept his head down, trying to make himself as small as possible. Aubrie, sensing his terror, subtly angled her chair to create a small shield between him and the front of the room while she took meticulous notes for them both.

Dr. Alistair, who knew his students well, noticed Jordan's uncharacteristic lack of engagement. His brightest student was normally a wellspring of insightful questions. Today, he looked pale and withdrawn. When the professor posed a question to the class, his eyes swept past Jordan for a moment with a look of quiet understanding before he called on another student.

The lecture felt like an eternity. When it was finally over, Aubrie began packing their bags.

"Jordan? Aubrie?" Dr. Alistair's kind voice came from beside them. He had walked over as the other students were filing out. "Is everything alright today? Jordan, you seem a bit under the weather."

Aubrie looked at Jordan, who couldn't meet the professor's gaze. "He's having a really difficult day, Dr. Alistair," she said, her voice low. "A personal family matter."

Dr. Alistair nodded, his expression full of empathy. "I understand completely," he said. He looked directly at Jordan. "Son, don't you worry about today's lecture. Whatever is going on, you just focus on taking care of yourself. If you need an extension on the next problem set, you have your friend here send me an email. We'll work it out."

Jordan looked up, his eyes glassy, and managed a single, grateful nod.

As Aubrie wheeled him out of the quiet lecture hall, the weight of the day felt immense. His mother's war wasn't just a legal battle; it was an assault on the very life he was fighting so hard to build, threatening to extinguish even the light from his beloved stars.
