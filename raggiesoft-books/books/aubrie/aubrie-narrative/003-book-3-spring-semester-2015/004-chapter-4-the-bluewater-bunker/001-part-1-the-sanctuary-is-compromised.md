# Part 1: The Sanctuary is Compromised

The first Saturday of Spring Break felt less like a vacation and more like a prison sentence. In the sterile, beige extended-stay suite at the Hotel at Poly, the air was thick with tension. Jordan, Aubrie, and Madison were cooped up, the stress of the impending guardianship hearing a constant, heavy presence. Daniel Brooks, their guardian for the day, sat in an armchair by the window, a quiet and watchful sentinel.

"I'm going stir-crazy," Madison said, pacing the small living area. "We should at least go down to the pool."

"Too public," Daniel said without looking up from his newspaper. "We stay put."

Just then, his phone buzzed with a text message. His eyes scanned the screen, and his entire demeanor shifted from relaxed vigilance to sharp alarm.

"It's Caleb," he said, his voice a low growl. "He's been doing a quiet loop of the lobby and parking garage. There's a man in the lobby asking the front desk questions about 'a student in a wheelchair staying here.' Matches the PI's description."

As if on cue, his phone rang. It was Caleb. Daniel put it on speaker.

"Dad, he's still here," Caleb's voice whispered urgently. "He's trying to be slick, but he's watching everyone who comes and goes. And Dad... I heard him ask the clerk if they had a guest registered under the name 'Aubrey Fuller'."

A wave of cold terror washed over the room. Their fortress had been found.

"Okay," Daniel said, his voice calm but hard as steel. "Do not engage. Get out of there without being seen. We're moving. Now." He hung up the phone and looked at the three pale faces staring at him.

"He's here," Daniel stated. "In this hotel. This place is blown. We need to get out of Blacksburg. We need to go somewhere he won't think to look. Today."

Panic flickered in Jordan's eyes. "But where can we go?"

It was Madison who answered, her voice ringing with sudden, fierce conviction. "My parents," she said. "In Bluewater. It's over an hour away, a different county. He won't follow us there." She grabbed her phone, already dialing. "Let me call them."

The group listened in silence as Madison explained the situation to her mother. "Mom? It's Madi... Yes, we're okay, but we have a huge problem. The PI, he found us at the hotel... We need a safe place, Mom. For the rest of Spring Break, until the hearing... Can we come to you?" She listened, her anxious expression slowly melting into one of profound relief. "Really? Oh, thank you! Thank you, Mom! We'll pack up and leave right away."

She hung up and turned to the group, a determined smile on her face. "It's a go. My parents are expecting us. The Bluewater Bunker is officially open."

Daniel was already on the "Jordan Comms" group chat, his thumbs flying.

Daniel B: \[URGENT\] ALL HANDS. SANCTUARY COMPROMISED. CONVOY RALLY AT HOTEL PARKING GARAGE, LEVEL 3, IN 15 MINUTES. WE ARE BUGGING OUT. DESTINATION: BLUEWATER.

The brief illusion of safety had shattered. They were no longer hiding; they were running.
