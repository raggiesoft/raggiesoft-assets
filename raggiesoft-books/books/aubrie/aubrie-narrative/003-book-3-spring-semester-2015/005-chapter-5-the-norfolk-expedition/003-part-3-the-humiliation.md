# Part 3: The Humiliation

The courtroom was cold, formal, and intimidating. On one side of the aisle sat Aubrie Sinclair and her lawyer, Mr. Davies, looking confident. On the other side sat Jordan with his lawyer, Ms. Gable. He was not alone. Behind them, filling two entire rows of the gallery, sat a silent, unshakeable wall of support. His chosen family was there—Aubrie Jean, Madison, Zoe, Lauren, Hannah, Mark, and Sarah. And with them, demonstrating a solidarity that was breathtaking, was the entire Brooks family: Daniel, Laura, and all six of Zoe's siblings, along with Chloe's husband, Ben Carter. All fifteen of them sat there, present and prepared to testify if the court wished.

The judge, a stern-looking woman with sharp eyes, looked over her glasses at Mr. Davies.

"Before we begin," the judge said, her voice sharp, "I want to address the matter of jurisdiction. Your petition states your son resides in Virginia Beach, yet my clerk informs me he is a documented, full-time resident of Montgomery County. This case should not be on my docket. Why are you wasting this court's time?"

Mr. Davies, already on the defensive, stammered, "Your Honor, his... his official DMV records still list Virginia Beach. That was his permanent address when he enrolled..."

The judge cut him off. "His DMV records? Not his university records? Not his *actual* place of residence? You are on thin ice, counselor. I am allowing this to proceed today *only* to put the matter to rest, not because it deserves to be here". She motioned for him to continue.

Mr. Davies presented his case first, painting the exact picture Aubrie had described: a tragic story of a disabled, incapacitated boy being held captive by a manipulative group and hidden away in a basement.

Then, Ms. Gable stood up. She did not use emotion; she used facts.

"Your Honor," she began, her voice crisp and clear, "My client, Mr. Jordan Fuller, is not incapacitated. He is a student in excellent standing at Commonwealth Polytechnic Institute, enrolled in a demanding STEM curriculum as an Astrophysics major". She handed a document to the bailiff. "Exhibit A: a certified copy of Mr. Fuller's first-semester transcript. As you can see, he made the Dean's List".

A murmur went through the court.

"Furthermore, the petition for conservatorship is baseless," Ms. Gable continued. "The petitioner claims Mr. Fuller cannot manage his own affairs. The facts show he is extraordinarily competent. When his mother cut off his phone, he did not fail; he immediately acquired a new VoIP number and, subsequently, his own mPhone on a new family plan". "He has proactively sought financial literacy from his support system, created a budget, and made the sound decision to avoid high-interest credit cards".

She held up another document. "Exhibit B: a copy of Mr. Fuller's VITA-prepared 1040 tax return. Mr. Fuller proactively filed his own taxes to claim his own exemption *before* his mother could fraudulently claim him as a dependent".

"Finally, the petition for guardianship claims he cannot manage his own health. This is the most offensive claim of all. Upon discovering his mother's 'medical deception' regarding his anti-vax status, Mr. Fuller *independently* vowed to get caught up on all his shots and has already received his first vaccine". "He initiated his own physical wellness plan with his friends and was an active participant in creating the 'Jordan Comms' chat and Buddy System to ensure his own safety".

"Far from being a passive victim, Mr. Fuller has demonstrated remarkable self-advocacy," Ms. Gable stated. "He proactively changed his preferred name to 'Jordan' with the university and his professors". "He formally changed his permanent address with the registrar to Christiansburg, filed a 'no contact' order against Ms. Sinclair with university housing, and voluntarily filed a report with CPI Police stating he was not a missing person". "These are the actions of a competent adult, Your Honor, not an incapacitated child."

The judge looked over her glasses at the evidence, then turned her attention to Jordan.

"Mr. Fuller," the judge said, her voice softening slightly. "Do you feel you are in danger?"

"No, Your Honor," Jordan said, his voice clear and steady. "For the first time in my life, I feel completely safe."

"And it is your wish to continue living independently from your mother, with the support of your friends and the Brooks family?"

"Yes, Your Honor. It is."

The judge nodded, then Ms. Gable spoke again. "And Your Honor, if I may add one final point regarding the scheduling of this hearing."

"Proceed, counselor."

"I formally requested a hearing date during my client's scheduled Spring Break to minimize the academic disruption. Opposing counsel claimed to be unavailable for that entire week, forcing this hearing to take place today and causing my client, a Dean's List student in a demanding STEM curriculum, to miss several days of classes. It is our position that this was a deliberate tactic designed to cause my client distress and harm his academic standing."

The judge’s gaze then fell upon Aubrie Sinclair and her lawyer, and all softness was gone. The jurisdictional issue was bad enough, but the scheduling information was the final straw. Her voice dripped with incredulity.

"Mr. Davies," she said, "you have brought a petition for incapacity for a young man who is on the Dean's List at a top university. A young man who has taken numerous, documented steps to secure his own independence. A young man whose legal residence isn't even in this city. And a young man who has been forced to miss his studies to travel across the state because you were 'unavailable' during his break."

The judge shook her head, a small, disbelieving laugh escaping her lips. It wasn't a sound of humor; it was a sound of sheer exasperation.

"This is not a case of incapacity; this is a case of a parent being unable to let go of her adult child. This petition is a waste of this court's time." She looked directly at Aubrie Sinclair. "Ma'am, I would strongly advise you to cease this course of action. You are dangerously close to this being considered legal harassment."

With a sharp crack, her gavel came down. "This petition is dismissed, with prejudice."

It was over. Aubrie Sinclair sat frozen, her face pale with shock and public humiliation. Across the aisle, Jordan was enveloped in a silent, tearful group hug by his sixteen-person family. She had brought the full force of the law to bear, and the law, along with two entire rows of loving people, had just laughed her out of the room.
