# Part 6: Homecoming

The convoy arrived at Bennett Hall late Tuesday evening. As the Brooks family helped them unload their bags, Jordan insisted on giving every single member of the Group of 17 a heartfelt, grateful hug. He was exhausted, but he was free.

"I just want to go to my bed," he whispered to Aubrie, “This dorm, The Old Lady on the Hill, was the first place I had ever found peace, starting with the night you first climbed into my Twin XL bed to chase away my nightmares.”

"It's too late for a bus ride tonight," she said softly, understanding the unspoken need behind his words. "But I promise, we will absolutely go for one tomorrow after class. If we have time in between classes, I'll even take you to the Transit Center just so you can watch them."

He nodded, the promise enough for now. After final goodbyes with the Brooks, the Group of 8 made their way to Suite 214. Jordan gave each of his friends another tight hug before they all began to disperse. Mark headed to his room on the third floor, Sarah to hers on the fifth, and Hannah, Lauren, and Zoe went through the connecting bathroom to Room 216.

This left Madison, Jordan, and Aubrie alone in Room 214.

Jordan looked at Aubrie, his eyes full of a weary need. "Will you... will you sleep with me tonight?" he asked. He then looked at both of them, his voice a quiet whisper. "And... can we all be in our birthday suits?"

Both women agreed without hesitation. They understood. It wasn't about sex; it was about comfort, trust, and shedding the last formal, restrictive layers of the horrible day they had just endured.

As they got undressed, Madison spoke softly. "You know, Jordan... our promise still stands. When you're ready." Aubrie nodded in agreement, but added, "But tonight... tonight we just sleep. We're all completely worn out."

It was time for bed. Madison, true to her word, climbed up into her own upper bunk, Bed C. She turned off the main lights, leaving only a small desk lamp glowing softly: the nigh light that helped Jordan every single night.

Aubrie helped Jordan transfer from his chair into his bed, Bed A. She then slid in beside him, immediately becoming his Big Spoon, wrapping her arms around him in a tight, fiercely protective embrace. He lay on his back, with her curled against his side, his head resting on her chest, her arm a secure shield around him. He thought of the impossible day—his victory in court, the long journey home, and now this. This perfect, quiet peace. This was a very special day, with the most special person he had ever known. He was home. They all slowly drifted off to sleep, utterly exhausted but completely safe.
