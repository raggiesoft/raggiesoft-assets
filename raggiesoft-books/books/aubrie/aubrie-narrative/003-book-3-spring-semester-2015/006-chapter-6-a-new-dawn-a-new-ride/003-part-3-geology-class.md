# Part 3: Geology Class

The 10:30 AM Planetary Geology class felt like a breath of fresh, clean air after the suffocating tension of the past week. Jordan was engaged, asking questions, his mind finally free to absorb the wonders of his chosen field.

As the class ended at 11:50 AM, Dr. Petrova asked Jordan and Aubrie to stay for a moment.

"Jordan, Aubrie," she began, her expression full of a sympathy that went beyond a typical professor-student relationship. "I received the official notice about your absence on Monday. I just wanted to check in and see how you're both doing."

"We're okay," Aubrie said softly. "It was... a lot."

Dr. Petrova nodded, a look of weary understanding in her eyes. "I can only imagine," she said. "I won't pretend to know the details of your situation, but I have just spent the better part of a year in family court finalizing a rather... bitter divorce. I know how draining that process can be, and the idea of going through it while trying to manage a full course load is just unthinkable."

Her voice took on an edge of quiet anger. "When the Guardian ad Litem interviewed me about you, Jordan, I told her exactly what I believe: you are a bright, engaged student who always brings thoughtful discussion to this class. You did not need this kind of disruption hanging over your head."

She gave them a reassuring smile. "I know there's a problem set due this Friday at midnight. That's the official due date for the class," she said, then lowered her voice slightly. "But for you two, that's a soft deadline. Take the weekend if you need it. I will not assess any late penalty. Focus on getting caught up and taking care of yourselves."

"Thank you, Dr. Petrova," Jordan said, his voice full of genuine gratitude. "Really. Thank you."

Aubrie's phone buzzed.

Jordan Comms: Madison: \[LOGISTICS\] We're all outside the Geo building. Starving! Hurry up, nerds! 😜 Mark: Tell Jordan we saved him a spot at the lunch table.

Aubrie smiled. Their army was waiting.

After profusely thanking Dr. Petrova for her compassion, they left the classroom. As they exited into the hallway, they were met with the six best faces in the world. Mark, Hannah, Sarah, Lauren, Madison, and Zoe were all there, their smiles bright and welcoming.

The relief of having his family back around him was a physical force. One by one, Jordan accepted hugs from them—a cheerful squeeze from Lauren, a quiet, fierce one from Sarah, a logical but firm embrace from Zoe. Then Mark stepped up, and Jordan met his brother's hug with a new, solid confidence.

He turned next to Madison. After spending a week sheltered by her family's incredible kindness in Bluewater, something had shifted. He looked at her and felt a wave of profound love and gratitude that was just as deep and real as what he felt for Aubrie. The hug he gave her was longer, tighter, full of unspoken thanks for the sanctuary she and her family had provided.

Finally, he turned to Aubrie, his anchor. Their hug was one of deep, settled love and partnership. From across the small circle, Aubrie saw the way he had held Madison, and she felt no jealousy in her heart. She only felt a profound sense of joy. The pact was real. It was working.

The group fell into their familiar, protective formation and headed for the dining hall. Jordan was practically buzzing with excitement, ready to walk into that noisy, crowded cafeteria not as a target, not as a boy in hiding, but as Jordan Fuller, a free man, surrounded on all sides by his family.
