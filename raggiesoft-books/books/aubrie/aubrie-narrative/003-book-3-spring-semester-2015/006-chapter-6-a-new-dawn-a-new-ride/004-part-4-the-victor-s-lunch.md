# Part 4: The Victor's Lunch

The Group of 8 walked together from The Walton Hall of Sciences, their chatter light and happy. Madison pushed Jordan's wheelchair while Aubrie walked alongside, their hands quietly intertwined. The simple act of walking to lunch as a group, free from the weight of the court case, felt like a miracle.

Jordan looked up at Aubrie, his heart so full it felt like it might burst. She was here. She was real. She was his. She smiled down at him, a brilliant, loving smile, and gave his hand a gentle squeeze. He then looked up at Madison, who was steering him expertly through the crowds of students. She caught his eye in the reflection of a glass door and gave him a warm, cheerful smile of her own.

Yes, Jordan, Aubrie thought, feeling his happiness as if it were her own. You are welcome to be dating Madison and me at the same time. You are welcome to be romantic with all five women of the pact. There is no jealousy here, only love.

Yes, Jordan, Madison thought, feeling the same profound sense of rightness. You can have both of us. All of us. We made a pact, the five of us, and I still want to be a part of it. I don't have any jealousy either. Both women, and the others in their pact, were completely at peace with the extraordinary, loving family they were building.

As they reached the entrance to the dining hall, Sarah pulled out her phone.

They arrived at The Newman Dining Commons, and the group navigated the various food stations, filling their trays. At the register, they all paid with a simple tap of their student IDs, the cost deducted from the Commonwealth Credits on their meal plans.

They found a large round table and all sat together, with Aubrie and Madison taking their places on either side of Jordan. The rest of the group watched them, their hearts full. They could all see it clearly: Jordan was still deeply in love with Aubrie, but after the week in Bluewater, he was now just as clearly falling for Madison, too. And everyone was okay with it. Their pact was real, and it was beautiful. (The only one who didn't know the full extent of the new sexual intimacy rules was Mark, but he didn't need to. He trusted them, and that's all that mattered).

"I'm so glad Mr. Evans was so understanding," Hannah said as they ate. "It makes this week so much less stressful."

"Agreed," Zoe added. "All of my professors have been accommodating. It seems the university's official notice about our excused absence was effective."

They talked and laughed, their conversation easy and light. The court battle was officially in their rearview mirror. Jordan sat in the middle of it all, a free man, having lunch in the cafeteria with his entire family, and it was the most wonderful, normal thing in the world.
