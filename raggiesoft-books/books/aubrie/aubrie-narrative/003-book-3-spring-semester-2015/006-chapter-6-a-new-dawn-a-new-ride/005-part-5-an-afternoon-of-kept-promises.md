# Part 5: An Afternoon of Kept Promises

After their triumphant lunch, the Group of 8 walked together from the dining hall. As they neared the science complex, Jordan quietly told Aubrie he needed to use the restroom. True to her promise, she didn't leave his side. The group located a nearby gender-neutral, accessible restroom, and Aubrie went in with him to help.

While they waited in the hallway, the phones of the other six friends buzzed.

Jordan Comms: Daniel B: \[UPDATE\] Quick progress report from Geneva Street! (Attached: 3 images showing demolition work starting in the new house's basement). The contractors are moving fast!

A wave of fresh excitement passed through the group as they huddled around a phone, looking at the first pictures of their future home taking shape.

When Jordan and Aubrie emerged, the group eagerly showed them the photos. Jordan's face lit up. It was all real.

It was now time for the group to split up for their various afternoon classes. Jordan received a round of goodbye hugs. When Madison's turn came, after the hug, he gave her a quick but meaningful kiss goodbye. She blushed brightly, her smile full of genuine happiness. Aubrie watched the exchange, her own heart full. Seeing Jordan so openly share his affection with another woman she loved and trusted was a beautiful affirmation of their pact.

Finally, it was just the two of them, heading to their Astronomy 2 lecture. The instructor, Dr. Alistair, gave them a warm, knowing smile as they took their seats. After the class ended, he made his way over to them.

"Jordan, Aubrie," he said kindly. "I was so glad to hear the good news from Ms. Gable. I trust you are both doing alright?"

"We are now, thank you," Aubrie replied.

Dr. Alistair's expression was full of empathy. "I know Jordan was particularly upset about missing the Pluto and the Kuiper Belt lab on Monday." He leaned in conspiratorially. "I spoke with the TA. There's another lab section starting in fifteen minutes—it's the same lab instructor you have on Mondays—and she has two open spots. If you're feeling up to it, I would be happy to walk you over and explain the situation so you can make it up today."

The look of pure, unadulterated joy that washed over Jordan's face was breathtaking. The one piece of his academic life he thought had been stolen from him was being handed back. "Yes!" he said, his voice full of excitement. "Yes, please!"

Dr. Alistair walked with them to the lab, had a quiet word with the instructor, and with a final, proud nod, left them to their work. Jordan and Aubrie took their station, their minds finally free and focused. His victory was complete. He was back in his sanctuary, studying the distant, orderly worlds of the Kuiper Belt, his life and his future once again his own.
