# Part 1: A View of the Future

The convoy pulled up to the house on Geneva Street, and the Group of 8 piled out of the cars, stretching after the ride from campus. The group began the familiar process of moving their belongings into the Brooks' basement. As they had done many times before, Caleb and Owen expertly carried Jordan down the internal stairs, while Lauren followed carefully behind them with his wheelchair.

Once they were settled, Caleb wheeled Jordan toward the basement's sliding glass door. "Hey Jordan, come check this out," he said, calling for the rest of the group to join them. "Dad has a surprise for you."

He led them into the backyard. The group stopped and stared in awe. The series of small survey stakes from the beginning of the year was gone. In its place stood a massive, winding wooden ramp that snaked its way down the steep hill, connecting the front yard of the Brooks' house on Geneva Street to the future back patio of their own home at the bottom of the hill. The ramp was for the most part complete; the structure was fully in place and just needed its code-compliant handrails and a few cosmetic touches like weather-sealing and staining. It would be ready soon.
