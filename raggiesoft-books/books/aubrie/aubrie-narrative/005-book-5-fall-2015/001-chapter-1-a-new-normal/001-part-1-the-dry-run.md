# Part 1: The Dry Run

The Thursday before the Fall 2015 semester was set to begin, the entire Group of 8 stood at the Falling Branch Park & Ride, a tangible excitement buzzing between them. They had spent the summer settling into the Brooks basement, and now it was time to establish their new school-day routine.

"Okay," Zoe said, holding up her phone like a stopwatch. "The mission is to time the full morning commute. We need to know exactly how much time to give ourselves in the morning."

The first express bus to arrive was the RVT Roanoke Express. They all boarded for the quick 30-minute highway trip, which included a brief stop at New River Mall Christiansburg, before continuing on to the CPI Transit Center. From there, they spent the next hour walking around campus, verifying the buildings for their new classes, ensuring Jordan's accessibility accommodations were squared away, and visiting the University Card Center to officially switch to smaller meal plans now that they were commuting.

With their administrative tasks complete, they headed back to the transit center. The next bus back to Falling Branch was the Bluewater Transit Maroon Express. The 30-minute express ride was filled with a happy, productive chatter as they planned out their new domestic life.

"I'm so glad we switched to the smaller meal plan," Lauren said, looking at her updated student ID card. "It'll be nice to have dinner at home every night."

"Agreed," Aubrie said. "But it's good to have the breakfast and lunch option, just in case we're running late in the morning and don't have time to cook."

"I like it," Jordan said, a smile on his face. The simple act of planning their own meals, of making their own financial decisions, felt like a quiet victory.

The decision was made. They would all switch to a smaller meal plan, a practical choice that reflected their new status as independent homeowners. As the bus pulled into Falling Branch, Zoe clicked the stopwatch on her phone. They now had their baseline. They had a plan. They were ready for their sophomore year.
