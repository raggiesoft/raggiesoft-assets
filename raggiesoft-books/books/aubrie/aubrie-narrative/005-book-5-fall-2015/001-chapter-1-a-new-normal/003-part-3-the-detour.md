# Part 3: The Detour

Sunday morning came too soon. After packing up their rental house in Kill Devil Hills, the group loaded into the two vehicles, a little sad to leave but filled with the happy, sun-drenched memories of the weekend.

As they got ready to pull out, Jordan spoke up from the passenger seat of the minivan, where Hannah was driving.

"Can we... can we try a different way home?" he asked, his voice hesitant. "The direct route. West on U.S. 158 to Barco, then take State Route 168 up through Chesapeake to I-64, and then take 460 home from there."

A heavy silence fell over the car. "Are you sure, Jordan?" Madison asked gently from the back seat.

"I want to try," he said, his voice gaining a bit of strength. "I need to see if I can just... drive through it. Focus on getting home."

Hannah looked at him, her eyes full of a deep, sisterly concern. She saw the resolve in his face. "Okay," she said softly. "We'll try. But the moment—the *second*—it's too much, we turn around. Deal?"

"Deal," he whispered.

Their spirits were high as they left Kill Devil Hills. They crossed the Wright Memorial Bridge, leaving the Outer Banks behind, and for the first thirty minutes of the drive, everything was fine. Jordan was focused, concentrating on the road ahead, on the promise of getting back to their new home.

As they neared the town of Barco, North Carolina, Hannah instinctively moved the minivan into the left-hand turn lane. Her instinct was correct.

A large, green overhead gantry sign came into view, spanning the highway. It showed an arrow pointing straight ahead for State Route 168 North to Norfolk. The left lane she was now in was marked for U.S. 158 West towards Elizabeth City.

The moment the sign registered, Jordan gasped, a sharp, ragged intake of breath. A low moan escaped his lips, and his hands flew up to cover his face as his body began to tremble. His attempt to face his demon had failed. The demon was too strong.

Hannah did not hesitate. She had already prepared for this outcome. She did not ask if he was okay. She just acted. When the traffic light turned green, she made the left turn, heading west onto U.S. 158, taking the southern route via Elizabeth City and U.S. 64 to avoid Hampton Roads entirely. The SUV behind them followed without question.

No one said a word. They didn't need to. Jordan had wanted to see if he could win a battle against his past, and he couldn't. And his family, his true family, was not going to subject him to a single moment more of that war.
