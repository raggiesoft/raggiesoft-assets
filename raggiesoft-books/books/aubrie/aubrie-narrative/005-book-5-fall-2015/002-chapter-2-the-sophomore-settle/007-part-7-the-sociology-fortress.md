# Part 7: The Sociology Fortress

Breakfast at the Newman Dining Commons finished just before 8:00 AM. The Group of 8 still had an hour to wait before their first class of the day. This was the new, inefficient reality of their commute, a trade-off for the safety of the Brooks' basement and the necessity of beating the morning rush.

The class they shared, SOC 101 (Introductory Sociology), was a classic sophomore requirement, held in one of the largest lecture halls on campus.

As they walked across campus, Jordan could feel the familiar, low-grade anxiety thrumming under his skin. A large, crowded lecture hall was a sensory minefield of noise, motion, and flickering fluorescent lights—a known trigger for both him and Aubrie.

They arrived at the hall at 8:45 AM, and it was already a swirling chaos of students filing in, shouting greetings, and slamming down laptops. Jordan instinctively reached for Aubrie's hand, who in turn gripped Zoe's.

This was the group's first test of the semester. They moved as a single, cohesive unit, their mission clear: secure a defensible position. They headed for the designated accessible seating area at the back of the tiered auditorium.

"Okay, here," Mark said, directing traffic. He and Lauren, the group's physical "flanks," positioned themselves on the outer chairs of the row, creating a small, protective wall. Zoe and Sarah filed in next.

This allowed Jordan to wheel into the open wheelchair space, with Aubrie sitting on one side of him and Madison on the other, cocooning him from the worst of the noise. Hannah sat at the end, completing their "fortress." They had successfully claimed an entire row, a bubble of safety in the overwhelming environment.

At 9:00 AM on the dot, the professor began the lecture. The class was a standard 50-minute session. Jordan, anchored by Aubrie's hand on one side and Madison's on the other, was able to focus. He was safe. He was surrounded by his family, and no one could get to him.
