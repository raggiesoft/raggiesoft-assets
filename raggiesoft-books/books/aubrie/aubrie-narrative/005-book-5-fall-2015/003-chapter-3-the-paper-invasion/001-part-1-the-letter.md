# Part 1: The Letter

It happened on a quiet Tuesday evening in October. The Group of 8 had settled into a comfortable routine, living in their fortress in the Brooks' family basement. Some were upstairs in the kitchen helping Laura with dinner prep, while others were in the basement, doing homework and relaxing.

Upstairs, Daniel was sorting through the day's mail when he stopped, a heavy feeling settling in his stomach. He held up a letter for Laura to see. It was a crisp, official-looking envelope from the Department of the Treasury, Internal Revenue Service. And it was addressed to Aubrey Jordan Fuller.

Laura’s face paled. They both knew that name was a landmine.

They didn't just hand it over. They went downstairs and found Jordan in the main basement area, chatting with Aubrie Kelly and Madison. The four of them—Laura, Daniel, Aubrie, and Madison—formed a quiet, protective circle around him.

"Jordan," Daniel began gently, his voice calm and steady. "A piece of official mail came for you today." He paused, giving Jordan a moment to prepare. "It's from the IRS, and... it has your legal name on it."

Jordan's breath hitched. He looked at the envelope Daniel was holding out, and the sight of the name—his old name, the name his mother used, the name he was trying to legally kill—was like a physical blow. The world tilted. His carefully constructed peace shattered.

The meltdown was severe. His body trembled, and a low, keening sound of pure distress escaped his lips. Aubrie and Madison were there instantly, their hands on his shoulders, grounding him. Daniel and Laura stood as silent, steady sentinels, ensuring he was safe. They didn't try to stop it; they simply weathered the storm with him.
