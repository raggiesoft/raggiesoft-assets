# Part 5: The Fever and the Fortress

Laura returned to the basement a few minutes later, Daniel right behind her. They carried a tray with a steaming bowl of chicken noodle soup, a sleeve of saltine crackers, and a glass of dry ginger ale.

"Alright, honey," Laura said softly. "Let's try and get some of this into you."

Jordan pushed himself up against the pillows, but the movement made the room swim. He felt like he was burning up from the inside out. Aubrie and Madison, his two safest people in the world, wouldn't leave his side. They sat flanking him on the queen bed, a quiet, protective perimeter. Aubrie gently took the tray from Laura and placed it on his lap.

He took a few sips of the ginger ale, the cool carbonation a small relief. "I can't be sick," he whispered, his voice raspy. "We have class on Wednesday."

"Don't you worry about Wednesday," Daniel said firmly, sitting at the foot of the bed. "You just worry about resting. You know what this is, Jordan. It's just your body fighting off one last thing your mother left you vulnerable to. You're getting your catch-up shots, but you're not fully armored yet. This is just... the last battle."

The word "battle" seemed to flip a switch in his feverish mind. His anxiety, which had been a low hum of physical misery, suddenly sharpened, latching onto the other, larger threat looming over him.

"The letter," he said, his eyes finding Daniel's. "The CP87A. What if... what if they call me to an audit and I'm sick? What if she uses this? What if she tells them I can't even handle a cold?"

"Hey, stop right there," Daniel said, his voice cutting through the rising panic. "We talked about this. That fight isn't for today. We have all our paperwork ready to roll. We have your lease, your bank statements, your class schedule... we have everything. All we do now is wait for the IRS to do their audit, and when they do, we'll bury her in facts. She has no case. You've got this."

Jordan nodded, but the reassurance, as logical as it was, couldn't penetrate the fog of the fever. His nerves felt raw, exposed.

Seeing the fear still in his eyes, Aubrie and Madison moved in closer. They didn't say anything. They just held him. Aubrie wrapped her arms around his back from one side, and Madison mirrored her from the other, their combined embrace a firm, steady pressure.

He sank into their hold, closing his eyes. The logical words from Daniel were helpful, but this... this was what he needed. He felt their hugs pressing the frayed, anxious nerves back into his mind, calming the static. He was safe. He was burning up, and he was worried, but he was home, and his two most trusted people in the world were literally holding him together.
