# Part 6: The Fortress of Comfort

Saturday evening settled over the Brooks' house, but the usual happy, chaotic energy was muted. After a small, forced dinner of soup, crackers, and dry ginger ale, Jordan was clearly fading. The low-grade fever, combined with the profound emotional exhaustion from the Afton Mountain incident and the lingering anxiety about the IRS notice, had left him feeling utterly depleted. His muscles ached, and his limbs felt heavy and uncooperative.

"I think... I think I just need to lie down for good," he mumbled, his voice raspy. "I'm just... done."

Getting him ready for bed required "all hands on deck". His fever made him weak and shaky, and he didn't have the strength to manage the dressing techniques he had been so proud of learning. There was no hesitation from the group. Mark and Hannah provided the steady strength to help him stand and pivot, while Aubrie and Madison, his "safest people," handled the gentle, close work of helping him out of his day clothes and into soft pajamas. Zoe and Lauren prepped his bed with fresh blankets, and Sarah got his medications and a fresh glass of water. He felt miserable, but the group’s seamless, judgment-free teamwork kept any feelings of humiliation at bay.

With Jordan finally settled in one of the queen-sized portable beds, the rest of the group looked for a way to wind down.

"Well, tomorrow is a wash," Mark said, stretching. "We're not taking him anywhere with a fever. We need a serious, all-day distraction."

He walked over to the large, well-stocked media shelf in the basement living area, scanning the titles. Suddenly, he stopped. "No. Way," he breathed, pulling a large, deluxe Blu-ray box set from the shelf. He held it up for the room to see.

"Is that...?" Madison asked, her eyes going wide.

Mark grinned, tapping the cover. "The Silver Gauntlet of Aethel: 25th Anniversary Definitive Aethelian Tome Edition".

"Oh, I *love* that movie!" Hannah exclaimed. "It is of my all-time favorites. That 'A Sister's Love' theme song is pure 80s cheesy fantasy bliss!".

"Mine too!" Sarah agreed. "It’s just so perfectly epic".

"This isn't just the theatrical cut, ladies," Mark said with reverence. "This is the 2012 Blu-ray. This is the legendary *Leviathan Cut*."

A respectful silence fell over the group. "The 12-hour, 21-minute one?" Zoe asked, her analytical interest clearly piqued.

"The very one," Mark confirmed. "We are *so* watching this tomorrow. It's the perfect sick-day movie. This will be the first time any of us have seen it."

The decision was unanimous. Tomorrow would be dedicated to Kaelan, Kaela, and the epic synthesizer score of *The Silver Gauntlet of Aethel*.

With a plan set for the next day, the group began to prepare for the night. There was no question about the sleeping arrangements. Jordan was sick, feverish, and anxious.

"Madi, you take that side," Aubrie said quietly, pointing to the left side of Jordan's bed. Madison nodded, grabbing her pillow.

Aubrie slid in on Jordan's right. He was already half-asleep, burning up between them. He mumbled, still fighting the residual anxiety from the day. "The letter... she's not..."

"Shh," Aubrie whispered, putting a cool hand on his forehead. "We've got you. Daniel's right. We're ready for her. Just rest."

Madison wrapped an arm around his other side. He was cocooned, physically held by his two autistic safe people. He finally let go, sinking into the fever and the profound, absolute safety of their embrace.
