# Part 7: Sunday – The Long Wait

Jordan woke on Sunday morning to a world of muffled aches and a heavy, feverish warmth. He was still cocooned between his two safest people. Aubrie was on his right, her breathing soft and steady, and Madison was on his left, her arm draped protectively over his chest. He felt miserable, but the bone-deep terror from the day before was gone, held at bay by their combined presence.

"Morning, sicky," Madison murmured, her voice thick with sleep as she felt him stir. "How's the fever?"

"Still here," Jordan rasped, his throat sore. "I feel like I'm burning up".

"Okay, first things first," Aubrie said, sitting up and pushing her dark hair from her face. "Operation: Get Cleaned Up. We're not going anywhere today, so we're all staying in pajamas, but nobody's going to feel better until they've had a shower".

The process was a slow, coordinated effort. While the rest of the group took turns in the basement's accessible shower, Aubrie and Madison focused on Jordan. His fever left him weak and dizzy, so they patiently helped him, their actions a seamless blend of intimacy and practical care, reinforcing the trust of their pact.

While they were busy, Mark took charge of getting the main living area situated for their marathon movie day. He fluffed pillows on the large sectional sofa, set up extra blankets on the portable beds, and meticulously hooked up the Blu-ray player. He slotted the first disc of *The Silver Gauntlet of Aethel: 25th Anniversary "Definitive Aethelian Tome" Edition*, a look of reverent excitement on his face.

Just as the group, now all in fresh, comfortable pajamas, was helping Jordan get settled into a nest of blankets on the sofa, Daniel and Laura came down the internal stairs. They were carrying trays laden with a full, hot breakfast—scrambled eggs, toast, bacon, and fruit for the group, and a simple, comforting bowl of oatmeal and more dry ginger ale for Jordan.

"Heard we have a long day of 80s cheese ahead of us," Daniel said with a warm smile. "You're going to need provisions."

The group ate together, the atmosphere cozy and safe. The fear of I-64 and the anxiety of the IRS letter were still present, but they were outside the walls of this basement sanctuary.

"Alright," Mark said, taking the empty plates. "The moment of truth. Twelve hours and twenty-one minutes of pure, unadulterated cheesy fantasy bliss. Everybody ready?"

Jordan, tucked securely between Aubrie and Madison on the sofa, pulled a blanket up to his chin and nodded. He was sick, he was exhausted, but he was home.

Mark hit "play." The lights dimmed, and the room filled with the soaring, epic synthesizer score of "A Sister's Love (Main Theme)," officially beginning their long day's journey into the Gauntlet.
