# Part 11: The Intermission Feast

The epic, synthesized score of The Silver Gauntlet of Aethel was replaced by the welcome, real-world sound of Laura Brooks calling down the stairs. "Dinner, everyone!"

"You're not going to want to miss this!" Daniel's voice added. "Your mom made her famous baked ziti!"

A cheerful, hungry groan went through the basement. The group, still in their pajamas, paused the movie and began to stir. As Jordan started to push himself up from the sofa, he felt a little less dizzy than he had at lunchtime.

"Whoa, hold on, sicky," Hannah said, stopping him with a gentle hand on his shoulder. "Temperature check first."

She retrieved the thermometer, and they waited. When it beeped, she checked the reading and smiled, a look of genuine relief on her face. "99.8," she announced. "It's going down. Still a fever, but it's an improvement!".

"See? The 80s cheese is curing him," Mark joked.

The group, insisting on not breaking their movie cocoon, opted to eat in the basement. Laura and Daniel brought down the feast: a massive, steaming casserole dish of baked ziti, a basket of garlic bread, and a large salad. For Jordan, whose appetite was slowly returning with the easing of the fever, Laura brought a small bowl of the ziti alongside his now-standard glass of dry ginger ale.

They ate, their conversation buzzing with the revelations from the *Leviathan Cut*.

"I just can't get over Kaela's entire rebellion subplot," Sarah said, her mouth full of pasta. "In the theatrical release, she's just... there. Here, she's actively waging a psychological war against Malakor!"

"And the 'Sacrifice or Queen?' monologue!" Madison added. "It makes him so much more complex than just a cackling villain. He actually *wants* her to join him!"

Jordan ate his small portion, his anxiety from the I-64 trigger and the IRS letter finally feeling distant. He was still sick, but he was surrounded by his family, his fever was dropping, and he was immersed in a world of epic synthesizer scores and crystal spiders. He was, despite everything, perfectly content.
