# Part 12: The Pact of Gratitude

After the hearty baked ziti dinner, a comfortable, sleepy quiet settled over the Brooks' basement. The Group of 8, all still in their pajamas, relocated from the dining area to the main living space. The *Leviathan Cut* of *The Silver Gauntlet of Aethel* was paused, *Book III* waiting patiently for Monday. Mark and Hannah claimed one of the queen-sized portable beds, cuddling up to read companionably.

Jordan, his fever still a low, steady 99.8°F, was settled on the large sectional sofa, a thick blanket draped over his lap. He felt weak from the sickness, but his mind was clear and calm. The day's immersion in 80s fantasy, combined with the profound safety of the basement, had successfully pushed all his anxieties about I-64 and the IRS to the background. He was simply present, surrounded by his family.

He looked at the five women of his pact—Aubrie, Madison, Lauren, Sarah, and Zoe—who were chatting quietly on the other beds. He felt a profound, overwhelming wave of gratitude and a simple, powerful need for comfort.

"Guys?" he asked, his voice quiet but clear in the relaxed room. "I... I'm still feeling a little shaky from the fever. Would you... can we just cuddle for a bit? All of us?"

The request was met with immediate, warm smiles. There was no hesitation.

"Of course, J," Madison said, moving to sit beside him on the sofa, instantly taking his hand.

One by one, the other four women joined them, forsaking the beds to pile onto the sofa with him. It was a familiar, loving "puppy pile". Aubrie, his safest person, settled on his other side, her head resting on his shoulder. Lauren and Sarah curled up at their feet, leaning against his legs, their warmth radiating through the blankets.

Finally, Zoe, her own part in the movie-talk done, came and sat on the cushion next to him. Jordan turned his head to look at her, his eyes full of an emotion that was specific and deep.

"Zoe," he whispered, his voice thick with feeling.

Zoe looked at him, her analytical gaze softening. "Are you feeling worse? Do you need the thermometer?"

"No," Jordan said, shaking his head. "I just... thank you".

She looked puzzled. "For what? I didn't do anything."

"For this," Jordan said, gesturing vaguely to the safe, warm basement around them. "This is your *home*. Your childhood home. And you and your parents...". He had to pause, his throat tightening. "You just... opened it. You took me in when I had nowhere else to go. You and Daniel and Laura, you treated me like I was your own son".

He looked at her, and the gratitude in his eyes was absolute. "I'm just so grateful to you, Zoe. You gave me this. You gave me a place to be safe."

Zoe, who lived in a world of data and logic, was visibly moved. She didn't offer a statistic or a logical reason. She simply leaned her head against his shoulder, a silent, powerful acknowledgment of his words. The six of them sat like that for a long time, a quiet, tangled mass of limbs and blankets, cocooned in the absolute safety of their found family.

As the evening grew later and the inevitable need for sleep began to take over, the comfortable pile began to break apart.

"Alright," Madison sighed, stretching. "Time to actually get into beds."

Aubrie and Madison, as his safest people, instinctively moved toward Jordan's designated bed. But Jordan stopped them with a soft word. "Wait."

He looked at Aubrie, then at Madison, his two anchors, and they both saw the gratitude in his eyes and gave him small, understanding nods. He then turned his gaze back to Zoe.

"Zoe... tonight... would you sleep with me?"

She looked surprised, glancing quickly at Aubrie and Madison, who were both smiling encouragingly. There was no jealousy, only understanding.

"It's just..." Jordan hurried to explain, his gratitude for her family still filling his voice. "Being here. In your house. After everything your parents did for me... I just want to be close to you tonight. It makes me feel safe".

Zoe’s usual pragmatic expression melted completely. She understood. This wasn't about the pact's romantic rotation; it was an act of profound, familial gratitude. "Okay, Jordan," she said softly. "Yes. I will."

The rest of the group found their places. Mark and Hannah took their bed. Aubrie, Madison, Lauren, and Sarah paired off on the remaining two queen mattresses. Zoe helped Jordan transfer from his chair into his bed, and she slid in beside him. He immediately turned on his side, cuddling close, his feverish body relaxing against her. He was home, in the house of the family that had saved him, held by the friend who had made it all possible.
