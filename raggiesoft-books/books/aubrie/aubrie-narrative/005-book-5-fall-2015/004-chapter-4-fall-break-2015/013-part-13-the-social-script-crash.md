# Part 13: The Social Script Crash

Jordan woke up slowly, disoriented in the dim morning light of the basement. His first sensation was the dull, stubborn heat of his low-grade fever, a persistent, uncomfortable static. His second was a profound, grounding warmth at his back. He wasn't wedged between Aubrie and Madison as he had been when he first fell asleep. He was spooning with someone else.

He turned his head on the pillow, his heart giving a small jolt. It was Zoe. She was already awake, her analytic gray eyes focused on the screen of her mPhone as she read.

He tensed. Oh, no. What did I do? He was in bed with Zoe. His eyes shot across the dark room in a panic, searching for his two safest people.

On the adjacent queen-sized portable bed, Aubrie and Madison were also awake, sitting cross-legged and chatting quietly with Lauren and Sarah. They looked over, sensing he was awake. And they smiled. Not tight, angry smiles. They were the warm, genuine, "good morning" smiles of a normal Sunday.

The operating system in Jordan's mind, already made fragile by the fever and the trauma of the I-64 gauntlet, slammed into a fatal, catastrophic error.

His entire life, his mother had programmed him with one absolute, immutable social script: intimacy is exclusive, possessive, and a weapon. His brain, searching for the correct response file, executed: On_Cuddle_With_Zoe(). It then called the required dependency: Find_Response(Aubrie.Jealousy.dll).

The system returned: null.

It tried again: Find_Response(Madison.Anger.dat).

The system returned: null.

His programming had no file for this. He was cuddling another woman, and the two most important women in his life *did not consider it cheating*. Aubrie's and Madison's simple, happy smiles were a data packet his mind had no way to process. It was a System.IO.FileNotFoundException() at the very core of his social being.

It was too much. It was an act of love and trust so unconditional, so radical, that his entire understanding of the world fractured. A low, wounded sound tore from his throat as the system crashed. A single sob, then another. He began to cry, his body shaking with the overwhelming, terrifying, *positive* input. He was descending into a full, system-failure meltdown.

Zoe felt him begin to shake. Her analytical mind, which ran with the cool, clean logic of a C# program, immediately began processing the new data streams.

As Jordan’s sobs grew, Zoe didn't pull away. She processed that he was overwhelmed by a logical, positive input he wasn't prepared for. She liked this data; it was an efficient, supportive, multi-vector system. This was a logical extension of the pact, and if it was logical, it was right. This system would be highly efficient for future, long-term family planning.

She calmly tightened her embrace, pulling his trembling body closer. "Shh," she whispered, her voice a low, steady, analytical hum. "The data is fine, Jordan. The system is stable. Your script is just outdated. We are the new variable. Just process the input. You're safe."

Her steady, grounding presence, combined with the complete lack of panic from Aubrie and Madison, was the only thing that could firewall the crash. He was having a meltdown simply because he was, for the first time in his life, unconditionally and completely loved by everyone, without conflict.
