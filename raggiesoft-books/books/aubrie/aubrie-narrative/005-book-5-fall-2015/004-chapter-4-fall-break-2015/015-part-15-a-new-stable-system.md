# Part 15: A New, Stable System

Jordan was completely overwhelmed. The "system crash" was absolute, a profound emotional overload that left him shaking and sobbing in the center of a three-person cuddle. He was trapped in a feedback loop: the very proof that he was safe—the fact that his two "safest people" were *endorsing* his intimacy with Zoe by joining in—was the exact data his legacy social scripts could not compute.

Zoe, still holding him, registered the new data points as Aubrie and Madison pressed in, adding their warmth and reassurance.

It was a long, slow process. The three women held him, speaking in low, gentle, murmuring tones, offering a constant, steady stream of positive, physical, and verbal data. Gradually, the violent, racking sobs subsided into quiet, exhausted tremors. The storm had passed, leaving him utterly drained and non-verbal, his ability to speak temporarily locked away by the sheer intensity of the experience.

The rest of the group had given them space, but now, hearing the quiet, Daniel called down the stairs. "Everyone okay down there? Breakfast is ready."

The group was, in fact, starving. As the trio on the bed slowly untangled, Hannah moved in with the thermometer for a temperature check. "99.1," she announced with a relieved smile. "It's definitely going down".

Daniel and Laura brought down the trays of food. For the group, it was scrambled eggs, bacon, and toast. For Jordan, seeing his fever was breaking and his appetite returning, Laura had prepared something more substantial than just soup: a small bowl of oatmeal with brown sugar and a piece of dry toast, alongside his requisite ginger ale.

Jordan sat up, cocooned in blankets between Aubrie and Madison, and ate every bite. He felt weak, but the profound sense of peace and security was a more powerful tonic than any medicine. He was exhausted, but he was also fascinated by the movie. He pulled out his mPhone and typed a note, showing it to Aubrie:

"Can we finish the movie? I need to see how Kaelan and Kaela's story ends."

"Absolutely," she said, her smile brilliant.

The group began to resettle. Mark, who had been quietly observing the morning's intense emotional display, came over as Jordan finished his breakfast. He understood, on a level Tom never could have, that this pact wasn't a threat; it was a testament to the trust they had all built.

"Just so you know, J," Mark said, his voice full of the easy, platonic love he had for his friend. "What you have with the girls is amazing. And that trust... it goes for *all* of us. Hannah and I both love you to pieces. You are always, always welcome to hug and cuddle with her, just like you do with me. We cherish that. You're our brother".

Hannah, standing beside him, beamed and nodded her complete agreement. "Always, Jordan. You're our family".

Jordan looked at them, his eyes shining with fresh tears, but these were quiet ones of pure gratitude. He nodded, his throat too tight to speak, but his heart full. He was loved. Romantically by five, and platonically, with just as much fierce loyalty, by two.

He felt a hand squeeze his shoulder. It was Mark. "Alright, brother," he said. "Let's see how this thing ends." Mark walked over to the Blu-ray player, remote in hand, ready to press play on *Book III: The Dawn of the Twins*.
