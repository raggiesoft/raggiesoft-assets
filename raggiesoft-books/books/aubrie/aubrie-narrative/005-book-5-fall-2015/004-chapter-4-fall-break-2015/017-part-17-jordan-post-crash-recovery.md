# Part 17: Jordan Post-Crash Recovery

The long, slow, and emotionally taxing process of calming Jordan from his system crash was finally over. The meltdown had passed, leaving him in a state of quiet, feverish exhaustion and situational muteness. As the rest of the group quietly returned to the basement, Zoe, still holding Jordan’s hand, ran a final diagnostic.

ZOE.Systems.Logs.KernelLog: Fever.State == Diminishing (99.1F).

ZOE.Systems.Logs.KernelLog: Hypothesis: The 'Pact = true, Jealousy = false' variable has been successfully compiled into Jordan.OS.

ZOE.Systems.Logs.KernelLog: The legacy 'SocialScript.dll' is no longer causing a critical exception.

ZOE.Systems.Logs.KernelLog: Conclusion: The new system is stable. Jordan is successfully processing concurrent, non-conflicting intimacy data. Proceeding with current parameters.

It was now lunchtime. Daniel and Laura, having been given the "all-clear" by Zoe, came downstairs with sandwiches for the group. For Jordan, Laura had his specific request: a peanut butter and jelly sandwich, a bag of chips, a pickle spear, and a soda that was *not* ginger ale.

The family gathered on the beds and sofa, eating in a comfortable, peaceful silence. Jordan was propped up on the sofa, still weak but clearly feeling better, his fever down. Mark, eating his own sandwich, observed his friend. He saw the remaining fragility and the way Jordan still seemed almost hesitant, as if waiting for the other shoe to drop. Mark decided to eradicate that fear for good.

He looked at Hannah, his girlfriend. "Hey, Han," he said, his voice intentionally casual but full of warmth. "Go sit with J. He looks like he could use a proper cuddle buddy.".

Jordan’s head snapped up, his eyes wide with surprise as he looked at Mark.

Hannah, whose love for Jordan was fiercely platonic and sisterly, smiled. She instantly understood what Mark was doing—showing Jordan, in the most explicit way possible, that his trust was absolute and that he was nothing like Tom. "Good idea," she said warmly.

She moved to the sofa, sat next to Jordan, and pulled him into a firm, grounding, and completely platonic hug, resting his head on her shoulder. Mark looked on from his chair, a look of pure, uncomplicated happiness on his face. He was overjoyed that Jordan's needs were being met, not as a burden, but as a normal, everyday act of love by their family.

ZOE.Systems.Logs.KernelLog: New input detected. Thread 4 (Mark): Initiated(Platonic_Cuddle_Protocol, Target=Jordan, Agent=Hannah). Thread 4 (Mark): EmotionalState == Secure, Supportive, (Jealousy=False). Thread 5 (Hannah): Executed(Platonic_Cuddle_Protocol). EmotionalState == Supportive.

ZOE.Systems.Logs.KernelLog: Monitoring Thread 3 (Jordan)... Jordan.OS.State == Stable. Event(Mark/Hannah Cuddle) processed as 'Positive'. Jordan.OS.State: SituationalMutism == False. Vocalization services restored.

ZOE.Systems.Logs.KernelLog: Conclusion: The 'Jealousy = false' variable is now confirmed system-wide, applying to both romantic (Pact) and platonic (Mark/Hannah) relationships. This is a robust, healthy social dynamic. System is operating at peak efficiency.

As Jordan relaxed into Hannah's safe, sisterly embrace, he felt the last, tight knot of his old, broken social programming finally unravel. Seeing Mark actively *encourage* his girlfriend to cuddle him platonically was the final piece of data he needed. His voice, locked away by the morning's crash, returned in a shaky whisper.

"Thanks, Han," he murmured into her shoulder.

"Anytime, Jordan," she whispered back, hugging him tighter. "Always."

He let out a long, shaky breath. "That was a good lunch," he said, his voice a little stronger. He looked at the paused TV. "I want to finish the movie."

"Good," Daniel said, his voice full of pride. "But first, what's the plan for tomorrow? It's your last day of Fall Break.".

Jordan looked at his "parents", then at his friends. His mind was clear. "Tomorrow," he said, his voice gaining strength, "we finish *The Gauntlet*. And then... I want to get that IRS file ready. I want to be prepared, so I'm not worried about it when classes start."

Daniel clapped his hands together, a brilliant smile on his face. "An excellent plan, son. We'll finish the movie, and then we'll prepare for the counter-attack."
