# Part 26: The Bluewater Confirmation

The Bluewater Transit Maroon Express hummed along the highway, carrying the family toward Bluewater. In her two-seat row, Zoe remained deep in a state of analytical flow, her fingers tapping away at her tablet as she continued to architect the rules for Fin# in her finsharp.md file.

Across the aisle, Jordan was quiet, watching the world pass by. He felt calm, recovered from his fever, but his mind was still busy processing the profound emotional events of the last two days. He looked at Aubrie and Madison, his two "safest people," who were sitting in the row directly facing his wheelchair space.

"I'm... still thinking about this weekend," he said, his voice just loud enough to be heard over the bus's engine.

Madison looked up from her mPhone, her smile warm. "Thinking about that 12-hour movie?".

"No," Jordan said, a small blush rising on his cheeks. "About... Zoe. About... everything."

Aubrie’s gaze softened. She knew exactly what he was asking. He wasn't just processing the meltdown; he was processing the *reason* for the meltdown and the passionate, affirming kisses that had followed. He needed one last, daylight confirmation that the new script was real.

"Jordan," Aubrie said, her voice clear and carrying, not bothering to whisper. "Your feelings for Zoe are a *good* thing. They are perfectly allowed".

"She's right, J," Madison added, just as clearly. "We love Zoe. We love *you*. Seeing you two together, seeing you kiss her... it makes us happy. You're allowed to have romantic feelings for all five of us. That's the whole point of our pact".

They said it openly, knowing the rest of their family—Mark, Hannah, Lauren, Sarah, and Daniel and Laura—could hear them. It wasn't a secret; it was a declaration.

In the row behind them, Daniel and Laura Brooks looked at each other and shared a quiet, profound smile. They had heard the whole exchange. They felt a wave of immense pride for the man Jordan was becoming—a man learning to accept and give love freely. And they felt a deep, abiding gratitude for their daughter, Zoe. Her logical, pragmatic mind and her open heart were the very reasons this incredible, loving family had been brought into their lives.

Just then, the automated voice on the coach announced, "Next Stop: Bluewater Transit Center."

Zoe saved her file and put her tablet away. The bus pulled into the downtown hub at 9:45 AM sharp. As the bus hissed to a stop, the family gathered their things.

"There's the Orange Line, right on time!" Lauren said, spotting their connecting bus waiting across the platform.

Knowing the transfer was tight, she jumped up. "I'll run ahead and let the driver know we have a wheelchair coming from the Express!". She dashed off the bus, waving to the other driver, a perfect example of their group's seamless, supportive teamwork.
