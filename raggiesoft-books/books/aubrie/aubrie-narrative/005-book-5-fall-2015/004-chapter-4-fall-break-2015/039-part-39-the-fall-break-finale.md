# Part 39: The Fall Break Finale

In the warm, quiet aftermath of their new, profound intimacy, a contented exhaustion settled over the basement "mini apartment". The five women of the pact were all, as Jordan had seen in the mall, "glowing", and he felt a deep, resonant peace.

"Okay," Aubrie said, her voice a sleepy, happy murmur. "I think it's time to get washed up for bed".

Jordan, feeling more relaxed and physically grounded than he had ever been in his life, nodded. "A bath," he said. "I'd really like a bath, not just a shower".

With Mark and Hannah still upstairs in Zoe's old room, the pact members moved to help. "We've got you," Lauren said, already moving to the bathroom. As the strongest of the five, she took charge of the physical transfer, and Zoe, as the "host" in her childhood home, followed to help manage the logistics.

The process was a seamless, respectful act of care. Lauren and Zoe assisted him into the tub, their actions practical and gentle, and then gave him his privacy while they and the other women took turns freshening up.

When he was done, Lauren and Zoe helped him transfer back into his wheelchair. Aubrie was waiting with a large, warm towel, drying him off while whispering how loved he was, her voice a soft, steady affirmation of the night.

Back in the bedroom area, Jordan, feeling a surge of independence, wheeled himself over to his pajamas. He pulled the soft shirt over his head on his own. Then, bracing against the side of the bed, he managed to pull his pajama pants up to his hips before getting stuck. "A little help?" he asked, not with a sense of failure, but with the easy confidence of someone asking a partner for a hand.

Madison, already in her own pajamas, laughed softly. "You got it, J." She quickly pulled the waistband the rest of the way up.

He was exhausted, a deep, profound, and happy exhaustion. He transferred onto one of the two queen-sized portable beds. As he had requested, Aubrie and Madison, his two "safest people", slid in on either side of him.

On the other queen bed, Lauren, Sarah, and Zoe piled in together, creating their own "puppy pile".

A comfortable, sleepy silence filled the room, broken only by Lauren.

"I can't believe Fall Break is over," she murmured into her pillow. "It feels like it went by in five minutes".

"Back to classes tomorrow," Zoe added, her voice already half-asleep.

Jordan, held securely between Aubrie and Madison, couldn't agree more. This brief break had fundamentally rewritten his entire operating system. He had survived the I-64 gauntlet, vanquished the last of his mother's "rootkits", and solidified an intimate, loving, and *safe* pact with five incredible women. He was sad the break was over, but he was ready for tomorrow.
