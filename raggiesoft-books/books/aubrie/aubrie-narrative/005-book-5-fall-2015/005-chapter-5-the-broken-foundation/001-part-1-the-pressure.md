# Part 1: The Pressure

The 9:05 AM start time for SOC 101 always felt too early. On Friday, November 13th, 2015, the massive lecture hall was buzzing with the chaotic energy of students settling in for the last class before the weekend. The Group of 8, moving as their usual cohesive unit, filed into the auditorium.

They headed for their established fortress in the accessible row at the back. Mark and Hannah took the outer chairs, creating a protective wall. Lauren, Sarah, and Zoe filed in, claiming their spots. Jordan wheeled his chair into the open space at the end of the row. As she always did, Aubrie grabbed a loose chair and pulled it up to sit right beside him, while Madison took the fixed seat on his other side.

The professor began the lecture on social stratification. Jordan tried to focus, but his mind was buzzing with a low-grade anxiety. The looming threat of the IRS audit—a "paper invasion" started by his mother's lies —felt like a constant, dull pressure, and the complex math for his Calculus class was a mountain he still had to climb every day. He reached for Aubrie's hand under the table, her presence a familiar, steadying anchor.

About twenty minutes into the lecture, Jordan felt a strange, new sensation. It wasn't the familiar buzz of his anxiety. It was a heavy, crushing pressure building in the center of his chest, as if someone were stacking books on his ribs. He felt a sudden wave of nausea, and a dull, aching pain started to radiate up into his jaw.

He tried to shift in his chair, suddenly breaking out in a cold, clammy sweat. This wasn't panic. This was physical. He let go of Aubrie's hand and placed both of his own flat on his chest, rubbing the spot, trying to make the frightening pressure go away.
