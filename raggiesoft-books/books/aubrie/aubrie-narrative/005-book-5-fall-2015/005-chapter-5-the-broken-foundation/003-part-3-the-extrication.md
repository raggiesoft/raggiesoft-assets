# Part 3: The Extrication

The lecture hall doors at the bottom of the auditorium burst open, and two paramedics rushed in, one carrying a monitor/defibrillator and a "first-in" bag, followed immediately by EMTs with a gurney and a stair chair.

"We got a call for a 19-year-old male with chest pain?" the lead paramedic called out, scanning the room.

"Up here!" Mark yelled, waving frantically from the back row.

The team, seeing the logistical challenge of the tiered seating, left the gurney at the bottom and sprinted up the steps with the monitor and the collapsible stair chair. The students in the intervening rows scrambled to get out of their way.

The paramedics swarmed Jordan. "Sir, my name's Mike. We're here to help you. What's your name?"

"Jordan..." he gasped, his hand still pressed to his chest.

"Okay, Jordan. We're going to take good care of you," Mike said, his voice a calm, professional anchor in the chaos. He turned to his partner. "Let's get him on oxygen and run a 12-lead. Now."

As his partner applied a nasal cannula, he looked at Jordan's chart. "You're 19?" he asked, his surprise visible for only a second before his training took over. Jordan's obvious obesity and acute distress meant they had to treat this as a worst-case scenario.

"Jordan, are you allergic to aspirin?"

Jordan, pale and terrified, managed to shake his head.

"Good." Mike popped four baby aspirins into his hand. "I need you to chew these for me. All of them."

"His friends are right here!" Aubrie cried, holding his other hand.

"We need space to work, ma'am," Mike said, firmly but not unkindly, as his partner attached the EKG leads to Jordan's chest.

The monitor whirred, and a long strip of paper printed out. The lead paramedic ripped it off and his eyes went wide. He showed it to his partner, who swore under his breath.

"He's 19," his partner whispered in disbelief.

"Monitor doesn't lie," Mike said, his voice now steel-sharp with urgency. "He's having a massive STEMI. We have to move. *Now*."

He turned to the Group of 8. "He's having a major heart attack. We're taking him to New River Valley Montgomery. We're moving him to the stair chair right now. We need the aisle completely clear."

Mark and Hannah moved to help lift him, but the paramedics had it. In one swift, practiced motion, they transferred Jordan from his wheelchair to the stair chair and strapped him in.

"We're coming with you!" Aubrie pleaded, her face streaked with tears.

"You can't ride in the ambulance," Mike said over his shoulder as the team began their rapid, careful descent down the steps. "Meet us in the ER. We're running hot."

As the paramedics disappeared out the door, the Group of 8 stood in the now-silent lecture hall, stunned. Aubrie turned to the group, her eyes wild with panic. "How do we get there? The bus..."

Zoe, her voice shaking but her mind already on the logistics, was one step ahead. "The 9:45 AM Maroon Express is the next bus to Christiansburg. It stops at New River Mall. From there we can get a cab or—"

"No," Mark said, grabbing his and Hannah's bags. "It's too slow. My car's at the house. We'll get Daniel. We'll meet him there."

"Go!" Lauren urged. "We'll follow on the bus! We'll get all his things!".

The group split and ran, leaving a stunned lecture hall behind them.
