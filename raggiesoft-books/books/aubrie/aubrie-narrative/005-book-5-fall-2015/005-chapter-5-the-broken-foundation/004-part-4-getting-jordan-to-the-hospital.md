# Part 4: Getting Jordan to the Hospital

## Section A: The Ambulance

The transfer from the stair chair to the ambulance gurney was a terrifying blur. Jordan felt hands lifting him, straps being tightened across his chest and legs. Through the chaotic motion, he caught one last, agonizing glimpse of Aubrie's tear-streaked face and Mark's, which was pale with a fear that mirrored his own.

Then, the heavy ambulance doors slammed shut, sealing him inside. The sound was deafening, a final, terrifying thud of separation.

The siren wasn't on yet. They weren't moving.

*Why aren't we moving?* he thought, his panic rising.

The paramedic named Mike was leaning over him, holding the oxygen mask in place. "Okay, Jordan, we're just getting you stabilized," Mike said, but his movements were lightning-fast. Jordan felt a sharp prick in his arm as an IV line was secured. Another paramedic was confirming the EKG readings from the monitor, which was beeping in a fast, frantic rhythm.

"Dispatch, this is Medic 3," Mike's voice cut through the air, suddenly all business as he spoke into a radio. "We are 10-23 at CPI, Walton Hall. We have a 19-year-old male, 5'7", approx 340 pounds, presenting with a critical STEMI. We are 10 minutes out from New River Valley, requesting a cath lab activation. Vitals are..."

The radio crackled a response Jordan couldn't understand. It didn't matter. *19-year-old male. Critical STEMI.* The words, so clinical and detached, confirmed his reality. This wasn't a drill.

Suddenly, the ambulance lurched into motion, and the siren ripped through the air with a deafening *whump-whump-whump* that vibrated through the gurney and into his bones. The world narrowed to a pinpoint of agonizing pressure. Jordan was staring at the ceiling, feeling every bump and turn as the vehicle raced toward the hospital.

*My chair...* A new jolt of panic hit him. *Where's my chair?* He realized, with a sickening lurch, that they had left it behind. It was with his friends, back on campus. He was being taken away from everything, even his own legs.

Mike was back over him, his face filling Jordan's vision. "Just stay with us, Jordan. You're doing great. We're almost at New River Valley," his voice said, distant and tinny.

Jordan couldn't respond. He was 19. This wasn't real. He tried to think, but his thoughts were a jumble of panic and pain. The paramedic was speaking again, not to him. "His pressure is dropping. Let's push another".

Jordan felt a cold sensation rush up his arm from the IV. He closed his eyes, the crushing weight on his chest making it impossible to breathe. He wasn't just anxious about the IRS anymore ; he was terrified he was going to die, right here, in the back of an ambulance, on his way to a hospital he'd never even seen before.

## Section B: The Wrong Bus

The five women—Zoe, Aubrie, Madison, Lauren, and Sarah—sprinted from Walton Hall, a frantic, disorganized mass of terror, heading for the CPI Transit Center. Aubrie was sobbing openly, her feet stumbling as Madison held her arm, pulling her along. Lauren and Sarah, who had instinctively grabbed Jordan's folded wheelchair as they fled the lecture hall, struggled to run while carrying the heavy, awkward piece of equipment between them.

"He's going to be okay, he's going to be okay," Madison repeated, though she wasn't sure if she was trying to convince Aubrie or herself.

Zoe was in the lead, her mind running on pure adrenaline. Her last log entry was still echoing in her processor: Target: 9:45 AM Maroon Express. *Faster bus. Express bus. Get to Christiansburg. Get to the hospital.*

They burst through the doors of the transit center. Zoe's eyes immediately scanned the bays for the distinctive Bluewater Transit coach.

ZOE.Systems.Logs.SystemSchedulerLog, "Arrived at CPI Transit Center. Target bus 'Maroon Express' is on time at Bay B. 11-13-2015 09:41:28"

"This way!" Zoe yelled, pointing toward the bay. But as she ran, her eyes caught the large, static map of the regional transit system. She stopped dead.

ZOE.Systems.Logs.KernelLog, "CRITICAL ERROR: 'SystemSchedulerLog' 09:40:17 entry is invalid. 11-13-2015 09:41:30"

ZOE.Systems.Logs.KernelLog, "Maroon Express (Target) route path does \*not\* intersect with 'New River Valley' (Destination). 11-13-2015 09:41:30"

ZOE.Systems.Logs.KernelLog, "Panic-state planning error. Defaulted to commuter route, not medical route. 11-13-2015 09:41:31"

"Stop!" she shouted. The group piled into her. "Wrong bus! I was wrong!".

"What?!" Lauren cried, her face pale. "Zoe, what are you talking about?"

"The express bus doesn't go to the hospital! It bypasses it!" Zoe said, her voice tight with a rare, panicked error. She pointed across the transit center. "The hospital is on the *local* route. We need Route 1!"

ZOE.Systems.Logs.SystemSchedulerLog, "Recalculating... Correct vector is 'Blue_Ridge_Transit.Route1' (Local). 11-13-2015 09:41:31"

The group didn't wait. They sprinted in the new direction, Lauren and Sarah now half-dragging the folded wheelchair. The 1 FALLING BRANCH bus was preparing to pull away. "Wait!" Aubrie screamed, waving her arms.

The bus driver saw the frantic group and the wheelchair and, with a sigh, kept the doors open. The five women tumbled on board, a mess of backpacks and tears. Lauren and Sarah quickly manhandled the folded wheelchair into the open securement area, and they all collapsed into the nearest seats. They had made it. They were on the right bus, heading for the hospital.
