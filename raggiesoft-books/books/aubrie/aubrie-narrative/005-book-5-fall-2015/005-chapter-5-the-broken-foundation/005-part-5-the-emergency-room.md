# Part 5: The Emergency Room

The ambulance gurney burst through the "Ambulance Only" automatic doors of New River Valley Hospital Montgomery. The ceiling lights of the hallway streaked past Jordan's vision in a terrifying, sterile blur. He was wheeled directly into a cardiac trauma bay, a flurry of activity and voices.

"19-year-old male, STEMI," Mike the paramedic recited to the ER doctor as the team transferred Jordan from the gurney to a hospital bed. "Vitals unstable, 12-lead was conclusive."

"Get him on our monitor!" the doctor ordered. Jordan felt them cutting off his t-shirt, the cold air hitting his chest, followed by the clammy-cold stick of new EKG pads. A nurse drew blood from his arm. He was terrified, the pain in his chest a constant, crushing weight.

He was stabilized, but he was alone.

Minutes later, the bus group tumbled into the waiting room, where Daniel, Laura, Mark, and Hannah had just arrived, their faces ashen.

"What's happening? Where is he?" Aubrie demanded.

Before Daniel could even speak to the triage nurse, a doctor emerged. "Family for Jordan Fuller?"

Daniel and Laura stepped forward. "We're his emergency contacts," Daniel said, his voice firm.

"He's stabilized for the moment," the doctor said. "The cardiologist is on his way. He's conscious, but he's scared. He's asking for 'Aubrie.' I can let two of you go back."

"Go," Daniel said, nodding to Aubrie and Zoe.

Aubrie and Zoe were buzzed through the double doors. Aubrie was pushing Jordan's folded wheelchair, which she'd refused to leave behind, and his backpack was slung over her shoulder. They navigated the chaotic ER until a nurse pointed them to Bay 3.

They parted the curtain. Jordan was lying on the gurney, still in his jeans and sneakers, but his chest was bare and covered in a web of wires.

The sight of him, so pale and vulnerable, shattered Aubrie's composure. She dropped the wheelchair handle and rushed to his side, openly crying.

"Jordan! Oh my god," she sobbed, grabbing his hand. "We... we thought..." She was terrified he was going to die. But another, colder fear was right behind it. "Your mom," she whispered, her voice frantic. "What if *she* finds out? What if she comes here? What if she tries to take you back home?"

"Hey..." Jordan rasped, his voice weak. He tried to squeeze her hand. He wanted to calm *her* down. He reached his other hand out to Zoe, who took it instantly. Her hand was shaking, but her grip was firm.

"She can't take him, Aubrie," Zoe said, her voice low. "Daniel and Laura are out front. They are his legal emergency contacts. She has no authority here."

They sat like that for a long time, Jordan holding both their hands. Finally, Zoe, ever practical, knew the others were desperate for an update. "I'm going to switch with someone," she whispered. "I'll be right back".

A minute later, the curtain moved, and Madison slipped in. "Hey, J," she said softly, her eyes red but her voice trying to be strong. "How's our guy?"

Jordan refused to let go of Aubrie's hand, but he reached his free one out to Madison, who took it immediately.

Just then, a nurse came in, all business. "Just need to draw some more blood, Mr. Fuller," she said briskly. "The cardiologist wants to check your enzyme levels."

As she worked, Jordan found his voice. "What happens now?" he asked.

"Now we wait," the nurse said, snapping a cap on the vial. "The doctor will be back with the results when they get them".

She swept out of the bay. Jordan let his head fall back onto the gurney's hard pillow. He was in the Emergency Room. He was brought here by ambulance. His chest *still* ached. And now, he was just... waiting. The sheer annoyance of it all was a small, human feeling that cut through his terror.
