# Part 6: The Nuclear Medicine Lab

A short while later, a different technician entered their bay, pushing a gurney. "Jordan Fuller?" he asked, looking at a clipboard. "We're going to take you down to Nuclear Medicine for an echocardiogram."

Jordan, Aubrie, and Madison all looked at him, confused.

"A *what*?" Madison asked, her voice laced with new anxiety.

"An echocardiogram. It's an ultrasound of his heart," the tech explained.

Jordan's brow furrowed in confusion. "An ultrasound?" he asked, his voice raspy. "I thought... I thought that's how you looked at babies?".

The tech gave a small, clinical smile. "Same technology, different target. We're not looking for a baby; we're looking at your heart muscle, checking the valves, and seeing how badly it's been damaged by the attack."

The word "damaged" hit Aubrie like a physical blow. She clutched Jordan's hand tighter, her knuckles white.

"Can we go with him?" Aubrie asked, her voice trembling.

"You can come down to the lab with us, but you'll have to wait in the hallway during the procedure," the tech said. "It's a sterile area".

The tech and an orderly helped Jordan transfer from the ER gurney to the transport gurney. Aubrie and Madison walked on either side, their hands resting on his arm, as he was wheeled through the maze of hospital corridors. They still had his folded wheelchair and backpack with them.

They arrived at the Nuclear Medicine department, a quiet, cold hallway that felt worlds away from the chaos of the ER.

"Okay, we'll take it from here," the tech said. "We should be about 30 minutes."

Jordan looked at his two friends, his eyes wide with a new, quiet fear. He was being taken away from them again.

"We'll be right here, J," Madison promised, squeezing his arm. "We're not going anywhere."

"We love you," Aubrie whispered, leaning down to kiss his forehead.

They watched as he was wheeled through a set of heavy, automatic doors, which hissed shut behind him, leaving them alone in the hallway. Aubrie immediately slumped against the wall, her shaky legs giving out as a fresh wave of tears came. Madison sat beside her, wrapping an arm around her, and they both stared at the closed door, waiting.
