# Part 7: The Agonizing Wait

The heavy doors to the Nuclear Medicine lab hissed open, and Aubrie and Madison jumped to their feet. The tech wheeled Jordan's gurney back out into the hallway.

"Jordan!" Aubrie was at his side in an instant, her face pale and streaked with tears. She was "scared out of her mind". "Are you okay? Did it hurt? What did they say?"

"I'm... okay," Jordan rasped, reaching for her hand. The procedure hadn't hurt, but the cold gel and the technician's grim silence had terrified him. "They didn't say anything. Just... took pictures."

The tech gave them a professional nod. "The cardiologist will review the images and be in to talk to you."

The trip back to the Emergency Room was a long, heavy silence. Aubrie and Madison walked on either side of the gurney, holding his hands, their fears momentarily quieted by the simple fact that he was back with them.

They arrived back in the ER bay, and the tech helped Jordan transfer back to his original bed. The nurse returned, checked his vitals, and said, "The doctor will be in with your results as soon as they're available."

And then, they were left alone. The curtain was drawn, and the only sounds were the steady beep of his heart monitor and the distant, muffled chaos of the ER. All they could do was wait. The adrenaline of the initial crisis was fading, replaced by a slow, agonizing dread.

After what felt like an hour, Madison, seeing Aubrie's vice-grip on Jordan's hand, knew she needed to update the others. "Aubrie, I'm going to go switch out with someone," she said softly. She then turned to Jordan, her voice gentle. "Who do you want, J? Who do you want to see next?"

Jordan didn't have to think. He was scared, and he needed the group's moral anchor. "Hannah," he whispered. "I want to see Hannah".

Madison nodded, squeezing his hand. "I'll go get her."

She slipped out and returned a minute later with Hannah. Hannah's mom friend energy was a wave of calm. She came in and quietly took the chair Madison had been in, her presence steady and reassuring, a silent promise that he was not alone.

The minutes in the ER bay stretched into an agonizing, silent eternity. Jordan lay on the gurney, the beep of the heart monitor the only sound, his terror a cold, heavy weight in his chest. Aubrie was on his left, her head down, her hand gripping his so tightly her knuckles were white. She hadn't let go, not even for a second, since he'd returned from the lab. On his right, Hannah sat, a pillar of "mom friend" strength, her hand resting on his arm in a steady, grounding presence.

The curtain flew open, and the ER doctor returned, his face grim.

"Jordan," he began, his voice serious. "The results from the echocardiogram are back. It confirms what the EKG told us: you are having a significant heart attack. The ultrasound shows a severe blockage in one of your main coronary arteries".

Aubrie let out a small, strangled sob and pressed Jordan's hand to her face.

"This isn't something we can just treat with medication," the doctor continued. "We need to intervene immediately. The next step is a procedure called an angioplasty, where we go in and try to open the artery with a balloon. But we can't do that down here."

He took a breath. "I've spoken with the cardiologist. We're admitting you to the main hospital, to the cardiac floor, effective immediately. We're arranging a transport team to move you to your inpatient bed right now".

*Admitting me? Main hospital?* Jordan's mind reeled. This was getting worse, not better. He looked at Aubrie's terrified face, then at Hannah. "I don't... I don't want to be alone," he whispered, his voice trembling.

The doctor, seeing the raw fear in the 19-year-old's eyes, gave a compassionate nod. "You won't be. Your family is in the waiting room?"

"All of them," Hannah confirmed, her own voice shaky.

"I'll have a nurse bring them back," the doctor said, bending the rules. "You can see them all before the transport team arrives".

A moment later, the curtain was pulled back again, and the small ER bay was suddenly, beautifully, overwhelmingly full. Daniel and Laura Brooks rushed in first, their faces a mask of parental love and controlled fear. They were followed immediately by the rest of the Group of 8: Madison, Zoe, Lauren, Sarah, and Mark.

They surrounded his bed, a 10-person-strong fortress of silent, powerful support. Jordan, overwhelmed by the bad news but even more overwhelmed by their presence, reached out. Hands immediately found his. Aubrie on one side, Madison on the other. Hannah, Lauren, Sarah, and Zoe all had a hand on his arm or leg. Mark and Daniel stood at the foot of the bed, a solid wall of strength.

"The transport team is on their way," the doctor said softly from the doorway. "We're moving him in five minutes."
