# Part 2: The Inpatient Room

The transport team wheeled Jordan's gurney into the cardiac step-down room, and the entire Group of 10—the Group of 8 plus Daniel and Laura Brooks—piled in right behind them. The room instantly felt small and hot, thick with the smell of antiseptic and the group's collective, silent panic. Jordan, exhausted and scared, needed to decompress. He was just glad the hospital was letting all of them stay.

Aubrie was still plastered to his side, sobbing quietly and refusing to let go of his hand. Mark, seeing she was about to be in the way, grabbed a visitor's chair and slid it as close to the bed as possible. "Here, Aubrie," he said gently. She sank into it, never breaking her grip on Jordan's hand.

A new nurse entered, her voice kind but firm. "Okay, folks, I need some room to work. We need to get him settled". Her eyes landed on Aubrie. "Honey, I need you to let his hand go, just for one minute, so we can get him onto the bed and hooked up to our monitors".

With a pained, shaky breath, Aubrie finally, reluctantly, released his hand. The loss of contact felt like a jolt to Jordan. The nurse and an orderly expertly slid him from the gurney to the inpatient bed, their movements quick and practiced as they attached new EKG leads and checked his IV line.

Once they were done, Aubrie's hand immediately found his again.

As the adrenaline from the ambulance ride faded, Jordan realized he was *starving*. He hadn't eaten since breakfast, hours ago. "I'm... I'm really hungry," he whispered to the nurse.

The nurse gave him a sympathetic look. "I know, sweetie. But after a heart event, we can't just give you food. I need to check with the cardiologist and see if you're NPO—that means 'nil per os,' or nothing by mouth".

She stepped out for a moment and returned, her face apologetic. "I'm sorry, Jordan. The doctor confirmed. You are NPO for now".

A wave of pure, grumpy frustration washed over Jordan. He was terrified, his chest ached, and now he was hungry. But he understood. He just sighed and squeezed Aubrie's hand, settling in for what he knew was going to be a very long, very terrible night.
