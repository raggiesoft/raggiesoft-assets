# Part 3: The Fork in the Road

Jordan was relieved to be out of the chaotic, noisy ER and in a quiet, private inpatient room. The Group of 10 was gathered around him, the silence thick with unspoken fear. Aubrie was still clutching his hand, and Daniel and Laura stood near the foot of the bed like sentinels.

The door opened, and the cardiologist from the ER entered, his expression calm and direct.

"Okay, Jordan, we're all settled in," the doctor began. "Here's the plan. That test in the lab confirmed our suspicions: you have a severe blockage in a major artery. We need to open it."

He looked directly at Jordan, Daniel, and Laura. "We have two paths forward. Path one, the one we're planning for, is a balloon angioplasty. We'll go into the cath lab, I'll thread a wire up to the artery, and inflate a small balloon to open it. If it works, the relief will be immediate. You'll be in the hospital for a couple of days to recover, and then you'll be back to your old self".

Jordan squeezed Aubrie's hand, a flicker of hope. *A couple of days.*

"However," the doctor said, his tone shifting, "I need to be frank. Your age and the nature of this blockage are highly unusual. If the artery is too calcified, or if the plaque is too hard, the balloon won't work. If it fails, you will need an immediate Coronary Artery Bypass Graft, or CABG. That is open-heart surgery. It's a procedure we cannot perform here; you would be transferred to Star City in Roanoke. The recovery from a CABG is not days. It's a long, slow, and painful process—we're talking months, not weeks".

The room was deathly silent, the weight of the two outcomes settling on them.

"We are scheduling you for the angioplasty later this afternoon, as soon as an operating room is available," the doctor finished. "A transport team will be by when they're ready. For now, just rest."

The doctor left. The group was stunned. Jordan, processing the "good" scenario, looked up at Aubrie, his eyes pleading.

"Aubrie... if it works... just for the couple of days... will you stay?" he whispered. "I don't want to be alone".

"I'm not going anywhere," she promised immediately, her tears starting to fall again. "I'm staying right here".

Daniel, ever the provider, immediately shifted into action. "Okay, that's the plan. Laura, let's go get her some supplies from the house. We'll be back as soon as we can". He promised to return with pillows, blankets, and a change of clothes for Aubrie. As they left, the room suddenly felt emptier.

Jordan turned his gaze from the door and looked at Mark, who was standing nervously by the window. His voice was a raw whisper.
