# Part 5: The Cath Lab

The inpatient room door swung open, and two members of the cardiac transport team entered. "Mr. Fuller? We're ready for you in the cath lab."

Jordan's heart hammered against his ribs. This was it. The moment that would decide if he was "back to his old self" in two days, or facing a "long, slow, and painful" recovery from open-heart surgery.

As the team began to unlock his hospital bed, Aubrie, who was still in the chair beside him, instinctively started to stand, her hand ready to grab his. But she stopped herself. She remembered the ambulance transport and the firm, kind-but-clear instructions from the medical staff: *stay out of the way*. She clenched her fists at her side, her knuckles white, and just... walked beside him as they pushed the bed into the hallway.

The entire Group of 10 followed, a silent, terrified procession.

They traveled through a maze of corridors until they reached a set of large, automated double doors with a red line painted on the floor. The lead transporter stopped. "This is as far as you all can go," he said gently. "We'll take good care of him."

Jordan looked up at the 10 faces of his family, his eyes wide with a fear so profound it left him breathless. He was about to go through those doors alone.

"Wait," he whispered, his voice cracking. The transport team paused.

"I... I want a hug," he said, his voice trembling. "From everyone. One last hug, please".

The nurse on the team looked at the terrified 19-year-old and then at the 9 other anxious faces in the hallway, and her professional demeanor softened. "Be quick," she nodded.

One by one, they surged forward, leaning over the gurney.

Mark was first. "You got this, brother," he said, giving Jordan a firm, quick hug.

Hannah followed, her "mom friend" instincts taking over as she smoothed his hair back, her hug warm and protective. "We will be right here when you wake up. We love you."

Lauren, Sarah, and Madison followed, each giving him a quick, tearful, loving embrace.

Zoe stepped up, her face pale but her expression firm. She didn't just hug him; she took his face in her hands. "The data is clear, Jordan," she whispered, her voice a low, analytical anchor. "You are strong. Your system will recover. We will be here". She hugged him tightly.

Daniel Brooks leaned in, his hug strong and fatherly. "We're proud of you, son. Be strong. We'll see you in a little while."

Laura was next, her hug fierce. "We love you so much, honey. We're right here."

Aubrie was last. She collapsed onto the gurney, her face buried in his neck, her sobs muffled against his hospital gown. "I love you," she cried, her voice desperate. "I love you. Don't leave me. Please."

Jordan hugged her back with what little strength he had. "I won't," he promised. "I'll be right back."

"We have to go, ma'am," the nurse said gently. Aubrie pulled back, her face a mask of tear-streaked terror. The doors hissed open. Jordan's last sight was his ten-person family, his entire world, standing in the hallway as he was pushed into the sterile, unknown room beyond.

He was wheeled into an operating room that was bright, cold, and overwhelmingly technological. The lights on the ceiling were massive, blindingly bright saucers. A team of nurses in blue scrubs moved around him, their voices muffled by masks.

"Okay, Jordan, we're going to move you over to the operating table," a nurse said. They slid him from the bed to a narrow, hard table.

A man in a mask leaned into his view. "Hi Jordan, I'm Dr. Hayes. I'm your anesthesiologist. We're going to give you some medicine to help you sleep".

The cardiologist from his room appeared. "Alright, Jordan, we're ready to get started," he said, his voice reassuring through the mask. "We're going to try that balloon angioplasty, just like we talked about. You'll be asleep for the whole thing."

"I'm placing a mask over your face now," Dr. Hayes said. "I just want you to take a few deep breaths for me. Count backward from ten".

Jordan saw the clear plastic mask come down. He smelled the faint, chemical scent of the anesthetic. He looked at the blinding lights above him, thought of Aubrie's face, and started to count.

"Ten... nine..." his voice was a shaky whisper.

"Eight... sev..."

His eyes felt like lead.

"...six..."

He was gone.
