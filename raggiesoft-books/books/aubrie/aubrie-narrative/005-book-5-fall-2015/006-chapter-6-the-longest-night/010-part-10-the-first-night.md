# Part 10: The First Night

Sleep was impossible. Jordan lay stiffly in the hospital bed, staring at the ceiling, while Aubrie dozed in fitful, 20-minute bursts on the vinyl sofa.

At 11:54 PM, the door to the room *clicked* open, spilling bright hallway light into the darkness. Aubrie jolted awake, her heart pounding, her first thought a panicked, *What's wrong now?* Jordan groaned as a night nurse entered, her shoes squeaking on the linoleum.

"Evening, folks," she whispered, though it felt like a shout in the silent room. "Just time for our midnight vitals check".

Jordan felt very grumpy. He'd been poked and prodded all day, and he finally just wanted to be left alone. "Again?" he rasped.

"Afraid so, sweetie," the nurse said, already wrapping the blood pressure cuff around his arm. "We have to check on you every few hours. It's the rule".

Aubrie watched with a worried, exhausted gaze, her mind getting a crash course in the relentless rhythm of hospital care. The cuff inflated with a *whir*, squeezing Jordan's arm. The nurse clipped the pulse oximeter onto his finger, its red glow illuminating his hand. She jotted the numbers on a chart.

"Alright, all done," she said. As she was about to leave, Jordan spoke up.

"Wait... I need to use the bathroom".

Instinctively, Aubrie stood up, ready to help as she had done so many times before. But the nurse held up a hand. "I've got him. He's a fall risk, and he's hooked up to the monitors. It's a job for us".

Aubrie stopped, flushing. She understood, but she hated it. She hated feeling useless, hated that she couldn't even perform this simple, intimate act of care for him. She watched as the nurse brought over a urinal and helped Jordan, her back turned to them to provide a sliver of dignity.

When the nurse had finished and stepped out, Aubrie looked at the en-suite bathroom. "I might as well go, too," she mumbled.

She emerged a minute later and walked to his bedside. The room was dark again. "J?" she whispered.

He turned his head. "I need a hug," she said, her voice small and trembling.

Jordan, weak as he was, lifted his arms. She leaned down, careful of the IV line and the EKG wires, and held him, burying her face in his shoulder. It was a quick, desperate, grounding hug.

"Try to sleep," she murmured, pulling away. She retreated to her lumpy sofa-bed. Jordan closed his eyes, the beep of the monitor the only sound, and tried to find oblivion.

It felt like only minutes had passed when the *click* of the door and the spill of light jolted them awake again.

*You have got to be kidding me,* Aubrie groaned to herself in mock surprise.

"Okay, Jordan," the nurse's voice said, far too cheerfully. "It's just after 2:00 AM. Time for our next check".

Jordan let out a sound of pure, grumpy despair. As the cuff whirred on his arm again, the nurse got another piece of equipment ready.

"Alright," she said, "and now for the fun part. The doctor wants that arterial sheath out of your arm".

Jordan looked at his arm, where the failed angioplasty had been performed.

"We have these pressure bands on your arm to keep the artery from bleeding," the nurse explained. "I'm going to come in *every 15 minutes* for the next hour or so, to slowly let a little air out of them, one at a time. We have to make sure you don't bleed".

Jordan and Aubrie just stared at her, horrified. *Every 15 minutes?* Sleep was now an impossibility. This was going to be the longest night of their lives.
