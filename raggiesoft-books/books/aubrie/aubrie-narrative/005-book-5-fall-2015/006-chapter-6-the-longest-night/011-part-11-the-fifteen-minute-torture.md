# Part 11: The Fifteen-Minute Torture

The room was plunged back into a near-total darkness, broken only by the steady, glowing numbers of the heart monitor. Jordan lay stiffly, staring at the ceiling, his body aching, his stomach hollow and angry from the forced NPO status. On the vinyl sofa, Aubrie had curled up, but she wasn't sleeping. She was just watching him, her face a pale silhouette against the dim window.

It felt like only a few seconds had passed before the inevitable, jarring *click* of the door latch echoed in the silence.

The door swung open, spilling the bright, cold light of the hallway back into their room. The nurse’s shoes squeaked on the floor.

"Just me again," she whispered cheerfully. "Time for the 2:15 check."

Jordan let out an audible, grumpy groan. He squeezed his eyes shut as the nurse turned on the small task light over his bed. Aubrie sat up, her heart doing its now-familiar panicked jolt.

The *whir* of the blood pressure cuff inflating was a sound they were both beginning to hate. The nurse jotted down the reading.

"Okay, Jordan," she said, her voice clinical. "Letting a little more air out of the next band now".

He felt her fingers on his arm, followed by the faint, slow *hiss* as the pressure was incrementally released. The nurse watched the site for a moment, dabbed it with gauze, and nodded.

"Looks perfect. No bleeding. You're doing great. See you in fifteen minutes."

She clicked off the light. The door *clicked* shut. They were in darkness again.

"This is..." Jordan rasped, "the worst."

"Just try to rest," Aubrie whispered from the sofa, though she knew it was impossible.

He must have dozed for a few minutes, because the next *click* of the door yanked him from a hazy, anxious dream.

"2:30, folks," the nurse's voice chirped.

"Ugh," Jordan groaned, not even opening his eyes this time. He just held his arm out, a resigned sacrifice.

The *whir* of the cuff. The *hiss* of the next band.

"See you in fifteen."

Click.

Aubrie, for her part, had managed to drift into a fitful, exhausted sleep, her head propped awkwardly on the vinyl armrest. The 2:45 AM *click* of the door made her jerk upright, a small, terrified gasp escaping her lips.

"It's just me, honey. 2:45 check."

Whir. Hiss.

"Almost done. Just one more to go. I'll be back at 3:00 AM for the last one".

Click.

For the next fifteen minutes, they both just lay there, staring at the door in the darkness, waiting for it. The anticipation was almost worse than the interruption.

Promptly at 3:00 AM, the door opened.

"Alright, Jordan, last one," the nurse said, her voice full of practiced sympathy.

The cuff whirred. She then began the slow, final deflation of the last pressure band. With a gentle tug, she removed the entire apparatus from his arm.

"There we go. All done with that," she said. "The hard part's over."

Jordan let out a breath he felt like he'd been holding for an hour.

"Now," the nurse continued, "I just need to come back for your 4:00 AM vitals check."

A fresh groan of despair came from Jordan. Aubrie muffled a laugh that was half a sob into her pillow.

"After that, you're clear until the 6:00 AM check," the nurse promised. "You two try and get some real sleep now, okay?"

The door clicked shut, leaving them in the quietest, most profound darkness of the night. They were finally, truly, alone for more than fifteen minutes. The relief was so immense it was physically painful.

Jordan let his head fall to the side, looking at Aubrie's small, huddled shape on the sofa. He was exhausted. He was starving. And he was terrified of the bed that was waiting for him in Roanoke. But in that moment, as he listened to the steady, quiet breathing of the girl who had refused to leave him, he felt a single, powerful flicker of peace. He wasn't alone. And for now, that was enough.
