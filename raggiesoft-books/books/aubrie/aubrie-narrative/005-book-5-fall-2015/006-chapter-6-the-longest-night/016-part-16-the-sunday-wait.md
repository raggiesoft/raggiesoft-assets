# Part 16: The Sunday Wait

The 6:00 AM vitals check on Sunday, November 15th, brought the same bad news. No bed. Jordan was still NPO, and now, after nearly 48 hours without food, he was weak and deeply irritable.

Madison was a wreck, having endured the same lumpy, sleepless night as Aubrie.

At 7:00 AM, the door opened, and a fresh rotation arrived. Daniel, Laura, and a well-rested (but still worried) Aubrie came in, bringing with them Lauren.

"My turn," Lauren said softly, holding up her own overnight bag.

Madison nearly cried with relief. She gave Jordan's hand a final squeeze. "I'm going to go pass out. Be good," she mumbled, grabbing her things.

Daniel pulled the doctor aside in the hallway. "What's the hold-up? He can't stay like this."

"Roanoke is full," the doctor said, his frustration clear. "It's the only Level 1 heart center in the region. We're on the list. As soon as a CVICU bed opens, he's the next one out. I'm hoping it's today, but I can't promise it."

Defeated, Daniel returned to the room where Lauren was taking her place in the chair beside Jordan's bed. The agonizing wait continued.
