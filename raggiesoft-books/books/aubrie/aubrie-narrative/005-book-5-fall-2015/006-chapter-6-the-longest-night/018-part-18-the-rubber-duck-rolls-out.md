# Part 18: The "Rubber Duck" Rolls Out

The transport team, pushing a high-tech critical care gurney, entered Jordan's room. "Okay, Mr. Fuller, it's time to go. The bed is ready for you in Roanoke."

The Group of 10, who had been keeping a tense vigil, stood back. As the team began to disconnect Jordan's monitors, Zoe stepped forward, her mind racing.

"Wait," she said, addressing the lead paramedic. "I-81 is a variable. A bad wreck, especially with trucks, could stop you for hours. Why aren't you taking a helicopter? It's faster."

The paramedic, busy checking an IV line, didn't look up. "Because a chopper ride is a hundred thousand dollars, and insurance will deny the claim instantly," he said, his voice blunt. "It's not considered medically necessary. We have a clear ground route. Barring a catastrophe, we'll have him there in 45 minutes."

The logic was cold, but absolute. They were at the mercy of the interstate.

The entire family followed the gurney as Jordan was wheeled out of the cardiac unit and down to the ambulance bay. The convoy was already waiting, Daniel's minivan, Caleb's SUV, and Mark's sedan parked in a line, ready to move.

One by one, the group gave Jordan a final, desperate hug before he was loaded into the ambulance. The doors slammed shut, and the ambulance pulled out, its sirens silent for now as it navigated the hospital campus. The convoy fell into its practiced formation behind it.

As they pulled onto U.S. 460, heading for the I-81 North on-ramp, Daniel's voice crackled over the walkie-talkie app.

"Alright, this is the Rubber Duck to the convoy," he said, his voice low and steady. "We're 10-23 with our package, eastbound. Watchtower, Pig Pen, you got a copy on me?"

"That's a 10-4, Rubber Duck," Caleb's voice came back instantly. "Watchtower is secure behind you".

"Big 10-4 from the Pig Pen," Mark added.

They approached the I-81 interchange, but as they neared the ramp, they saw a terrifying sight: a sea of red taillights. The ramp itself was blocked by a state trooper, flares flickering on the pavement. The ambulance, which had been in the lead, slowed and pulled onto the shoulder just before the exit.

"Rubber Duck to convoy, hold your position," Daniel ordered.

The ambulance driver hopped out and walked back to Daniel's minivan, his face grim.

"You're not going to believe this," the driver said, leaning in the window. "Just got the alert. Massive pile-up on I-81 North, looks like two semis tangled up. Both northbound lanes are blocked for hours."

A wave of cold panic went through the car. Aubrie, in the seat behind Daniel, let out a small, terrified sound.

"We're diverting," the driver continued. "We have to take U.S. 11 through Christiansburg and Shawsville, all the way into Roanoke. It's going to be slow, but it's the only way".

Daniel just nodded, his knuckles white on the steering wheel. "We'll be right behind you."

The driver ran back to his ambulance. Daniel picked up his phone.

"Rubber Duck to convoy. Change of plans. The interstate is shut down. We're diverting to U.S. 11. It's going to be a long, slow ride. Stay tight. Pig Pen, Watchtower, you copy?"

"10-4, Rubber Duck," both Mark and Caleb replied, their voices heavy.

Inside the ambulance, Jordan, alone and terrified, felt the vehicle lurch forward, bypassing the highway ramp and continuing on the slow, local road. He heard the *click* of traffic lights, the slowing and starting of town driving. His 45-minute emergency transfer had just turned into a grueling, two-hour crawl, and there was nothing anyone could do but sit, wait, and pray.
