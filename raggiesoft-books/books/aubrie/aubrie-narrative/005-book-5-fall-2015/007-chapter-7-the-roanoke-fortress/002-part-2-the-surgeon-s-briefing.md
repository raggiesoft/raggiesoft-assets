# Part 2: The Surgeon's Briefing

Later that evening, the door to Jordan's prep room hissed open. Lauren, who had been dozing in the recliner, sat up straight, her heart pounding.

A man in surgical scrubs entered, his face masked but his eyes kind and focused. "Jordan Fuller? I'm Dr. Hassan, the cardiothoracic surgeon who will be leading your team tomorrow."

Jordan nodded, his throat too dry to speak. Lauren instinctively tightened her grip on his hand.

"I want to walk you through exactly what we're planning for tomorrow morning," Dr. Hassan said, pulling up a stool. "You're here because the angioplasty at New River Valley failed. That blockage is too hard, too calcified, for a simple balloon to open. So, we are going to build a detour around it. That procedure is the CABG, or Coronary Artery Bypass Graft."

He made eye contact with Jordan, his voice calm and expert. "To do this safely, we will have to temporarily stop your heart and place you on a heart-lung bypass machine, which will take over the work of breathing and circulating your blood".

Jordan's eyes widened, and he swallowed hard.

"We will make an incision down your chest and divide your breastbone to give us access to the heart," Dr. Hassan continued, his tone clinical and reassuring. "We will then take a healthy artery from your chest wall and graft it onto your coronary artery, completely bypassing the blocked section. In your case, we are planning for a single bypass, which is good news".

"Your surgery is scheduled for 6:00 AM tomorrow". He paused, letting that sink in. "This is a long, meticulous procedure. It will take most of the day".

"Afterward," he said, "you will be transferred directly to the CVICU—our intensive care unit. You will still be unconscious and will have a breathing tube in place, connected to a ventilator, which will breathe for you while the anesthesia wears off".

"What about...?" Lauren started, her voice trembling, "What about us? His family?"

Dr. Hassan gave her a compassionate look. "I will come out and speak to your family in the waiting room the moment the procedure is finished". He then looked back at Jordan. "We will allow your family to visit you briefly in the CVICU tomorrow evening, once you are settled".

"But I need you to understand, Jordan," the doctor stressed, "you will be under heavy sedation to help you heal. You will likely not be aware of their presence, and you probably won't remember their visit at all".

"After that," he said, standing up, "you'll need to rest. We do not allow overnight visitors in the CVICU; the unit is too active". "My team will come for you at 6:00 AM. For tonight, your only job is to let Lauren here worry for you and try to get some sleep."

The surgeon nodded to them both and left the room.

Jordan stared at the spot where the doctor had been, the words "stop your heart" and "divide your breastbone" echoing in his mind. He was terrified. He turned his head and looked at Lauren, his anchor in this new, sterile, terrifying place. She was just as scared as he was, but she squeezed his hand.

"We'll be right outside," she promised. "All of us."
