# Part 5: The Last Night

After the emotional video call with their family, a heavy, quiet dread settled back into the CVICU prep room. Lauren helped Jordan get settled for the night, the reality of the 6:00 AM surgery looming over them.

"You should try to get some sleep, J," Lauren whispered, adjusting the pillows on the recliner, which was a clear step up from the vinyl sofa at New River Valley but still not a bed.

"You too," Jordan rasped. His eyes were wide in the dim light. "But Lauren... just so you know... they don't *let* you sleep."

Lauren looked at him, confused. "What do you mean?"

"At the other hospital... after the... after it failed," he stammered, "they came in all night. Vitals checks. Every two hours. Woke us up every time." He remembered the 15-minute torture of the sheath removal, followed by the 2-hour checks. "It's going to be a long night."

Lauren’s heart sank, but she forced a brave smile. "Okay. A long night. We can do this."

She changed in the en-suite bathroom, swapping her day clothes for a comfortable night shirt and sleep shorts. She settled into the recliner, pulling the blankets up. "Goodnight, J. Try to rest. I'm right here."

Jordan just nodded, staring at the ceiling, listening to the steady, electronic *beep... beep... beep* of his heart monitor.

Sleep was impossible. Just as Lauren finally started to drift off, the door *clicked* open, spilling bright hallway light into the room.

"Just me," a night nurse whispered, her voice still loud in the silence. "Midnight vitals check."

Lauren sat bolt upright, her heart hammering. The nurse was efficient, the *whir* of the blood pressure cuff a now-hated sound. She checked his IV, jotted notes on a chart, and left. The room was dark again.

"You weren't kidding," Lauren mumbled, trying to find a comfortable position.

*Click*. 2:00 AM. The light. The *whir* of the cuff.

"You've got to be kidding me," Lauren groaned, her voice thick with sleep. The nurse just gave her a sympathetic smile.

*Click*. 4:00 AM. The light. The *whir*.

Lauren was now fully, miserably awake. She was exhausted, her body stiff from the recliner, and she was starting to feel grumpy. She finally understood why Aubrie had looked like a zombie back at New River Valley. This wasn't just "staying"; it was a marathon of interruptions.

She looked over at Jordan, who was just staring at the ceiling, his face pale and resigned. He was still NPO, and she knew he must be starving. Her own hunger was a dull ache, but she refused to complain, pushing it down.

The door *clicked* open one last time. It was 5:45 AM.

"Good morning, Jordan," the nurse said, her voice different this time—brisk and purposeful. "This is our last check."

She took his vitals, but then, a new team arrived. The anesthesiologist, Dr. Hayes, and the surgical transport team.

"Alright, Mr. Fuller," Dr. Hayes said, his voice calm. "It's 6:00 AM. It's time to go."

This was it. Lauren stood up, her entire body trembling. The rest of their family, who had been gathering in the waiting room since 4:30 AM, were alerted and filed into the room, their faces pale and full of a desperate, silent love.

The pre-op "goodbye" ritual began again, but this time it was heavier, more final. This was not a test run.

One by one, the Group of 17 leaned over the gurney.

"You got this, brother," Mark said, his voice thick as he gave Jordan a firm hug.

"We're so proud of you, son. We'll be right here," Daniel said, his own hug strong and fatherly.

Madison, Zoe, Sarah, and Hannah all followed, their hugs and kisses quick, tearful, and full of desperate love.

Aubrie was last. She collapsed onto the gurney, her face buried in his neck, her sobs muffled against his hospital gown. "I love you," she cried, her voice desperate. "I love you. Don't leave me. Please."

Jordan hugged her back with what little strength he had. "I won't," he promised. "I'll be right back".

"We have to go, ma'am," the nurse said gently.

Aubrie pulled back, her face a mask of tear-streaked terror. The team unlocked the gurney and began to push it toward the door.

Jordan's last sight was his seventeen-person family, his entire world, standing huddled in the doorway as he was pushed into the sterile corridor beyond.

He was wheeled into an operating room that was bright, cold, and overwhelmingly technological. The lights on the ceiling were massive, blindingly bright saucers.

"Alright, Jordan, we're ready to get started," the surgeon's voice said from behind a mask. "Dr. Hayes here is going to give you some medicine to help you sleep".

"I'm placing a mask over your face now," the anesthesiologist said. "I just want you to take a few deep breaths for me. Count backward from ten".

Jordan saw the clear plastic mask come down. He looked at the blinding lights above him, thought of Aubrie's face, and started to count.

"Ten... nine..." his voice was a shaky whisper.

"Eight... sev..."

He was gone.
