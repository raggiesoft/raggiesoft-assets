# Part 2: The Coming Storm

A few hours after the CPAP crisis, Jordan was deemed stable enough to be moved. He was transferred from the high-intensity CVICU to a private step-down recovery room on the same floor. The new room was quieter, felt more like a normal hospital room, and, most importantly, had a small reclining chair that signaled a new phase of his recovery: the hospital would allow one overnight visitor.

Daniel and Laura Brooks were with him, having taken over the watch from Aubrie and Madison, who were now in the waiting room with the others. It was just after 12:00 PM on Tuesday, November 17th. Daniel turned on the room's small television, keeping the volume low. They tuned into the local noon news, catching the weather report.

"And as we look ahead, folks," the meteorologist said cheerfully, "we're tracking a significant low-pressure system. It's too early to predict exact totals, but we could be looking at some *accumulating snow* in the mountains, possibly starting as early as tomorrow morning".

Laura and Daniel exchanged a worried look. A major snowstorm was the last thing they needed. Daniel pulled out his phone and stepped into the hallway to make a call to the "Jordan Comms" group in the waiting room, patching in the students who were back at CPI.

"Okay, team," he said quietly. "The plan is still on. Zoe, you're taking the first watch tonight?"

"Affirmative," Zoe's voice came back, clear and logical. "The rest of the group is attending their afternoon classes to keep up". "My last class ends at 3:15". "I'll head straight to the CPI Transit Center, but I will have just missed the 3:15 PM RVT Roanoke Express. I will have to wait for the 4:15 PM departure".

Daniel processed the new timeline. "So you'll be getting here much later, then."

"Correct," Zoe confirmed. "The ride to Campbell Court is 60 minutes". "I will arrive there at 5:15 PM. Assuming a smooth 5-minute transfer to the \#51 bus, my new ETA at the hospital is approximately 5:30 PM."

"Understood," Daniel said. "Laura and I will wait for you to relieve us. Now, about tomorrow... Aubrie, with this snow coming, you should plan to take the Wednesday shift. We'll drive you here in the morning, but you need to pack as if you're being snowed-in with him".

"I'll be ready," Aubrie promised from the other end of the call.

The plan was set. Daniel hung up and went back into the room. Jordan was dozing fitfully, his body exhausted, his mind mercifully quiet. Daniel and Laura settled in for a long afternoon, waiting for Zoe's arrival.
