# Part 3: The Early Arrival

Back at CPI, Zoe’s 3:15 PM class was abruptly canceled; the instructor was sick. Her academic day was suddenly over at 12:30 PM. She immediately sent a text to the "Jordan Comms" chat.

Jordan Comms:

Zoe: \[LOGISTICS\] My 3:15 class was canceled. I'm done for the day. Heading to the transit center now to catch the 1:15 PM RVT Roanoke Express. New ETA at the hospital is approx 2:30 PM.

Daniel and Laura, still on watch in Jordan’s room, saw the message. "Zoe's on her way," Daniel said, relieved. "She's coming early."

Zoe boarded the 1:15 PM coach bus at the CPI Transit Center. As it sped down I-81, she watched the clouds, which looked heavy and ominous, pressing down on the mountains. By the time the bus pulled into the Falling Branch Park & Ride stop thirty minutes later, the first light snow flurries had begun to fall.

She arrived at Roanoke's Campbell Court transfer station at 2:15 PM. The snow was no longer light; it was coming down hard now, thick and steady. She made the quick transfer to the waiting \#51 bus for the five-minute ride to the hospital.

When the bus dropped her off, she saw that the snow was starting to stick to the grass, and the hospital's maintenance crews had already spread salt on the sidewalks and roadways. The storm was here, a day ahead of schedule.

She checked in at the CVICU desk and went straight to Jordan's room. Daniel and Laura greeted her with tired, grateful smiles. Jordan was dozing fitfully, his face pale and pained.

"Zoe?" he mumbled, blinking his eyes open as she approached the bed.

"I'm here," she said softly. She didn't hesitate, leaning down to wrap him in a firm, grounding hug, careful of the wires on his chest.

"I came as fast as I could." She pulled back and glanced out the room's window. The light snow was beautiful, swirling against the glass, but deeply concerning.

"The snow, Jordan," she said, her voice full of a new, quiet worry. "It's starting. It's a day early".

She stared out the window, her logistical mind processing the new variables. The rotation plan was compromised. If this blizzard was as bad as predicted, the roads would be impassable by morning. She realized, with a sudden, absolute certainty, that she was going to be snowed in at the hospital with him.

Zoe was perfectly okay with this; she would protect him. But her analytical mind also processed the emotional data: she knew, with absolute certainty, that Jordan would rather be snowed in with Aubrie.
