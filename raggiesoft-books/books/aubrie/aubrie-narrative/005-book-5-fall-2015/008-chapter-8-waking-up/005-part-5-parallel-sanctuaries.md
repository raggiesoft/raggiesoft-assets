# Part 5: Parallel Sanctuaries

The snow was no longer a beautiful, swirling novelty; it was a rapidly escalating threat. Back at CPI, just before 3:00 PM, a university-wide alert buzzed on everyone's mPhones: "Due to worsening weather conditions, all remaining classes and campus activities are canceled for the day".

The "Group of 8" (minus Zoe) met at the CPI Transit Center, which was descending into a controlled chaos of students trying to get home. The digital signs for the RVT Roanoke Express and the Bluewater Transit Maroon Express were both flashing "ON TIME," presenting Aubrie with an agonizing choice.

"I... I should go to Roanoke," she said, her voice tight with panic as she looked toward the Roanoke-bound bus. "Zoe's all alone. What if he needs me?"

"Aubrie, no," Hannah said, putting a firm, sisterly arm around her. "Zoe's safe in the hospital. We're not. Look at this." She pointed to the snow, which was now coming down in thick, heavy sheets. "The safest thing for all of us is to get to Geneva Street, together. We'll be useless to them if we get stranded on I-81."

"She's right," Mark added, his voice full of brotherly concern. "We stick together. We go home, we hold down the fort. That's the plan."

Aubrie looked from the Roanoke bus to the faces of her family. They were right. It was the logical, safe move, even if it felt like a betrayal. She gave a small, defeated nod.

The Bluewater Transit Maroon Express coach bus pulled into its bay. The group boarded, their CPI IDs a familiar flash of plastic. They were lucky to get seats; the bus was packed with other commuters trying to beat the storm.

"I'll text Zoe," Aubrie said, pulling out her mPhone as the bus pulled away for the 30-minute ride to Falling Branch. "I'll tell her we're heading for the house. We'll video message as soon as we're safe."

Back in Jordan's room at Star City, Zoe's phone buzzed with Aubrie's update. She read the text and felt a wave of relief. The rest of her system was moving to a secure node.

ZOE.Systems.Logs.SystemSchedulerLog: Node(CPI) classes canceled. Agents(Group_of_7) diverting to Node(Geneva.Street). 11-17-2015 15:05:11

ZOE.Systems.Logs.KernelLog: Agent(Aubrie).EmotionalState == Anxious. Decision == Correct. 'Safety.In.Numbers' protocol is active. 11-17-2015 15:05:12

ZOE.Systems.Logs.KernelLog: All system nodes are now accounted for and secure. 11-17-2015 15:05:13

She put her phone away as a nurse entered, this one in different-colored scrubs. "Alright, Jordan," the nurse said, her voice cheerful. "I'm from Respiratory. Time to get those lungs working. We need to prevent pneumonia."

The nurse explained that normally, cardiac patients would be up and walking the corridors by now, but she understood that his cerebral palsy made that specific exercise impossible.

"So, we're starting with this," she said, holding up a small, plastic contraption. "This is your new best friend, an incentive spirometer. I need you to breathe into this, as deep as you can, ten times every hour you're awake."

Jordan looked at the device. He took a breath to try, but the simple act of inhaling deeply sent a sharp, agonizing pain through his breastbone. He winced, a low groan escaping his lips.

"I know it hurts," the nurse said sympathetically. "That's how you know it's working. It's the most important job you have right now."

As the nurse left, Jordan looked at Zoe, his eyes full of a new misery. He was in pain, he was scared, and he was still incredibly hungry.

"Zoe," he rasped, "am I... am I still NPO? Can I please eat?"

Zoe, seeing his distress, pressed the call button. When the floor nurse returned, Zoe advocated for him. "He's in a lot of pain, and he's starving. Has his diet been updated since the transfer?"

The nurse checked the computer. "Ah, good news!" she said, smiling. "Dr. Hassan just updated your chart. We're advancing you to a 'cardiac-clear liquid' diet. It's not much, but it's a start. I can get you some broth and some ClearSet."

Jordan almost cried with relief. It was the best news he'd had all day.

"And..." he added, his face flushing, "I... I really need to use the restroom."

"Of course," the nurse said, her voice all business. "Zoe, would you mind stepping out for just a moment?"

Zoe nodded, her mind immediately seizing the practical opportunity. She gave Jordan's hand a final squeeze.

"Okay," she said, looking at Jordan. "While you're... busy... I'll run down to the cafeteria and grab some dinner to go. I'll be right back, and we can eat together."

Jordan managed a weak nod, grateful.

Zoe slipped out of the room, giving the nurse privacy, and headed for the cafeteria, her mind already calculating the most efficient route. The long, slow, and painful road to recovery had officially begun.
