# Part 10: The Vitals Check Torture

Zoe settled into the recliner for the long haul, her laptop open. She wasn't just watching; she was working, her mind finding a familiar, comforting rhythm in the logical architecture of Fin#. Jordan, exhausted from the day, drifted into a light, uneasy sleep, the steady *beep* of his heart monitor the room's only soundtrack.

At 11:00 PM, the door *clicked* open.

"Evening check," the night nurse whispered, flipping on the small task light over Jordan's bed.

Jordan groaned and stirred, his body tensing. Zoe, who had been deep in a coding sprint, simply looked up, observed the nurse, and logged the interruption.

ZOE.Systems.Logs.SystemSchedulerLog: 'Protocol: Vitals.Check' initiated. 11-17-2015 23:01:05

ZOE.Systems.Logs.KernelLog: Agent(Jordan).Status == Woken. Emotional.State == Annoyed. 11-17-2015 23:01:10

ZOE.Systems.Logs.KernelLog: System.Admin(Nurse) is efficient. Protocol complete. 11-17-2015 23:03:45

The nurse left. The room was dark again. Zoe, confident in her ability to handle logical, scheduled interruptions, closed her laptop and her eyes, calculating she could achieve several 90-minute REM cycles between checks.

She was wrong.

It felt like minutes later when the door *clicked* open again. 1:00 AM. The light. The *whir* of the cuff.

Zoe jolted awake, her heart pounding. She had been in a deep sleep, and the sudden noise and light felt like an assault.

ZOE.Systems.Logs.KernelLog: WARNING: Unscheduled system interrupt. 11-18-2015 01:00:15

ZOE.Systems.Logs.KernelLog: Correction: 'Protocol: Vitals.Check' initiated. This is a scheduled interrupt. 11-18-2015 01:00:16

ZOE.Systems.Logs.EmotionalStateLog: Internal.State == 'Annoyed'. Sleep.Cycle == 'Interrupted'. 11-18-2015 01:00:17

Jordan, who was now used to it, just grumbled and held out his arm in his sleep, his body conditioned to the routine. The nurse finished and left. Zoe let out a frustrated sigh. She now understood Aubrie's and Lauren's exhaustion on a data-driven, experiential level. This was inefficient.

*Click*. 3:00 AM.

Zoe was pulled from a dream about compilers. She sat up, her hair a mess, and glared at the door. "Again?" she mumbled, her voice thick with sleep.

The nurse, unfazed, did her work. *Whir. Beep. Click*.

Zoe fell back into the recliner, her logical preparation completely defeated by her body's simple, desperate need for sleep. She was grumpy, she was exhausted, and she finally, truly, understood.

*Click*. 5:00 AM.

"I hate this," Zoe groaned into her blanket.

Jordan, who had been awake for the last hour, just made a small, sympathetic sound. He hated it too, but he also knew it was part of the system designed to get him healthy again. He just wanted the night to be over. He was ready for the next phase.
