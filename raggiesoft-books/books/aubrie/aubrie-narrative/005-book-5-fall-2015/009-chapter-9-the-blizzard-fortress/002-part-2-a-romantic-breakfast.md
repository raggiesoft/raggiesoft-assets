# Part 2: A Romantic Breakfast

Zoe returned a few minutes later with her own to-go container of cafeteria food, just as an orderly was delivering Jordan's breakfast tray. The sight of real food—a small bowl of oatmeal and another of scrambled eggs—made Jordan feel almost human again.

"This," he said, his voice raspy, "is the best-looking meal I have ever seen."

Zoe, seeing he was stuck in the bed, pulled the chair close, sitting right next to him so they could eat together. She tried to make it feel like a romantic meal, arranging his tray so he could reach everything easily and opening her own food with a sense of shared experience rather than just medical necessity.

Jordan ate slowly, the simple, soft food a profound comfort. He was mainly appreciating just having female company. He missed Aubrie terribly, but he understood the reality of their situation. He glanced at the window, where the blizzard was no longer a beautiful swirl; it was a raging, chaotic wall of white.

"She's not coming, is she?" he whispered, his voice small.

Zoe's gaze followed his to the window. "No," she said softly, but with her usual, grounding certainty. "She's stuck in Christiansburg. Buses are not running. All non-essential services are shut down. Daniel and Laura will not, and cannot, drive in this. We are officially snowed in".

The finality of it, of being trapped here, made the room feel colder. Jordan put his spoon down, his exhaustion and fear returning. What he needed most right now, more than food, was comfort. He needed something his mother had never given him. He needed hugs.

He looked at Zoe, his "safe person," his anchor in this storm. He didn't even have to ask. She saw the look in his eyes, put her own breakfast aside, and moved from the chair to sit carefully on the edge of his bed.

She leaned in, wrapping her arms around him in a firm, meaningful, and deeply comforting embrace. Jordan sank into it, his eyes closing as he held onto her, the steady warmth of her hug a firewall against the blizzard, his pain, and his fear.
