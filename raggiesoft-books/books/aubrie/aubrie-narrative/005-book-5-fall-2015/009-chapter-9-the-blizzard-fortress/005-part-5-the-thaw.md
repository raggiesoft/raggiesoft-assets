# Part 5: The Thaw

By the afternoon of their third day snowed-in (Thursday, November 19th), a change occurred. The relentless, howling wind outside the hospital window finally quieted, and the thick, horizontal torrent of snow eased into a gentle, light snowfall. The blizzard system was finally moving east, away from Roanoke.

Jordan, who had been dozing in the recliner, noticed the change in the light. He looked out the window. "Zoe... is it stopping?"

Zoe, who had been sketching out Fin# architecture on her laptop, looked up. "Affirmative. The storm is breaking. It looks like the roads might be clear by tomorrow."

A huge, relieved smile broke across Jordan's face. "I get to go home soon," he whispered. The thought of returning to the Brooks' basement felt like a miracle.

As evening approached, Zoe stood up and stretched. "Okay, I'm heading to the cafeteria to get my dinner. I'll bring it back so we can eat together when your tray arrives". Remembering Jordan's rule about the hospital staff, she grabbed her clothes and disappeared into the en-suite bathroom to change before leaving the room.

She returned just as Jordan's dinner tray was being delivered. His diet had been advanced again. Tonight, it wasn't just broth; it was a small, heart-healthy portion of baked cod, a scoop of unsalted mashed potatoes, and some soft, steamed carrots. Zoe had brought back an identical meal for herself, continuing her research into his new dietary needs.

They ate together, the quiet companionship a comfort in the sterile room. As Zoe ate, she noticed her athletic shorts felt a little looser than they had a few days ago. The stress, the poor sleep, and the hospital food were having an effect. She was definitely losing weight.

Her analytical gaze shifted to Jordan. He looked smaller in the hospital bed, his face thinner and paler. The days of being NPO, followed by clear liquids and tiny soft meals, must be having a similar effect.

"I think I'm losing weight from all this," she said, more to herself than to him. "I wonder if you are, too".

She paused, her brow furrowing as she processed the logistical problem. "How would they even check your weight?" she mused aloud. "You can't stand on a standard scale".
