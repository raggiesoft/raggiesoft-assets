# Part 1: The Basement Sanctuary

The Rubber Duck convoy pulled onto Geneva Street late Sunday afternoon, the familiar sight of the Brooks' home a profound relief. The trip from Roanoke had been quiet, Jordan too weak and the rest of the group too emotionally drained to talk.

The "human elevator"—Caleb and Owen, supported by Mark—expertly transferred Jordan from the minivan, their movements practiced and gentle.

"Alright, son, welcome home," Daniel said, his voice heavy with relief as they got him inside. "We need to get you settled. You've got a choice: we can set you up in the first-floor guest room. It's right near the main bathroom, might be easier on you".

Jordan, his body aching from the long ride, immediately shook his head. He looked toward the internal stairs leading down.

"No," he whispered, his voice raspy. "I want to be in the basement".

"It's a lot of up and down, J," Laura said gently.

"It's home," he insisted. His autistic mind didn't register the first floor as his territory. The basement—the king bed, the sofa, the desks—that was his sanctuary. He just wanted to rest.

Daniel understood. "The basement it is."

The team carefully carried him downstairs. The rest of the group followed, bringing in the bags and medical supplies.

"Alright, let's get you on the sofa," Mark said, ever practical. "We can put on the game, or we can finish *The Gauntlet*...".

Hannah, seeing Jordan flinch at the very idea of noise, quickly intervened. "Guys, no," she said softly. "He doesn't need TV. He needs quiet".

Aubrie and Madison, who had witnessed his exhaustion in the hospital, nodded in agreement. "He hasn't really slept in three days," Madison explained. "He just needs to sleep".

Mark and Hannah immediately understood. "Right. Sorry, J. Quiet it is."

They helped him get settled on the large sectional sofa. He lay down, and Aubrie sat at the end, lifting his head onto her lap. Madison pulled a large armchair close, sitting beside them, her hand resting on his arm. The rest of the group quietly unpacked or sat nearby, their presence a silent, protective wall.

Jordan let out a long, shaky breath, the sterile, beeping terror of the hospital finally fading. He looked up at Aubrie, his eyes full of a lingering question. "Are you... are you really okay? About Zoe?"

Aubrie smiled, gently stroking his hair. "Jordan, I was *so happy* you had Zoe. I was terrified you'd be alone in that storm." She leaned down, her voice a soft, steady promise. "I still love you. And I meant what I said back in 2014. My promise is good".

She looked at Madison, who smiled and nodded, confirming the pact.

"You are allowed to be romantic with all five of us," Aubrie continued, "That includes Zoe. That includes Madi. That includes Lauren and Sarah". "And once you are healed—once you are *completely* off these sternal precautions—I won't care if you have sex with all five of us, either".

She brushed a tear from his cheek. "The *only* reason I don't want you doing anything right now is because it's not safe. You just had your chest broken open. Any demanding physical activity is the last thing you need".

Madison took his hand. "She's right. We just want you to heal. The rest can wait."

Jordan looked at his two original roommates, his two safest people, and closed his eyes. He was home. He was safe. He was loved. He finally, truly, slept.
