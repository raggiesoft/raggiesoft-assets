# Part 5: The New Sleep Protocol

After his bath, Jordan felt clean, exhausted, and deeply at peace. The rest of the family was quietly relaxing in the basement, the atmosphere warm and safe. As true bedtime approached, Jordan looked at the large, new king-sized bed that now dominated the sleeping area—the bed they had all picked out after the Fall Break.

"Okay," he said, his voice quiet but happy. "I'm ready to sleep. I want the puppy pile".

The five women of the pact smiled, ready to accommodate him. But before anyone could move, Daniel Brooks, who had been observing from the desk, stepped forward.

"Whoa, hold on there, son," Daniel said, his voice gentle but firm. He stopped the plan right there.

Jordan looked up, confused. "What? But... the bed is big enough."

"The bed is plenty big," Daniel agreed. "But you are not. Not right now. You're a high-risk patient on strict sternal precautions. Six people in one bed, no matter how big, is not safe". "Someone could roll over in their sleep, jostle you, and bad things could happen. I can't let you risk that."

Jordan’s face fell, but he understood the logic.

"But," Daniel continued, seeing his disappointment, "this is your home, and you need to feel safe. The compromise is this: you are allowed one person in the bed with you".

Jordan didn't have to think. He looked at Aubrie. "I want Aubrie," he said.

"Done," Daniel said. "As for the rest of you," he looked at the other four women, "we're bringing the old portable queen beds back out. We'll set them up right here. You can all be in the same room, so Jordan knows you're with him, but he will be *safe* in his own bed".

The solution was perfect. While Daniel and Mark retrieved the two portable queen beds from storage, the women decided on the new arrangement. Aubrie would be with Jordan. Zoe and Lauren would take one queen bed, and Sarah and Madison would take the other.

With the sleeping situation resolved, the "upstairs" family said their goodnights. Daniel and Laura went around, giving each of their "kids" a hug, ending with Jordan.

"You rest, son," Daniel said. "We'll see you in the morning."

Mark and Hannah did the same. "Goodnight, guys," Mark said. "Glad you're home, brother."

The four of them headed up the internal stairs. Daniel and Laura went to their master bedroom on the first floor, while Mark and Hannah headed to Zoe's old childhood bedroom, which she had happily given over to them as their permanent room while the new house was being built.

Downstairs, the five women of the pact helped Jordan transfer one last time, getting him settled safely in the middle of the new king-sized bed. Aubrie slid in beside him, wrapping an arm gently around his middle. On the other side of the room, the other four women settled into their own beds, their quiet chatter a comforting, familiar sound in the darkness. He was home, and he was safe.
