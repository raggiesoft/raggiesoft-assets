# Part 7: A New Resolve

The rest of Monday passed in a quiet, domestic blur. Jordan, physically weak but mentally clear, dozed on the sofa. Laura and Daniel worked from home, their presence a quiet, constant comfort. The house, however, felt unnaturally empty.

Jordan missed the chaotic, bright energy of his friends. He knew they were at CPI, scrambling to catch up on the classes they had all missed while sitting in hospital waiting rooms. He was relieved their instructors were being sympathetic and working with them.

His own academic future felt less certain. He was still waiting for the official hospital documentation he would need to submit to the university to get his own excused absences. As he lay there, his mind drifted back over the last year.

He looked at Daniel and Laura, who were working quietly at the dining table.

"Daniel? Laura?" he said, his voice quiet but firm.

They both looked up from their laptops immediately, their full attention on him.

"I just... I need you to know," he said, his voice gaining strength. "I'm not dropping out. I'm going to pass this semester. We're so close to the end".

Laura smiled, her eyes soft. "Honey, you just had open-heart surgery. No one would blame you if you needed to take an incomplete."

"No," Jordan insisted, pushing himself up slightly on the cushions. "I'm not letting her win. I've come too far." His gaze became distant, reflective. "A year ago... I was just a shy, scared boy. I was terrified of everything. I didn't even know how to get dressed by myself".

He looked down at his own hands, resting on the blanket. "I'm not him anymore. I'm not letting her—or this—take my classes away from me. I'm going to pass."

A slow, proud smile spread across Daniel's face. He looked at the young man on the sofa, a man he was so proud to call his son.

"I know you will, Jordan," Daniel said. "And we're right here. We'll help you with whatever you need to get it done."
