# Part 9: The Fortress Returns

The quiet of the basement, where Jordan had been dozing and working with Daniel and Laura, was suddenly and wonderfully shattered. The door at the top of the stairs opened, and a wave of voices, laughter, and thumping backpacks announced the return of the rest of the Group of 8.

Jordan, resting on the sofa, pushed himself up as they spilled into the room. They were all happy to see him, their faces bright with relief. They had missed him terribly and taken the first, most crowded bus they could catch just to get home sooner.

The sudden, chaotic influx of seven people, all talking at once, was a sensory jolt. After the profound quiet of the day with just Daniel and Laura, the noise was overwhelming. Jordan tensed for a moment, but it didn't escalate into a meltdown; instead, he relaxed, a wide, genuine smile spreading across his face. He had missed this. The quiet had been the unnatural part; *this* chaos was his normal. This was home.

They descended on the sofa, and the affection protocol began immediately.

"Good to see you holding court, man," Mark said, giving him a firm, one-armed brotherly hug.

Lauren and Sarah were next, leaning in to give him tender, romantic hugs and gentle romantic kisses, their relief at seeing him home and safe palpable.

Hannah, his sister, came over, her face full of warmth. She gave him a tight, sisterly I Love You hug. "We missed you today," she said softly, and then gave him an equally sisterly I Love You kiss on his forehead.

Zoe, seeing he was stable and surrounded, gave his shoulder a firm, loving squeeze before moving to her desk to take her post as the evening's logistician.

Finally, the original Room 214 trio settled in. Madison took her familiar spot in the armchair next to the sofa. Aubrie, her shift as his primary comfort detail beginning, gently helped Jordan shift so he could lay his head in her lap.

"How was class?" he mumbled, already relaxing into her touch.

"Long," Aubrie said, gently stroking his hair. She handed him a folder. "Here are all the notes from Dr. Alistair's lecture. I took a copy for you. The homework is already up on the classroom online portal; it looks straightforward".

Jordan looked at the notes, his head in Aubrie's lap, Madison's hand resting on his arm, and the sound of Zoe planning his medication schedule in the background. He was happy to have the chaos back. The recovery was going to be long, but he wasn't doing it alone.
