# Part 2: The Black Friday Compromise

The day after Thanksgiving, Jordan woke up feeling restless. He was healing, but he was also incredibly bored. He had been confined to the sofa in the basement for days, and the world outside the small windows felt a million miles away. He watched the rest of the Group of 8 chatting, and he finally voiced the desire that had been building.

"I want to go out," he announced. "It's Black Friday. Everyone is out. I... I want to ride the bus".

The room went quiet. Aubrie looked at Daniel, her eyes wide with worry.

"Absolutely not," Daniel said immediately. His voice was not unkind, but it was an iron-clad veto.

"But I feel fine!" Jordan protested, trying to sit up straighter. "I just want to see something other than this basement."

"Jordan," Daniel said, walking over to the sofa. "A bus is not a car. It's jerky, it has hard stops, and it's full of people. You are still on strict sternal precautions. One bad bump, one person stumbling in the aisle, and you're right back in the hospital. It is not safe. I'm putting my foot down again".

Jordan's face fell, but he couldn't argue with the logic. He was still in pain.

Laura, seeing his disappointment, stepped in. "But, Daniel," she said gently, "he's right about one thing. He can't stay cooped up in here forever. It's not good for his mind."

"I agree," Daniel said, nodding. "So, we'll compromise. We are *not* getting up for 5 AM doorbusters, and we are *not* setting foot in a single busy store". "But," he continued, a small smile forming, "I see no reason why we can't take a drive. We can get you out of the house, see the mountains, maybe find a quiet drive-thru for a (heart-healthy) snack. Just see the world beyond this room".

Jordan's expression brightened instantly. It was perfect.

The next day, the "human elevator" of Caleb and Owen helped him up the stairs. Daniel had the minivan waiting. Jordan carefully settled into the front passenger seat.

"Okay," Daniel said, handing him a firm, small pillow. "This is your heart pillow. You hold this against your chest, and the seatbelt goes over it". "It will brace your sternum and keep the belt from putting too much pressure on the incision. No exceptions."

Jordan did as he was told, hugging the pillow to his chest as Daniel buckled the seatbelt over it. The rest of the family piled into the van and the other cars. For the first time in almost two weeks, Jordan was out of the house, surrounded by his family, and safely on his way to see the world.
