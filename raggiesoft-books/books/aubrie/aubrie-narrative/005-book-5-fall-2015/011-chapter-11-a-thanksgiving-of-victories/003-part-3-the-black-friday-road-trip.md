# Part 3: The Black Friday Road Trip

With Jordan securely buckled in the front seat of the minivan, heart pillow hugged to his chest, Daniel pulled out of the driveway. The rest of the family, including Laura and the Group of 8, followed in Caleb's SUV and Mark's sedan. This wasn't a shopping trip; it was an excursion, a mission to get Jordan out of the house and reconnect him with the world.

Their first destination was the heart of the chaos: Spradlin Farms. Daniel navigated the minivan slowly through the massive parking lot, which was a swirling vortex of stressed shoppers and circling cars.

"I cannot believe this," Jordan murmured, his eyes wide. He had never seen so many people in one place, all moving with such frantic energy.

"This, son," Daniel said with a chuckle, "is why we stay home."

They continued on, driving past the New River Mall Christiansburg mall. The scene was the same: a parking lot filled to capacity, with lines of people visible even from the road.

After navigating the consumer chaos, Daniel drove them to a place that felt more like home: the Commonwealth Polytechnic Institute campus. The contrast was immediate. The campus was a ghost town, empty and quiet for the holiday break. They took a slow, quiet lap around the main academic buildings, and Jordan looked at the familiar, empty walkways with a sense of fondness.

Finally, Daniel headed west on U.S. 460, taking them out toward the mountains. The air was clear, and the view of the bare, sleeping ridges of the Appalachians was beautiful. They just drove, the quiet hum of the minivan a comfortable, safe cocoon.

As they returned to Geneva Street hours later, Jordan felt a deep, peaceful exhaustion. He had remained safely in the car the entire time, just as promised. He hadn't bought anything or ridden a single bus, but he was so glad to at least have had the chance to head out of the house and see the scenery. The world was still out there, and for the first time since his surgery, he felt like he was still a part of it.
