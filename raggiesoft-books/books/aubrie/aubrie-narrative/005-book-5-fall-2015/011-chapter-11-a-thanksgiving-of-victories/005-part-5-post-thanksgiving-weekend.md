# Part 5: Post Thanksgiving Weekend

The rest of the Thanksgiving weekend settled into a new, quiet routine of recovery. Jordan, driven by his stubborn need to regain his independence, made the basement his own personal physical therapy gym.

"I want to practice my transfers," he announced on Saturday morning.

He spent the day in a slow, painful, but determined loop: practicing the pivot transfer from the sofa to his wheelchair, resting, and then practicing the more difficult transfer from his wheelchair to the king-sized bed. He wanted to do it on his own, but his sternal precautions made him cautious. Mark and Caleb, acting as spotters, stood nearby, their presence a silent, strong safety net.

"You're leaning too much on your right arm, J," Mark would advise gently. "Use your legs more for the pivot. Don't pull with your chest."

It was exhausting, but by Saturday night, he had successfully and safely moved himself from sofa to chair to bed and back again. He accepted their help when he was too tired, but the small victories were his alone.

On Sunday evening, however, a familiar dread began to creep in. The Group of 8 was packing their backpacks, checking bus schedules, and getting their notes ready for class.

"You're all leaving tomorrow," Jordan said quietly from the sofa. It wasn't a question.

"We have to, J," Hannah said gently. "Finals are in two weeks. We're already behind from being at the hospital".

He looked at Daniel and Laura, who were helping organize the chaos. "And you... you're going back to work?"

"We have to, son," Daniel said.

A cold, familiar panic started to rise in Jordan's chest. "So... I'm just here? Alone?"

"Absolutely not," Daniel said, his voice so firm and immediate that it stopped Jordan's rising anxiety cold. He was putting his foot down. "I already made a call. You will not be alone for a single minute."

The solution arrived an hour later in the form of Megan, Zoe's oldest sister. She had her laptop bag and an overnight duffel. While Megan was saying hello to Jordan, Zoe pulled her aside for the official briefing.

"Okay," Zoe said, her voice low. "Here's the protocol update. He's stable, but he's post-op and autistic. He's on a strict medication and exercise schedule, which is all in the binder. But the most important variable is psychological. He needs grounding. He likes hugs. A lot. Are you comfortable with that?"

Megan, who had the same kind, practical eyes as her mother, just smiled. "Of course, Zoe. He's family. Whatever he needs".
