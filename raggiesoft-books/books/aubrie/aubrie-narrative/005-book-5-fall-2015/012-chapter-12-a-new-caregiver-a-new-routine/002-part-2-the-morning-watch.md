# Part 2: The Morning Watch

The quiet of the basement was a comfort. Jordan, still weak but feeling secure, rested his head on Megan's lap as she sat on the sectional sofa. She fulfilled the promise she'd made to Zoe, her hand gently stroking his hair in a way that was sisterly and profoundly comforting. This was family. She, like her parents and siblings, considered Jordan one of their own, and she would look out for him. They sat like that for a long time, Jordan drifting in a light, peaceful doze while Megan quietly scrolled through her phone.

Finally, she glanced at the clock. It was just after 7:30 AM.

"Hey, Jordan," she whispered, "you awake? You ready for some breakfast?"

He stirred, blinking. "Yeah," he rasped, his throat still sore. "Breakfast sounds good."

"Okay," she said softly. "I'm going to have to move you, kiddo."

She gently helped him adjust, lifting his head and shoulders so she could slide out from under him. She arranged the pillows so he was sitting up comfortably against the arm of the sofa.

Megan went to the small basement kitchenette. She pulled a large tub of yogurt from the fridge, along with a container of mixed berries and sliced bananas. She prepared two bowls, layering the yogurt and fruit to make a healthy parfait for both of them. She returned to the sofa with a tray.

"Here you go," she said, handing him his bowl. "One heart-healthy breakfast. And I brought you some orange juice".

Jordan looked at the juice. It wasn't his first choice, but he knew it was good for him, so he took it with a grateful nod.

"And... your morning meds," Megan added, placing the small paper cup with his pills on the tray.

They ate in a comfortable, easy silence. Jordan enjoyed the fresh fruit and yogurt; it was a welcome change from the blandness of the hospital food.

With breakfast done, they still had some time before Megan's workday officially began at 8:00 AM.

"So," Megan said, leaning back in the armchair opposite him. "Zoe tells me you're studying astrophysics. That sounds... intense."

Jordan's face lit up, his exhaustion momentarily forgotten. "It is," he said, his voice gaining a bit of its old energy. "But it's amazing. It's all just... systems. Big, logical, predictable systems. Like the way a star is born, or how it dies. It just... it makes sense."

"Huh," Megan said, genuinely interested. "Zoe's the same way. She's always been about systems. I guess that's why you two get along so well."

"She's brilliant," Jordan said, a small, soft smile on his face. "She's... she's one of my safe people."

Megan's smile widened. She was so proud of her little sister. "You're good for her, too, you know. You've brought this whole crazy, wonderful family into her life. She's never been happier."

They talked for a few more minutes, the conversation easy and familiar. As 8:00 AM approached, Megan gathered her empty dishes.

"Well," she said, "the boss calls. I'm going to get my workday started at the table, but I'll be right here. You just yell if you need anything, okay? Water, help getting up, another hug... anything."

"Okay," Jordan said, feeling a profound sense of calm. "Thank you, Megan."

"Anytime, Jordan. Anytime."

She settled in at the dining table, her laptop open, and the quiet, rhythmic tapping of her keyboard became the new, comforting soundtrack to his recovery.
