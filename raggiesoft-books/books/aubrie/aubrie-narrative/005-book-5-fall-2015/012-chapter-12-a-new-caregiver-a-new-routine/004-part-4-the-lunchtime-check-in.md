# Part 4: The Lunchtime Check-in

At 11:30 AM, Megan's mPhone chimed, signaling the start of her lunch break. She finished her work email and stood up, stretching. "Alright, Jordan, my break starts now. That means your break starts now. You hungry?"

"Starving," he admitted.

"Good. Me too."

Megan went to the basement kitchenette and prepared a simple, heart-healthy lunch for them both: turkey sandwiches on whole wheat, a banana for each of them, and two glasses of juice. She brought the two plates over to the living room area where Jordan was resting on the sofa.

As she handed him his plate, she sat down on the sofa cushion next to him. "You look like you could use one of these," she said, opening her arms.

Jordan smiled gratefully and leaned into her, accepting the warm, sisterly hug. He had been craving physical affection all morning, and he felt his anxiety ease as she held him. She didn't let go immediately; she just held him as they balanced their plates.

Just as they were about to eat, Megan's laptop chimed with an incoming video call from the Jordan Comms chat. She accepted it, and the screen filled with the seven familiar, smiling faces of the Group of 8, huddled around a table in the noisy Newman Dining Commons.

"Hey, guys!" Madison's voice called out. "How's our patient doing? Megan, you're not letting him get away with anything, are you?"

"He's being a model patient," Megan laughed, her arm still draped comfortably around Jordan's shoulders.

Jordan smiled at the screen. "It's too quiet here. I miss you guys."

"Aww, we miss you, too!" Aubrie said. Her face was beaming as she saw Jordan cuddled securely with Megan. She felt a profound sense of peace. She had been worried, but seeing him so clearly safe and getting the physical affection he craved from someone she trusted by way of Zoe, she knew everything was okay.

"How are classes?" Jordan asked, taking a bite of his sandwich.

"Long," Mark grumbled. "You're not missing much. But we're getting all your notes," he added.

"We have all of your assignments copied," Zoe confirmed, her voice cutting through the cafeteria noise. "All data is being backed up to our shared drive."

"We'll be home as soon as our last class is done," Lauren promised. "Try not to have too much fun without us."

"Okay, break's almost over," Hannah said, her "mom friend" instincts kicking in. "We gotta run. We love you, J! We love you, Megan!"

A chorus of "I love yous" and waves goodbye filled the screen, and then the call ended, leaving the basement in silence once more.
