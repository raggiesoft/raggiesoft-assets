# Part 7: The Fortress Returns

The basement door flew open just after 5:30 PM, and the quiet of the afternoon was replaced by the loud, happy chaos of the Group of 8 returning home. They were tired from their long day and the crowded bus ride, but the sight of Jordan sitting up on the sofa, and the incredible smell of Megan's cooking, made them all break into smiles.

"Dinner is ready!" Megan announced cheerfully from the kitchenette.

A chorus of "You're a lifesaver, Megan!" filled the room as they dropped their backpacks. But before food, there was the ritual.

Jordan, smiling, braced himself. One by one, they came to the sofa. Mark and Hannah gave him strong, loving, platonic hugs. Then came the pact. Lauren, Sarah, Zoe, and Madison all leaned in, giving him deep, romantic hugs and affectionate, lingering kisses, each one a clear, loving "welcome home".

Aubrie was last. She settled onto the sofa cushion beside him, displacing Megan's previous spot, and took his face in her hands. "I missed you," she whispered, giving him a deep, passionate kiss that left him breathless.

Megan watched this all from the kitchenette, her heart full. She saw the open, romantic affection of the five women, and she saw the complete lack of jealousy. Aubrie, the *girlfriend*, was smiling as Madison leaned over to kiss Jordan's other cheek. Megan found herself wanting, desperately, to be a part of that. She wanted to join the pact.

The feeling was almost immediately followed by a wave of guilt. She was the older sister, the caregiver. She couldn't risk upsetting this beautiful, stable family they had all built, not when she was just starting to feel wanted again for the first time in years. She decided, firmly, that she would never broach the subject. She would be content with her platonic sisterly role.

"Alright, everyone, eat while it's hot," she said, breaking her own reverie and bringing plates to the coffee table.

The nine of them ate together in the basement, the conversation lively.

"So, how was the real world?" Jordan asked, taking a bite of the heart-healthy chicken.

"Loud," Mark grumbled. "Crowded. You're not missing much. But we got all your notes."

"And the bus was packed," Madison complained. "I had to stand almost the whole way from CPI."

"How was it here?" Aubrie asked, her hand resting on Jordan's knee.

"It was... quiet," Jordan admitted. "Too quiet. I'm not used to it anymore. I was glad when Megan started cooking, just to have some noise".

Zoe, who had been eating, looked over at her sister. "Megan, how was the care protocol? Any issues?"

"He was a perfect patient," Megan smiled. "We did his breathing exercises, took his meds, and... we handled the bathroom logistics."

Zoe's face broke into a rare, brilliant smile. She was so happy and relieved that her sister had been able to handle the hands-on toileting aspect of his care without being grossed out or uncomfortable. It confirmed, in her mind, that her family was the perfect, logical support system.

"Thank you, Megan," Zoe said, her voice full of genuine gratitude. "That's... a significant logistical help."

"It was no bother at all," Megan said, meaning it. "That's what family's for."
