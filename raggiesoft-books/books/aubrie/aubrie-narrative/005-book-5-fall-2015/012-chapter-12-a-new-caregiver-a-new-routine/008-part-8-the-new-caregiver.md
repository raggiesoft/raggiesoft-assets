# Part 8: The New Caregiver

The evening wound down, and the happy chaos of the group’s return settled into a sleepy, domestic peace. It was time for Jordan to get ready for bed.

"Okay," Aubrie said, standing up. "Time for the washing protocol." She looked at Hannah and Lauren, who were already moving to help.

Zoe, ever the logistician, glanced at her sister. "Megan, this is part of the daily care. We'll need your help."

This was the moment. Megan felt a small jolt of anxiety. She had wanted to earn their trust, but this was the ultimate test. It was one thing to offer a hug; it was another to help with his most private, intimate care. She saw the five women of the pact all looking at her, not with suspicion, but with simple, trusting expectation. She wouldn't let them down.

"Of course," she said, her voice calm and steady.

The three women—Hannah, Lauren, and Megan—wheeled Jordan into the accessible basement bathroom. "Alright, J, transfer protocol," Hannah said, moving to take one side.

"Wait," Megan said, her mind assessing the situation. "With his sternum, a two-person pivot might be awkward. Let me try something." She positioned the wheelchair next to the shower bench. "Put your arm here, Jordan. That's it. Let me do the work."

In one surprisingly strong and fluid motion, Megan lifted Jordan from his chair and settled him securely on the bench. Hannah and Lauren stared, amazed. She was strong enough to do it by herself, even with his post-op fragility.

Zoe, who had followed them in with the binder, logged the new data with a small, proud smile. Her sister was the new, most efficient agent for this task.

"I've got this, guys," Megan said, her voice taking on a gentle, clinical confidence.

Hannah and Lauren, seeing she was in control, nodded and stepped back. Following the rules in the binder, Megan was meticulous, ensuring the incision on his chest stayed perfectly dry. She carefully washed his hair and his body. It was an act of clinical... care, and as she washed him, she necessarily had to touch his intimate areas.

Her mind was a whirl. *This is just care. It's medical. He's my brother. He's Zoe's family.* But as she performed the task with a gentle, respectful hand, she felt that strange, warm pull again. She was deeply happy to be trusted this much, but she immediately reinforced her internal boundary. *This is a sister's job. This is not romantic.*

When the shower was over, she gently dried him and helped him get dressed into a fresh set of pajamas. She wheeled him back out into the main room, where the rest of the pact was waiting.

They were so proud of Megan for stepping up and handling his most intimate care with such grace. Aubrie rushed over and gave her a fierce hug.

"Thank you, Megan," Aubrie whispered, her voice thick with gratitude. "I... thank you."

"Anytime," Megan said, hugging her back.

It was finally time for bed. Megan helped Jordan with the last transfer of the day, moving him from his wheelchair into the center of the massive king-sized bed, where Aubrie was waiting for him.

"Thank you, Megan," Jordan whispered, his voice full of a new, deeper trust. He held out his arms for one last hug goodnight. She leaned down and held him, a warm, sisterly embrace.

The rest of the Group of 8 came over for their own goodnight hugs and kisses. Finally, Jordan settled into the bed, and Aubrie curled up beside him, her body a familiar, comforting presence. She was always careful to keep her weight off his chest.

"Goodnight, guys," Mark said. He, Hannah, and Megan headed up the internal stairs, leaving the basement to the pact.

Zoe, Lauren, Sarah, and Madison all headed to their own queen beds, turning off the lights as they went. The room settled into a safe, quiet darkness, and Jordan, surrounded by all the women he loved, finally let himself rest.
