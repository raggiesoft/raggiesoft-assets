# Part 2: The Morning Watch

"Alright," Megan said, her voice warm in the quiet basement. "Breakfast time. I'm starving." She moved to the kitchenette. "How do you feel about scrambled eggs and wheat toast?"

"Sounds good," Jordan replied.

He watched as she worked with an easy efficiency. She made scrambled eggs, and when the wheat toast popped, she put it on plates, carefully adding jelly but no butter. She poured two glasses of orange juice.

She brought both plates to the table and sat down next to him. Jordan looked at his plate, then at hers. They were identical. He realized she was intentionally following his heart-healthy diet, just so he wouldn't feel different or left out. A warm feeling spread through his chest, one that had nothing to do with his incision.

They ate together, the silence comfortable. When they finished, Megan glanced at the clock. "Well, we've got about thirty minutes until I have to log in," she said.

"Megan?" Jordan asked, his voice hesitant. "Before you start... can I have another hug?"

"Of course," she said softly. She moved from her chair and leaned down, wrapping him in a firm, warm, heartfelt hug as he sat in his wheelchair.

Jordan held on, not wanting to let go. He was enjoying having the company so much. He felt her relax into the embrace, not pulling away. He felt that same strange, fluttering warmth from the night before, the butterflies that he'd been trying to ignore. He was really starting to like Megan. He trusted her.

He wondered if he could add her to his list of Safe People, a list that, until now, had only ever included Aubrie, Madison, and Zoe.

The hug lasted a few seconds longer than just a simple, sisterly hug. As he held her, a new, confusing thought flickered in his mind: *Do I want this?* He felt her take a slightly shaky breath, as if she was thinking the exact same thing. The air between them felt charged, and for a moment, the whole world seemed to stop.

But the moment passed. Megan, with a small, almost imperceptible sigh, gently pulled back, her smile warm but her eyes a little flustered.

They talked for a few more minutes, the conversation easy and familiar. As 8:00 AM approached, Megan gathered her empty dishes.

She stood up and took her plate to the kitchenette, stacking it in the sink before returning to the table. Jordan watched her, his heart hammering. He was so confused.

She sat back down in her chair, right next to him, and pulled her laptop closer. "Time to get to work," she said with a final, warm smile.

Jordan nodded, turning to his own laptop. But he knew, with a certainty that thrilled and terrified him, that he was already looking forward to her lunch break

"Okay," she said, her voice a little thicker than before. "Work time."
