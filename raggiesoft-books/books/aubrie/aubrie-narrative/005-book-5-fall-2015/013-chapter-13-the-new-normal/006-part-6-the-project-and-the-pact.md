# Part 6: The Project and the Pact

The basement hummed with a quiet, focused energy. While Daniel and Laura cooked dinner upstairs, the Group of 8 was clustered around the dining table, their laptops open. This was their first official work session for the English 102 final project.

"Okay," Zoe said, acting as the project manager. "We need a rough draft of the outline by Monday. I'll create the shared document".

Jordan, energized, was at the head of the table in his wheelchair, directing the conversation. "The introduction has to define the problem. It's not just a bad map. It's a failure of information. Students can't use resources they don't know exist."

"He's right," Mark added. "We can propose a new, interactive map that uses a clean database. It's not even hard. The current one is just lazy."

Megan sat on the sofa nearby, listening to them work, her heart swelling with pride. She was their biggest cheerleader, and she loved seeing them all, especially Jordan, so engaged and passionate. She knew they were building a proposal that could genuinely help people.

Just as Zoe typed the final bullet point for their outline, Daniel's voice called from the top of the stairs. "Dinner's coming down!"

The group let out a happy, hungry cheer. Daniel and Laura came down with the feast: baked salmon, brown rice, and roasted asparagus. It was a heart-healthy meal for everyone, a new standard the whole family had embraced in solidarity with Jordan.

They all gathered in the living room area, balancing plates on their laps. The conversation immediately turned to a recap of the day.

"You're lucky you were here, J," Mark grumbled. "The 6:00 AM bus was so packed, I thought I was going to have to stand in the aisle."

"And my last lecture was a total snoozefest," Madison added.

"It was just... quiet here," Jordan said, looking at Megan with a grateful smile. "It was too quiet until Megan started her workday. I'm glad you guys are all back."

As they talked, the bond between Jordan and Megan was visible to everyone. He listened intently to a story she was telling, his gaze warm and focused. She laughed, her hand resting on his arm in an easy, familiar gesture.

Zoe, ever the analyst, saw this new data point. She caught Aubrie's eye from across the room and gave a subtle tilt of her head toward Megan and Jordan, a silent query: *Are you processing this?*

Aubrie already knew. She saw the way Jordan looked at Megan, and she saw the happy, sisterly affection Megan gave him. Aubrie's heart was happy. She turned her gaze to Megan, and when she looked over, Aubrie gave her a slow, brilliant, and completely unambiguous smile. It was a silent, powerful signal: *You are welcome. If you want this, the door is open.*

Megan, catching the look, blushed and quickly looked down at her plate, her heart hammering. She couldn't. She was too old. It wouldn't be right.

Aubrie, seeing the blush, just kept smiling. She knew the seeds were planted. She leaned her head on Jordan's shoulder, her happiness complete. The rest of the pact, seeing the exchange, felt it too. They were all happy to be home for the weekend, back in their fortress, together with Jordan.
