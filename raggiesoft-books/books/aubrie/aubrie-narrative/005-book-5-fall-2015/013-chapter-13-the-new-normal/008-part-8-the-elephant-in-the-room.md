# Part 8: The Elephant in the Room

It was finally time for bed. Megan, seeing the Group of 8 start their wind-down routines, knew it was time for her to find her own space. She wanted to stay in the basement, close to the warmth and energy of the group, hoping what Zoe had said earlier about exploring her feelings was true.

She walked over to the sectional sofa and picked up the pillow and blanket that were already draped there. She wanted to be near Jordan, but she didn't want to intrude. She glanced at the massive king-sized bed, the space he had carved out with the pact. She knew Aubrie was the one who slept in bed with him.

For a fleeting moment, Megan pictured herself climbing into that bed with them. *No, scratch that,* she thought immediately, her cheeks flushing. *That would be asking way too much*. She wouldn't push her luck. The sofa was safe.

"Alright, team, we're heading up," Mark announced. He and Hannah said their goodnights, gave Jordan and Megan their hugs, and headed upstairs to Zoe's old room.

Before being wheeled to his own bed, Jordan stopped in front of Megan. "Goodnight, Megan," he said. "Thank you... for today."

"Goodnight, Jordan," she replied, her voice soft.

She stepped forward to give him his goodnight hug. As his arms wrapped around her, the hug felt different. It wasn't just sisterly; it was charged, and it lingered. The five women of the pact watched the exchange, and to them, it was obviously romantic. They exchanged small, knowing smiles. They were all okay with it.

As he held her, Jordan debated kissing her. He wanted to. But he hesitated, his old anxieties flaring. It was too soon. He decided against it and pulled back, giving her a small, shaky smile.

"Time for bed, J," Zoe said, her voice gentle.

She wheeled Jordan into the master bedroom area, and the other four women of the pact followed. Zoe closed the door, giving them privacy.

The five of them began their own bedtime routine, but the tension was thick.

"Okay," Madison said, sitting on the edge of the king bed. "We have to talk about the elephant in the room".

"Megan," Aubrie said, her voice soft but clear.

Jordan looked at them. "She... she's amazing."

"We know, J," Lauren said. "We see the way you look at her. And we see the way she looks at you".

Zoe, ever the analyst, got straight to the point. "The data is conclusive. There is mutual romantic interest between you and my sister. This is a new, positive variable."

Jordan looked at Aubrie, his oldest fear still present. "But... are you sure? You're all... okay with it?"

Aubrie took his hand. "Jordan, look at me. We are prepared to welcome Megan into the pact if she wants. That's the whole point. We love you. We trust you. And we are starting to really love her. She fits".

"She really does," Sarah added. "She's... she's one of us."

"So," Madison grinned, "if she wants in... we're good with it. The only question is, are *you*?".

Jordan looked at the faces of the five women he loved. He thought about Megan, her strength, her kindness, her butterflies.

"Yes," he said, his voice firm. "Now that I know you're all ready to include her... I would want that. Very much".

Aubrie smiled, her heart full. "Good. Then the pact of six is officially on the table. Now we just have to wait for her to be ready to ask."
