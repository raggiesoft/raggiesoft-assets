# Part 9: A New Dawn

Saturday morning. Jordan woke slowly, pulled from the most beautiful, vivid dream he had ever had.

He wasn't in the basement, and his chest didn't hurt. He was sitting on the sofa, his body healthy and whole. He was surrounded by his entire pact, all six women. Aubrie, Madison, Zoe, Lauren, Sarah, and Megan were all there, their faces glowing, their hands resting on their own round, pregnant bellies. They were looking over blueprints for the nursery, all of them talking and laughing together. Daniel and Laura were there, beaming with excitement, holding up catalogs for cribs. There was no jealousy, no fear... only a profound, perfect peace.

He opened his eyes.

The dream dissolved. He was back in the basement, in the king-sized bed. The first sensation he felt was the familiar, dull ache in his sternum. A profound wave of sadness washed over him. It wasn't real. He was sad to wake up, to be pulled from a reality that had felt so perfect.

"Hey..." a soft voice whispered.

Aubrie was awake beside him, spooning him carefully, her arm draped over his side. She had felt him stir, had sensed the deep, shuddering sigh he let out. She shifted to look at his face in the dim morning light and saw the unmistakable track of a tear on his cheek.

"Jordan? What's wrong?" she asked, her voice full of immediate, quiet concern.

He turned his head on the pillow, his eyes shining. "I had a dream," he whispered, his voice thick with emotion. "It was... us. All of us. In the new house. And everyone... you, Madi, Zoe, Lauren, Sarah... and Megan... you were all..." He couldn't finish the sentence, but she understood.

"You were all pregnant," he finally managed. "And you were all so happy. There was no jealousy. It was just... our family."

Aubrie’s heart filled to bursting. She smiled, a brilliant, radiant smile as she processed his words. He was sad because he *wanted* that reality. He was sad because he had woken up *from* it.

"Oh, Jordan," she whispered, her face a picture of pure bliss. She leaned in, kissing his cheek. "Don't be sad. Don't you see? That wasn't just a dream. That's the *plan*."

She saw the look in his eyes when he'd said Megan's name. She'd seen it last night, too. "Megan belongs with us," Aubrie said, her voice a soft, steady promise. "I know it. And I'm ready to welcome her. We all are".

It felt like just yesterday she had walked into Room 214 and found a terrified boy in the middle of an autistic meltdown. A boy who didn't even know how to get dressed by himself. And now... now he was a man, her partner, dreaming of a future with *all* of them. A man who knew how to ask for help when he needed it.

She brushed the tear from his face. "That future? It's real. We're building it. But it starts today."

She smiled, her energy bright and new. "And today, we have a plan. You are going out to breakfast". "Your first meal out since the hospital. The whole family is going—all eight of us, plus Mom, Dad, and Megan".

"Now," she said, carefully sliding out of the bed, "let's get you ready to face your beautiful Saturday morning".
