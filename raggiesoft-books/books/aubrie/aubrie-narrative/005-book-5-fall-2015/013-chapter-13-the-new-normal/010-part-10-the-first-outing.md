# Part 10: The First Outing

The plan was set, and the energy in the basement was bright and excited. The full Group of 17 was mobilizing for Jordan's first restaurant meal since his surgery. Daniel and Laura coordinated the convoy.

"Okay," Daniel said, "Megan, you take your car. Jordan, you'll ride with her". He knew his son would feel more independent in a regular sedan. "Aubrie, Madison, you two ride with Megan". The rest of the family would pile into the minivan.

Getting into Megan's car was a careful process, but Jordan, now practiced in his transfers and still on sternal precautions, managed it with her help. He settled into the front passenger seat, heart pillow firmly against his chest, and buckled in. Aubrie and Madison climbed into the back seat.

The small convoy pulled out of Geneva Street and headed for Blacksburg, the drive a welcome change of scenery for Jordan. He looked out the window, just happy to be out of the house, his mind focused on the simple, wonderful prospect of a meal he didn't have to eat on a sofa.

As they drove up U.S. 460, Aubrie leaned forward from the back seat, her voice warm. "Megan, I'm so glad you're able to be with Jordan during the day. It really puts my mind at ease knowing he's not alone".

"Me too," Madison added, chiming in. "It's perfect. He needs someone he can trust."

Megan felt a hot blush creep up her neck. She gripped the steering wheel, her knuckles white. She could hear the subtext, the words they weren't saying. This was more than just gratitude; this felt like... permission. It felt like his two "safe people" were trying to *imply* that she was welcome to get romantically close to him, but they were too scared to just come out and say it. Her heart hammered in her chest.

She glanced at Jordan. He was oblivious to the subtext, just looking forward to breakfast, a calm, happy smile on his face.

"Oh, it's... it's no bother at all," Megan finally stammered. "I'm just happy to help. He's family."

She kept her eyes on the road, her mind a confusing whirl of hope and her own "I'm too old" anxiety.

They pulled into the parking lot of "The Smokin’ Anvil," a small, popular breakfast-and-lunch spot in Blacksburg. The rest of the family was already parking. Jordan just looked at the restaurant, his stomach rumbling. He was ready to eat.
