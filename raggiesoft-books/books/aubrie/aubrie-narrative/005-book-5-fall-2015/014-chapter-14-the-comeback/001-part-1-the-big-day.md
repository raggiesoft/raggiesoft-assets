# Part 1: The Big Day

The 5:15 AM alarms were a harsh, digital intrusion, but they were now a familiar part of the day. Jordan woke on the new king-sized bed, Aubrie's steady breathing a comfort beside him. Today was different, though. He wasn't just facing another day of recovery; he was going *back*. Today was his English 102 presentation.

He carefully turned his head, wincing at the familiar pull in his sternum. Across the room, on the sectional sofa, Megan was just starting to stir, pushing her hair out of her face. A warm, happy feeling spread through his chest. He was so happy to see her again, that she was *here*. He knew she was sleeping on the sofa because she didn't want to intrude on the king bed, the space he shared with the pact, but just having her in the same room felt right.

The basement was a flurry of activity as the Group of 8 got ready for their 6:00 AM bus. The care protocol was efficient. Hannah and Lauren helped him with the careful pivot transfer from the bed to his wheelchair, while Aubrie and Madison helped him get washed and dressed for his big day on campus.

"You ready for this, J?" Mark asked, giving him a firm, one-armed brotherly hug. "You're gonna kill it."

The pact of five came next, one by one, for their romantic kisses and hugs.

"We'll save you a seat right in the front row," Madison whispered.

"We'll be waiting for you," Aubrie added, giving him one last, lingering kiss. "I'm so proud of you."

They grabbed their bags and, with a final chorus of "Good luck!" and "See you later!", the Group of 8 headed up the stairs, leaving for CPI.

The basement was suddenly, profoundly quiet.

"Well," Megan's cheerful voice cut through the silence. "Looks like it's just you and me for a bit."

She came over, her eyes bright. "Breakfast first, then we can worry about your presentation. How are you feeling?"

"Nervous," Jordan admitted, "but... good. Ready."

"Good." She smiled and went to the kitchenette. She followed their established routine, making them both a heart-healthy breakfast of scrambled eggs and wheat toast with jelly, and pouring two glasses of orange juice. She sat at the dining table with him, her presence a familiar, comforting start to his important day.
