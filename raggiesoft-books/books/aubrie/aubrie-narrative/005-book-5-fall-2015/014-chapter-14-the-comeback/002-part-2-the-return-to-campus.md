# Part 2: The Return to Campus

Later that day, it was time to go. Megan, who had taken a paid personal day from work just for this, helped Jordan transfer from the sofa to his wheelchair. He was nervous, but the excitement of returning to campus was a powerful motivator.

She helped him into the front passenger seat of her car. He was glad Megan was the one driving him. He was really starting to enjoy her company, just as much as he enjoyed being with the five women of the pact. He found himself wanting her to join them, to make the pact of five a pact of six, but he had no idea how to even word that thought. It felt impossible, so he pushed the confusing feeling down.

Megan placed his heart pillow firmly against his chest, and he held it as she buckled the seatbelt over it. As they pulled out of Geneva Street, he looked forward to just seeing CPI again.

Megan had purchased a visitor's parking pass ahead of time, and she hung Jordan's new handicap placard from her rearview mirror as they arrived on campus. The university was busy, and Jordan’s anxiety spiked.

"It's going to be hard to find a spot," he murmured, scanning the packed lots near his classroom building.

"We'll find one," Megan said calmly. Just as she spoke, the taillights of a car in a handicap spot right near the main walkway flashed on. The car pulled out, and Megan slid the car in. "And there it is," she grinned. "Perfect timing".

She got out and retrieved his wheelchair from the trunk, helping him with the careful, slow transfer from the car seat. Jordan, clutching his heart pillow tightly to brace his sternum, settled into his chair.

"Okay," he said, getting his bearings and looking at the network of sidewalks. "We need to go up this path, and then take the first left...".

Megan chuckled softly, cutting him off. She stepped behind his wheelchair, her hands settling on the grips.

"Jordan, I probably should have mentioned this earlier," she said, a playful smile in her voice. "I'm a CPI alumnus. I know this campus like the back of my hand. All you have to do is tell me which building we're going to. I'll get us there".

Jordan looked up at her, surprised and impressed. "Walton Hall," he said.

"Walton Hall it is," Megan said, pushing him forward. "Let's go show them what you've got."
