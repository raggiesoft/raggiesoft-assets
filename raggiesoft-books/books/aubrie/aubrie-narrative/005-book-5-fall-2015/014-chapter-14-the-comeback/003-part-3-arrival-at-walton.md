# Part 3: Arrival at Walton

Megan navigated the campus pathways with practiced ease, pushing Jordan’s wheelchair right to the accessible entrance of Walton Hall. As they entered the familiar lobby, Jordan’s heart gave a happy jolt. The rest of the Group of 8 was already there, clustered by the doors to the lecture hall, waiting for him.

"He’s here!" Madison cheered softly.

They all converged on him, their faces bright with smiles. The hellos were a careful, practiced ritual. The women of the pact gave him gentle, loving kisses, and Mark and Hannah gave him soft, one-armed hugs, all of them extremely careful of his chest.

"You ready for this, J?" Mark asked, his voice full of pride.

"I'm ready," Jordan said, and he meant it. He was so "happy to see everyone" and just to be "back on campus," even for a little while.

A few minutes later, Dr. Evans, the professor, opened the classroom door. "Alright, folks, it's 9:00 AM. Let's get started. The first group can set up.".

"That's us," Zoe said, picking up her bag.

"Okay," Megan said, squeezing Jordan's shoulders. "You're up. I'll be right out here in the lobby waiting for you when you're done".

She found a quiet bench to sit on, and Jordan wheeled himself into the lecture hall, his family surrounding him as they moved to the front of the room. He was nervous, but he was also deeply happy. He understood the conditions needed to be controlled for his safety. He knew he wasn't here for the day. He was here just for class, and as soon as it was over, he would head home with Megan. It was a perfect, surgical strike, and he was ready to nail his part of the presentation.
