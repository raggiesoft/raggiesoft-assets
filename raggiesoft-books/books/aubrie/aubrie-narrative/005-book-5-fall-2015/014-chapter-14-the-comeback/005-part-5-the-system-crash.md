# Part 5: The System Crash

Megan wheeled Jordan back through the bustling campus to her car, which was still in the perfect handicap spot. The walk was quiet; Jordan was lost in his own thoughts, the adrenaline of the presentation fading. She helped him with the careful, practiced transfer into the front passenger seat, settled his heart pillow against his chest, and buckled him in.

She walked around to the driver's side and slid into her seat. She put the key in the ignition but paused for a moment, letting out a satisfied sigh. "You did so good, Jordan. I was so proud of you."

She turned to look at him, but he wasn't looking at her. He was staring through the windshield at Walton Hall, at the students milling about, backpacks on, heading to their next class.

He had just tasted it. For fifty minutes, he had been a normal student again. He had been with his friends, participating, *winning*. And now, he had to leave. He had to go back to the basement, back to being a patient.

His whole world collapsed.

"I want to go back," he whispered, his voice trembling. "I want to go to my next class".

"Jordan, we can't," Megan said gently. "The plan was just for this one..."

"NO!" he suddenly roared, the sound shocking in the small car. "I want to go back!"

He began to thrash in the seat, his body tensing. A low, guttural sound, the one Daniel had warned them about, ripped from his chest as his autistic meltdown took over.

Megan’s blood ran cold. He was going to pop his stitches. He was going to break the wires. This wasn't a meltdown they could ride out; this was a medical emergency, and she was "alone".

"Jordan, no! Stop!" she commanded, her voice high with panic. "Your chest! You have to stop tensing!"

But he was gone, lost in the storm, his body arching against the seatbelt.

She had seconds. She couldn't grab him; she'd pull on his chest. She couldn't just yell; he couldn't hear her. She had to *ground* him.

In a desperate, instinctive move, Megan unbuckled her own seatbelt, threw herself across the console, and straddled his lap, pinning his lower body against the seat with her own weight. It was a shocking, intimate act, but it left his chest completely free.

"Jordan! Listen to my voice!" she screamed, her face inches from his. She grabbed his shoulders, her fingers digging in, applying firm, steady pressure *away* from his sternum. "You are safe! But you are going to hurt yourself! You MUST stop tensing! Squeeze my hands! Squeeze them, NOW!"

She peeled his hands off his pillow and forced them to grip hers, pushing all her strength into his. "Focus on my hands, Jordan! Not your chest! My hands! You are safe! I've got you!"

He thrashed, the guttural sound still vibrating, but she held on, her entire body a grounding, desperate shield. She was crying, terrified, but she didn't let go.

"I've got you," she said again, her voice breaking. "I'm right here. You are not alone. I've got you."

Slowly, agonizingly, the pressure began to recede. The thrashing lessened. The horrible, low-frequency sound in his chest subsided, replaced by a deep, shuddering, painful sob.

He was back. He was crying, his body trembling with the aftershocks, but he was no longer rigid.

Megan, shaking so hard she could barely breathe, didn't move. She just collapsed against him, her head on his shoulder, her arms still wrapped around him, her legs still pinning him safely. She held him, her own tears of terror and relief soaking his shirt, as he cried into her hair.

"It's okay," she sobbed, her voice muffled. "You're okay. I've got you. I'm not letting go."

She had done it. She had calmed the storm, all by herself. And in the process, in that terrifying, desperate moment, she had become his safe person.
