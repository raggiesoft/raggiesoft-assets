# Part 2: The Invitation

As the evening wound down, the energy in the basement shifted from crisis to a deep, protective comfort. It was time for bed. The group went through their usual rituals, changing into pajamas and getting ready to sleep.

Aubrie, who usually claimed the single spot next to Jordan in the king bed, finished brushing her teeth. She came out of the bathroom, looked at Megan—who was sitting on the sofa, still looking shaken and lost—and then looked at the rest of the pact. A silent, unanimous decision was made.

"Okay," Aubrie said, her voice soft but firm. "Bedtime."

She walked over to Megan. "And tonight, you're sleeping in the king bed. With Jordan."

Megan's head snapped up, her face a mask of panic. "Oh, no, I... I can't. Aubrie, that's your spot. I'm fine on the sofa. Really. I don't want to intrude".

"It's not about the sofa," Madison said gently, coming over to sit on the coffee table in front of her. "It's about *us*. We want you with him. We want you to be safe. We want you to be comforted".

"But I..." Megan stammered, looking at Jordan, who was watching her with a hopeful, gentle expression. "It's not... I'm 27. He's 19. It's not appropriate. I'm Zoe's sister".

Zoe, who had been listening to her sister's internal barrier, stepped forward. "Megan, your 'age barrier' logic is flawed. Let's analyze the data".

She held up her fingers, ticking off the points.

"One: Jordan is nineteen. He is a legal, consenting adult, a fact that has been established in a court of law". "Two: You are twenty-seven. You are also a legal, consenting adult". "Three: You are upset and in need of comfort after a traumatic event". "Four: Jordan, a safe person, is offering that comfort". "Five: The entire pact, his primary partners, are *insisting* you take that comfort".

Zoe looked her older sister in the eye. "Therefore, the only variable preventing this optimal, supportive outcome is your own, irrelevant social anxiety. You should sleep in the bed".

"She's right," Lauren said, joining the group. "That guy Alex was a jerk because he wouldn't listen to you. We're not him. We're *listening* to what you need, even if you're not saying it."

"We want you here, Megan," Sarah added, her voice soft. "You're family."

Megan looked at Aubrie, her eyes desperate for an out. "But Aubrie... I can't take your place."

Aubrie just smiled, her heart full of bliss. "You're not taking my place, Megan. You're *joining* it. We're welcoming you. This is what we do. We share. This is the pact. Now, are you really going to make Jordan sleep alone after that jerk upset you?"

Megan looked at the five women surrounding her, their faces open, loving, and completely devoid of jealousy. It was an overwhelming, powerful force. She looked over at Jordan, who was holding the blankets back on the empty side of the bed, his expression one of quiet, hopeful invitation. Her internal barrier, the one she had held onto for weeks, finally crumbled under the weight of their acceptance.

"Okay," she whispered, her voice thick with tears. "Okay."

The rest of the pact, their mission accomplished, smiled and headed for their own queen beds on the other side of the room. Megan, still shaking slightly, slipped into the king-sized bed.

Jordan immediately shifted closer, his body warm, and wrapped an arm around her, pulling her close in a non-strenuous, profoundly comforting cuddle.

"It's okay," he whispered. "You're safe here."

She cried into his shoulder, not from sadness, but from overwhelming relief. She was finally, truly, home.
