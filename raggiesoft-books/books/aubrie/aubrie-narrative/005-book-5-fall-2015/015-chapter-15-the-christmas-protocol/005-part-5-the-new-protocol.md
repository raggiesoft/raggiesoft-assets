# Part 5: The New Protocol

Saturday morning, December 26th, was bright and calm. No one had to get up early for class or work. The group slowly assembled in the basement living room. Jordan was already on the sectional sofa, a comfortable blanket over his legs.

Megan was the last to emerge, feeling shy and uncertain after the events of the previous night. She had slept in the king bed, cuddled against Jordan, and it had felt more right than anything in her life. But now, in the light of day, her anxieties came flooding back. *Was it a one-time thing? Was she just the pity case?*

She went to the kitchenette to start making coffee, keeping her back to the room.

"Megan? Can you come sit with us?"

It was Aubrie's voice. Megan turned. The other five women of the pact were all gathered, sitting on the sectional and the armchairs around Jordan. It felt like an intervention.

"Am I... in trouble?" Megan asked, her voice small.

"In trouble?" Aubrie laughed, a warm, gentle sound. "Megan, no, you’re not in trouble. We just... we need to talk. Formally."

Megan nervously took a seat in the armchair opposite Jordan.

"First of all," Aubrie said, her gaze soft and steady, "how are you feeling? After that... *jerk* Alex last night."

"I'm... okay," Megan said. "I'm just glad he's gone. And I'm so sorry if I brought all that drama here."

"You didn't," Madison said firmly from the other side of the sofa. "He did. And you're one of us. You're family. You bring *nothing* here but you."

Megan looked down, her heart hammering. "I... I still feel weird about last night. About the bed. I know I'm just Zoe's sister, and I'm so much older than all of you, and I don't want to make things... weird".

Zoe, who had been listening intently, finally spoke. "Megan, your 'age barrier' logic is flawed. Let's analyze the data".

She leaned forward, her expression serious. "One: Jordan is nineteen. He is a legal, consenting adult, a fact that has been established in a court of law. Two: You are twenty-seven. You are also a legal, consenting adult. Three: The existing pact of five, which holds 100% of the social and romantic equity in this relationship, is giving its full, unanimous blessing. Four: Jordan is clearly reciprocating the emotional bond. Therefore, the eight-year age difference is an irrelevant variable".

"What Zoe is trying to say," Lauren chimed in, smiling, "is that we see the way you look at him. And we see the way he looks at you."

"We've been waiting for you to catch up," Sarah added softly. "You just... you fit, Megan."

Megan looked at Aubrie, her eyes wide with disbelief. "You mean... you all... you *want* me to... to join the pact?"

"We want you to be part of this family, in whatever way makes you happy," Aubrie said. "And that means we need to teach you the rules. Because you're not an outsider. You're one of us now. Which means, first of all..."

Aubrie stood up, walked over to Megan, and gently pulled her from the armchair. She sat her on the sofa, right next to Jordan.

"It means," Aubrie continued, "that you are allowed to be romantic with him. 100%. We *want* you to. And I'm not talking about a sisterly kiss on the cheek. I mean *kiss* him".

Megan stared at her, her entire body frozen in a state of hopeful shock.

"He's right here," Aubrie said, her face radiating pure bliss. She looked at Jordan. "Do you want to kiss Megan?"

Jordan, who had been watching this all unfold with a look of pure adoration, just nodded, his eyes locked on Megan. "More than anything," he whispered.

"Go on," Madison encouraged from his other side. "It's okay. We're all right here. There is no jealousy in this room".

Megan's hands were shaking. She looked at Jordan, at his soft, waiting expression. Slowly, she leaned in. He met her halfway. Their first, real, romantic kiss was gentle, hesitant, and full of all the unsaid things from the past few weeks. Then, as she felt him relax, it deepened. It was a long, slow, loving kiss, and when they finally pulled apart, Megan was breathless. She looked around. The other four women were just smiling.

"See?" Lauren said. "No lightning bolts. Just us."

"Now," Aubrie said, sitting back down, "for the second rule. The one with the medical constraint."

"What Zoe means," Madison picked up, her voice practical, "is that this pact also includes sex. Once Jordan is recovered. He's already had sex with all five of us. We all know about it. It's part of the agreement".

"And just so you know, as a practical matter," Zoe added, her voice clinical and non-judgmental, "all five of us are on birth control. It's how we can have a sexual relationship with Jordan without worrying about pregnancy right now, before we're ready".

"We'd suggest you get on it, too," Sarah added softly. "Just so you're ready, for when *he's* ready".

"But that's the key," Aubrie stressed, her voice firm, "he's not ready *right now*. He is on strict sternal precautions. He cannot do anything strenuous. It's not safe. So that is 100% off the table, for *all* of us, until his doctor gives him the all-clear in a few weeks".

"We just need you to know," Lauren said, "that when that day comes, the invitation extends to you, too. Fully. With our complete blessing. No jealousy. Ever".

Megan just stared at the five of them, and at Jordan, tears streaming down her face.

"I... I don't know what to say," she whispered.

Jordan took her hand, his thumb stroking her knuckles. "Just say yes," he said, his voice thick with emotion.

Megan looked at him, then at her sister Zoe, at Aubrie, at Madison, at Lauren, at Sarah. Her new family. "Yes," she cried, and this time, when she kissed him, it wasn't hesitant at all.
