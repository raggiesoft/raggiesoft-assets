# Part 7: The Lesson

Megan was still reeling, her mind trying to process the fact that she had just passionately tongue-kissed Jordan in front of his five other girlfriends, and they had applauded.

"See?" Aubrie said, her voice soft. She was sitting on the other side of Jordan, and she took his hand. "This is the pact. This is how it works. No jealousy. Just... more family. More love."

To prove the point, Aubrie leaned in. Jordan, without hesitation, turned his head and gave her a deep, loving kiss, just as passionate as the one he had given Megan. Megan watched, her mind braced for... something. A flicker of triumph from Aubrie? A look of possession?

There was nothing. Just love.

When they parted, Madison, who was perched on the armchair, grinned. "Okay, my turn before he gets tired."

She leaned over, and Jordan met her halfway. They shared a long, familiar, romantic kiss. Again, Megan watched. No jealousy.

Lauren and Sarah, who were sitting together on the floor, were next. "Us, too," Lauren said, and they both moved in. Jordan gave each of them a loving, intimate kiss in turn, his gaze warm and affectionate for both of them.

Megan was stunned. Her entire concept of relationships was being rewritten in real-time.

Finally, there was only Zoe.

Jordan turned to her. Megan’s stomach did a backflip. *Okay, this is the real test,* she thought, her old anxieties flaring. *That's her sister. He just kissed me, he just kissed them, and now...* She was expecting Zoe, her logical, analytical sister, to be disgusted, or at least awkward.

Zoe simply leaned forward from her spot at the coffee table. "My data indicates it's my turn," she said, a small, rare, teasing smile on her lips.

Jordan smiled and met her kiss. It was just as romantic, just as deep, and just as full of love as the others.

When they pulled back, Jordan looked flushed and happy. Zoe, however, looked completely unfazed. She just looked at Jordan, then turned her analytical gaze to her sister. She wasn't angry. She wasn't disgusted. She just looked... proud. She gave Megan a small, validating nod.

Megan's world tilted on its axis. The last, final wall of her old social conditioning crumbled. It was real. No jealousy. No competition. Just... love.
