# Part 9: A Health Firewall for Jordan

The conversation happened quietly one evening in the Brooks' basement during the Winter Break of 2015. Jordan was still in the early, painful stages of his recovery from open-heart surgery, and his world was the sectional sofa. He was still on strict sternal precautions, a constant, aching reminder of his own fragility.

The seven of them—Jordan, Aubrie, Madison, Zoe, Lauren, Sarah, and Megan—were gathered in the living area. The rest of the group was watching a movie, but Jordan was quiet, his mind clearly somewhere else. He had been thinking all day about the CVICU, about the ventilator.

"Hey," he said, his voice quiet, cutting through the movie's dialogue. The group immediately muted the TV.

"I was just thinking," Jordan said, his voice raspy. "When I was in the hospital... when I was on the ventilator... if something had gone wrong, who would the doctors have listened to? Who is my legal next-of-kin?"

A cold, heavy silence fell over the room. They all knew the answer. It was *her*.

"He's right," Zoe said, her voice sharp and analytical. "We need a legal firewall. His mother has proven she is a persistent threat. Jordan needs a lawfully married spouse. For power of attorney. For medical decisions. To legally and permanently revoke Aubrie Sinclair's access to him."

"But that's the part that's so unfair," Jordan said, his autistic frustration surfacing. The logic was infuriating. "The law is illogical. It doesn't account for a family like ours. Polygamy is illegal. I can't marry all of you. But I *want* to. You are all my coequals."

"It *is* unfair," Aubrie agreed, her own autistic mind hating the inefficient, arbitrary rule.

"It may be unfair, but it's the law," Zoe countered. "We have to choose one person for the paperwork."

A new quiet fell over the group as they processed the weight of this. Jordan looked at the faces of the six women he loved. How could he choose?

It was Madison who broke the silence. She shifted from her spot on the floor, propping herself up on an elbow to look directly at Aubrie.

"It has to be you, Aubrie," she said, her voice soft but certain.

Aubrie looked surprised, her brow furrowed. "Mads, why? We're all..." She trailed off, but her thought was clear in the eyes of everyone present: *We're all in love with him as coequals. The pact is six, not one.*

"Because you were first," Madison said, her voice full of a fierce, protective love. "That very first night. When he was so broken and scared he couldn't even speak."

Megan, who was cuddled on Jordan's other side on the sofa, nodded, her voice thick with emotion. "She's right, Aubrie. The rest of us... we're the house. But you're the foundation. It's the only thing that's logical."

*Broken?* The word echoed in Jordan's mind, a strange and distant descriptor. He didn't feel broken. He felt whole, strong, surrounded by a love so complete it had rebuilt him from the ground up. He looked down at his own hands, steady and sure, and realized with a jolt that he hadn't seen that shy, terrified boy in the mirror for a very long time.

Madison continued, her voice unwavering. "You were the one who crawled into that bed and just held him. You didn't ask questions, you didn't try to fix him. You were just... there. You were the first person to show him an ounce of kindness in his entire life. That's a place none of the rest of us can ever claim. It's only right that it's you."

Tears welled in Aubrie's eyes as she looked at Madison, a wave of overwhelming gratitude washing over her.

Jordan reached out, his hand covering both of theirs. He looked from Madison's clear, steady gaze to Aubrie's tear-streaked face, his heart so full it felt like it might burst.

"I love you both," he whispered. He then looked around at the other four women, his gaze lingering on each of them. "I love all six of you. So much. And I am so, so grateful that this... us... works. That there's no jealousy, no heartache, no confusion or competition. Just this."

"We will always be coequals," Zoe said, her voice firm with conviction. "That never changes."

"But Madison's right," Lauren added softly, and Sarah nodded in agreement. "That first night... that's the reason. It has to be."

Jordan took a deep, painful breath, his chest aching. His decision was made, driven by the fresh trauma of his surgery. He looked at Aubrie. "They're right. It has to be you. When I was in that hospital, I was terrified. If I am ever helpless like that again... I need *you* to be the one making my medical decisions. I need you to be the firewall that protects me, and our family, from *her*."

And so, surrounded by the warmth and weight of their collective love, they all agreed. It was their path forward. They would all remain coequals in love, life, and intimacy (once his sternum healed), but when the time came, Aubrie would be the one to legally marry Jordan. The reason was singular and sacred: she was the one who had climbed into bed and comforted a scared, broken boy on his very first night of freedom, an act of kindness that had planted the seed for the extraordinary family that had bloomed around them. It was a foundation that could never be equaled.
