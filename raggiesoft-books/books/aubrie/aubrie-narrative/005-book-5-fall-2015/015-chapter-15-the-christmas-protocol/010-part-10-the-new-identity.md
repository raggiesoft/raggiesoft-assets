# Part 10: The New Identity

After the pact had agreed that Aubrie would be the one to legally marry Jordan, a heavy silence settled over the group. Jordan, still resting on the sofa, looked down at his hands, his anxiety returning.

"I'll marry her," he said, his voice quiet but firm. "But... I won't give her my name."

The group looked confused. "J, what do you mean?" Madison asked gently. "Normally, the bride takes the groom's name".

"I don't care," Jordan said, his voice hardening with an adamancy they hadn't heard before. "I hate that name. 'Aubrey' is *her* name. 'Fuller' is the name of a father I never knew. I am adamant that I want to shed both of them".

This was a new, complex problem. If he married Aubrie, she would become "Aubrie Fuller," legally adopting the very name he was trying to destroy.

Mark, who had been sitting next to Hannah, had been quiet, contemplating his own future with her. He looked at Jordan, his brother, struggling with this identity trap.

"So don't give her your name," Mark said, his voice cutting through the silence.

Everyone turned to him.

"It's 2015, guys," Mark said. "It's just paperwork. What if Aubrie just... doesn't change her name?" He looked at Aubrie. "You just stay 'Aubrie Kelly.'"

Then he turned back to Jordan. "And *you* change yours. You go to court, you file the paperwork, and you legally change your name to whatever you want".

Jordan stared at him. "Change... my name?"

"Yeah, man," Mark said, a warm, brotherly smile spreading across his face. "You want to be 'Jordan Kelly'? Do it. You're an adult. You can."

Jordan processed this. Jordan Kelly. It felt right. But... "My middle name," Jordan whispered. "Jordan is my middle name. I'd... I'd be 'Jordan Kelly.' I wouldn't have a middle name."

Mark's smile widened. He hadn't just been thinking about his future; he'd been thinking about their family. "I've got an extra," he said quietly. "If you want it. It's 'Shawn'".

The room went silent.

"Jordan... Shawn... Kelly," Jordan tested the name, his voice thick with emotion. He was shedding the identity of his abuser (Aubrey) and the name of the father he never knew (Fuller) and adopting the names of his wife (Kelly) and his brother (Shawn).

He looked at Mark, his eyes shining. "Jordan Shawn Kelly," he said, his voice firm. "Yes."

A wave of joyous agreement swept the room. The entire group of eleven—the Group of 8, Megan, and Daniel and Laura, who had been listening from the stairs—all loved the idea. It was perfect.

Hannah, who had been holding Mark's hand, was crying softly, her heart full. She looked at Mark, then at Jordan and Aubrie.

"You know," she said, her voice watery but happy, "we've been talking about this too. Mark and I. Our future."

She looked at Mark, who nodded, giving her the floor.

"We're so in love," she said, her gaze fixed on her boyfriend. "And you two," she looked at Jordan and Aubrie, "are the foundation of this whole family. What if... what if we did it together?"

"What do you mean?" Aubrie asked.

"A double wedding," Hannah beamed. "All of us. The four of us. Mark and I get married in the same ceremony that you and Jordan do". She pictured it. "Two brides and two grooms at the front of the aisle, exchanging vows, and getting legally married at the same time".

The idea was brilliant. It wasn't just Jordan's legal protection; it was a celebration of the two core, stable relationships that anchored their entire world.

The entire group loved the idea. It was the perfect plan.
