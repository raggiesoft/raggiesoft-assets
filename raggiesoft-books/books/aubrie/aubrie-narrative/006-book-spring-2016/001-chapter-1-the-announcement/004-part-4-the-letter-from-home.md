# Part 4: The Letter from Home

Zoe read the title aloud. "To the scalpers, the bots, and the fans asking about the front row."

She scrolled down, and the group read the message together.

"To everyone wondering why the floor seats for The Crucible show vanished before the public sale even started:

We didn't start this band in a boardroom in Los Angeles. We didn't get discovered on a reality show. We started this band in a sweaty, unheated garage at the corner of **Progress Street NE & Turner Street NE**—just one block from campus—trying to play loud enough to keep warm.

We learned how to be a band in the cramped, soundproof practice rooms of Henderson Hall, staying late after class until the janitors kicked us out. We played our first gigs at the local bars downtown for tips and pizza.

Blacksburg isn't just a tour stop on a spreadsheet. This is where we found our sound. This is where we found each other. This is our home.

And the way we were raised, when you come home for a celebration, you don't make your family sit outside. You take care of them first.

So, we made a call. We told the promoters and the ticket brokers to back off. We reserved the entire field and the lower bowl exclusively for the people who walk the same halls we did.

To the general public: We love you, and we can't wait to see you in the stands. It’s going to be a legendary night.

To the Forger Nation student body: The front row is yours. The barrier is yours. The energy is yours. We aren't just playing for you; we're playing with you. Bring the noise.

See you at The Crucible.

— Cassidy, Ryan, Evan, Holly, & Tyler (Class of '89)"

Jordan sat back, staring at the screen. The Stardust Engine, the biggest band in the world, hadn't forgotten. They were CPI alumni, and they had used their power to ensure that he, Aubrie, Madison, and Megan would be right there in the front row.

"Forgers take care of Forgers," Mark said quietly, summing it up perfectly.

"Bring the noise," Jordan whispered, a smile spreading across his face. "Oh, we will."
