# Part 1: The Shuttle

On the afternoon of Saturday, May 21, 2016, the Group of 11 gathered at the **Falling Branch Park & Ride**. The lot was transformed. Usually a quiet commuter hub, it was now a bustling staging ground filled with fans in maroon and orange.

Waiting at the curb wasn't the usual scheduled bus. It was a 60-foot articulated Blue Ridge Transit bus, its digital destination sign cycling between two high-energy messages:

CRUCIBLE SHUTTLE

...and then...

GO FORGERS!

The front door stood open, and a loud, clear automated voice called out on a repeated loop for the crowd:

"Crucible Shuttle. GO FORGERS! ... Crucible Shuttle. GO FORGERS!"

"Look at that," Mark grinned, pointing as the sign flashed over and the voice boomed. "They really pulled out all the stops."

"Blue Ridge Transit suspends all regular service for Tier 1 events," Zoe explained, checking her watch. "Every bus they own is running shuttle loops. It's the most efficient way to move 60,000 people."

Jordan wheeled himself to the front of the line. The driver deployed the ramp—simple and fast, unlike the coach lifts.

"Heading to the show?" the driver asked with a smile. He was wearing a Stardust Engine t-shirt over his uniform.

"Front row," Jordan beamed.

They boarded the massive bus. It didn't make the usual stops. It didn't wind through town. It roared onto the highway, bypassing the traffic jams, and drove straight to the stadium.

When they arrived, the bus pulled into the dedicated **Crucible Bus Loop**, dropping them off right at the foot of the stadium gates. Jordan looked out the window at the massive crowd and the looming stadium. The shuttle had delivered them right to the doorstep of history.

As the Group of 11 bus pulled into **The Crucible Bus Loop**, Jordan looked out the window at the adjacent "Stadium Gold" parking lot.

He saw a line of cars stuck in gridlock, waiting to pay. A frustrated driver in a sedan was handing a wad of cash to an attendant.

"Look at that sign," Mark said, pointing. "**EVENT PARKING: \$50. CASH ONLY.**"

"Inefficient," Zoe noted, shaking her head. "They are paying a premium for the privilege of sitting in traffic."

Their bus driver, meanwhile, opened the doors. "Alright folks, we're here! Thanks for riding the shuttle. Enjoy the show!"

No wallets. No swiping. No cash. They just rolled off the bus, right to the front gate, for free.

"Fifty bucks to park," Daniel Brooks chuckled, guiding Jordan toward the security checkpoint. "I love this school, but I love not paying for parking even more."

"That's why we take the bus," Jordan smiled. "The *real* VIP experience."
