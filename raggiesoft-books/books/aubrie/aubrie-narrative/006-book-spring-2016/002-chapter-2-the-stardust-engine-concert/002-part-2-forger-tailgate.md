# Part 2: Forger Tailgate

The sun was high and bright over Blacksburg on Saturday, May 21, 2016. The area surrounding **The Crucible** had been transformed. It wasn't a football game day, but the energy was identical, if not more intense.

The university had sanctioned an official pre-concert tailgate, but with a specific twist: the inner perimeter, the massive paved plaza directly in front of Gate 1, was cordoned off by heavy steel barricades.

Jordan wheeled himself up to the checkpoint, flanked by his fortress of family. Before they even reached the staff members scanning tickets, they were met by large, high-contrast signs zip-tied to the barricades at eye level.

> NOTICE OF FILMING AND LIVE RECORDING
>
> Today's event is being recorded for commercial release. By entering the "Forger Nation" Tailgate Plaza and The Crucible, you consent to being filmed and recorded as part of the "Live at The Crucible" concert film and live album. Be loud. Be proud. You are part of history.

"Look at that," Mark said, grinning as he read the sign. "We're not just watching the show. We're in the show."

"That means the documentary crew is probably inside the plaza right now," Zoe noted, scanning the area. "We should ensure our energy levels are optimal."

Jordan tapped his **Poly Pass** on the scanner held by event staff. The device chirped, verifying his student status and his Tier 1 ticket.

"Welcome in, Jordan," the staffer smiled. "Enjoy the show."

He rolled through the barrier, passing the sign and crossing the threshold into the exclusive zone. The plaza was a sea of maroon and orange, but instead of jerseys, everyone was wearing band merch. It was a massive, exclusive block party for the people who made the university what it was.

There were food trucks serving local favorites (including a **Smokin' Anvil** stand serving BBQ), corn hole games set up with band logos, and a DJ blasting the band's deep cuts. And true to the sign, Jordan saw a camera crew moving through the crowd, capturing the joy of the fans.

"This is incredible," Megan said, looking around at the thousands of people who, like her, were alumni back home for the night.

"It's Forger Nation," Jordan said, a grin splitting his face. "It's just us."

They found a spot near the center, claiming a table. They spent the afternoon eating, laughing, and soaking in the atmosphere of a stadium that had once terrified him, now turned into the ultimate safe space—and the set of his favorite band's movie.
