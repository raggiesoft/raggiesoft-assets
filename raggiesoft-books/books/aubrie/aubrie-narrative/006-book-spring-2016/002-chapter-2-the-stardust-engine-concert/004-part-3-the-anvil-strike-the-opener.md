# Part 3: The Anvil Strike (The Opener)

The stadium was pitch black. The massive crowd of 60,000 was buzzing, a low-frequency hum of anticipation.

Jordan sat in the front row, gripping his wheel rims. He looked at the massive stage. The setup was dark, but he could see the silhouette of a giant prop in the center: a massive, iron anvil.

Then, a single spotlight hit center stage, illuminating **Ryan O'Connell** holding his battered, original 1986 electric guitar. A single, high feedback note whined through the speakers.

"Thirty years ago..." Ryan said into the mic, his voice echoing across the silent field. "We were just five kids in a garage on Progress Street. We didn't have a record deal. We didn't have a tour bus. All we had was a cassette tape and an idea."

He looked out at the darkness.

"We entered a contest to write a song for *this* stadium. We wanted to make something loud enough to scare the hell out of anyone who dared to come into our house!"

The crowd roared, a deafening sound of recognition.

"For thirty years, you guys have been stomping your feet to our song every single Saturday!" Ryan shouted. "So tonight, for the 30th Anniversary... let's see if we can break the seismographs! IGNITION!"

TING!

The single, high-pitched metallic note rang out. Clear and cold.

BOOM!

60,000 people stomped in unison. The ground shook.

TING! BOOM!

The rhythm sped up—*TING-BOOM, TING-BOOM*—building immense tension.

Then, Ryan slammed his hand down on the strings. The heavy, distorted industrial riff exploded, and the stage lights blasted Maroon and Orange into the night.

"The lights go dark, the tension builds..."

Ryan’s vocals were gritty and powerful, a call to battle. When the chorus hit, the entire stadium screamed the response.

"IGNITION!" (Crowd: IGNITION!) "This is the Forger's Call!"

Jordan threw his head back, screaming the words along with his family, feeling the vibration of the stomp in his chest. This wasn't just a song; it was a physical force.

As the song crashed to its chaotic, pyrotechnic finale, Ryan shouted one last time over the feedback.

"WELCOME TO THE CRUCIBLE!"

The crowd noise was deafening. Ryan smiled, breathless.

"Thank you! Oh, it feels so good to be home," he said, wiping sweat from his brow. "We wrote this next one about that feeling... that feeling of finally finding your own personal paradise... right here on this campus."

He signaled the band, ready for the next track. Jordan sat back, his heart racing, his face glowing. The concert had begun.
