# Part 4: The Anthem in the Dark

The sun had fully set, and the anticipation in **The Crucible** was reaching a fever pitch. Jordan, Aubrie, Madison, and Megan were settled into their prime spots in **Section 104, Row A**. They were right against the barrier, perfectly positioned for the show.

A massive camera boom, suspended on a crane, swooped down over the crowd, its lens capturing the sea of maroon and orange. Jordan cheered, waving his **Ceremonial Ticket Stub** at the lens, hoping to make the final cut.

"Get that thing out of my face!"

The shout came from directly behind them. Jordan turned his head. A man in the row behind the accessible section—likely a guest of an alumnus given his lack of school colors—was standing up, yelling at a nearby security supervisor.

"I paid good money for these seats!" the man shouted, pointing at the camera crane. "I didn't pay to have a camera blocking my view every two minutes. I didn't sign a release for this! You can't film me without my permission!"

The security supervisor, a woman who looked like she had been dealing with this all night, didn't even blink. She calmly pointed a finger toward the massive digital scoreboard at the end of the field.

On the screen, in fifty-foot-tall letters, was the disclaimer: **NOTICE: FILMING IN PROGRESS.**

"Sir," the supervisor said, her voice polite but bored. "You consented when you scanned your ticket. It was on the purchase page. It is on the signs at the gate. It is on the scoreboard right now. If you do not wish to be recorded, you are free to leave the venue. Otherwise, please sit down."

The man sputtered, looking around for support. "This is ridiculous! Who even does a live album anymore?"

"We do!" Madison yelled, turning around in her seat with a dazzling, defiant grin. "We're Forger Nation! Smile for the camera!"

The students around the man laughed and cheered. Realizing he was outnumbered and legally wrong, the man grumbled and sat back down, crossing his arms.

Jordan turned back to the stage, sharing a conspiratorial grin with Aubrie. He looked at the camera boom swinging overhead again. He didn't care about his permission; he *wanted* the world to see him here. He wanted the proof that he had survived.

"Ready for your close-up?" Aubrie asked, squeezing his hand.

"Born ready," Jordan said.

Moments later, the stadium lights suddenly cut out, plunging The Crucible into a profound, absolute darkness. A roar went up from the crowd of 60,000, but it wasn't the aggressive, territorial roar of a football game that Jordan remembered with such dread. This was different. It was a wave of pure, unified anticipation.

A single, brilliant white spotlight snapped on, cutting through the black void to hit the center of the stage. It illuminated **Cassidy O’Connell**, the band's lead singer. She was standing at the mic stand, gripping it with both hands, vibrating with energy.

Behind her, the band kicked in with a soft melody that shook the ground under Jordan's wheels. It was loud, powerful, and unfamiliar. Jordan leaned forward, intrigued. He didn't know this track.

Cassidy leaned into the mic, her voice booming over the massive sound system, shouting over the roaring crowd and the driving beat.

"Blacksburg! We've been all over the world... but there is no better feeling... Than ending this entire tour... right here at home, in The Crucible!"

The crowd screamed, a sound of pure love. Jordan felt the vibration in his chest, rattling his ribs. Two years ago, that vibration had felt like a threat. Tonight, it felt like a heartbeat.

"Are you with us one last time, Forger Nation?" Cassidy shouted. "Let's go!"

The band surged forward, the guitars crunching and the drums thundering. Jordan watched, mesmerized by the spectacle, the lights sweeping over the sea of maroon and orange. He was enjoying the show, feeling safe between Aubrie and Madison, but he still didn't know what was coming.

Then, at exactly one minute and fifteen seconds in, the instrumental intro cut to a rhythmic pulse, and Cassidy began to sing.

Woke up this morning, the world was gray... Didn't know the colors had all faded away...

The lyrics hit Jordan like a physical shockwave.

His eyes widened, and a gasp stuck in his throat. He knew those words. He knew them by heart. It was **"Electric Color"**—his favorite song, his anthem. But this wasn't the synth-pop track from the 1987 record Mark had played for him in the basement. This was something new. It was a massive, roaring rock anthem version, transformed for the stadium.

Been living in a room with the curtains drawn tight... Then you flipped a switch and turned on the light...

Jordan’s heart exploded with pure, unadulterated joy. The realization washed over him, hot and electric. They were playing *his* song.

In that single instant, the dark, traumatic memory of the "Homecoming Humiliation"—the 38-0 scoreboard, the panic, the flight at halftime—was simply deleted. It was overwritten by this moment. The gray world of his mother's control, the fear of this stadium, the shame of his past... it all evaporated under the bright, crashing waves of the music.

He turned his head and looked at Aubrie. She was watching him, a brilliant smile on her face, singing along. She was the one who had flipped the switch.

He reached out and grabbed her hand, squeezing it as hard as he could. She squeezed back, anchoring him in the present, far away from the gray, and ready for the color to explode.
