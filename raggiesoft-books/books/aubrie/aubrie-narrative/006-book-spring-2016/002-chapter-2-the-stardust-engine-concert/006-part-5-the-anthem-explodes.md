# Part 5: The Anthem Explodes

The driving beat from the verse didn't let up; it intensified. As Cassidy finished the line about flipping the switch, the tension on stage broke, and the song exploded into the massive, hook-heavy chorus.

Oh, I'm seeing in electric color!

The stage erupted with light. Brilliant beams of neon teal, magenta, and violet shot into the night sky, illuminating the entire bowl of The Crucible. Massive stadium drums thundered, and a soaring synthesizer melody filled the air, lifting the crowd off their feet in a single, unified wave of joy.

Jordan didn't flinch. He didn't cover his ears. He threw his head back and laughed. The sheer, overwhelming volume of the moment washed over him, cleansing him of the fear that had lived in this space for two years. He wasn't scared. The noise wasn't an assault; it was a song. It was the sound of 60,000 people sharing happiness.

It's an electric-colored feeling, And it all started with you!

Madison grabbed his left hand and sang at the top of her lungs, her face glowing in the stage lights. Jordan squeezed back, singing with her, his voice lost in the roar but his spirit soaring.

He looked to his right. Zoe, usually the most reserved and analytical member of their family, was dancing in the aisle. She wasn't analyzing the acoustics or checking the time; she was spinning, her arms in the air, lost in the rhythm. Even Mark and Hannah were swaying together, caught up in the anthem.

The song moved into the bridge. The beat got stronger, the tension building with every measure. The lights pulsed faster. The energy was reaching a fever pitch.

The beat is getting stronger I can't hide it any longer...

Suddenly, the music cut out completely.

The stadium fell into a hushed, electric silence, leaving only the hum of the amplifiers and the collective held breath of the crowd. **Ryan O'Connell**, the lead guitarist, wheeled into the spotlight next to Cassidy. He looked out at the sea of faces—his fellow alumni.

"Can you feel that, CPI?" Cassidy whispered into the mic, his voice intimate and echoing. "That's the sound of a new world. No more gray. Just... color."

Jordan felt a chill run down his spine. It felt as if the band were speaking directly to him, acknowledging the gray prison he had escaped and the vibrant life he had found.

Cassidy pointed a finger toward Ryan. "Here comes the solo!"

Ryan struck a note that screamed through the stadium. The solo soared, a high, piercing melody that seemed to reach up and touch the stars above them. The lights went wild, sweeping across the stands.

Jordan watched, mesmerized. He looked at the field where the football team had fallen, where he had felt so small and defeated. That ghost was gone. This wasn't the place where he had failed. This was the place where he was celebrating. He was surrounded by his pact, by his family, and he was seeing everything in electric color.
