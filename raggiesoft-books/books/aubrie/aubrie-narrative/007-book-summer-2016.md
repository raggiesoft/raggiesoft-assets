# Book: Summer 2016

## Chapter 1: The Foundation

### Part 1: The Pact

The conversation happened quietly one evening in the basement "Adults' Wing" of the newly completed compound.

It was the summer of 2016. They had just finished their sophomore year at CPI, and while graduation was still two years away, the air was thick with the excitement of settling into their permanent home.

The **seven** of them—Jordan, Aubrie, Madison, Zoe, Lauren, Sarah, and **Megan**—were sprawled out together in their familiar "puppy pile" in the **Sunken Sanctuary**. They lay on the massive, custom-built foam mattress that filled the pit, a sea of comfort that held them all safely.

They had been discussing the practicalities of their future, and the topic of legal marriage had come up.

"It should be one of us," Zoe said, her voice cutting through the comfortable silence with characteristic logic. "For legal reasons. Power of attorney, next of kin... we need a firewall against his mother if something goes wrong."

A gentle quiet fell over the group. Jordan looked at the faces of the women he loved, a wave of emotion making it hard to speak.

It was Madison who broke the silence. She shifted, propping herself up on an elbow to look directly at Aubrie.

"It has to be you, Aubrie," she said, her voice soft but certain.

Aubrie looked surprised, her brow furrowed. "Mads, why? We're all..." She trailed off, but her thought was clear in the eyes of everyone present: *We're all in love with him as coequals.*

"Because you were first," Madison said, her voice full of a fierce, protective love. "That very first night. When he was so broken and scared he couldn't even speak."

*Broken?* The word echoed in Jordan's mind, a strange and distant descriptor. He didn't feel broken. He felt whole, strong, surrounded by a love so complete it had rebuilt him from the ground up. He looked down at his own hands, steady and sure, and realized with a jolt that he hadn't seen that shy, terrified boy in the mirror for a very long time.

Madison continued, her voice unwavering. "You were the one who crawled into that bed and just held him. You didn't ask questions, you didn't try to fix him. You were just... there. You were the first person to show him an ounce of kindness in his entire life. That's a place none of the rest of us can ever claim. It's only right that it's you."

Megan, who was curled up on Jordan’s other side, nodded slowly. "She's right," she said softly. "I came later, and I love this family more than anything, but even I can see it. Aubrie is the foundation we all built on."

Tears welled in Aubrie's eyes as she looked at Madison and Megan, a wave of overwhelming gratitude washing over her.

Jordan reached out, his hand covering Madison’s. He looked from her clear, steady gaze to Aubrie's tear-streaked face, his heart so full it felt like it might burst.

"I love you both," he whispered, his voice thick with emotion.

He then looked around at the other four women—Zoe, Lauren, Sarah, and Megan—his gaze lingering on each of them.

"I love all **six** of you. So much. And I am so, so grateful that this... us... works. That there's no jealousy, no heartache, no confusion or competition. Just this."

"We will always be coequals," Zoe said, her voice firm with conviction. "That never changes."

"But Madison's right," Lauren added softly, and Sarah nodded in agreement. "That first night... that's the reason. It has to be."

And so, surrounded by the warmth and weight of their collective love, they all agreed. It was their path forward. They would all remain coequals in love and life, but when the time came, Aubrie would be the one to legally marry Jordan.

The reason was singular and sacred: she was the one who had climbed into bed and comforted a scared, broken boy on his very first night of freedom, an act of kindness that had planted the seed for the extraordinary family that had bloomed around them. ## Chapter 2: The Double Wedding

### Part 1: The Morning Of

The morning of **Saturday, July 16, 2016**, dawned bright and clear over the newly completed Kelly Family Compound on U.S. 11. The usual calm of their rural sanctuary was replaced by a low, buzzing hum of excited energy.

Down in the basement "Adults' Wing"—the massive, custom-built space they had moved into just weeks before—the first to stir was Hannah. She was too keyed up to sleep, her heart a happy flutter in her chest. She slipped out of the "Anderson Suite," the private bedroom she shared with Mark, leaving him to sleep for a few more precious minutes. As she padded past the double doors of the adjacent "Pact Suite," she smiled. Behind those doors lay the "Sunken Sanctuary," where Jordan and his six partners—Aubrie, Madison, Zoe, Lauren, Sarah, and Megan—were likely still entangled in their massive, comfortable "puppy pile."

She headed up the main stairs to the great room and found she wasn't the first one awake. Daniel and Laura Brooks were already in the massive kitchen, the aroma of fresh coffee filling the air.

"Morning, sweetie," Laura said, her smile as warm as the sunrise streaming through the large windows. "Couldn't sleep either?"

"Not a wink," Hannah admitted with a grin.

One by one, the rest of the family emerged from the basement.

- **Mark**, looking surprisingly well-rested as he joined Hannah from their private suite.

- **Zoe and Lauren**, the first to emerge from the Pact Suite, already reviewing the logistical timeline on a tablet with quiet efficiency.

- **Sarah and Madison**, practically vibrating with excitement.

- **Megan**, checking her camera gear to ensure she captured every moment for the family archives.

- And finally, **Jordan and Aubrie**, holding hands, their faces glowing with a serene, profound happiness born of the deep, committed love they shared with their entire pact.

Breakfast was a deliberately simple affair, a small island of normalcy in a day that would be anything but. There was no big, fancy meal. Toast was made, fruit was passed around, and Jordan, true to his routine, sat at the massive dining table with a simple bowl of cereal and milk, a comforting and familiar start to the most important day of his life.

After breakfast, the organized chaos began. The group split off to shower and get dressed in their respective suites. The sounds of hairdryers, laughing, and last-minute steaming of dresses and suits filled the house.

Finally, it was time. Looking resplendent in their wedding attire—the women in their beautiful, coordinated dresses, Mark in his sharp suit, and Jordan looking handsome and confident in his own tuxedo—they all gathered in the great room.

Caleb Brooks, who had arrived earlier from his own place, gave his dad a nod. "I'm heading out now, Dad. I'll get the van parked near the ballroom and wait for you all there." With a wave, he was gone, their quiet backup plan in motion.

The family then walked down the long driveway together to the newly paved bus stop at the end of their property. As the **Blue Ridge Transit Route 3** bus—a standard 35-foot city bus running its regular route between Christiansburg and Radford—approached and hissed to a stop, Jordan felt a surge of pride.

He maneuvered his wheelchair onto the ramp and into the bus with a practiced ease he hadn't possessed just a few short years ago. He didn't need a special ride. He didn't need a favor. He was taking himself to his own wedding.

His family filed in behind him, their happy chatter filling the front of the bus. As the vehicle pulled away from their home and headed toward the **Christiansburg Pepper Plaza Transit Center**, Jordan looked out the window, his hand held firmly in Aubrie's.

He was independent. He was free. And he was surrounded by everyone he loved.

### Part 2: The Final Touches

The bus ride to **Pepper Plaza** was short and filled with a happy, nervous energy. As they disembarked, the weather seemed to smile on them; it was a perfect, glorious **July day**, warm and sunny, with a gentle summer breeze rustling the full, green leaves on the trees.

Caleb Brooks was already there, waving from the parking lot where he had stashed the family van, having successfully transported the decorations and supplies ahead of the bus.

The venue, "**The Blacksburg Ballroom**," was a beautifully restored historic building right next to the courthouse. The main ballroom was on the second floor, and the family took the large elevator up, the doors opening to reveal a sun-drenched space with high ceilings and polished wood floors.

The room was already largely set up—the chairs arranged in neat rows, the flowers delivered—but it needed the family's personal touch to be perfect. **Zoe**, armed with a clipboard and a determined expression, immediately stepped into her role as the day's logistical commander.

"Okay, team, we have thirty minutes until doors open," Zoe announced, her voice calm but authoritative. "Let's optimize this setup."

She pointed to the tables. "**Lauren**, let's make sure the centerpieces are perfect. **Megan**, can you help her? And please keep that camera ready; we need documentation of the 'before' state."

"On it," Megan said with a bright smile, adjusting the strap of her camera before moving to help Lauren tweak a floral arrangement.

"**Mark**, can you and **Sarah** double-check the sound system? We need to ensure the microphones are balanced for the vows," Zoe continued.

"**Hannah**, let's you and I make sure the guest book table is exactly right. We need the pens tested and the signage visible."

While the others buzzed around with their tasks, creating a hive of purposeful activity, **Daniel and Laura Brooks** took a moment to stand with the two people at the center of it all.

"How are you feeling, son?" Daniel asked Jordan, placing a warm, grounding hand on his shoulder.

"Good," Jordan said, and he meant it. He looked around the beautiful, sunlit room, at the faces of his family all working together—Megan laughing with Lauren, Mark testing a mic check with Sarah, Zoe and Hannah aligning the guest book. "I feel... ready."

"You look beautiful, sweetie," Laura said to **Aubrie**, reaching out to gently adjust a small, stray piece of her hair. "Both of you do."

She looked over at **Hannah** across the room, who was beaming as she worked next to Zoe. Laura’s eyes misted over slightly. "All of you. This whole family looks beautiful today."

The room was filled with a quiet, purposeful hum. It wasn't the frantic energy of a stressful event, but the calm, coordinated effort of a family working together to make sure every detail was perfect. They weren't just setting up a venue; they were putting the final, loving touches on the first day of the rest of their lives.

### Part 3: The Gathering

With the last centerpiece adjusted and the final sound check complete, a hush of anticipation fell over The Blacksburg Ballroom. It was time.

**Daniel and Laura Brooks**, acting as the patriarch and matriarch for the entire extended clan, took their places near the entrance to greet the arriving guests. They were joined by the women of the pact—**Madison, Zoe, Lauren, Sarah, and Megan**.

While the others stood in a receiving line, **Megan** moved with a happy, purposeful energy. She balanced her role as a greeter with her role as the family archivist, pausing between hugs to snap beautiful, candid photos of guests entering the sunlit space, capturing the genuine joy on their faces.

The two couples—**Jordan and Aubrie**, and **Mark and Hannah**—remained tucked away in a small side room, out of sight until the ceremony began, holding hands and breathing in the reality of the moment.

A small, elegant sign on an easel near the guest book set the tone for the event: *"Choose a seat, not a side. We're all family here."*

The first to arrive were the extended **Brooks clan**—a happy, boisterous flood of aunts, uncles, and cousins who enveloped Daniel and Laura in warm hugs. They were followed by **Hannah’s family (the Parkers)** and **Mark’s family (the Andersons)**, who had traveled in from different parts of the state.

There was a joyful energy as the families met, many for the first time. They shook hands and introduced themselves not as separate entities or in-laws, but as the newly connected branches of one large, sprawling family tree. Caleb, Owen, Lucas, and Jacob Brooks acted as ushers, guiding everyone not to specific sides, but simply to open chairs, physically mixing the families together.

Soon, the room began to fill with a diverse mix of friends and colleagues. Classmates from **CPI**, their faces familiar from late-night study groups and shared projects, came to support their friends.

For Jordan, watching from the crack in the side room door, seeing them arrive was particularly meaningful. These were people who knew him simply as "**Jordan**," the intelligent and kind classmate from Astrophysics, not as the sheltered, terrified boy from his past. A few of his coworkers from his part-time job at the campus **library** arrived, clapping him on the shoulder in spirit, wishing him well.

The absence of certain people was a quiet, unspoken statement of the family's victory. **No one from the Sinclair or Fuller families was present.** They hadn't been invited; they didn't even know this day was happening. Jordan's past was completely and totally severed from his present.

Every person in the room was there because they loved and supported him, Hannah, Mark, and Aubrie for *who* they were now.

As the guests found their seats, filling the two curved sections of the hall, the room buzzed with happy, low chatter. There was no division, no "bride's side" or "groom's side." There was only a single, unified community, a testament to the life and the family the eight of them—plus Megan, Bob, and Emily—had built together, ready to witness the beautiful ceremony that was about to begin.

### Part 4: The Vows

The music began, a soft, beautiful melody that silenced the happy chatter in the ballroom. All eyes turned to the wide central aisle.

At the front, **Mark Anderson** stood waiting, his expression a perfect mix of awe and adoration. Beside him, **Jordan Kelly**, dressed in a sharp tuxedo, sat in his wheelchair. His hands were resting calmly in his lap, but his heart pounded a steady, joyful rhythm as he locked his gaze on the doors at the back of the room.

**Hannah** came down the aisle first, on the arm of her father, looking radiant and serene. She joined Mark, and the two of them beamed at each other, a silent promise passing between them.

Then, the music swelled. **Aubrie Jean** came down the aisle on the arm of her own father, Thomas Kelly. He was a kind-faced man with eyes that shone with pride and unshed tears as he escorted his daughter to her future.

The room was packed with the people who made this moment possible. In the front rows sat the families who had embraced this unconventional union without hesitation.

- **Daniel and Laura Brooks**, the parents who had taken them all in.

- **Diane and David Scott**, Madison’s parents, sat beaming. Between them sat two teenagers who were watching with wide, awestruck eyes: **Bob Scott** and **Emily Jenkins**.

- **Sarah’s father**, Mr. Barnes, sat proudly nearby, having fully embraced the family that had given his daughter a home.

- **Lauren’s parents**, the Blacks, were there, witnessing their daughter’s happiness.

Thomas placed Aubrie's hand in Jordan's, gave his new son-in-law a warm, approving nod, and then took his seat. The two families, now officially joined, smiled at each other.

The two couples stood together, a perfect, happy foursome, ready to make their vows.

But before the officiant could begin, he smiled kindly. "Before we proceed," he announced, "there is another vow that must be made first. A promise to be made between family."

He looked to the first row, where **Madison, Zoe, Lauren, Sarah, and Megan** sat together.

They stood and came to the front, forming a semi-circle around Jordan and Aubrie.

The room went completely still. Everyone present understood the unique dynamic of this family. They knew that Jordan loved all **six** of these women as equal romantic partners. This ceremony wasn't about saying goodbye; it was about honoring the structure that allowed them all to thrive. It was an acknowledgment that while they were all his wives in spirit, Aubrie was the foundation upon which their lives were built.

**Lauren** stepped forward first, her voice clear and strong, addressing not just the couple, but the entire room. "Our family is built on a promise," she began, "a pact of love and support that extends to everyone standing up here. As part of that pact, the six of us share a deep, loving, and romantic bond with Jordan. Today, as he and Aubrie take this beautiful and important step into marriage, we want to honor that pact, and their union, with a promise of our own. This is the Releasing of Jordan."

She turned her warm gaze to Jordan. "You took my broken heart and showed me what real, selfless kindness was. I release you to the one who taught you first."

**Sarah** came next, her eyes shining with tears. "You took in a stranger and called her your family from day one. I release you to the one who is your truest home."

**Zoe** followed, her words logical yet full of profound emotion. "Our cooperative dynamic is the most efficient and supportive system I have ever been a part of. To ensure its continued success, I release you to her."

**Megan** stepped forward next. She looked at Jordan, the boy she had helped nurse back to health, the man who had become her heart. "You gave me a place to belong when I was lost. I release you to the one who built the foundation."

Finally, **Madison** took Jordan's free hand. "I was there in Room 214 with you two," she said, her voice thick with love and history. "I watched a shy, scared boy who was afraid of the dark become the man who stands before us today. And I know he became that man because you, Aubrie Kelly, were the first light in his darkness."

After the “Releasing of the Groom” ceremony, Madison gently placed Jordan's hand in Aubrie's. The five women—his partners, his loves, his family—returned to their seats, leaving a profound and sacred silence in their wake. There was not a dry eye in the house.

The officiant then turned to Mark and Hannah.

"I, **Mark Shawn Anderson**," Mark began, his voice thick with emotion, "take you, **Hannah Ashlee Parker**, to be my wife. They say every good team needs a rock, an anchor. You're my anchor. You're the anchor for all of us. I promise to be your rock, to support you, to love you, and to build a life with you that is as strong and steady as you are. I love you."

Hannah smiled through her tears. "I, **Hannah Ashlee Parker**, take you, **Mark Shawn Anderson**, to be my husband. You are the kindest, most decent man I have ever known. You see the best in everyone, and you make us all better for it. I promise to love you, to laugh with you, and to always be your partner in this crazy, wonderful life we've built. I love you, and I am so proud to become Hannah Parker Anderson."

Then, all eyes turned to Jordan and Aubrie. But before Jordan could speak his vows to her, Mark stepped forward slightly.

"Jordan," Mark said, his voice firm with brotherly love. "You are the brother of my heart. In front of everyone here, I offer you my middle name, **Shawn**, to take as your own, as a sign of our bond."

Jordan looked at his best man, his brother, and nodded, his eyes shining with unshed tears. He then turned to his bride.

"Aubrie," he began, his voice clear and steady. "Before I met you, my world was small, and it was dark. I didn't know what it felt like to be safe. You were the first person who ever saw me, not just my chair or my diagnosis. You didn't just open a door for me; you built a whole new world and invited me in. I promise to spend the rest of my life exploring that world with you, to love you, and to be the man you always knew I could be."

Aubrie looked at him, her heart in her eyes. "Jordan... I used to look up at the stars and feel so small, so alone. I felt like a single particle drifting in a giant, empty universe. The day I met you, it was like a star fell to earth. You are my other half, my missing piece. You make my universe whole."

She took a deep breath, her voice full of a love that was a gift.

"And so, I, **Aubrie Jean Kelly**, take you, Jordan, to be my husband. And I offer you my last name, **Kelly**, to take as your own, to make our family whole."

The officiant beamed. "By the power vested in me, and with the new names you have so lovingly chosen, I now pronounce you, and you, husbands and wives."

The room erupted in a joyous, tearful standing ovation. But the celebration wasn't starting just yet. Here, in front of his family, **Jordan Shawn Kelly** had just been born, but in the eyes of the Commonwealth of Virginia, there was one final, necessary step to take. The reception would begin in exactly as long as it took for a court hearing. Everything had been meticulously pre-approved by a sympathetic Judge and Clerk of Court; the hearing was a formality, a final seal on a done deal. It was time to make it legal.

### Part 5: The Legal Seal

As the last echoes of the joyful applause faded in the ballroom, the officiant smiled and addressed the guests.

"Ladies and gentlemen, the reception will begin shortly. Please enjoy the refreshments and each other's company while the families handle one final piece of official business next door. The happy couples will join you soon."

While the guests began to mingle, the **"Group of 17"**—the two newlywed couples, the five other women of the Pact (Madison, Zoe, Lauren, Sarah, and Megan), the Brooks parents (Daniel and Laura), the Scott parents (David and Diane with Bob and Emily), Sarah's father, and Lauren's parents—slipped out the side door.

Their destination was just a few steps away: the **Montgomery County Courthouse**.

Weeks ago, Daniel Brooks had made a special appointment. He had explained the unique circumstances of Jordan's life and his wedding day to a sympathetic court clerk, who had passed the story along. The paperwork was ready; the docket was cleared.

They entered the quiet, stately building and were ushered immediately into a small, private courtroom where a judge was waiting for them, his expression kind and welcoming.

"Mr. Fuller," the judge began, his voice gentle but formal. "Please approach."

For the very last time in his life, he was **Aubrey Jordan Fuller**.

He wheeled himself forward, his massive, blended family forming a silent, supportive phalanx behind him.

The legal proceedings were brief, a formality that had been well-prepared in advance.

The judge reviewed the petition, his eyes scanning the documents before him.

"This court," the judge said, looking up and directly at Jordan, "recognizes that a name is more than just a word. It is the foundation of an identity. It is clear to me that the name 'Aubrey Jordan Fuller' represents a past that this young man has, with courage and with the love of this extraordinary family, overcome."

He picked up a pen and signed the official order with a firm, deliberate flourish.

He then looked up, his formal demeanor replaced by a warm, genuine smile.

"By the power vested in me by the Commonwealth of Virginia, I hereby grant this petition. **Mr. Kelly**," he said, the new name ringing with the force of law, "congratulations on your wedding day. Please consider this my gift to you."

It was done. Aubrey Jordan Fuller was gone, erased by the stroke of a pen.

He turned his wheelchair and faced his family. He was, now and forever, legally and officially, **Jordan Shawn Kelly**.

Together, the entire happy, complete family walked out of the courthouse and back toward their reception, ready to celebrate the first day of their new life.

### Part 7: The First Night

The last **Route 3** bus of the evening hummed quietly as it carried them home along the dark ribbon of U.S. 11. The boisterous energy of the reception had faded, replaced by a comfortable, contented exhaustion. Outside the windows, the humid July air was filled with the song of crickets.

Caleb Brooks, having already driven the van back with the decorations, was waiting for them at the end of the long driveway when the bus pulled away.

The walk up to the house was quiet. Inside the massive, silent compound, the two happy couples officially parted ways for the night.

Mark scooped a sleepy Hannah up into his arms and carried her, bridal-style, toward their private suite on the second floor, whispering goodnight to the others.

The remaining **seven**—Jordan, Aubrie, Madison, Zoe, Lauren, Sarah, and **Megan**—made their way down to the basement "Adults' Wing."

The day, with all its joy and emotional weight, had taken its toll. Jordan, especially, was drained, both mentally and physically. He navigated his wheelchair into their master suite—the "Sunken Sanctuary"—and let out a long, tired sigh.

He wheeled himself to the edge of the pit and looked down. Bathed in the soft, ambient light, the massive, custom-built 15-foot bed looked like a calm blue sea of comfort waiting for them.

Aubrie came and knelt beside his chair. "Tired?" she asked softly.

He nodded, reaching for her hand. "So tired. Can we... can we just go to bed?"

He looked around at the **six** women who were his entire world.

"But," he added, his voice soft but firm, "I still... I want everyone here. I want the puppy pile."

A wave of gentle, understanding smiles went through the room. There was never any doubt.

"Of course," Madison said, her voice full of warmth. "Wouldn't have it any other way."

The pact still existed. The no-jealousy rule was as solid as the foundation of the house. **Aubrie Jean Kelly** was his wife, but **Madison Elizabeth Scott** was still his girlfriend, and his love for Zoe, Lauren, Sarah, and **Megan** was just as deep and real as it had been that morning.

There was a quiet, unspoken agreement as they all prepared for bed. This was their sanctuary, their most intimate space. As they had done many nights before, they slipped into their finest birthday suits, a comfortable and natural expression of the romantic bond they all shared.

Jordan wheeled his chair to the top of the gentle wooden ramp that ran along the wall. He rolled down the incline, the rubber tires humming on the smooth wood, until he reached the **Transfer Dock** at the bottom.

He parked his chair and locked the brakes. The seat was perfectly flush with the surface of the massive foam mattress.

With a simple, practiced shift of his weight, he slid from the hard seat of his chair onto the soft, welcoming surface of the bed. No lifting. No climbing. Just ease.

He scooted to the middle of the vast sea of foam, grounding himself.

**Aubrie** curled up on his left side, her head resting on his chest. **Madison** curled up on his right. **Megan**, moving with the comfort of someone who had truly found her home, settled in next to Madison. **Zoe, Lauren, and Sarah** found their familiar spots, the incredible 15-foot width of the pit giving everyone plenty of room to sprawl while still touching.

It was his first night as a married man, but nothing had really changed. He was simply home, surrounded by his family, safe in the heart of the pit.

He closed his eyes, listening to the soft, steady breathing of the six women he loved, and fell into the most peaceful sleep of his life.
