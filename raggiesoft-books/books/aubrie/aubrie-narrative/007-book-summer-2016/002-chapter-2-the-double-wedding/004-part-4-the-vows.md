# Part 4: The Vows

The music began, a soft, beautiful melody that silenced the happy chatter in the ballroom. All eyes turned to the wide central aisle.

At the front, **Mark Anderson** stood waiting, his expression a perfect mix of awe and adoration. Beside him, **Jordan Kelly**, dressed in a sharp tuxedo, sat in his wheelchair. His hands were resting calmly in his lap, but his heart pounded a steady, joyful rhythm as he locked his gaze on the doors at the back of the room.

**Hannah** came down the aisle first, on the arm of her father, looking radiant and serene. She joined Mark, and the two of them beamed at each other, a silent promise passing between them.

Then, the music swelled. **Aubrie Jean** came down the aisle on the arm of her own father, Thomas Kelly. He was a kind-faced man with eyes that shone with pride and unshed tears as he escorted his daughter to her future.

The room was packed with the people who made this moment possible. In the front rows sat the families who had embraced this unconventional union without hesitation.

- **Daniel and Laura Brooks**, the parents who had taken them all in.

- **Diane and David Scott**, Madison’s parents, sat beaming. Between them sat two teenagers who were watching with wide, awestruck eyes: **Bob Scott** and **Emily Jenkins**.

- **Sarah’s father**, Mr. Barnes, sat proudly nearby, having fully embraced the family that had given his daughter a home.

- **Lauren’s parents**, the Blacks, were there, witnessing their daughter’s happiness.

Thomas placed Aubrie's hand in Jordan's, gave his new son-in-law a warm, approving nod, and then took his seat. The two families, now officially joined, smiled at each other.

The two couples stood together, a perfect, happy foursome, ready to make their vows.

But before the officiant could begin, he smiled kindly. "Before we proceed," he announced, "there is another vow that must be made first. A promise to be made between family."

He looked to the first row, where **Madison, Zoe, Lauren, Sarah, and Megan** sat together.

They stood and came to the front, forming a semi-circle around Jordan and Aubrie.

The room went completely still. Everyone present understood the unique dynamic of this family. They knew that Jordan loved all **six** of these women as equal romantic partners. This ceremony wasn't about saying goodbye; it was about honoring the structure that allowed them all to thrive. It was an acknowledgment that while they were all his wives in spirit, Aubrie was the foundation upon which their lives were built.

**Lauren** stepped forward first, her voice clear and strong, addressing not just the couple, but the entire room. "Our family is built on a promise," she began, "a pact of love and support that extends to everyone standing up here. As part of that pact, the six of us share a deep, loving, and romantic bond with Jordan. Today, as he and Aubrie take this beautiful and important step into marriage, we want to honor that pact, and their union, with a promise of our own. This is the Releasing of Jordan."

She turned her warm gaze to Jordan. "You took my broken heart and showed me what real, selfless kindness was. I release you to the one who taught you first."

**Sarah** came next, her eyes shining with tears. "You took in a stranger and called her your family from day one. I release you to the one who is your truest home."

**Zoe** followed, her words logical yet full of profound emotion. "Our cooperative dynamic is the most efficient and supportive system I have ever been a part of. To ensure its continued success, I release you to her."

**Megan** stepped forward next. She looked at Jordan, the boy she had helped nurse back to health, the man who had become her heart. "You gave me a place to belong when I was lost. I release you to the one who built the foundation."

Finally, **Madison** took Jordan's free hand. "I was there in Room 214 with you two," she said, her voice thick with love and history. "I watched a shy, scared boy who was afraid of the dark become the man who stands before us today. And I know he became that man because you, Aubrie Kelly, were the first light in his darkness."

After the “Releasing of the Groom” ceremony, Madison gently placed Jordan's hand in Aubrie's. The five women—his partners, his loves, his family—returned to their seats, leaving a profound and sacred silence in their wake. There was not a dry eye in the house.

The officiant then turned to Mark and Hannah.

"I, **Mark Shawn Anderson**," Mark began, his voice thick with emotion, "take you, **Hannah Ashlee Parker**, to be my wife. They say every good team needs a rock, an anchor. You're my anchor. You're the anchor for all of us. I promise to be your rock, to support you, to love you, and to build a life with you that is as strong and steady as you are. I love you."

Hannah smiled through her tears. "I, **Hannah Ashlee Parker**, take you, **Mark Shawn Anderson**, to be my husband. You are the kindest, most decent man I have ever known. You see the best in everyone, and you make us all better for it. I promise to love you, to laugh with you, and to always be your partner in this crazy, wonderful life we've built. I love you, and I am so proud to become Hannah Parker Anderson."

Then, all eyes turned to Jordan and Aubrie. But before Jordan could speak his vows to her, Mark stepped forward slightly.

"Jordan," Mark said, his voice firm with brotherly love. "You are the brother of my heart. In front of everyone here, I offer you my middle name, **Shawn**, to take as your own, as a sign of our bond."

Jordan looked at his best man, his brother, and nodded, his eyes shining with unshed tears. He then turned to his bride.

"Aubrie," he began, his voice clear and steady. "Before I met you, my world was small, and it was dark. I didn't know what it felt like to be safe. You were the first person who ever saw me, not just my chair or my diagnosis. You didn't just open a door for me; you built a whole new world and invited me in. I promise to spend the rest of my life exploring that world with you, to love you, and to be the man you always knew I could be."

Aubrie looked at him, her heart in her eyes. "Jordan... I used to look up at the stars and feel so small, so alone. I felt like a single particle drifting in a giant, empty universe. The day I met you, it was like a star fell to earth. You are my other half, my missing piece. You make my universe whole."

She took a deep breath, her voice full of a love that was a gift.

"And so, I, **Aubrie Jean Kelly**, take you, Jordan, to be my husband. And I offer you my last name, **Kelly**, to take as your own, to make our family whole."

The officiant beamed. "By the power vested in me, and with the new names you have so lovingly chosen, I now pronounce you, and you, husbands and wives."

The room erupted in a joyous, tearful standing ovation. But the celebration wasn't starting just yet. Here, in front of his family, **Jordan Shawn Kelly** had just been born, but in the eyes of the Commonwealth of Virginia, there was one final, necessary step to take. The reception would begin in exactly as long as it took for a court hearing. Everything had been meticulously pre-approved by a sympathetic Judge and Clerk of Court; the hearing was a formality, a final seal on a done deal. It was time to make it legal.
