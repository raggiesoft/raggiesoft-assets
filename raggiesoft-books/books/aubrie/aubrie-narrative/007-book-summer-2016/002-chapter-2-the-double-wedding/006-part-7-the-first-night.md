# Part 7: The First Night

The last **Route 3** bus of the evening hummed quietly as it carried them home along the dark ribbon of U.S. 11. The boisterous energy of the reception had faded, replaced by a comfortable, contented exhaustion. Outside the windows, the humid July air was filled with the song of crickets.

Caleb Brooks, having already driven the van back with the decorations, was waiting for them at the end of the long driveway when the bus pulled away.

The walk up to the house was quiet. Inside the massive, silent compound, the two happy couples officially parted ways for the night.

Mark scooped a sleepy Hannah up into his arms and carried her, bridal-style, toward their private suite on the second floor, whispering goodnight to the others.

The remaining **seven**—Jordan, Aubrie, Madison, Zoe, Lauren, Sarah, and **Megan**—made their way down to the basement "Adults' Wing."

The day, with all its joy and emotional weight, had taken its toll. Jordan, especially, was drained, both mentally and physically. He navigated his wheelchair into their master suite—the "Sunken Sanctuary"—and let out a long, tired sigh.

He wheeled himself to the edge of the pit and looked down. Bathed in the soft, ambient light, the massive, custom-built 15-foot bed looked like a calm blue sea of comfort waiting for them.

Aubrie came and knelt beside his chair. "Tired?" she asked softly.

He nodded, reaching for her hand. "So tired. Can we... can we just go to bed?"

He looked around at the **six** women who were his entire world.

"But," he added, his voice soft but firm, "I still... I want everyone here. I want the puppy pile."

A wave of gentle, understanding smiles went through the room. There was never any doubt.

"Of course," Madison said, her voice full of warmth. "Wouldn't have it any other way."

The pact still existed. The no-jealousy rule was as solid as the foundation of the house. **Aubrie Jean Kelly** was his wife, but **Madison Elizabeth Scott** was still his girlfriend, and his love for Zoe, Lauren, Sarah, and **Megan** was just as deep and real as it had been that morning.

There was a quiet, unspoken agreement as they all prepared for bed. This was their sanctuary, their most intimate space. As they had done many nights before, they slipped into their finest birthday suits, a comfortable and natural expression of the romantic bond they all shared.

Jordan wheeled his chair to the top of the gentle wooden ramp that ran along the wall. He rolled down the incline, the rubber tires humming on the smooth wood, until he reached the **Transfer Dock** at the bottom.

He parked his chair and locked the brakes. The seat was perfectly flush with the surface of the massive foam mattress.

With a simple, practiced shift of his weight, he slid from the hard seat of his chair onto the soft, welcoming surface of the bed. No lifting. No climbing. Just ease.

He scooted to the middle of the vast sea of foam, grounding himself.

**Aubrie** curled up on his left side, her head resting on his chest. **Madison** curled up on his right. **Megan**, moving with the comfort of someone who had truly found her home, settled in next to Madison. **Zoe, Lauren, and Sarah** found their familiar spots, the incredible 15-foot width of the pit giving everyone plenty of room to sprawl while still touching.

It was his first night as a married man, but nothing had really changed. He was simply home, surrounded by his family, safe in the heart of the pit.

He closed his eyes, listening to the soft, steady breathing of the six women he loved, and fell into the most peaceful sleep of his life.
