# Part 2: The Cosmic Coincidence

The Latin 101 class ended. The "Group of 8" gathered their things, buzzing with the excitement of the reunion. As they filed out of the small classroom, the group began to split off.

"Alright, J, we'll see you at lunch," Mark said, giving him a supportive nod as he and Hannah headed toward the engineering quad.

It was just Jordan, Aubrie, and Jessica left in the hallway. Jordan turned to Jessica, his heart full but already bracing for the goodbye. "It was so good to see you, Jess. I still can't believe it. Which way are you heading?"

"Oh," Jessica said, pointing down the main walkway. "I have to get to the science complex. My next class is Astrophysics."

Jordan and Aubrie both stopped, staring at her. "No way!" Jordan said, his voice full of excitement. "Us too! You should walk with us".

Jessica immediately looked hesitant, her gaze flickering to Aubrie. She was acutely aware that this was his *wife*. "Are you sure?" she asked, her voice quiet. "I don't want to intrude on you two."

Aubrie, seeing the hesitation, immediately stepped in, her smile warm and genuine. "Intrude? Don't be silly. Of course you're walking with us". She looped her arm through Jessica's. "What professor do you have?"

"Dr. Alistair, at 10:30," Jessica replied.

Jordan and Aubrie stopped again, this time looking at each other in shared, stunned delight. "Us too!" Jordan nearly shouted. "And the Tuesday morning lab section?"

"Yes!" Jessica said, finally letting herself believe the coincidence. "I can't believe it!"

"Then it's settled," Aubrie said, her voice full of a happy finality as she started walking, pulling Jessica along with her. "You're sitting with us".

Jordan wheeled his chair forward, placing himself between Aubrie and Jessica. He was so excited. He couldn't believe it. The friend he had lost, the one his mother had stolen from him, was back. And now, by some miracle, she was in not only his Latin class, but also his Astrophysics class. His past and his present had just collided, and his world was, once again, made whole.
