# Part 3: An Unexpected Free Block

The trio made their way to the science complex, arriving at the large lecture hall for their 10:30 AM Astrophysics class. But when they got to the door, they saw a single piece of paper taped to the glass.

"Oh, look," Aubrie said, leaning in to read it. "Class is canceled. The professor is sick".

"Huh," Jordan said, a little disappointed, but mostly relieved. "Well, that gives us a 50-minute free block". He turned to Jessica, his face bright. "We can actually talk. We should go grab a drink at Newman Hall."

Jessica's smile immediately faltered. She glanced nervously at Aubrie, then back at Jordan. "Oh, I shouldn't... I mean, you two are married. I don't want to intrude on your time".

Aubrie just laughed, a warm, genuine sound that dismissed the very idea. "Intrude? Jessica, you're the first real friend he ever had. You're not intruding, you're catching up. We insist. We're not letting you go".

Jessica still looked hesitant, her high school memories of social rules and boundaries making her anxious. Aubrie, seeing this, decided to be more direct. She gently took Jessica's hand. Then she took Jordan's hand. She placed Jessica's hand into Jordan's and gently, deliberately, laced their fingers together.

"See?" Aubrie said, her voice full of bliss as she looked at their joined hands. "No jealousy. He's your friend. Hold his hand".

Jessica was stunned. She was holding a married man's hand, and his *wife* was smiling at her. It was the most radical, unconditional act of trust she had ever witnessed.

Jordan's grip tightened on her hand, a grounding, familiar connection. He needed to tell her. "Jess," he said, using the old nickname that only he was allowed to use. "So much has happened. I... I never got to thank you. My mother..."

At the mention of his mother, Jessica flinched, the memory still sharp and painful. She **remembers this** all too well. She wasn't just told to stay away; Aubrie Sinclair had found her home phone number. Jessica remembered the rude and nasty voice on the line, the sound of her own mother's shocked silence as Aubrie Sinclair went on a tirade, telling them that Jessica was a manipulative predator and was to have nothing to do with Aubrey ever again.

Seeing the fear flash in Jessica's eyes, Jordan's grip tightened. "It's okay, Jess. She's... she's not..."

"She's gone," Aubrie Kelly said, her voice calm and absolute. She sat on Jessica's other side, taking her free hand. "Jessica, I need you to hear this. **Jordan has cut his mother completely out of his life**".

Aubrie proceeded to give her the short, factual version of the war they had all survived: the failed Fall Break confrontation, the official no-contact orders filed with the university and the police, and the final, humiliating court battle where a judge had dismissed his mother's guardianship case with prejudice.

"She has no power," Aubrie finished. "She can't find him, and she can't hurt him. Not ever again. **You are absolutely welcomed back into Jordan’s life**".

Jessica looked from Aubrie's fierce, protective face to Jordan's, who was nodding, his eyes full of relief. The fear that she had carried for four years—the fear of that horrible woman—finally began to dissolve.

"Now," Jordan said, a quiet pride in his voice. "About that 4.0 GPA...".

He continued his story, telling her about the Homecoming Humiliation, and then his voice dropped. "But... it hasn't all been easy. Last year... Jess, I had a heart attack".

Jessica's head snapped toward him, her eyes wide with terror. "Wait, what? A heart attack? Jordan, you're only 20! What do you mean, a heart attack?".

Aubrie, sitting on Jessica's other side, took her hand. "It's true," she said softly, and she began to fill in the details of the Homecoming Humiliation, the failed balloon angioplasty, and the open-heart CABG surgery.

Jordan's grip tightened on Jessica's hand as his wife spoke, the memory of the trauma still fresh. Jessica was reeling, trying to process everything: his new life, his marriage, his near-death, and the impossible, wonderful fact that she was holding his hand while his wife, Aubrie Kelly, looked on with nothing but love. This was a family unlike any she had ever known, and she knew, with a sudden, dawning clarity, that she wanted to be a part of it.
