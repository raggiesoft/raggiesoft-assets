# Part 8: The Sofa Assumption

After dinner, the Group of 8 and Jessica migrated down to the basement, their designated territory. Mark and Hannah settled onto one of the queen beds to watch a movie. The others—Aubrie, Madison, Zoe, Lauren, and Sarah—piled onto the massive Alaskan King bed with Jordan, a familiar, comfortable "puppy pile."

Jessica stood awkwardly by the stairs, her overnight bag at her feet. She was an island in their ocean of intimacy. She assumed, logically, that she would be sleeping on the sectional sofa or in a guest room.

Megan, sensing her unease, got up from the table where she'd been working and sat with her on the sofa. "Hey," Megan said softly. "It's... a lot, isn't it?"

"I just... I don't understand," Jessica whispered, looking at the king bed where Jordan was cuddled between his wife, Aubrie, and Madison. "How is she... okay with this?"

"Because she means it," Megan said. "It took me a long time to get it, too. I'm 27, and my brain just about broke trying to understand. But the pact is real. The no jealousy rule is real. They just... love him. And they love each other. They're not just a couple; they're a system."
