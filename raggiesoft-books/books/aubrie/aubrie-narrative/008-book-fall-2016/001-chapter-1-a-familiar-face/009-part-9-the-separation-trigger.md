# Part 9: The Separation Trigger

As the hour grew later, Jessica knew it was time. She stood up, her stomach twisting. "Well," she said, her voice small. "I should probably figure out the sofa..."

The word sofa cut through Jordan's relaxed state. He had been fine, surrounded by his pact, knowing Jessica was in the room. But sofa meant separate. It meant other." It meant she wasn't *with* him.

His breath hitched. He sat up, his eyes wide with a familiar, rising panic. He looked at Jessica, and his mind was no longer in the safe basement; it was back on the bus platform, watching her walk away. He was terrified of waking up and finding her gone, a repeat of the trauma his mother had inflicted.

"No," he said, his voice tight. "You... you're not leaving, are you?"

"I'm just going to the sofa, Jordan..."

"No!" he said, his voice cracking. He started to rock, the low hum of his anxiety returning. "You can't. Please. Don't leave."
