# Part 11: The Rules of the Pile

The relief that washed over Jordan was visible; the rocking stopped instantly. He let out a long, shaky breath.

As Jessica hesitantly moved toward the massive Alaskan King bed, Hannah stepped in.

"Okay," Hannah said, her "mom friend" voice gentle but firm. "Welcome to the family, Jessica. Now, the rules."

Jessica tensed again. "Rules?"

"Just one, really," Hannah said, "and it's for his safety. You know he had open-heart surgery, right?" Jessica nodded.

"His sternum is surgically repaired," Hannah explained. "It's healed, but it's a lifelong change. That means the puppy pile is calm. No sudden weight on his chest, no jostling, and no pulling on his arms or chest. We're here to cuddle and keep him safe, not to be chaotic. We just... we have to be careful."

Jessica nodded, understanding. This wasn't just an invitation; it was an act of shared care.

She kicked off her shoes and climbed onto the massive bed. The six other women of the pact made room for her, and Jordan, with a look of profound, tearful gratitude, pulled her into the pile. She was home.
