# Part 15: The First Hug

It was time for bed. The Group of 8 had finished their evening rituals, and the quiet, calm energy of the basement was a healing balm after the chaos of Jessica's dorm room. Mark and Hannah, seeing the emotional night ahead, gave Jordan and the women supportive hugs before heading upstairs to their own room.

This left the seven women of the pact in the main living area. Aubrie, Madison, Zoe, Lauren, and Sarah all began their familiar puppy pile ritual, climbing onto the massive Alaskan King bed.

Jessica, however, stood awkwardly by the sofa, her overnight bag clutched in her hands. She was quietly sobbing, her body shaking as she processed the day. She was completely overwhelmed. Her mind was stuck on the scene from her dorm. It was not just the violation of her space; it was the betrayal. She had witnessed affairs in progress. She had seen two girls casually cheating on their boyfriends. Her old social programming was screaming at her: *You do not climb into a bed with another woman's husband. That is what my disgusting roommates did. That is an affair.*

Aubrie Kelly paused and looked over, her expression soft. "Jess? You're not sleeping on the sofa. Come on."

Jessica flinched, her sob catching in her throat. "Oh, no, I... I can't. That's... that's your bed. With... with your husband. I can't."

Her autistic mind was in a deadlock. Her old social programming was clear: You do not climb into a bed with another woman's husband. That is what my disgusting roommates did. That is an affair.

Aubrie, whose own autistic mind understood the need for clear, logical rules, knew she had to provide an explicit override. She slid off the bed and walked over to Jessica, leading her to the bed. She took her by the hands.

"Hey. Look at me," Aubrie said, her voice kind but firm. "This is not Forge Hall. You didn't just see a betrayal. You saw a violation *without* consent or respect. This," she gestured to the king bed, "is our family. It is built *on* consent and respect."

She began to walk Jessica toward the bed. "I am Jordan's wife," she continued, "and I am giving you permission".

Jessica tensed, her mind still fighting it.

Aubrie understood. She knew this would take time and she would need to be reminded a few times. "He needs his friends, Jess. He needs *you*. He had a meltdown yesterday because he was terrified of losing you again. We are *not* letting that happen."

She sat Jessica on the edge of the bed, then sat next to her, between her and Jordan. She took Jordan's hand, then took Jessica's hand, placing them near each other on the blanket. "This is okay," Aubrie said. "This is safe. This is what we do."

Jordan, sensing her hesitation, just gave her a small, hopeful smile. He didn't push, he just waited.

Jessica's mind was lagging, trying to process the new data. *The wife is encouraging this. The wife is sitting right here. This is not a violation. This is a new rule.* It was the first step. For tonight, she just sat on the edge of the bed, her hand near his, and let herself be part of the serene calmness until she finally, nervously, agreed to lie down on the very edge of the massive mattress.

Aubrie knew this reprogramming would need to be reminded a few times. She would have to lead this exercise every night, patiently demonstrating that in this family, it's perfectly acceptable to cuddle the man they all loved.

Jessica's social script was slowly rewritten; she began to accept that cuddling with a married man was okay, in this specific case with Jordan. The other women of the pact, seeing her hesitation, slowly gathered onto the bed, gently introducing Jessica to the concept of the puppy pile. They were all careful of Jordan's surgically repaired sternum, which was healed but still a lifelong consideration.

Finally, Jessica embraced Jordan, cuddling with him properly. It was the hug she had been wanting to give him since he was 16 years old, four long years in the making.

The physical contact, the reality of finally holding him, broke the last of her composure. She was sobbing quietly as she clung to him, not from sadness, but from a profound, overwhelming sense of relief.

Jordan just held her, his own tears running silently into her hair. "I missed you so much, Jess," he whispered.

"I missed you, too," she cried.

The rest of the women of the pact were in the bed, making sure that Jessica was okay. Aubrie, his wife, settled in on Jordan's other side, placing her hand on his arm. Madison, Megan, and the others found their spots, creating the full, calm, and protective puppy pile. They were a fortress of love, and Jessica was finally, truly, safe inside.
