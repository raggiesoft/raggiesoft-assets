# Part 3: The Roll Call

The massive floor of The Crucible—the football stadium—was transformed. Thousands of chairs were arranged in neat rows on the turf where the football team played (and occasionally lost). The stands were packed with families.

Jordan wheeled himself down the center aisle, surrounded by his fortress. The Group of 8 sat together, a block of maroon robes.

When the moment came for the College of Science, the line began to move. Jordan rolled up the ramp to the stage. He handed his card to the reader, a professor who smiled warmly at him.

The professor leaned into the microphone. His voice boomed across the stadium, echoing off the concrete walls where Jordan had once fled in terror.

"JORDAN SHAWN KELLY!"

The cheer that went up from Section 104 was deafening. It wasn't just polite applause. It was a roar. Daniel, Laura, Megan, Bob, Emily, Diane, David, and Sarah’s dad were all on their feet, screaming. On the field, the rest of the pact—Aubrie, Madison, Zoe, Lauren, Sarah, Hannah, and Mark—were cheering so loud they drowned out the announcer for a solid ten seconds.

Jordan wheeled across the stage. He shook the Dean’s hand. He took the diploma cover. He looked out at the sea of faces. He thought of the woman in Hampton Roads who was currently staring at a rejection letter from the IRS, insisting that "Aubrey" was hers.

He’s not, Jordan thought, gripping the leather folder. He’s gone. I’m right here.
