# Part 1: The Reveal

The construction on the U.S. 11 compound was finally complete. The Group of 8 (plus Jessica and Megan) stood in the threshold of the massive basement master suite.

The room was vast, bathed in light from the high clerestory windows that protected their privacy. But the floor was not flat. In the center of the room, occupying a massive footprint, was a sunken architectural feature.

"We couldn't buy a bed big enough," Daniel Brooks explained, wiping sawdust from his hands. "And we knew an Alaskan King—which is 9 feet by 9 feet—might be tight for eight people. So, we built something custom. We call it The Pit."

"It's huge," Jessica breathed.

Zoe stepped forward with her tablet. "I calculated the dimensions based on maximum occupancy and height variables. Specifically, the 'Emily Factor.' Since she measured 5'10" at age 15, we projected her final height could exceed 6 feet. Therefore, a standard 6.5-foot mattress was insufficient. The sleeping surface is 10 feet long to accommodate all potential heights."
