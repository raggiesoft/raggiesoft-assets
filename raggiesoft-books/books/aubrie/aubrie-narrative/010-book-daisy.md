# Book: Daisy

## Chapter 3: The Summer Solstice

### Part 1: The Diversion (June 19, 2020)

The call came late on the evening of June 19th. Hannah was in early labor, pacing the living room of the Compound. Mark was on the phone with the local hospital in Blacksburg, his face tightening with stress.

"Diversion?" Mark repeated, his voice rising slightly. "What do you mean you're on diversion?"

He listened for a moment, then hung up, turning to the anxious group gathered in the kitchen. "The maternity ward at Montgomery Regional is full. They’re at capacity and redirecting all incoming labor patients."

"Where do we go?" Hannah asked, gripping the back of a chair as a contraction hit.

"Roanoke," Daniel Brooks said immediately, grabbing the car keys. "Star City General."

Jordan flinched. The name **Star City General** sent a shiver through him. That was where he had spent the longest, most terrifying nights of his life recovering from his open-heart surgery. It was a place of trauma, monitors, and sternal precautions.

But he looked at Hannah, who was scared and in pain. "It’s a good hospital," Jordan said, his voice steadying. "They saved my life. They’ll take care of her."

### Part 2: The Parking Lot Vigil

The convoy sped up I-81, the highway empty in the deep of the night. When they arrived at Star City General, the reality of the pandemic hit hard.

"One support person only," the nurse at the entrance said firmly, her face obscured by a mask and shield. "Just the father."

Mark hugged Hannah tight. "I've got you." He turned to the family—Daniel, Laura, Jordan, Aubrie, and the rest of the pact—who were standing by the sliding doors. "I'll text you. Every five minutes."

Mark and Hannah disappeared inside. The rest of the family walked back to the parking lot. They couldn't come in, but they wouldn't leave.

"We stay," Aubrie said, helping Jordan settle comfortably in the passenger seat of the minivan. "We wait right here."

For hours, the Group of 17 (minus Mark and Hannah) held a vigil in the hospital parking garage. They sat in their cars with the windows rolled down, passing phones back and forth, waiting for updates.

For Jordan, sitting in the shadow of the building where he had once laid helpless, the waiting was a strange reversal. **Five years ago**, they had waited for him in this very building while he fought for his life in the CVICU. Now, they were waiting for a new life to begin.

### Part 3: The Arrival (June 20, 2020)

The night slowly turned to gray, then to the soft pink of dawn. It was the morning of the Summer Solstice, the longest day of the year.

At **5:43 AM**, just as the sun crested over the Blue Ridge Mountains, Mark’s text came through to the group chat.

**Mark:** *She's here. Daisy May Anderson. 7lbs 4oz. Everyone is healthy.*

A cheer erupted in the parking garage, echoing off the concrete walls. Tears streamed down Jordan’s face. The hospital that had been a place of fear was now, forever, the place where his niece was born.

Minutes later, Mark initiated a video call. The family huddled around Daniel’s phone.

There was Hannah, exhausted but glowing in the hospital bed, holding a tiny, pink bundle wrapped in a standard hospital blanket.

"Hi, everyone," Hannah whispered, her voice raspy. "Meet Daisy."

Jordan looked at the screen, at the tiny face of the next generation. "Hi, Daisy," he whispered. "I'm Uncle Jordan."

### Part 4: The Solstice Protocol

A few days later, Mark and Hannah brought Daisy home to the Compound. The family gathered on the back patio, keeping a safe distance at first due to the pandemic protocols, just watching the baby sleep in her bassinet.

"June 20th," Zoe mused, checking the date. "She really waited for the Solstice."

"The day with the most light," Aubrie said, smiling at Jordan. "It fits. After everything... the darkness of the surgery, the pandemic... she came with the light."

Jordan looked at his wives—Aubrie, Madison, Zoe, Lauren, Sarah, Megan, and Jessica. He looked at the baby. An idea formed—a logical, beautiful system for their future.

"We should aim for this," Jordan said. "When we start... for us. We should try to have them on the Solstice. Like Daisy."

Zoe’s mind ran the calculations immediately. "To achieve a mid-June birth date, conception must occur within a specific window in late September or early October of the previous year. It allows for efficient batching of resources and shared birthday celebrations."

"Super Birthdays," Madison grinned. "One massive party every few years. All the cousins together."

"The Solstice Protocol," Sarah agreed.

It was decided. The **Procreation Pact** now had a schedule. They would wait, helping raise Daisy and surviving the pandemic, and then, they would aim for the **Solstice of 2024** to begin the first wave of the Kelly children.
