# Part 1: The Diversion (June 19, 2020)

The call came late on the evening of June 19th. Hannah was in early labor, pacing the living room of the Compound. Mark was on the phone with the local hospital in Blacksburg, his face tightening with stress.

"Diversion?" Mark repeated, his voice rising slightly. "What do you mean you're on diversion?"

He listened for a moment, then hung up, turning to the anxious group gathered in the kitchen. "The maternity ward at Montgomery Regional is full. They’re at capacity and redirecting all incoming labor patients."

"Where do we go?" Hannah asked, gripping the back of a chair as a contraction hit.

"Roanoke," Daniel Brooks said immediately, grabbing the car keys. "Star City General."

Jordan flinched. The name **Star City General** sent a shiver through him. That was where he had spent the longest, most terrifying nights of his life recovering from his open-heart surgery. It was a place of trauma, monitors, and sternal precautions.

But he looked at Hannah, who was scared and in pain. "It’s a good hospital," Jordan said, his voice steadying. "They saved my life. They’ll take care of her."
