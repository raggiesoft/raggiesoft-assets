# Part 2: The Parking Lot Vigil

The convoy sped up I-81, the highway empty in the deep of the night. When they arrived at Star City General, the reality of the pandemic hit hard.

"One support person only," the nurse at the entrance said firmly, her face obscured by a mask and shield. "Just the father."

Mark hugged Hannah tight. "I've got you." He turned to the family—Daniel, Laura, Jordan, Aubrie, and the rest of the pact—who were standing by the sliding doors. "I'll text you. Every five minutes."

Mark and Hannah disappeared inside. The rest of the family walked back to the parking lot. They couldn't come in, but they wouldn't leave.

"We stay," Aubrie said, helping Jordan settle comfortably in the passenger seat of the minivan. "We wait right here."

For hours, the Group of 17 (minus Mark and Hannah) held a vigil in the hospital parking garage. They sat in their cars with the windows rolled down, passing phones back and forth, waiting for updates.

For Jordan, sitting in the shadow of the building where he had once laid helpless, the waiting was a strange reversal. **Five years ago**, they had waited for him in this very building while he fought for his life in the CVICU. Now, they were waiting for a new life to begin.
