# Part 4: Making It Official

It was Madison who brought up the topic, her tone gentle and curious. "So, Jordan," she began, "back there when you were talking to Aubrie, you said you wanted to go by Jordan. Is that... like, for real? Is that what you want all of us to call you?"

Jordan looked at Aubrie, who gave him a small, encouraging smile. He felt a surge of confidence. "Yeah," he said, his voice firmer than it had been all day. "It is. I've always hated 'Aubrey.' I want to be Jordan here." He glanced at the door to their room. "I just wish the card on the door didn't..."

"We can ask Henry to change it," Aubrie said immediately, her support unwavering. "I bet he can print a new one. Let's go see him right now."

A few minutes later, Jordan, Aubrie, and Madison were sitting in the small, functional Resident Advisor office down the hall. Henry sat at his desk, and he motioned for the three of them to take the chairs opposite him. As Jordan wheeled up next to Aubrie, he nervously reached out and took her hand under the table. She responded instantly, her fingers lacing through his, holding his hand firmly in her lap. It was the first time they had held hands in front of anyone else, a quiet, public declaration of their budding connection.

"Hi, Henry," Jordan began, feeling braver with Aubrie's hand in his. "Um... so the name on my door card is 'Aubrey Jordan Fuller.' But I go by my middle name, Jordan. I was wondering if it would be possible to get a new card that just says 'Jordan Fuller'?"

Henry smiled warmly. "Yeah, absolutely, man. Not a problem at all. I can print you a new door card right now." He started typing. "But we should do one better," he continued. "The door card is just for the hallway. You'll want all your professors and the university staff to know. Let me show you something."

He turned his monitor toward them and guided Jordan through the CPI student portal to the "Personal Information" section. He pointed to a field. "Right here. It says 'Preferred Name.' If you type 'Jordan' into this field, that's the name that will show up on all the official class rosters for your instructors. It'll also be the name that shows up for advisors, the library, everywhere."

He then leaned forward, offering some practical advice. "Now, the system can take a day or so to update, and some professors print their rosters on day one and never look again. The best thing you can do is send a quick, polite email to each of your instructors tonight, before classes start tomorrow. Just introduce yourself and let them know you go by Jordan."

"Wow," Jordan said, feeling a sense of empowerment he'd never known. "Okay. Thank you, Henry. So much."

"No problem at all," Henry said, handing him the freshly printed door card that read, simply, Jordan Fuller.

They left the office and headed back to their suite, Jordan and Aubrie still holding hands. He had a mission for the evening: to email his professors and formally introduce himself to the world, for the very first time, as Jordan.

"I'll walk back with you guys and swap that card out right now," Henry offered, grabbing the small, freshly printed name card from his desk.

The three of them walked with him down the short hall back to Room 214, where the other suitemates were now talking quietly in the adjoining room. Henry used a small tool to pop open the clear plastic holder on the door frame. He slid out the old card, with the typed name Aubrey Jordan Fuller, and handed it to Jordan as a small souvenir. Then, he slid in the new one.

The effect was immediate and powerful. There, on the door to his new home, were the words: Jordan Fuller.

Jordan stared at it, a deep sense of satisfaction washing over him. It was real.

"Now, just so you know," Henry said, tapping his own plastic CPI ID card that hung from a lanyard around his neck. "This doesn't change your official university ID. I can change the door card because it's just for display, but the main CPI ID has to match the legal name from your birth certificate for security and official records."

He continued, wanting to be clear. "So your ID card will still say 'Aubrey Jordan Fuller' next to your photo and student number. But don't worry. Because you updated your preferred name in the portal, whenever you swipe your card—at the dining hall, the library, anywhere—the name that will pop up on the staff's screen will be 'Jordan.' It's just the physical card itself that can't be changed without a legal court order."

Jordan nodded, understanding. He touched the wallet in his pocket where his own new ID was sitting. A small, new flicker of dissatisfaction was there, the knowledge that "Aubrey" would still follow him around on that piece of plastic. But for now, this was a huge victory.

"Thank you, Henry," Aubrie said sincerely. "For everything."

"No problem at all," Henry said with a final, warm smile. "Enjoy the rest of your night. Welcome to Bennett Hall, Jordan." He gave a small wave and headed back down the hall to his office.

Jordan, Aubrie, and Madison stood there for a moment longer, just looking at the door. The first official piece of his new life was finally in place.
