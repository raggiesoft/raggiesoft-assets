# Part 2: Worlds Aligned

The university cafeteria on a Sunday morning was a low hum of activity. As the six of them found a large table by a window, the easy chatter of the group resumed. But a subtle shift was occurring. While Madison, Zoe, Lauren, and Hannah discussed class schedules and debated the merits of different coffee creamers, a quiet, powerful gravity began to pull Jordan and Aubrie into their own orbit.

It started, as it always did with them, with the stars.

"I was looking over the syllabus for Astronomy 101 again last night," Aubrie began, her voice a little softer now that she was speaking just to him. "Dr. Alistair is starting with the life cycle of stars. I can't wait to get into nebula formation".

Jordan’s small smile faded. He flinched, his gaze dropping to the table. "Astrophysics..." he mumbled, the word tasting like failure. "I... I'm really worried about that class. My mom... she just... she signed me up for it. I don't know anything about it. The math..."

He trailed off, the full weight of his mother's trap settling on him. He had been set up to fail.

Aubrie looked at him, her expression shifting from simple excitement to a deep, immediate understanding. She saw his fear. "Hey," she said, leaning forward, her voice quiet but certain. "It's okay. People make it sound harder than it is. It's not just math, Jordan. It's... it's about the patterns."

She saw his gaze flicker up, and she pressed on, speaking their shared, rare language. "People are confusing, right? The rules are always changing". "But the stars... the laws of physics, the way a star collapses into a white dwarf... it’s a process. It has an order. You can understand it".

This was it. This was the core of their connection. For them, being autistic meant finding solace in systems, in the deep, comforting logic of things that made sense. She was handing him a lifeline.

"Look," she said, her voice full of a new promise. "It's my favorite subject. I'm not going to let you fail. We're in the same class, and the same lab. We'll sit together. We'll study together. I'll be right there with you. I promise."

Jordan looked up at her, his expression full of a fragile, dawning hope. His mother had pushed him into an insurmountable maze, and this girl had just handed him a map and a flashlight. *She'll be right there with me.* This was the light at the end of the tunnel. He saw in her a passion he didn't have, but a logic he understood. She saw in him a mind that worked just like hers, one that *could* understand, if just given the chance.

Across the table, Madison watched them, a knowing, affectionate smile playing on her lips. She nudged Zoe gently. "Look at them," she whispered. "They're in their own little nebula over there".

Zoe observed them, her analytical mind cataloging the data. The lowered voices, the intense eye contact, the way he leaned into her space as she spoke. "A strong neuro-social bond is forming," she stated, her clinical observation barely masking her own complex feelings. "His anxiety is visibly decreasing. It's a predictable and positive outcome".

Lauren, overhearing, just smiled. "It's sweet".

The bubble was finally, gently pierced when Madison decided to tease. "You two are going to be insufferable in that class, you know that? The professor is going to ask a question and you'll both just start speaking fluent astrophysics to each other".

Aubrie’s head snapped up, a deep blush coloring her cheeks as she realized how engrossed they had been. "Oh! No, we were just… talking about the syllabus".

"Mmhmm, the 'syllabus'," Madison said with a wink.

Aubrie wasn't ready to admit the depth of what she was feeling. It was too new, too potent. "He's just... easy to talk to," she deflected, busying herself with her orange juice.

After breakfast, as they walked across the main quad to locate their classroom buildings for the next day, Aubrie naturally fell into place beside Jordan's wheelchair. The crisp autumn air was a refreshing change from the stuffy cafeteria.

"You really... like it that much?" Jordan asked quietly, his voice full of a genuine curiosity. "The stars?"

Aubrie didn't have to think. "I do," she said, looking up at the blue sky. "When you look at the stars, I feel small," she said. "But in a good way. Like all the things that are hard down here... they don't matter as much, because we're all just spinning around on a tiny rock in this huge, incredible place".

He looked at her, his expression full of wonder. She wasn't just offering to help him pass; she was offering him a sanctuary. She was showing him the beauty his mother had tried to use as a weapon.

"I... I'd like to feel that way," he said.

As he spoke, his hand, which had been resting on the arm of his chair, brushed against hers as she walked. The contact was brief, accidental, but it sent a jolt of electricity through them both. They pulled their hands away quickly, a shared, shy smile passing between them.

In that look, a universe of unspoken things was communicated. It was a silent acknowledgment that they were two strangers from different worlds who had, against all odds, found they were made of the same stardust.
