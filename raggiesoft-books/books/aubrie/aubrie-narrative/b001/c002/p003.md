# Part 3: A Different Kind of Constellation

They returned to the suite after their campus walk, the air still buzzing with the energy of the day. Jordan, feeling the pleasant exhaustion of social and physical activity, retreated to his room to quietly organize his textbooks, leaving the five young women to congregate in Room 216. They collapsed onto the various beds, a comfortable silence settling over them for a moment.

It was Madison who broke it, her voice gentle but direct as she looked at Aubrie. "Okay. Teasing aside. Are we going to talk about the massive gravitational pull happening between you and Jordan, or just let it become a silent supernova?"

Aubrie, who was sitting on the floor leaning against her bunk, hugged her knees to her chest. The blush that rose to her cheeks was faint this time, replaced by a look of deep, serious contemplation. She took a breath, and instead of deflecting, she finally let the truth surface.

"I am," she said, her voice barely above a whisper, yet it filled the room. "I'm completely gone for him. More than I thought was possible this quickly." She looked up, her gaze sweeping over her friends. "It feels... important. Like he's a puzzle piece I didn't know I was missing. But..." She paused, choosing her next words carefully. "I don't think he's just *my* puzzle piece."

Her eyes settled on Madison, then on Zoe. "I see it with you guys, too. I see the way you look at him. And... the strange thing is, it doesn't make me feel angry. It doesn't make me feel jealous."

"It doesn't?" Zoe asked, her voice laced with genuine surprise. Her own logical mind had been trying to categorize her feelings, and jealousy seemed like a predictable, if undesirable, variable.

"No," Aubrie affirmed, shaking her head. "Jealousy feels selfish. And nothing about what we're building here feels selfish. It's the opposite of that." She leaned forward, her passion making her words resonate with a powerful clarity. "Think about him. He's spent his entire life being made to feel like a burden. How could we possibly turn around and make him the center of a bunch of drama or competition? It would just be another way of making him feel like a problem to be solved."

Lauren, who was sharing the single bed with Hannah, nodded thoughtfully. "She has a point. I mean, Hannah and I both have boyfriends, and we love them, but the way we all feel about Jordan is... different. It’s not romantic for us, it’s… protective."

"Exactly," Hannah agreed, grateful Lauren had voiced it perfectly. "It's like he's our family. You don't get jealous over family, you just want them to be happy and safe."

"Right!" Aubrie said, seizing on their words. "So if he's happy talking to Madison, or feels safe with Zoe, or cared for by me... that's all that matters. His happiness is the goal. I don't need to hog him to be part of that. In fact, it feels better not to."

Madison looked at Aubrie, her expression full of a newfound respect. "That's... incredibly mature, Aubrie. And you're right. His happiness comes first. Period."

Zoe nodded slowly, her internal conflict settling into a new, logical framework. "An emotional support system is stronger when it's a network rather than a single point of contact. A cooperative dynamic minimizes conflict and maximizes the subject's well-being." It was the most Zoe way imaginable of saying, *I agree, and I love the idea.*

A quiet, powerful understanding bloomed in the room. It wasn't a formal pact, not yet. But it was a silent agreement, a shared philosophy. They were not rivals for Jordan's affection. They were a constellation, each a different point of light, and their collective purpose was to illuminate his world. Through the open doorway, they could see him in his room, carefully placing a label on a binder, completely unaware that the five women next door had just fundamentally defined the kind, open-hearted, and extraordinary terms of his new life.
