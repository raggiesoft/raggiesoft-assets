# Part 2: The Broken Elevator

It was noon. Jordan wheeled himself toward Newman Commons, his stomach tight with a mix of hunger and anxiety. This was his first time navigating to a dining hall by himself. He had been there with the group, but this felt different, a small test of the independence he wasn't sure he had.

Newman Commons, like Bennett Hall, was a massive brick building built into one of the campus's notorious hills, with entrances on different levels. He found an accessible entrance on the lower level, near the delivery bays, but he saw no clear path to the main dining area above. He was lost.

His mPhone buzzed in his pocket. It was a text from Aubrie.

Aubrie: Hey! I’m here. Just inside the main entrance, by the stairs. Waiting for you! :)

Jordan’s heart sank. He was at the *delivery* entrance, not the one they had used as a group. He was clearly in the wrong place. He texted her back, his hands fumbling slightly.

Jordan: I think I'm at a different door. The lower level? I'm looking for the elevator but I don't see one.

He wheeled back and forth on the concrete patio, feeling a small jolt of panic. A cafeteria worker, pushing a large cart of supplies, saw him looking lost.

"You looking for the way in, honey?" she asked kindly.

"Yes," Jordan said, relieved. "I'm supposed to meet my friend, but I can't find the elevator."

The worker sighed, her face full of sympathy. "Ah, yeah, that's because the main one's broken. Has been all morning". "Hang on, let me get my manager. He can take you up the freight elevator".

A moment later, a man in a polo shirt with a manager's name tag came out. "Hi there," he said. "Sorry about our elevator. Let's get you the scenic route".

He badged them through a heavy door marked "Staff Only." Jordan followed him into a maze of loud, steamy kitchens. The clanging of pots, the hiss of industrial dishwashers, and the shouting of the line cooks was a sensory assault. The manager led him to a large, metal freight elevator and swiped a key. They rode up, and when the heavy doors opened again, Jordan found himself emerging from an unmarked employee door, right into the main, chaotic dining area.

He saw her immediately. Aubrie was standing right where she said she'd be, looking anxiously toward the entrance.

Her face lit up when she saw him, but her smile faltered as she saw *his* expression.

Jordan looked at her, and the anxiety from the broken elevator, the sensory overload of the kitchen, and the sheer frustration of being lost all combined into a deeper, more profound terror. *What if this isn't real?* The brief, terrifying moment of being lost, of being unable to find her, had felt too much like his old life. He was terrified that this was all a dream, a cruel trick, and that he would wake up any second, back in his mother's apartment, forever at her mercy.

Aubrie saw the raw fright on his face. She didn't know the full cause, only that her friend was scared. She didn't hesitate. She rushed to him, closing the distance, and pulled him into a very, very meaningful hug, holding him tightly, her arms a firm, grounding anchor, right there in the middle of the dining hall.

"Hey," she whispered, her voice fierce. "You're okay. You found me. You're safe."

She felt him trembling, and her heart ached. She realized something was deeply scaring him, more than just a broken elevator. She wished, with a sudden, powerful longing, that she could just stay with him, be his shield for the rest of the day. But she knew she couldn't. They both had their respective afternoon classes.

"Come on," she said, finally pulling back but keeping her hand on his shoulder. "Let's go get lunch. Together".
