# Part 4: Tuesday - An Unexpected Dimension

On Tuesday, the group discovered another strange twist of fate. Madison was the first to arrive for her 10:00 AM Health 201 class. A moment later, Lauren and Hannah walked in.

"No way," Lauren said, laughing. "You're in this class too?"

"It fulfilled the physical education requirement and fit my schedule," Zoe stated, walking up behind them as if it were the most logical thing in the world.

Just as the five of them were marveling at the coincidence, Aubrie and Jordan came through the door. The room erupted in quiet, disbelieving laughter. All six of them, from different majors and backgrounds, had independently signed up for the same two-credit elective: "HSC 201: Dimensions of Human Sexuality."

The professor began by passing out the syllabus. As the suitemates scanned the schedule of topics, they saw the expected units on nutrition, substance abuse, and mental health. Then, their eyes fell on the mid-semester schedule: *Week 7: The Male Reproductive System (Textbook, Ch. 5)* *Week 8: The Female Reproductive System (Textbook, Ch. 6)*

Madison shot a playful, wide-eyed look at Lauren, who was trying very hard not to giggle. But Jordan wasn't laughing. He was staring at the words with an expression of pure, unadulterated curiosity.

His entire life, this information had been forbidden territory. Every year in public school, his mother had diligently checked the box on the permission slip, opting him out of the Family Life and Sexual Education units. She called the topics "inappropriate for him." While his classmates went to health class, he was sent to the library to read. But here it was, listed plainly in a college syllabus. It wasn't inappropriate; it was just biology. It was just another system, like the solar system. And for the first time in his life, no one was going to send him to the library. A quiet, profound sense of freedom settled over him.
