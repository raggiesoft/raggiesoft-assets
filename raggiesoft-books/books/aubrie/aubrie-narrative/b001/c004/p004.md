# Part 4: Time, Love, and Tenderness

Lauren didn't know how long she had been crying, only that she was enveloped in the safest, most secure embrace she had ever known. After she had collapsed into his arms, Jordan had carefully maneuvered her to sit on the edge of his bed while he remained beside her in his chair, never once letting go. His arms were a firm, unwavering circle around her, and she clung to him, desperately needing the solid, physical proof that she was not alone. The other four women watched from their spots around the room, a silent, supportive vigil.

First, he offered her time. He held her tightly through the storm of her sobs, and then through the quiet, shuddering aftermath. He didn't rush her. He didn't ask questions. He simply let her exist within his embrace, giving her the time and space to let the first wave of pain wash over her without judgment. His steady presence was a silent promise that he would wait with her in the darkness for as long as it took.

From her bunk, Aubrie watched them, her heart aching with a beautiful, profound love. She saw the boy she adored holding their heartbroken friend, and she felt no jealousy, only a deep sense of rightness. This was the living embodiment of their pact. This was care.

Then, he offered her tenderness. While still holding her firmly with one arm, Jordan noticed her hands were clenched into fists in her lap, her body still rigid with humiliation and anger. With his free hand, he gently reached out and uncurled her fingers, his touch feather-light and soothing. He then spotted the box from Tom on the floor beside her. Without breaking the embrace, he nudged it with his foot, pushing it under the bed and out of sight. He was tenderly removing the sources of her pain, both internal and external.

Finally, as her breathing began to even out against his chest, he offered her love. Not the selfish, conditional love she had just lost, but a pure, unconditional care.

"I feel so pathetic," she mumbled into his shoulder. "I can't believe I let him talk to me like that."

"You're not pathetic," Jordan whispered into her hair, his voice soft but certain. "You just trusted someone. Trusting people is not a weakness, Lauren. It's a strength. He was the one who was too weak to deserve it."

His words, delivered within the absolute security of his hug, landed differently. They bypassed her defenses and went straight to her wounded heart, soothing the hurt. A final, cleansing tear rolled down her cheek. She felt the last of the tension leave her body, melting into the comfort he offered. She had thought she needed to scream or rage or break something, but she had been wrong. She just desperately needed a hug. And Jordan, without question or hesitation, had given her one that felt strong enough to begin putting the broken pieces of her back together.
