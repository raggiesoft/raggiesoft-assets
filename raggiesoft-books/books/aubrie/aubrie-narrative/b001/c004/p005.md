# Chapter 4 Sidebar: The Day After: Sarah's Stand

The text messages had come in last night, while Sarah was studying in her room on the fifth floor. They were from her roommate, Jessica, who had been downstairs in the lobby.

Jessica (8:21 PM): Oh my god, Sarah, you need to get down to the lobby. Your boyfriend Tom is here.

Jessica (8:22 PM): He's screaming at that girl Lauren from 214. The one in the suite with the guy.

Jessica (8:23 PM): He's breaking up with her. He just accused her of... wait. He's saying she was obsessed?

Jessica (8:24 PM): Oh my god. Sarah... he was dating her. He was cheating on you with her. And now he's publicly humiliating her to cover it up.

Sarah’s blood had run cold. A minute later, the door to her dorm room flew open. Jessica stood there, breathless and pale, her eyes wide with shock.

"Sarah, I... That was... it was the most horrible thing I've ever seen."

Sarah stood up, her phone clutched in a white-knuckled grip. "The texts. Jess, what *happened*? What did he do?"

"He humiliated her!" Jessica said, throwing her bag on the bed. "He was screaming. He called her obsessed, said she'd been 'making moves' on him. It was all a lie, Sarah. He was dating her. He was cheating on you".

Sarah just stared, her mind racing, trying to make the pieces fit. "Lauren... from 216? But... that's insane. The whole building knows the story".

"I *know*!" Jessica said, pacing her side of the room. "That's what I'm saying! We all know about the clerical error! We all know the guy is in a wheelchair and it's the only accessible suite in the building". "Everyone knows they're just a weird, nice group who decided to make it work. And frankly," she added, "the only person *he* seems to actually be 'making moves' on is the *other* girl, Aubrie. The one who has the same name".

Jessica stopped pacing and looked at her roommate, her eyes full of pity. "He was lying, Sarah. He was covering his tracks. He didn't just break up with her; he stood in that lobby and he tried to destroy her, just to save his own skin."

Sarah's blood, which had been cold with shock, now began to boil with a cold, clear fury. Her mission was set.

The next morning, she found Tom at "The Daily Grind," the campus coffee shop. He was feeling smug, having controlled the narrative, framing himself as the loyal boyfriend. He felt powerful.

His confidence faltered when Sarah slid into the chair opposite him, her expression a mask of cold fury.

"Hey, babe," he said, forcing a smile. "I was just about to text you. What's up?".

"Don't," she said, her voice low and cutting. "I know what happened last night, Tom".

He immediately launched into the story he had perfected in his head. "Oh, Sarah, it was awful. That girl, Lauren? She's been trying to make moves on me for weeks... I finally had to shut it down, hard. I told her I loved you...".

He leaned back, expecting sympathy. He got none.

Sarah let out a short, humorless laugh. "That's a good story, Tom. It's very creative. But it's not true".

Tom's face went pale. "What are you talking about?".

"My roommate, Jessica? She was in the lobby," Sarah stated, her voice cold as ice. "She texted me. *While it was happening*. She knows you're *my* boyfriend. She watched you scream at your *other* girlfriend, Tom. The one you were cheating on me with".

She leaned forward, her eyes narrowing. "And on top of that, I live in Bennett Hall. The whole dorm knows the *real* story about the housing error. We've all been talking for days about the guy, Jordan, who got placed in that suite, and how his five roommates decided to stick together... And frankly, the only person anyone is making 'moves' on in that group is the roommate he's clearly falling in love with, a girl named Aubrie".

She sat back, letting the facts dismantle his lie. "So, you can stop. Your story doesn't track. Not even a little bit".

Her voice hardened. "What I do know is what I heard from people who were there. That you didn't just break up with her. You stood in a public lobby and you humiliated her. You tried to destroy her character with lies. That's the part I can't forgive. It shows me who you really are".

She stood up, her decision absolute.

"So, we're done," she said with a finality that left no room for argument. "And unlike you, I'm not going to scream it in public. I'm just telling you to your face that I want nothing more to do with you. Ever. Lose my number".

Without waiting for a reply, Sarah turned and walked out of the coffee shop, leaving Tom sitting there, stunned and exposed. His lies had been useless.

As Sarah walked purposefully across campus, her mission was clear. She knew Tom had slandered Lauren. She had to find her. She had to make sure she was okay. She headed straight for her own dorm, Bennett Hall.
