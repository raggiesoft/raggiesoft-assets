# Part 4: An Addition to the Pact

Lauren brought Sarah back to the suite, walking in with an arm linked through hers. "Everyone," she announced, her voice stronger than it had been in days. "This is Sarah. She's our friend now."

The group, seeing the genuine alliance between the two women, welcomed her without hesitation. Jordan offered her a warm, kind smile, immediately recognizing another soul who had been wounded by Tom's cruelty.

Over the next few hours, Sarah fit in as seamlessly as Mark had. She laughed at Madison's jokes, listened intently to Zoe's explanation of a physics problem, and talked about music with Hannah. She also watched, with wide, curious eyes, the easy, open affection the women showed Jordan. She saw the way Lauren held his hand, the way Madison kissed his forehead, the way Aubrie looked at him with a love that could power a star.

Later that evening, after they had all shared pizza on the floor of Room 214, Aubrie found a quiet moment with Sarah. "You've been through hell, too," Aubrie said gently. "You also deserve a place to heal. A place with kindness and no judgment." She smiled her peaceful, generous smile. "Our pact... what we have with Jordan... it's open to you, too. If you want it."

Sarah looked at Jordan, who was across the room, laughing at something Mark said. She looked at the circle of women who had taken her in without question. And she looked at Aubrie, this incredible girl who saw love not as a finite resource to be hoarded, but as an infinite, healing light to be shared.

Tears welled in Sarah's eyes again, but this time, they were tears of pure, unadulterated gratitude. "Yes," she whispered. "I'd like that."

The stage was now set. The two women Tom had tried to tear apart had not only become friends, but had both found refuge—and the potential for a new, kinder romance—in the one place Tom would never understand.
