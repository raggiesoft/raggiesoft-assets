# Part 5: The Unbeliever

A new, gentle rhythm established itself in the suite. The pact, born from trauma, was now a quiet, living thing. It was Monday night, which had quickly become a sacred tradition for the group: wrestling night.

"Are you guys ready?" Mark called out from Room 216, where he and Hannah were grabbing snacks. "The pre-show for *Global Wrestling Federation: Monday Night Uprising* is about to start!"

In Room 214, Jordan was looking up match predictions on his laptop. Lauren, who was sitting on the edge of his bed, leaned over and gave him a gentle, lingering kiss on the cheek. "Don't get your hopes up," she teased. "'Rocket' Rick Riley is definitely going to beat Dr. Destruction tonight."

"Never," Jordan shot back with a grin, the easy affection a balm on her still-healing heart. "Dr. Destruction has the best finishing move in the whole GWF."

"You're both wrong," Mark said, walking in with an armful of chips and soda. "Tonight's all about the tag-team championship." He high-fived Jordan. "You see the away game Saturday? Total domination. I'm telling you, we're going all the way to the conference championship this year." Mark, a diehard season ticket holder, never missed a university football game, home or away.

"You say that every year," Madison said, ruffling Jordan's hair as she passed by. "Are we going, or am I going to have to listen to you two debate the finer points of a piledriver all night up here?"

"It's not a debate, it's a strategic analysis!" Jordan and Mark said in unison, then laughed.

Even the girls, who initially had no interest in wrestling, had come to love the weekly ritual. They loved the drama, the spectacle, and most of all, they loved the sheer, unadulterated joy it brought Jordan and Mark. Aubrie, watching Jordan laugh so freely, felt her heart swell. This was their new normal—a chaotic, loving, jumbled mix of football, wrestling, and unwavering support. It was perfect.

"Alright, let's go," Aubrie announced, taking charge. "I want to get our couch cluster before another floor takes it."

The seven of them headed down to the lobby, a happy, cohesive unit, ready for the show.

The dorm lobby was buzzing with its usual weeknight energy, but the suite family had successfully claimed their favorite spot: a cluster of mismatched but comfortable sofas arranged in a U-shape around the largest TV. *GWF Uprising* was in full swing, the sounds of bodyslams and a roaring crowd filling the air.

The scene was one of pure, baffling bliss. On the main sofa, Jordan sat in the middle, a picture of quiet contentment. His left arm was wrapped around Sarah, who was leaning comfortably against his side. His right arm was around Lauren, who had her head resting on his shoulder. On the sofa to the left, Aubrie, Zoe, and Madison were curled up, shouting advice at the television. On the right, Hannah and Mark were cuddled together, sharing a bowl of popcorn.

It was into this scene that Tom walked. He was just cutting through the lobby on his way to the vending machines when he stopped dead, his brain short-circuiting.

He saw Lauren. *His* Lauren, he still thought of her, tucked under the arm of that guy in the wheelchair, looking... happy. Peaceful, even. Then his eyes snapped to the other side of the guy. And his blood ran cold.

It was Sarah. It was *his* Sarah. His two ex-girlfriends, together, cuddling with the same person. It was impossible. It didn't compute.

His gaze darted desperately to the other couch, searching for the dark-haired one—Aubrie, the supposed girlfriend. The one Lauren had used as an excuse. He found her. She wasn't crying. She wasn't screaming. She wasn't even looking angry. She was laughing, her head thrown back in delight at something one of the wrestlers did.

As if feeling his stare, Aubrie looked over at the central couch. She gave Jordan and the two girls with him a brilliant, loving smile and a thumbs-up. At that exact moment, as if to twist the knife, Lauren lifted her head and gave Jordan a quick, affectionate kiss on his jaw. Sarah, on the other side, adjusted herself to get even more comfortable, snuggling deeper into his embrace.

And Aubrie just... beamed. She looked on with pure, unadulterated joy. There was no jealousy. There was only blessing.

Tom couldn't breathe. He felt a rage so profound, so confusing, it left him dizzy. His world was built on rules of possession, of jealousy, of ownership. This scene defied all of it. This was a world he couldn't comprehend, a happiness he couldn't control or poison. He clenched his fists, a strangled, guttural sound catching in his throat, and without ever making it to the vending machine, he turned and stormed out of the lobby, utterly defeated by a love he could never, ever understand.
