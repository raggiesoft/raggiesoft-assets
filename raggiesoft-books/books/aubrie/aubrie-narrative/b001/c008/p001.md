# Part 1: The Door Decorating Contest

It was Monday, September 22nd, 2014, and the campus was already electric with the energy of Homecoming Week. Jordan had been at CPI for a month, and for the first time in his life, he was thriving. He was getting good grades, he was surrounded by his new "Suite 6" family, and he finally felt like he belonged.

That evening, Madison Scott burst through the door of Room 214, her hands full of maroon and orange streamers and a flyer.

"Okay, team, listen up!" she announced, her voice full of her usual bubbly energy. "The dorm door decorating contest is officially on. And we are going to *win*."

Zoe, looking up from her desk in Room 216, pushed her glasses up. "Statistically, Madison, the odds of winning a subjective contest against hundreds of other doors are low."

"Not if we have an artistic edge," Lauren chimed in, walking out of 216 with Hannah. As a Graphic Design major, her eyes were already sparkling. "I am so in. What's the plan?"

"I'm glad you asked," Madison grinned. "We don't just do *one* door. We do *both*. A coordinated, two-part masterpiece."

Zoe's interest was suddenly piqued. "A linked diptych. That's a more efficient use of our shared resources and increases visibility. Okay, I'm in. What's the design?"

Jordan, who had been watching from his desk, felt a new, unfamiliar flutter of excitement. His mother had always called school spirit "frivolous," but here, it felt like... fun. "What about our mascot?" he offered quietly. "The Forgers?"

"Yes!" Aubrie said, immediately latching onto his idea. "We can have the Forger on one door..."

"...and on the other," Lauren picked up, her mind already working, "we can have the VDU Monarch mascot, completely crushed!"

A cheer went up from the small group. "Crush Norfolk!" Mark yelled, poking his head in after hearing the commotion. He and Sarah had just arrived for their nightly visit. "I love it. The Forger's Hammer!"

The rest of the evening was a happy, chaotic mess of paint, construction paper, and glitter. They spread their supplies out in the hallway, claiming the territory outside both doors. Lauren, as the group's lead artist, took charge, sketching a massive, muscular CPI Forger in brilliant maroon and orange for the door of Room 216. For Room 214, she drew a cartoonishly sad and defeated VDU Monarch (representing "Norfolk"), cowering under a giant hammer.

Jordan, to his own surprise, was put in charge of the lettering, his steady hands proving perfect for the task. He sat in his wheelchair, meticulously painting the bold words: GO FORGERS! on one door, and BEAT NORFOLK! on the other. He had never felt so included.

Late that night, the newly-christened "Group of 8" stood back to admire their work. The two doors were magnificent. Room 216 was a glorious, powerful tribute to the Forgers, and Room 214 was a hilarious, confident taunt to their rivals.

Jordan looked at the two doors, his hands stained with paint, and then at his seven friends, who were all laughing and covered in glitter. He wasn't an outsider watching a school activity; he was a part of it. This was his home, and this was his team.
