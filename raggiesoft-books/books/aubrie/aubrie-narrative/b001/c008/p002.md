# Part 2: Chalk the Walk

The next day, Tuesday, September 23rd, was "Chalk the Walk," one of CPI's most beloved Homecoming traditions. For 24 hours, student organizations and dorm groups were given a patch of sidewalk on the main walkway bordering The Commonwealth Green—the university's massive central quad—to create a "tasteful" chalk masterpiece.

Lauren, as their resident Graphic Design major, was in her element. "Okay, we're just translating the door design," she announced, sketching out a plan on a notepad. "We can't be as aggressive as 'Crush Norfolk,' but we can be *creative*."

Their assigned patch of concrete became their new canvas. With Lauren directing, the entire Group of 8 got to work. Mark and Sarah, laughing, were put in charge of filling in the huge block letters for "GO FORGERS!" Aubrie and Madison worked on a detailed, colorful CPI logo.

Jordan, in his wheelchair, was given a bucket of bright orange chalk and tasked with creating the border. He wheeled himself along the edge, drawing a thick, neat line, his arm aching but his heart full. He wasn't on the sidelines watching; he was a participant. Hannah and Zoe worked beside him, filling the interior of the square with a solid maroon background.

The final piece was Lauren's. In the center, she drew a brilliant, stylized CPI Forger's Hammer resting triumphantly on a cartoonish, cracked-in-half VDU Monarch's crown. It was spirited, clever, and perfectly tasteful.

They stood back to admire their work, their hands and jeans covered in chalk dust. Their square was a bright, defiant splash of maroon and orange on The Commonwealth Green. Jordan looked at what they had made together, another small, tangible piece of his new identity. He was a Forger, and for the first time in his life, he was proud of who he was.
