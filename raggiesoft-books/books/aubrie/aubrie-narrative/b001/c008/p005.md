# Part 5: Game Day

Saturday, September 27, 2014, arrived with the roar of a campus-wide celebration. The mood was one of pure, unadulterated hype.

In Suite 214/216, the Group of 8 was getting ready. Mark and Sarah had come over early, and the whole group was decked out in maroon and orange. Jordan, with Aubrie’s help, had pulled his new CPI Forgers t-shirt on over his head. He felt a profound sense of belonging. He was no longer the boy his mother had dropped off "hoping that he would fail"; he was a successful student, a part of a family, and today, he was a Forger.

"You all ready for this?" Mark said, his energy infectious. "The \#3 team in the nation is about to teach Norfolk a lesson!"

Jordan grinned, caught up in the excitement. He, too, was ready to see his new home team crush the abstract enemy. He turned to Aubrie, who was adjusting her CPI baseball cap.

"It's weird," Jordan said, making casual conversation. "Why does everyone just call them Norfolk? Is that the VDU mascot's name or something?"

Aubrie laughed, assuming he was just confused about the sports nickname, not the geography. After all, he was from Virginia Beach, just like she was from Chesapeake. "No, J, 'Norfolk' is just the city where VDU is located. It's like how they probably all call us 'Blacksburg' or something."

She then, completely unintentionally, provided the final, devastating piece of the puzzle. "It's right next to Virginia Beach. And Chesapeake, where I'm from. It's all part of Hampton Roads".

The words hit Jordan with the force of a physical blow.

His mother had sheltered him so completely, his world was so small, that he had genuinely perceived "Norfolk" and "Virginia Beach" as two separate, unrelated worlds. He'd never been *to* Norfolk. He'd only ever heard his mother speak of it with disdain.

But now, the data compiled in his head with electric clarity: VDU = "Norfolk" (the team). "Norfolk" = A city (new info). That city = Virginia Beach (his "home"). Virginia Beach = His Mother.

The realization was a physical jolt, but it was immediately followed by a fierce, triumphant surge of adrenaline. This wasn't just a game. This was a symbolic *war*. This was his new life, his sanctuary, his \#3-ranked, powerful new family (CPI) about to get revenge for him. They were about to publicly, systemically, and completely *annihilate* the symbolic representation of his old, abusive life (VDU/Norfolk).

He wasn't scared. He was *thrilled*.

"Jordan? Are you okay?" Aubrie asked, seeing the sudden, intense look on his face.

"I've never been better," he said, his voice low and full of a new, hard energy. "I *really* want us to win."

He gripped the push-rings of his wheelchair, his knuckles white. Logically, how could they lose? His new, safe, powerful world was about to get its righteous victory. He entered The Crucible not as a nervous fan, but as a confident soldier, ready to watch his new home give his old one the beating it deserved.
