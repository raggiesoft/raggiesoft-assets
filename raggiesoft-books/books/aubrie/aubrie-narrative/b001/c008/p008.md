# Part 8: The Second Quarter

The second quarter began with the \#3-ranked CPI Forgers on offense, the ball near their own end zone. The mood in The Crucible was no longer celebratory; it was a tense, angry buzz.

"Okay, this is where we wake up," Mark said, his voice tight. "Watch this. The coaches will have made adjustments."

The Heisman-candidate quarterback, clearly rattled, tried to force a big play. He dropped back and launched a deep pass toward his star receiver. But the VDU safety, who had clearly studied the game film, read the play perfectly. He jumped the route, intercepted the pass, and ran it back 30 yards.

The small VDU fan section was ecstatic. The rest of the stadium—over 60,000 people—was completely silent.

Jordan’s anxiety spiked into full-blown dread. He gripped the armrests of his wheelchair, his knuckles white. This wasn't a "fluke". This was a disaster.

VDU, with a short field, didn't get fancy. They just handed the ball to their running back. 5 yards. 4 yards. 5 yards. The CPI defense, built for speed and glory, had no answer for the slow, methodical, brutal assault. VDU punched it in for another touchdown.

CPI got the ball back. The panic was palpable. A quick, incomplete pass. A run for no gain. On 3rd and 10, the quarterback was sacked, a crushing 8-yard loss. They were forced to punt again.

Jordan was sinking. He had stopped watching the game and was just staring at the scoreboard, which felt like a personal accusation. Aubrie, seeing his distress, tried to reassure him. "It's okay, J, it's just the first half. We're a second-half team, we always are." But her voice was thin, and she was clearly trying to convince herself as much as him.

VDU got the ball and did the exact same thing. They ran the ball, ate up the clock, and marched down the field. The CPI defense, now visibly exhausted and demoralized, simply couldn't stop them. Another touchdown.

HOME: 0 VISITOR: 21

With less than a minute left, CPI made one last, desperate attempt. The quarterback fumbled the snap. VDU recovered. The VDU kicker trotted onto the field and nailed a 40-yard field goal as the halftime whistle blew.

The Marching Forgers began to take the field, but thousands of fans were already streaming for the exits. Jordan just sat there, frozen, staring at the final, humiliating score.

HOME: 0 VISITOR: 24

His "invincible" new home, his \#3-ranked sanctuary, was being systematically and effortlessly dismantled by the team from his mother's world. He was pale, non-verbal, and had begun to rock, a low, quiet stim as his mind tried to process the catastrophic failure. The "Homecoming Humiliation" was in full swing.
