# Part 10: The Retreat

The halftime show was just noise. Jordan was still rocking, his eyes wide and unfocused, his mind lost in the terrifying realization that his mother's world was winning.

"Okay, we're done. We're leaving," Mark said, his voice a low, firm command. The rest of the group didn't question it. Their priority was no longer the game; it was getting Jordan out.

The Group of 8 re-formed their protective fortress for movement. "Mark, you and I take point," Hannah said, her mom friend instincts kicking in. "Clear a path." Zoe, the logistician, immediately stepped behind Jordan's chair. "I've got him." Aubrie and Madison, his designated safe people, flanked the wheelchair, their hands on Jordan's shoulders, a constant, grounding pressure. Lauren and Sarah took the rear, acting as a buffer against the churning halftime crowd.

They moved as one unit, a small, determined island in a sea of maroon and orange. They fought their way against the tide of fans heading *toward* the concession stands, their faces grim. Mark, using his muscular build, gently but firmly navigated a path through the bottleneck at the concourse exit.

"Excuse us, coming through," he repeated, his voice polite but unyielding.

The moment they were outside the stadium gates, the roar of the crowd was replaced by the distant, muffled sound of the marching band. The air felt blessedly quiet, but the walk back to Bennett Hall was heavy with a new, somber energy. No one spoke.

They arrived at "The Old Lady on the Hill”, and the familiar, automatic *whirr* of the accessible door opener felt like a sigh of relief. They got Jordan into the elevator, rode up to the second floor, and finally, *finally* wheeled him into the safety of Room 214.

Zoe shut the door, and the lock clicked into place, sealing them off from the world. The game was still raging a few blocks away, but in their fortress, they were finally safe. Jordan was still trembling, but the rocking had begun to subside. He was home.
