# Part 12: The Fortress at Night

The laptop screen was dark, Jordan’s typed confession hanging in the heavy air of Room 214. The sheer emotional exhaustion of the day—the hype, the crash, the game, and the confession—had left everyone drained. It was time for bed.

Mark and Sarah, who were not residents of the suite, were the first to leave. They gave Jordan quiet, supportive looks, their goodnights a soft promise to be back tomorrow, before heading to their own rooms.

Lauren, seeing Jordan was exhausted and safe, gave him a gentle hug. "We've got you, J," she whispered, before she crossed through the connecting bathroom into Room 216 to get some sleep.

This left the original occupants of Room 214, plus Zoe. Zoe, her own analytical mind processing the massive, traumatic data dump, came over to Jordan. She leaned down and, in a rare act of overt comfort, wrapped him in a firm, grounding hug. "We will find a logical solution," she murmured, a promise. She then let go and headed into Room 216, leaving the three roommates alone.

Jordan was still situationally mute, his system completely depleted. He looked at Aubrie, his eyes wide and pleading. He needed her. He could not be alone tonight.

Aubrie and Madison understood immediately. They moved to help him, their actions a seamless, quiet dance of care. They gently helped him out of his CPI Forgers t-shirt and jeans, and into his soft pajamas, their movements practical and respectful. With that done, the two women changed into their own sleep clothes, the "no privacy" rule of their suite feeling less like a logistical necessity and more like a statement of their unconditional trust.

Madison came over and gave Jordan a tight, fierce hug. "We're not going anywhere," she promised. She then climbed the ladder to her upper bunk, Bed C, watching from above.

Jordan transferred from his wheelchair into Bed A, his narrow, single Twin XL. The mattress felt cold and empty.

A moment later, he felt a dip as Aubrie climbed in beside him. The bed was incredibly cramped, a tiny space never meant for two. But Jordan didn't care. He was willing to make the small space work if it meant a trusted person was in bed with him, hugging him.

Aubrie immediately turned on her side, becoming the big spoon. She wrapped her arms around him, holding him as tightly as she possibly could, her chin resting on his shoulder, her body a warm, solid shield against the world.

Jordan let out a long, shuddering breath he felt he'd been holding since kickoff. He was still non-verbal, his mind a quiet, exhausted static. But he was glad Zoe and Madison had hugged him before they'd gone to bed. And he was profoundly, overwhelmingly grateful for Aubrie. He was terrified, but he was not alone. He closed his eyes and, for the first time that day, felt a flicker of safety as he sank into her embrace.
