# Part 1: The Coming Storm

The middle of October brought a chill to the air and the welcome promise of Fall Break. The dorms were not closing for the short Saturday-to-Tuesday respite, and the suite was buzzing with low-key plans—a horror movie marathon, a trip to the pumpkin patch, and a massive, collaborative dinner.

Amidst the cheerful planning, however, Jordan grew quiet and withdrawn. His smiles were strained, and a familiar anxiety settled in his eyes. Madison, ever observant, cornered him by his desk on Friday afternoon.

"Okay, spill it," she said gently. "What's going on? You've been a million miles away all day."

Jordan sighed, the sound heavy with dread. "My mother is coming tomorrow morning," he said, his voice low. "To take me home for the break."

"Oh," Madison said, her cheerful demeanor softening into understanding. "And you don't want to go."

"I can't," Jordan admitted, the words tumbling out. "Every time I talk to her on the phone, she calls me Aubrey, asks when I'm going to stop this 'silly college experiment' and come home for good. She doesn't get it. She doesn't get *us*." He looked around the room, at the comfortable mess of their shared lives. "This is my home now. You guys... you're my family."

Just then, Aubrie Jean walked in and caught the end of his sentence. She came over and placed a hand on his shoulder. "Then you should stay here for the break," she said simply. "With your family. You're an adult, Jordan. The dorms are open. You don't have to go anywhere you don't want to go."

The idea was terrifying, but it was also a lifeline. For the first time, he realized he had a choice.
