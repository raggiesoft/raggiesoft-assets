# Part 2: The Confrontation

Saturday morning arrived not with a knock, but with the buzz of Jordan's phone on his desk. The caller ID read "Mom." He took a deep breath, glancing at Aubrie and Madison, who offered him small, encouraging nods. He answered.

"Aubrey, I'm downstairs in the lobby," his mother's voice chirped, sharp and expectant. "Come down and let me in. We need to get you packed."

"It's Jordan," he said, his own voice quiet but firm. "And I'm not coming down, Mom. I'm staying here for the break."

There was a moment of stunned silence on the other end, followed by an icy shift in tone. "We are not having this discussion over the phone. I am your mother. You will come down here this instant." She hung up before he could reply.

Down in the lobby, Jordan's mother fumed. She paced in front of the locked glass doors that led to the residential hallways, her plan foiled by a university card reader. She was not used to being denied. She watched for several minutes until a freshman boy, his arms full of laundry, came up to the door and swiped his card. As the lock clicked open, he struggled to pull the heavy door.

Seeing her opportunity, Jordan's mother rushed forward, putting on her sweetest, most helpless-looking face. "Oh, thank you, dear, could you hold that for me?"

The boy, just trying to be helpful and polite to someone who looked like a visiting parent, smiled and held the door open for her—a direct violation of the dorm's policy against allowing people to "tailgate" into secure areas.

"Thank you so much," she cooed, slipping past him. The moment she was through, her sweet expression vanished. With a determined fire in her eyes, she stormed down the hall to Room 214 and began pounding on the door.

Madison opened it, her eyes widening in shock to see the woman from the lobby now standing in their hallway. Before she could react, Jordan's mother pushed past her and into the room.

"Aubrey!" she snapped, her voice cutting through the quiet room. "What is the meaning of this? Who are all these girls?"

Her eyes darted from Aubrie Kelly to Madison, then to Zoe, Lauren, and Hannah, who had all gathered from Room 216 upon hearing the commotion. Her mind, poisoned by the very fears she had programmed into her son, leaped to the only conclusion she could imagine. "What have you done?" she shrieked, her voice a mix of fury and bitter accusation. "Did you... did you get all of them pregnant?"

Jordan was TERRIFIED. This was his worst nightmare. His mother had found his secret. She was here. She was right. He *was* a burden. He had failed. He flinched, his body curling inward as he looked at the floor, unable to speak. He was "Aubrey" again, a 16-year-old boy being told he had ruined someone's life.

"That's the most ridiculous thing I've ever heard," she retorted, taking his silence as guilt. "This little college fantasy is over. You can't take care of yourself. You need to be home with me." She grabbed his empty suitcase from the closet and threw it on his bed. "Pack your things. Now."

He was defeated. This was it. He had failed his experiment. He slowly, numbly, began to wheel his chair toward his dresser. He was going to do what he was told. He was going to crawl back to mommy.

He looked up, his eyes unfocused, and he saw them. He saw Aubrie Kelly, Madison, Zoe, Lauren, and Hannah, all standing there, watching him, their faces not full of judgment, but of fierce, protective anger *at his mother*. They weren't abandoning him. They were his fortress.

The terrified "Aubrey" receded, and Jordan, the competent young man, took control.

He stopped his wheelchair. He turned to face his mother.

"Stop!"

The word was loud, sharp, and commanding. It shocked the room into silence. Aubrie Sinclair flinched, her eyes wide. He had *never* spoken to her like that.

"This is my suite, Mom," Jordan said, his voice shaking but strong. "My name is Jordan. And I am not leaving. You are not welcome here."

"With all due respect, ma'am," Aubrie Jean Kelly said, immediately stepping forward to stand beside Jordan's chair, her presence a silent shield now that he had found his voice. "He's right. Jordan is happy here. He's succeeding. We take care of each other."

Jordan's mother whirled on her. This was the one. This was "Jessica 2.0," the "devil" who had corrupted her son.

"And who are you to tell me what my son needs?" she sneered. "Does he know what a *burden* he is? Are you helping him get dressed? Are you helping him in the bathroom? You'll get tired of it. Everyone does. He's too much for anyone but *me*."

"He's not a burden," Madison said firmly, stepping up to stand on his other side.

"Oh, I see," Sinclair hissed. "Then you're just trying to trap him. You know how *easy* it is to get a girl pregnant! He can't take care of himself, how can he take care of a baby? You're ruining your lives!"

It was her ultimate, practiced attack. But the 16-year-old boy she had programmed to fear this was gone.

A low, angry growl rumbled in Jordan's chest, a sound of pure disapproval for the eighteen years she had sheltered him.

"I know perfectly well how you get a girl pregnant, Mom," he said, his voice cold and steady. "And it is *not* from talking to her or hugging her".

Aubrie Sinclair's face went pale, her attack completely neutralized. What she didn't know was that these women had already given Jordan the education she had so viciously denied him. In their unconventional health lesson, they had sat with him in a safe, nurturing environment and had already taught him EVERYTHING he needed to know about his body, their bodies, and safe sex. Her ultimate weapon was useless.

The argument escalated, his mother's voice growing more shrill as she realized her weapons were failing and her son was not obeying. Finally, Zoe held up a hand. "That's enough," she said coolly. "You are in this room without an escort, which is a violation of building policy. I'm getting the Hall Director."
