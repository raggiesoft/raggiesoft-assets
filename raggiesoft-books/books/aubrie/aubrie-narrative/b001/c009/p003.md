# Part 3: The Choice

A few minutes later, Kelsey arrived, her expression calm but firm. Jordan's mother immediately tried to appeal to her. "Thank goodness. My son, Aubrey, is being held here against his will by these girls. He needs to come home with me."

Kelsey didn't look at his mother. She looked directly at Jordan. "Jordan," she said, her voice clear and professional. "You are eighteen years old. You are a legal adult and a resident of this university. The dorms are remaining open for the break. It is entirely your decision whether you wish to stay here or leave with your mother for the break. No one can force you to do anything. What do you want to do?"

The entire room held its breath. This was it. The moment of truth. He looked at his mother's furious, controlling face. Then he looked at the five women—and he knew Mark and Sarah were part of this too—who had shown him more kindness and acceptance in a few short months than he had ever known. His chosen family. He thought about a future free from her control, a future where he could just be *Jordan*. A future where he could, perhaps one day, ask Aubrie Jean to marry him, sealing his freedom forever.

He turned back to Kelsey, his voice steady and clear. "I'm staying here."

A strangled gasp came from his mother. "You can't do this to me, Aubrey!"

"His name is Jordan," Kelsey said coolly. "And he has made his decision. You need to leave now, or I will have campus security escort you from the building."

Defeated and shaking with rage, his mother gave him one last venomous glare and stormed out of the room, slamming the door behind her.

A profound, shuddering silence filled the space, and then it was broken as Jordan let out a breath he felt like he'd been holding for eighteen years. He was exhausted, shaking, but he was free. In an instant, his entire family was around him, their arms wrapping him in a massive group hug, grounding him in the joyful, undeniable truth of his new life. He was finally home.
