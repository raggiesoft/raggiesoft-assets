# Part 1: The New Lifeline and a Winter Pact

Saturday morning marked the official start of Fall Break, but the mood in Suite 214 was one of focused, problem-solving determination. The anger from the previous night had solidified into a protective shield around Jordan.

"Okay," Madison said, pacing the floor. "A new cell plan is the top priority, but none of us really have the money for that right now. That's a long-term problem we'll have to figure out. So, what do we do *now*, for this break?"

"His hardware is functional. The carrier service is what's been terminated," Zoe stated, her fingers already flying across her laptop keyboard. "A VoIP service is the most logical interim solution."

She turned her laptop around for the others to see. "There are apps that will assign him a new, real phone number that operates over the internet," she explained, making sure Jordan understood. "As long as you're connected to WiFi—*any* WiFi, here in the dorm, at the library, the student union, basically anywhere on campus—you'll be able to make calls and send texts just like normal. It completely bypasses the cellular network."

A collective sense of victory filled the room. Working together, Zoe and Aubrie downloaded the app onto Jordan's phone and guided him through the setup. Within minutes, he had a brand new, fully functional phone number.

"Okay," Aubrie said, her expression turning serious as she looked around the room. "Now for the most important rule. This number does not go to anyone outside of us eight. Especially not your mother. She can email. You decide if and when you answer."

Jordan nodded, looking at his new, private number. He had a lifeline again, one controlled only by his real family.

"This is excellent for communication when he's on campus WiFi," Zoe clarified, bringing them back to the original problem. "But the safety concern for when he's between buildings remains. The Buddy System protocol is still necessary."

As Madison created the 'Jordan Comms' group chat, Lauren voiced the question that was already forming in everyone's minds. "This is a perfect plan for this break. But... what about Winter Break? That's almost a month long. The dorms will be closed. She's going to expect you to go home."

The air grew heavy for a moment, the thought of Jordan being forced back into that house for weeks hanging over them.

"No," Aubrie said, her voice quiet but absolute. "He's not going."

"But where will we go?" Lauren asked.

"Well," Hannah spoke up, "my parents' house is just twenty minutes from here, so I'll be home. Mark and Sarah are local, too. And Zoe's family lives just down 460 in Christiansburg." This was a fact the group knew but hadn't fully considered the implications of; four of their tight-knit circle had chosen to live in the dorms for the independence and experience, not out of necessity. "But you guys," she said, looking at Lauren, Madison, Aubrie, and Jordan, "none of you are from the New River Valley."

"Then the four of you will get a hotel room," Zoe declared, already shifting into planning mode. "The logical solution is an extended-stay hotel near campus. We can find a suite with a kitchenette and a small living/dining area. It can be our home base for the break."

Madison's face lit up. "Yes! One of those suites with two queen beds would be perfect. So, Jordan and one of us in one bed, and the other two in the second. We can take turns sharing with him."

The 'Jordan Comms' chat immediately buzzed with activity as Hannah relayed the plan. Mark: My folks will be happy to have me home, but fair warning, I'm basically gonna live at your hotel room. Don't even think you're getting out of wrestling night. Sarah: Same! My house is boring. I'll bring the snacks. Zoe: I'll map out a budget and a list of potential hotels.

Jordan looked at the faces of his friends in the room and the buzzing phone in his hand. They weren't just solving today's problem. They were building a fortress for his future, walling off his mother's control with concrete plans born of fierce, unwavering love. She had tried to cut one cord, and in response, his family had woven an unbreakable net.

"Okay," Madison said, clapping her hands together with renewed purpose. "The winter pact is made. Now for the Fall Break relay. Who's up first?"
