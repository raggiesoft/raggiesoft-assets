# Part 2: The Handoffs in Action

The Fall Break weekend unfolded as a masterclass in compassionate logistics. The Buddy System, a plan born from crisis, was immediately put into practice, and the "Jordan Comms" group chat became the central nervous system of their family.

On Sunday morning, the first message appeared. Aubrie: With Jordan. Heading to 'The Daily Grind' in the student union for a change of scenery.

They sat for over an hour in the bustling campus coffee shop, sipping lattes and sharing a pastry. Here, surrounded by the university's powerful WiFi signal, Jordan's phone worked perfectly, allowing him to feel a sense of normal, easy connection with the rest of his friends via their chat.

Around noon, a new plan began to form in the group chat. Madison: I'm going stir-crazy. Anyone up for a trip into town? We could walk around downtown. Lauren: YES! I'm in. Aubrie: Great idea. We're at the student union. Meet us at the bus stop out front in 15.

The handoff was seamless. Aubrie went off to meet Hannah for a study session, while Madison and Lauren took over as Jordan's designated buddies for the afternoon adventure. The four of them—Jordan, Madison, Lauren, and Zoe, who decided to tag along—headed for the bus stop. As the large Blue Ridge Transit bus pulled up to the curb, its prominent "BT" logo gleaming in the sun, Madison got a mischievous look in her eye.

As she swiped her university ID, she leaned toward the driver, a friendly-looking man in his fifties. "Just so you know," she said with a playful grin, "we all know the *real* BT is the one back in my hometown, Bluewater."

The driver didn't miss a beat, looking at her in his rearview mirror with a tired, amused smirk. "That's funny, honey. My paycheck says Blue Ridge Transit, and we're the only ones running on game day. Sounds pretty real to me." He nodded toward the back of the bus. "Now find a seat before you hold up the whole route."

Madison laughed, completely satisfied with the familiar exchange. As the bus made its way from the campus grounds down into the heart of town, Jordan's phone went silent, the WiFi connection vanishing, but he felt no anxiety. He knew he wasn't truly disconnected.

When the bus reached their stop downtown, the group began to file out. As Madison passed the driver, she leaned in one last time with a smirk. "Sorry for calling you the fake BT," she whispered conspiratorially. The driver just shook his head, smiling. "Have a good one, Bluewater."

They spent the afternoon wandering the charming, historic streets of downtown Blacksburg. They browsed the windows of the quirky shops on Main Street and eventually ducked into a sprawling local bookstore with comfy armchairs and, most importantly, free WiFi. The moment they sat down, Jordan's phone chimed as it connected. A flood of messages from the "Jordan Comms" chat, which had been active while he was offline, poured in.

Hannah: Mark says to tell you Dr. Destruction's promo last night was epic and he can't wait to break it down with you. Zoe: (Attached PDF) Here is my initial research on hotels for Winter Break. Top three are highlighted. Aubrie: (photo of a squirrel looking menacingly at her from a tree branch) I think this guy wants my notes.

Jordan laughed, showing the picture to Lauren and Madison. His mother had cut his cellular service, but she couldn't cut him off from the constant, loving, chaotic stream of his family's life.

Later that afternoon, a new text came through on the group chat. Mark: Just got off my shift at the campus bookstore. I'm already downtown, I can give you all a ride back up the hill to the dorm if you want?

The final handoff of the day saw them all piling into Mark's slightly beat-up but reliable sedan. Back in the suite that evening, tired and happy, they all agreed the system had worked flawlessly. The Fall Break trial run was an absolute, unqualified success. They had proven they could navigate campus, travel into town, and keep Jordan safe, connected, and happy. His mother's attempt to isolate him had backfired spectacularly; it had only expanded his world.
