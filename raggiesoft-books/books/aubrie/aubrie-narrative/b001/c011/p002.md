# Part 2: The Final Hour

With all official notices filed, a tangible sense of relief and victory settled over Jordan. He had taken control. Back in the suite, the mood shifted from formal resolve to excited urgency as the entire eight-person family converged to pack.

"Okay, people," Zoe announced, looking at the clock on her phone. "The city bus from the CPI Transit Center to Falling Branch Park & Ride runs every fifteen minutes. Our target is the 4:45 PM bus, which gives us just under an hour. Operation Thanksgiving Pack-Up is a go."

What followed was a flurry of purposeful chaos. The group worked as a single, cohesive unit. Lauren and Sarah took charge of gathering toiletries and snacks. Hannah was at Jordan's closet, holding up sweaters for his approval. "Blue or grey?" she'd ask, and he'd give a quick nod.

Mark, without being asked, easily lifted a heavy box of textbooks from a high shelf that no one else could reach, placing it carefully by Jordan's bag. As he worked, he made a point to be relaxed and helpful. He wanted Jordan to see, unequivocally, that he was nothing like Tom. He saw the easy, platonic affection between Hannah and Jordan and felt nothing but happiness. Mark knew the 'official' story about Aubrie being Jordan's girlfriend, but he also understood the deeper, unofficial truth: Jordan was family to all of them, and Mark had no room for the kind of pathetic insecurity that had consumed Tom.

Aubrie and Madison worked together, going through a mental checklist of Jordan's essentials. "Charging cable for the chair?" Aubrie asked. "Got it," Madison confirmed, holding it up. "Noise-canceling headphones?" "In the side pocket of his backpack, right where he said," Madison replied.

Jordan sat in the center of it all, the calm director of his own needs, a quiet smile on his face. He was not being passively cared for; he was being supported by a team, his team.

"Alright," Zoe announced, "bus leaves in twenty. Final sweep."

They did one last check. The rooms, while still full of their long-term possessions, were stripped of the core items needed for the next five days. They grabbed their bags, ready for the short walk to the transit center and the bus ride that would take them to the safety of Zoe's house on Geneva Street, right next to the park and ride lot. Jordan led the way out the door, his chosen family—all seven of them—trailing right behind him.
