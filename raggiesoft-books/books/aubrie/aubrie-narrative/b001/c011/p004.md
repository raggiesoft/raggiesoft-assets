# Part 4: The Sanctuary

The group turned off Simmons Road SE onto the quiet, tree-lined cul-de-sac of Geneva Street. As they approached a handsome two-story house, the front door swung open and a woman with Zoe's kind eyes and a brilliant smile came out onto the porch, waving enthusiastically.

"You made it!" Laura Brooks called out, her voice full of genuine excitement. "We were just tracking your bus online! Welcome, welcome!" She gestured around the side of the house. "Come on around back! Your chariot awaits! Daniel's waiting to let you into your headquarters for the week!"

They followed a stone path that led around the house and began to slope downwards. The property was clearly built on a hill; the front door they had just seen was a full story above the backyard that stretched out below. Waiting at the bottom, standing by a large sliding glass door, was Zoe's father.

As the group started down the grassy slope, Daniel Brooks immediately came forward to meet Jordan. "Alright, Jordan, let me give you a hand here," he said, his voice calm and capable. "This slope's a bit of a beast."

Without any fuss, Daniel took firm control of the back of Jordan's chair, expertly guiding it down the incline, acting as a human brake and ensuring a smooth, safe descent. It was their first interaction, and it was one of immediate, practical trust.

Daniel slid open the glass door, and the group flooded into the finished basement that would be their home away from home. It was perfect. The space was warm and cozy, with a large sectional sofa, a television, and a small kitchenette along one wall. On a coffee table, a tray of snacks was already laid out.

Laura came down the internal stairs, carrying a tray of mugs. "Settle in, everyone. Make yourselves at home," she said, pouring steaming apple cider for them. "You're safe here."

"Alright," Daniel said, clapping his hands together with purpose. "Let's get this place operational." He pulled a large box from a storage closet and took out a single electric pump. "One pump, four queen-sized air mattresses. This might take a minute."

He plugged the pump into a wall outlet, and with a loud whir, the first mattress began to take shape on the floor. The noise drew an audience, and a moment later, Zoe's six older siblings began to filter down the stairs to see the new arrivals and offer their help.

Zoe laughed. "Everyone, this is my chaotic family. My older sisters, Megan and Chloe, and my four older brothers, Caleb, Lucas, Owen, and Jacob."

The Brooks siblings, a friendly and energetic bunch, didn't hesitate for a moment. They jumped right in to help the eight guests get situated. Caleb, the oldest, took over mattress duty from his dad. Lucas and Owen started helping Mark and the girls find spots for their duffel bags, while Jacob immediately engaged Jordan in a conversation about the video game he was playing.

Megan and Chloe took Lauren and Sarah under their wings. "Okay, we've got extra towels and blankets for you all in this closet here," Megan said, opening a door. "And Mom wants to know if anyone has any laundry they need to do."

As the four mattresses filled the floor space, creating a massive, comfortable landscape, Madison surveyed the scene. "Okay, bed assignments are simple," she announced. "Hannah and Mark, you get one." "Claimed!" Hannah laughed, grabbing Mark's hand.

"Jordan gets one," Madison continued. "And he'll need a buddy." "I'll take tonight," Lauren volunteered softly, "if that's okay, Jordan?" He gave her a warm, grateful smile and nodded. "Perfect," Madison said. "That leaves me, Aubrie, Zoe, and Sarah for the other two. Dibs on not being next to the snorer!"

The entire Brooks family helped the eight friends get settled, their unconditional acceptance making the space feel less like a temporary shelter and more like a true home.
