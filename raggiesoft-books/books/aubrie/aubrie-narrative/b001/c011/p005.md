# Part 5: Settling into Sanctuary

As the evening wound down, the chaotic energy of arrival softened into a comfortable, sleepy contentment. One by one, the Brooks siblings and their parents said their goodnights, heading up the internal stairs to the first and second floors of the house, leaving the eight friends to their own private domain.

The basement was now their fortress, filled with a sprawling city of four queen-sized air mattresses. The low light from a single lamp cast a warm, cozy glow over the scene.

"You know what sounds amazing right now?" Jordan said, breaking the quiet. "A real bath. Not just a quick shower."

The group looked at the basement bathroom. It was a standard, clean bathroom, but the tub was a simple tub/shower combo, with no grab bars or transfer bench. It wasn't handicap accessible.

"We've got you," Aubrie said immediately, without a hint of hesitation. Lauren stood up beside her. "Yep. The two of us can handle it."

What followed was not an act of romance, but one of pure, practical care, a dance of trust and physical support. Getting Jordan safely into and out of the tub was a careful, coordinated effort. Lauren provided the steady strength, her hands sure and firm, while Aubrie handled the logistics—getting the water to the perfect temperature, making sure towels and soap were within easy reach, and ensuring his dignity was protected throughout the entire process. It was another problem to be solved, and like every other problem they had faced, they solved it together.

Later, with everyone in their pajamas and ready for bed, a peaceful quiet settled over the basement. Hannah and Mark were already whispering to each other on their claimed mattress. The other girls were curled up on theirs. Lauren sat on the edge of the mattress she would be sharing with Jordan tonight, watching as Aubrie gave him a soft goodnight kiss before heading to her own bed.

There was no jealousy. No tension. Only love.

Lauren thought of Tom, of the possessive rage he would have felt at a scene like this. He would have seen rivals, betrayal, and suspicion. But here, there was only trust. Aubrie's complete, radical generosity was the foundation upon which this entire family was built. *It's Tom's loss,* Lauren thought, a surprising firmness in her heart. *And it's Jordan's gain. It's all of our gain.*

Jordan transferred from his chair onto the mattress, settling in beside her. "Goodnight, Lauren," he whispered. "Goodnight, Jordan," she replied, and as he wrapped an arm around her in their now-familiar, comforting spooning embrace, she felt the last of the day's stress melt away.

Across the sprawling, darkened room, a chorus of soft "goodnights" echoed. The sixteen inhabitants of the house on Geneva Street settled in for the night, but for the eight friends in the basement, it was something more. They had completed their exodus. They were safe, they were together, and they were finally home for the holiday.
