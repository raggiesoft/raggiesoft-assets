# Part 2: All Lines Cut

Sitting in her car, shaking with rage, Aubrie turned to her final, most powerful option: the authorities. She dialed the Blacksburg Police non-emergency number.

"Yes, I need to report a missing person," she said, her voice laced with practiced, maternal panic. "My son. Aubrey Fuller. He's a student at CPI, he's disabled, and I'm very worried."

The dispatcher's voice was calm. "One moment, ma'am." After a pause filled with the soft clicking of a keyboard, she returned. "Ma'am, we actually have a proactive note on file regarding this situation. The student has been in contact with Campus Police to assure them of his safety. His message was passed on to us, as well as to the Christiansburg Police and the Montgomery County Sheriff's office."

The dispatcher continued, "The student has made it clear that he is safe, that he is voluntarily spending the holiday with friends, and that he does not wish to have contact with you at this time. He is not considered a missing person. We cannot take a report. Is there anything else I can help you with?"

Checkmate. Every single avenue was blocked. Aubrie Sinclair lowered the phone, and finally, with no other moves left to make, she put the car in drive and pulled away from the curb, defeated.
