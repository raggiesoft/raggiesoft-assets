# Part 3: The Turkey Brawl

As 8:00 PM approached, the remnants of the massive feast were cleared away, and a new kind of anticipation filled the basement. The plates and platters were replaced with bowls of popcorn and cups of soda. The eight friends claimed their spots on the air mattresses and sofa, their eyes glued to the television. It was time for the main event of the holiday: the *Global Wrestling Federation's* annual two-hour special, the "GWF Turkey Brawl."

Just as the bombastic opening theme music began, Laura and Daniel Brooks appeared at the bottom of the stairs, followed by all six of Zoe's siblings.

"You don't think you're having all the fun down here by yourselves, do you?" Daniel said with a grin. "There's no way we're missing this."

The whole Brooks clan found places to sit, squeezing onto the sofa and grabbing pillows for the floor. The basement was now packed with all sixteen of them, a massive, boisterous audience ready for the show.

The main event was a championship match featuring the group's favorite villain, the massive and arrogant Dr. Destruction. He was on the verge of cheating his way to another victory, with the referee distracted and the current champion nearly defeated.

"Come on, ref, turn around!" Mark yelled at the screen, throwing a piece of popcorn in frustration.

Just as Dr. Destruction raised the championship belt to use as a weapon, the arena lights went out. A single, familiar chord of electric guitar blasted through the speakers—the entrance music of the very babyface hero he had put on the shelf months ago.

The arena on the screen erupted. And so did the basement on Geneva Street.

Everyone jumped to their feet. Madison was screaming, Mark was high-fiving Caleb, and even Zoe had a rare, wide grin of pure shock and excitement on her face. The returning hero stormed the ring and tackled Dr. Destruction before the villain could even react. What followed was not a match, but pure, cathartic revenge. The hero hit all his signature moves, leaving Dr. Destruction laid out flat in the center of the ring.

As the hero stood tall over his vanquished foe, he looked over at the dazed champion, who was slowly pulling himself up in the corner. He gave the champion a sharp, definitive nod, as if to say, "He's all yours. Finish it."

The hero then slid out of the ring, his revenge complete. The champion, seeing his opportunity, climbed to the top rope, delivered his own spectacular finishing move onto the unconscious Dr. Destruction, and rolled him over for the cover.

"ONE! TWO! THREE!" the entire basement roared in unison with the referee's count.

The room exploded in cheers. The champion had retained his title, and the hero had gotten his retribution. As the celebration died down and the show went to its closing credits, Jordan looked around the crowded room at his entire chosen family, all laughing and high-fiving. He realized this silly, over-the-top spectacle was a perfect metaphor for his own life. He had faced a villain who seemed unbeatable, but his own heroes had returned to help him win the fight. He caught Aubrie's eye from across the room, and she gave him a quiet, knowing smile that understood everything.

"Alright," Daniel Brooks announced, his voice full of good cheer as he stretched. "That's enough excitement for one night. We're all heading up."

One by one, the Brooks family said their goodnights, leaving the eight friends alone in their basement sanctuary. A comfortable, sleepy quiet began to settle over them as they got ready for bed.

"Okay, everyone, listen up," Madison whispered loudly, full of energy even as she climbed onto her air mattress. "Get some sleep. We have a very important mission tomorrow. Operation: Jordan's First Black Friday. BIG DAY TOMORROW!"

A chorus of sleepy "goodnights" filled the room as the lights went down. Tucked away safely in their fortress, the eight of them drifted off to sleep, ready for their next adventure together.
