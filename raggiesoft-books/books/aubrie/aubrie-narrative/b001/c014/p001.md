# Part 1: The Mission Briefing

The Brooks' basement was a hive of quiet activity long before the sun rose on Black Friday. The air smelled of freshly brewed coffee, and Laura Brooks was already bringing down trays laden with a filling breakfast of scrambled eggs and bacon.

Upstairs, Laura and Daniel Brooks were already in motion. Soon, they came down the stairs bearing trays laden with scrambled eggs, bacon, toast, and fruit. It was a massive, filling breakfast.

"Eat up, everyone," Daniel said, his tone warm but serious. "There will be no stopping for food. We go, we get what we need, and we come straight back." Alongside the breakfast plates, Sarah and one of Zoe's sisters were already packing a bag with snacks and water bottles. It might have seemed like overkill, but Daniel was taking absolutely no chances. He watched Jordan laugh with his sons, a fierce, protective instinct flaring in his chest. In a few short months, the boy had burrowed his way into Daniel's heart. This wasn't just his daughter's friend; he was starting to feel like one of his own. The thought that anyone would want to harm him was unacceptable, and Daniel would do everything in his power to prevent it.

After breakfast, it was time for final preparations. Hannah and Lauren, being the strongest of the women, helped Jordan into the bathroom one last time to get washed up and ready for the long day. Mark watched them go with a proud, easy smile, his complete lack of jealousy a quiet testament to his trust. Across the room, Aubrie felt a similar sense of peaceful certainty, watching the boy she loved be cared for by women she trusted as sisters. This was their normal.

With everyone completely ready, Daniel gathered the eight friends and his two oldest sons in the main area of the basement.

"Okay, everyone, listen up," he said, his voice a low, authoritative rumble. "The convoy will be three vehicles. Caleb, you'll take your SUV with Owen, Sarah, and Jacob. You take point. Mark, you've got Hannah and the rest in your sedan; you'll have our six. I'll drive the minivan. Laura will ride shotgun."

He then turned to Jordan. "Jordan, you'll be in the second row of the van for security. Who do you want with you?"

Jordan didn't hesitate. He couldn't explain why, couldn't articulate the specific physics of the comfort she provided, but faced with the overwhelming prospect of the Black Friday chaos, the only thing that felt truly safe was the thought of her arms around him. "Aubrie," he said quietly.

"Okay," Daniel nodded, his expression softening as he looked at Aubrie. "You're with him. Lauren, Madison, Zoe—you three are in the back row. We're keeping the core team together."

He looked around at the assembled group. "We all know the primary objective: the Commonwealth Wireless corporate store at Spradlin Farms to get Jordan his new mPhone and plan. That is our only mandatory stop. Everything else is secondary. Caleb and Owen, you're our eyes in the parking lot. You see anything that looks out of place, you text the 'Jordan Comms' group, and the mission is scrubbed. Everyone clear?"

A chorus of determined "yeses" answered him. They piled out of the basement through the sliding glass door, a small, brave army heading out into the cold morning, ready for their mission.
