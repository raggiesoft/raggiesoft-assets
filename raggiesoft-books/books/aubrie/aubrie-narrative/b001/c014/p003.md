# Part 3: The First Purchase

The three-car convoy parked strategically in the bustling Spradlin Farms lot. As planned, Caleb and Owen's SUV and Mark's sedan took up positions that provided a clear view of the area around Daniel's minivan. The "outer perimeter" got out first, scanning the area before giving the "all clear" on the group chat.

Daniel slid open the minivan door. With practiced ease, the group helped Jordan transfer into his wheelchair. The moment he was settled, Sarah stepped forward, wrapping him in a snug, protective hug that seemed to block out the noise of the crowded parking lot. It was a gesture he leaned into gratefully. He would only accept this kind of physical comfort from the females of his trusted Group of 8, and today it was Sarah's turn to be his primary anchor.

"I've got him," she said to the group, taking her place behind his wheelchair.

They entered the Commonwealth Wireless store as a single, multi-layered unit. It was busy, but the group used their numbers to create a pocket of calm around Jordan. While Mark and the Brooks brothers discreetly monitored the entrance, the rest of them focused on the mission.

Daniel approached a representative. "Hello," he said calmly. "We'd like to add a line to our family plan today."

What followed was a beautiful display of teamwork. As Daniel handled the account paperwork, Hannah and Madison showed Jordan the different mPhone models. He chose a sleek, dark blue, the simple act of making his own choice feeling monumental.

"Okay, you definitely need a case for that," Zoe said, already pulling up reviews on her phone and pointing him toward the most durable, practical options.

"Don't forget headphones!" chimed in Megan, one of Zoe's sisters.

Her brother, Owen, pointed to a display. "If you're getting an mPhone, you've gotta get the mPods Pro. The noise-canceling is insane."

Chloe, Zoe's other sister, appeared a moment later with a small box. "And you'll need this," she said, showing Jordan a charging brick that accepted two USB-C cables. "One for the phone and one for the mPods."

The transaction was complete in under fifteen minutes. The associate handed the newly activated mPhone to Jordan. Daniel smiled as he signed the final paper. "Welcome to the Brooks family plan, son."

Back outside in the crisp November air, Jordan looked down at the new phone in his hands—a device completely unknown to his mother, with a number only his true family had. He felt a surge of exhilaration.

"That was fun," he said, looking around at the bustling shopping center. "Can we... can we try another store?"

A nervous flicker crossed Daniel's face as he scanned the chaotic parking lot. He had accomplished the primary mission. But seeing the genuine excitement on Jordan's face, he couldn't say no. "Alright," he agreed, his voice firm. "But we stay on high alert. The second anything feels off, we are gone. No questions asked." He was searching for a threat that, unbeknownst to him, wasn't there.

They all piled back into the vehicles, but this time, there was a shift. "My turn," Madison said cheerfully, sliding into the captain's chair next to Jordan in the minivan. The hug rotation continued.

As they pulled out of their parking spot, Daniel made a new executive decision. "Alright team," he announced. "New objective. Blue Tech." He had noticed Jordan's old, slow laptop during the holiday prep. "That laptop of yours has seen better days. We're getting you an upgrade for your school work."

Madison squeezed Jordan's shoulder as their van led the convoy toward the massive electronics store. "Ready for the big leagues, Jordan?" she asked with a grin. "This is going to be a lot more crowded than a little cell phone store!"
