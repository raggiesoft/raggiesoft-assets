# Part 6: Truth or Dare

They returned to the Brooks' house that evening, tired, laden with shopping bags, but buzzing with the energy of a successful mission. After stowing their new treasures, they all collapsed in their fortress in the basement, full and happy, with no classes or work to worry about.

"Okay, I'm officially too tired to move, but too hyped up to sleep," Madison announced, sitting up from her air mattress. "This is a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity for a massive game of Truth or Dare!"

The group sat in a big circle on the floor, the air buzzing with playful energy.

"Okay, Mark, truth or dare?" Madison began. "Dare, obviously," he shot back with a grin. "I dare you to do your best impression of Dr. Destruction cutting a promo about conquering the kitchen." Mark jumped to his feet, puffing out his chest and launching into a hilariously over-the-top, gravelly-voiced monologue about how the refrigerator would "feel his wrath," which had the entire group in stitches.

It was Mark's turn. "Zoe, truth or dare?" "Dare," she said, a small, competitive smile on her face. "I dare you to give a promo, as the refrigerator, responding to Dr. Destruction's challenge." Zoe stood, and in a surprisingly deep and booming voice, delivered a promo about the "ice-cold fury" and "chilling power" of the Elite, promising that Dr. Destruction's reign would be put on ice. The performance was so unexpected and brilliant that everyone was crying with laughter.

"Lauren, truth or dare?" Zoe asked, her analytical glint returning. "Truth," Lauren said, feeling brave. "Who is a better kisser: your ex-boyfriend, or Jordan?" A chorus of "Oooohs" went through the room. Lauren blushed a deep crimson but answered without hesitation. "Jordan. Not even a contest."

It was Lauren’s turn. "Okay, Aubrie! Truth or dare!" "Dare!" Aubrie said, her eyes sparkling. "I dare you," Lauren said, her voice full of playful mischief, "to describe your perfect first date... with Jordan."

Aubrie’s blush deepened, but she leaned into the dare. "Well," she began dreamily, "it would start with a quiet dinner somewhere... and then maybe we'd go to the observatory... and it definitely wouldn't *end* with just a kiss on the cheek..."

A mischievous, boundary-pushing glint appeared in Aubrie's eyes as she scanned the circle, clearly formulating a dare that would take the game to an entirely new level. Before she could speak, Hannah held up her hands, cutting her off.

"Whoa, whoa, hold on there, missy!" Hannah said with a playful but firm laugh, pointing a finger at Aubrie. "I can see that look in your eye. I don't know what you're about to say, but I have a feeling we're all better off not finding out."

Aubrie's face bloomed into a deep crimson blush, but she immediately adopted a look of wide-eyed, perfect innocence. "Who, me?" she mouthed, looking as if she wouldn't hurt a fly.

Everyone—except for Jordan, who just looked confused by the sudden halt in the game—saw right through the act. They knew Hannah had just expertly diffused a situation that was about to get much more complicated.
