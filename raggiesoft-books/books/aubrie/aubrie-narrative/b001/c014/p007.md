# Part 7: The Serious Talk

Hannah's suggestion to watch a movie was met with a wave of relieved laughter, breaking the sexually charged tension that had begun to build. Hannah looked at Jordan and Aubrie, her expression gentle but serious.

"Hey, you two," she said quietly. "Can I talk to you for a second? In private."

The three of them slipped away into one of the empty upstairs bedrooms, closing the door behind them. The mood shifted instantly from public fun to private intimacy.

"Aubrie, be honest with me," Hannah began, her voice free of judgment. "What were you going to dare someone to do?"

Aubrie blushed, looking at her feet. "I was... I was going to dare Madison to strip," she admitted quietly. The unspoken cascade of events hung in the air between them: Madison's turn would have come next, and in the heat of the moment, the dare for Jordan to have sex with Aubrie would have been almost inevitable, if not immediately then in a few turns at most.

Hannah nodded slowly, her fears confirmed. She then turned her kind, steady gaze to Jordan. "And Jordan... what would you have done if someone had dared you two to do that? To have sex, right there in the living room?"

He looked at her, his answer simple and utterly honest. "I would have done it," he said. "I trust you guys."

Hannah's heart ached. It was the answer she was afraid of. She moved closer, her voice soft but firm. "I know you do, Jordan. And that's what scares me. We all love you so much, but you trust us *so much* that you might agree to something in a silly game that you're not really ready for. A first time... something that important... it should be special, and private, and a deliberate choice made between you and the person you're with. Not because someone dared you to. That's not fair to you. We could hurt you without ever meaning to."

A look of dawning horror crossed Aubrie's face. She hadn't been thinking about the consequences, only the excitement and the deepening of their bond. She realized with a sickening jolt that she had almost used the social pressure of a game to push Jordan into a monumental life event, a mistake that could have shattered the very trust she was trying to build. She felt deeply shaken.

"You're right," Aubrie whispered, her voice trembling slightly. "I'm so sorry, Jordan. I wasn't thinking."

"I'm not mad, Aubrie," Hannah said, putting an arm around her friend's shoulder. "I'm not. I just... we have to be so careful. We have to protect him, even from our own stupid ideas sometimes." She pulled both of them into a tight, three-way hug. "We're a family. We have to look out for each other."

When they returned to the living room, the rest of the group was already debating which movie to project onto the wall. Jordan and Aubrie rejoined the happy chaos, but it took a moment for them to fully re-engage, their minds still processing the weight of the conversation they just had. A new, more mature understanding of their responsibilities had just settled into place.
