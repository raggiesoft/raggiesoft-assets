# Part 1: A Return to the Old Lady

The Sunday after Thanksgiving was crisp and cool. Daniel Brooks pulled his minivan up to the second-floor entrance of Bennett Hall, and the eight friends began the process of unloading their bags, their laughter echoing in the quiet afternoon. They were happy, relaxed, and glowing from a holiday spent in a true sanctuary.

As they were transferring Jordan from the van into his wheelchair, a figure walking down the path from the library froze. It was Tom.

He stared, his mind struggling to process the scene before him. There was Lauren, looking happier and more at peace than he had ever seen her. And next to her, laughing and easily interacting with the group, was Sarah. His Sarah. His two ex-girlfriends, together, part of this bizarre, happy little family. And at the center of it all, the person they were all orbiting, was Jordan. Tom felt a surge of bitter disbelief. How could they all be so happy? How could Jordan, of all people, be the one they all loved?

Jordan saw him. He saw the confusion and rage warring on Tom's face. The old Jordan would have flinched, would have looked away, would have made himself smaller. But the Jordan who had returned from Thanksgiving was not the same one who had left.

He met Tom’s gaze from across the walkway, his expression calm and steady. Then, with deliberate grace, he turned to Lauren, who was helping him adjust a bag on his lap.

"Lauren," he said softly. She looked at him, and he leaned forward, capturing her lips in a gentle but confident kiss. She responded instantly, a soft, happy smile on her face as they parted.

Next, he turned to Sarah, who stood on his other side. He simply looked at her, and she, understanding completely, leaned down. He kissed her too, a gesture of shared healing and new beginnings.

Finally, he turned to his girlfriend, Aubrie Jean. This kiss was different. It was longer, deeper, a quiet declaration of the profound love that anchored their entire family.

After kissing all three women, Jordan looked directly back across the walkway at Tom. His expression wasn't one of anger or triumph. It was one of pure, unshakeable peace. It was a silent message, delivered with devastating clarity: *They are with me. They are happy. They are safe from you. You have no power here anymore.*

Tom stood there, utterly defeated. He couldn't comprehend the scene—the open affection, the lack of jealousy from Aubrie, the genuine happiness on Lauren's and Sarah's faces. It was a world that didn't operate by his rules of possession and control. With nothing to say and no power to wield, he simply turned and scurried away, a bitter memory fading into the distance.

Madison watched him go and let out a satisfied sigh. "Well," she said to the group. "I don't think he'll be bothering us again."

With a final wave to Daniel, the eight of them entered the upper lobby of The Old Lady, their fortress, more united than ever.
