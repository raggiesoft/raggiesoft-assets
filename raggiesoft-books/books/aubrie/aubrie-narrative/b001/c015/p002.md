# Part 2: A Change of Plans

The energy inside Suite 214 was electric. Fresh off their silent, definitive victory over Tom, the group was buzzing as they began to unpack from the Thanksgiving break. They were excitedly discussing their plans for the upcoming Winter Break, which was now just a few weeks away. The original idea had been to get a large, extended-stay hotel room for the four non-local members of the group.

It was Zoe who cleared her throat, her expression serious and business-like, which immediately silenced the room. "Okay, everyone," she began. "I have a logistical announcement to make regarding our plans for Winter Break."

She paused for effect. "I have officially canceled my research into extended-stay hotels. That plan is scrapped."

A wave of confusion and alarm washed over the group. Lauren sat up straight. "Scrapped? What do you mean? What about Jordan?"

"Yeah, Zoe, what gives?" Madison demanded, her protective instincts flaring. "We promised him! We promised he wouldn't have to go home to his mother. That was the whole point!"

Zoe held up a hand, a small, knowing smile playing on her lips. "The plan is off because there has been a significant development. My parents are waiting downstairs in the lobby. I'm going to go get them. They have something to tell you all."

Before anyone could ask another question, Zoe slipped out of the room and headed for the elevator. A few minutes later, she returned, holding the suite door open for Daniel and Laura Brooks, who entered with warm smiles.

"We thought this part of the conversation was better had in person," Daniel said, his eyes twinkling as he and Laura took in the scene of their extended family.

"Zoe is right," Laura said gently, her gaze soft and kind. "The hotel plan is off. Because we have a better offer."

Daniel took a deep breath, a huge, excited smile spreading across his face. "Your mother and I... we've been planning to move for a while now," he explained. "Our house on Geneva Street is wonderful, but with all our kids grown and out, it's more space than we need. We've been looking for something smaller for us, but with enough land to... well, to build on."

He looked directly at Jordan, his expression full of a father's love. "We just closed on it last week. It's a large plot of land, out in the rural part of the county, off U.S. 11 between Christiansburg and Radford. We're offering you all the family and guest wing. We're going to build a new home, a compound, for all of us. Together. From the ground up."

For a moment, there was stunned silence. Then, the room erupted. It was a joyous explosion of happy tears, laughter, and disbelief.

"Are you serious?! Our own HOUSE?!" Madison shrieked, launching herself at Laura for a hug.

Jordan sat in his chair, speechless, as Hannah and Sarah wrapped him in a hug from either side. He looked from Daniel's proud face to Zoe, who was now being crushed by the other girls. She gave him a triumphant wink.

His Winter Break wasn't going to be spent in a sterile hotel room. He was going home. And this time, they were all going to build it together.
