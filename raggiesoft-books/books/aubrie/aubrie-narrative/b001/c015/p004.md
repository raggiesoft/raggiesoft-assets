# Part 4: A Semester in Motion

The weeks between Thanksgiving and the end of the semester settled into a comfortable, productive rhythm. With his new mPhone providing constant cellular service, Jordan was always connected. The "Jordan Comms" group chat, once a lifeline for bridging WiFi gaps, transformed into a constant, real-time river of their shared lives—a blend of crucial logistics, inside jokes, and funny photos.

The evolution of the chat led to a new protocol, proposed by Zoe one evening after a logistical message from Lauren was nearly lost in a flurry of memes from Madison.

"We have a communication efficiency problem," Zoe had announced, ever the pragmatist. "The chat is becoming cluttered. I propose a new rule: any message pertaining to a handoff, location change, or other time-sensitive information must begin with the tag \[LOGISTICS\] in all caps. This will allow us to scan for critical updates."

The idea was adopted immediately and unanimously.

The system now hummed along with even greater efficiency. One afternoon, the group chat buzzed.

Jordan Comms: \[LOGISTICS\] Mark signing on. With J in the library, 3rd floor. Working on our Freud paper.

In the library, the two of them worked seamlessly. For the first time in his academic life, Jordan could dedicate his full mental energy to his studies, and his grades reflected it. He was thriving.

The relay system was flawless. After their last astronomy lab of the semester, Aubrie pushed Jordan's chair out into the cold December air where Zoe was waiting for the handoff.

Jordan Comms: \[LOGISTICS\] Aubrie signing off. Handoff to Zoe complete at science building.

After her goodbye hug with Jordan, Aubrie headed off to her class. Zoe stepped in for the hello hug. "Ready to go get coffee before our review session?"

Jordan Comms: \[LOGISTICS\] Zoe signing on. Getting coffee, then heading back to Bennett.

The fear of his mother was a distant echo, a ghost from another lifetime. Jordan’s world was no longer about survival; it was about living. His evenings were filled not with dread, but with the quiet joy of simply being with his family. His only "contention" now was the loving, playful kind with the one Aubrie he ever wanted to contend with: Aubrie Jean Kelly.

The final push of the semester, finals week, was upon them. But there was no panic. There was only focus, teamwork, and the promise of a long, happy, and well-deserved Winter Break waiting for them on the other side. They were almost there.
