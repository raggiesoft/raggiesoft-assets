# Part 7: The Final Exam

The day before his very last final exam of the semester—for Astronomy—his fever finally broke. He woke up feeling weak and washed out, but his mind was clear for the first time in nearly a week.

On Friday morning, he felt steady enough to make the trip. Hannah, his designated buddy for this final mission, pushed his wheelchair across campus to the CPI Testing Center. The sun was bright but the December air was cold.

They arrived at the entrance to the building. This was the handoff point, where he would go on alone. As was their ritual, he turned his chair to face her and opened his arms for the goodbye hug.

"Good luck in there," Hannah said, leaning down to give him a firm, grounding hug. "You've got this. I'll be right out here when you're done."

She pulled back as he nodded, feeling more centered. She took out her phone to update the group.

Jordan Comms: \[LOGISTICS\] He's here. Dropping him off at the Testing Center for his last final now!

Jordan took a deep breath and wheeled himself alone through the glass doors. This final academic challenge was one he had to face by himself, but he was fortified by the love of his family waiting just outside. He presented his ID, was shown to the accessible desk, and the proctor handed him the exam.

This wasn't just a test of his knowledge of astrophysics. It was a testament to his resilience. He looked at the first question—a complex problem about stellar evolution—and a small, confident smile touched his lips. He knew this. Every correct answer he bubbled in was a repudiation of his mother's control.

An hour later, he handed in his completed exam and wheeled himself back out into the bright, cold sun. Hannah immediately stood up from the bench, a huge, proud grin on her face. She sent a quick text as she walked to meet him. Mark was present with her.

Jordan Comms: \[LOGISTICS\] And he's out! The last final of the semester is officially DONE! Mission accomplished. Heading back to Bennett!

Jordan Comms: \[LOGISTICS\] Mark signing on. With Jordan, final exam complete. Hannah still with us.

She met him at the bottom of the ramp. "So?" she asked. "How'd it go, genius?"

"I think it went well," Jordan said, a feeling of profound triumph washing over him. He held his arms out.

Hannah beamed, wrapping him in a welcoming "hello" hug, the second half of their ritual complete. "I knew you would," she said, her voice full of pride.

As she took the handles of his chair to head back to the dorm, he felt a final sense of closure. The semester was over. He had faced down his past, battled a sickness he should never have had, and succeeded on his own terms. His future health, from this day forward, was firmly in his own hands. Mark walked alongside them, proud as ever for the man he was willing to consider his brother.
