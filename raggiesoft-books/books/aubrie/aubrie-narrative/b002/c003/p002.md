# Part 2: An Afternoon in Radford

The Maroon Express coach bus arrived at the bustling CPI Transit Center at 2:15 PM, right on schedule. The Group of 8 disembarked and, with the practiced efficiency of seasoned commuters, made their way to the bay for the Radford Connector bus.

Jordan Comms: \[LOGISTICS\] Zoe signing on for the group. Arrived at CPI Transit Center. Transferring to the Radford Connector now. ETA at McConnell Transit Center is approx. 2:45 PM.

The ride to Radford was a direct, thirty-minute trip with no other stops. The smaller city bus hummed along, taking them to the McConnell Transit Center on the University of Radford campus. From there, it was one final, ten-minute ride on the Wadsworth Street bus into a quiet, tree-lined residential neighborhood.

Sarah led them to a modest, well-kept single-story home. She knocked on the door, and it was opened by her father, Peter Black. He was a quieter man, with a kind, intelligent light in his eyes.

"Sarah, sweetheart. It's so good to see you," he said, his voice warm as he hugged his daughter. "And these must be your friends. Please, come in out of the cold."

The welcome was calm and gentle. The group settled into a living room filled with books and art, a comfortable, scholarly space. Peter's primary focus was on his daughter, but he made a point to engage with her friends, asking Jordan and Aubrie about their astrophysics studies with genuine curiosity.

After they had been chatting for a while, Peter looked around at the entire group, his gaze finally settling on Sarah.

"Sarah told me what happened with Tom," he said, his voice full of a deep, quiet gratitude. "And she told me how you all took her in, no questions asked. I want to thank you for that. For giving my daughter a safe place to land when she needed it most." His heartfelt words filled the quiet room, another parent validating the incredible found family his daughter had discovered.

As the afternoon light began to fade, the topic of dinner came up.

"Dad, you don't have to cook for all nine of us," Sarah said kindly. "Why don't we go to that little Italian place you like downtown? Our treat."
