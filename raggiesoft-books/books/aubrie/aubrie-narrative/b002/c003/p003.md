# Part 3: Dinner in Radford

The Italian restaurant, "Luigi's Trattoria," was a short walk from the McConnell Transit Center. Since Peter's sedan couldn't possibly accommodate the entire group, the decision was made to take the Wadsworth Street bus back towards the Transit Center and then walk a couple of blocks to the restaurant.

As they waited at the bus stop, the group collectively pooled fifty cents for Peter's fare. When the bus arrived, they all hopped on, finding seats scattered throughout. The short ride was filled with casual chatter, the easy companionship of a group comfortable in each other's presence.

Luigi's Trattoria was a warm, inviting space with checkered tablecloths, dim lighting, and the comforting murmur of conversations. The Group of 8 settled around a large round table with Sarah and her father.

Menus were perused, and soon orders were placed for pasta dishes, pizzas, and steaming plates of lasagna. The atmosphere was relaxed, a comfortable blend of the group's usual banter and a respectful acknowledgment of being guests in Sarah's hometown.

As they waited for their food, Peter cleared his throat gently. "So," he began, looking around the table at each of Sarah's friends, "Sarah has told me a little about everything that happened at the beginning of the semester." He paused, his gaze softening as he looked at his daughter. "It was a difficult time for her, and for me, being so far away."

Sarah reached out and placed a reassuring hand on her father's arm. "But it's okay now, Dad. These guys... they were amazing."

Peter's gaze returned to the group. "From the bottom of my heart, I want to thank you all for taking her in. For being there for her when she needed it most. Knowing she had such good friends made all the difference to me, even miles away."

A thoughtful silence fell over the table. Then, Sarah spoke, her voice clear and steady. "And you know, Dad, it wasn't just about needing a place to stay. What happened... with Tom... Lauren was the one he was having the affair with."

A ripple of understanding went through the group. Lauren nodded slightly, a quiet affirmation of her difficult past.

"But," Sarah continued, her voice gaining strength, "what could have been a really bad situation actually brought us all closer. We're not just friends anymore, Dad. We're... we're like one big, slightly chaotic family." She looked around at her friends, a genuine smile spreading across her face. Heads nodded in agreement.

Peter watched them, his eyes filled with a mixture of gratitude and admiration. "Well," he said, a small smile touching his lips, "it sounds like Sarah has found herself quite the family away from family."

The waiter arrived with their food, the aroma filling the air and breaking the emotional moment with welcome practicality. As plates were passed around, the conversation flowed easily throughout the meal, ranging from college classes to funny dorm stories. The bond between Sarah and her father was evident, a quiet, steady love that had clearly provided a strong foundation for her. And around the table, the Group of 8 shared knowing glances, their own unique, unexpected family strengthened by shared experiences and the unwavering support they offered one another.

As the dinner drew to a close, Jordan discreetly passed another fifty cents to Sarah. "For your dad's bus fare back home," he murmured.

Sarah nodded and, as they were getting ready to leave Luigi's, she slipped the coins to her father. "Thanks for dinner, Dad. We'll see you soon."

Peter hugged Sarah tightly. "You take care, sweetheart. And thank you all again," he said, looking at the group with genuine warmth before heading to the bus stop for his ride home.

The Group of 8 then walked back towards the McConnell Transit Center, ready to catch the Radford Connector back to CPI and begin the final leg of their long but fulfilling day.
