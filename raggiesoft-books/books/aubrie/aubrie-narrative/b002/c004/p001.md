# Part 1: The Christmas Morning Firsts 

Jordan woke on Christmas morning to the sound of his friends whispering excitedly to each other. He followed the Group of 8 upstairs, where the main living room was aglow with the lights from a massive, beautifully decorated Christmas tree.

As the others rushed toward the tree, Jordan stopped, his gaze fixed on the large, built-in bookshelf that took up one entire wall. Lined up perfectly along the thick main shelf, held up by heavy, decorative stocking holders, was a row of seventeen large, fuzzy socks. He stared at them, completely perplexed.

Laura Brooks, noticing his confusion, came over with a warm smile. "This one's yours, Jordan," she said, gently taking his hand and placing it on a soft, fuzzy stocking with his name neatly embroidered in gold thread.

He just stared at it. "I don't understand," he said quietly. "Why... why are there socks on the bookshelf?"

A moment of stunned silence fell over his friends as they realized the depth of what he didn't know.

Madison was the first to recover, kneeling beside his chair. "They're stockings, Jordan!" she explained gently. "It's a tradition. You hang them up, and a man named Santa Claus is supposed to come and put little presents inside them."

Jordan's brow furrowed. "Who's Santa Claus?"

Daniel Brooks stepped forward, placing a comforting hand on Jordan's shoulder. "He's just a fun story for the holiday, son," he explained. "A symbol of giving and magic. Go on, see what's inside yours."

Hesitantly, Jordan reached into the stocking. He pulled out a new pair of comfortable wheelchair gloves, a booklet on constellations, and his favorite candy bar. They were gifts, given for no other reason than to make him happy. It was a completely foreign concept.

The present opening that followed was a slow, guided discovery. When handed his first brightly wrapped gift, he simply held it, unsure what to do until Aubrie gently said, "You get to rip the paper off." The joy of giving was an even greater revelation. As he watched his friends' faces light up when they opened the small presents he had picked out for them, he felt a warmth spread through his chest that was more powerful than any heater.

The morning culminated in a huge, festive breakfast. Jordan sat at the table, surrounded by the loud, happy chaos of his chosen family, and felt a sense of peace he had never known was possible on this day.
