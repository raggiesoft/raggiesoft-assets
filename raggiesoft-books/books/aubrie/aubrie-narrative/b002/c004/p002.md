# Part 2: A Christmas Education

The massive breakfast was in full swing, the sounds of sixteen people (Ben Carter was with Chloe's family for the morning) talking, laughing, and passing platters of food filling the Brooks' home with a happy roar. Jordan sat at the table, quietly taking it all in—the stockings, the thoughtfully chosen presents, the sheer, unconditional joy of it all. It was completely new territory.

He finally turned to Aubrie, who was sitting beside him. "So... is this it?" he asked, his voice low and full of genuine curiosity. "Is this what Christmas is? Just... presents and a big breakfast?"

The question, so earnest and innocent, caught the attention of those sitting nearby.

"Oh, honey, no!" Madison said with a huge, cheerful grin from across the table. "This is just the beginning! There are Christmas movies! It is a federal law that you have to watch *Elf* and *A Christmas Story*."

"And football!" Mark added. "There are always three football games on."

One of Zoe's younger brothers piped up. "And you have to build a gingerbread house! And we have to make sure we leave cookies out tonight!"

Jordan looked completely baffled. "Leave cookies out for who?"

It was Laura Brooks who answered, her voice full of warmth as she explained the secular myth of Santa Claus, framing him as a symbol of generosity and magic for children. "It's all just part of the fun, Jordan," she concluded. "It's not about any one thing. It's about all of it. It's an excuse for family to be together, to slow down, to eat good food, and to just... be happy in each other's company."

"Statistically," Zoe chimed in with her usual deadpan delivery, "it's a period of increased retail spending and a calculated spike in domestic electrical grid usage." Everyone at the table laughed.

Jordan looked around at the loud, happy chaos. His whole life, December 25th had just been another day, quiet and tense, marked only by the fact that the mail didn't run. He never knew it was supposed to be *this*. This warmth. This community. This much love.

A new, hopeful desire filled him. He looked around the table at his entire chosen family. "Can we... can we do all of it?" he asked, his voice full of wonder. "The movies, and the games you guys were talking about... and the food... all of it? I want... I want more of this."

A resounding, joyful "YES!" echoed from around the table.

Daniel Brooks reached over and clapped him firmly on the shoulder. "Son," he said with a proud grin. "That's the plan. We're just getting started."
