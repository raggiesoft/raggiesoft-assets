# Part 3: The Board Game Gauntlet

After the lingering joy of the Christmas breakfast, Daniel Brooks clapped his hands together, a mischievous glint in his eye. "Alright, everyone!" he announced. "Time for a new Brooks family tradition! The First Annual Brooks Family Christmas Board Game Championship!"

A cheer went up from the younger Brooks siblings, while Mark, Caleb, and Owen exchanged knowing grins. The living room and the expanded dining area quickly transformed into a flurry of activity as various board games were pulled from shelves and closets.

True to form, the centerpiece of the afternoon became the intense (and highly theatrical) game of Warlords of the Sphere being played by Mark and the Brooks brothers. They set up their world map on the large dining table, meticulously arranging their plastic armies.

From the very first roll of the dice, the drama began. Caleb, with a dramatic sigh, accused Mark of "aggressive territorial expansion" after Mark strategically placed several units in North America.

"It's called sound tactical deployment, Caleb!" Mark retorted, puffing out his chest. "Something you wouldn't understand with your... questionable grasp of geopolitical strategy!"

Owen then chimed in, feigning betrayal as Caleb formed a temporary alliance with Mark against him. "I thought we were brothers!" he exclaimed, clutching his chest dramatically. "Stabbed in the back on Christmas Day! For shame!"

The "battle" raged on, filled with exaggerated gasps of shock, boasts of impending victory, and the strategic deployment (and occasional, perfectly legal but utterly ruthless) of game pieces. They pretended to call each other names – "You land-grabbing fiend!", "You backstabbing rogue!", "You strategic simpleton!" – all delivered with booming voices and theatrical gestures that had the younger kids giggling and Jordan watching with wide-eyed amusement.

He was starting to understand the dynamic between them. It was competition, yes, but it was also a performance, a way for them to connect and entertain each other. He could see the genuine smiles that flickered across their faces between the dramatic pronouncements.

Nearby, Laura, Hannah, and Sarah were engaged in a much quieter but no less intense game of Lexicographer's Duel, their concentration occasionally broken by the uproarious shouts from the Warlords of the Sphere table. Madison and the youngest Brooks siblings were having a blast with Scribble Scramble, their laughter echoing through the house as they tried to guess wildly inaccurate drawings.

And at a card table set to an accessible height, Jordan was taking his first tentative steps on a different kind of journey. With Aubrie patiently guiding him, he was playing Odyssey: The Great Journey. He had chosen it specifically, drawn to the winding, colorful path on the board that promised a whole lifetime of choices—choices he had never been allowed to make.

For the first time, he got to spin the colorful wheel and decide to "Go to College." He chose a little plastic car and put a peg in the driver's seat. He landed on a space and drew a card. "Congratulations! You're getting married!" Aubrie read with a smile, and he carefully added another peg to his car. A few spaces later, another card: "It's a baby girl! Add a daughter." A small, genuine smile touched his lips as he placed a tiny pink peg into the car next to his.

For a boy whose life had been dictated by the decisions of others, this simple game, with its simulated milestones and branching paths, felt like a profound and hopeful revelation. Nearby, Chloe and Ben were laughing as they argued over the rent for a property in their own game of Metropolis Magnate, but Jordan was lost in his own little world, a world where he was finally in the driver's seat.
