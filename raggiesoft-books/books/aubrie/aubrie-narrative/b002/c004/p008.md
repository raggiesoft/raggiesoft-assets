# Part 8: A Feast for a Family

The afternoon sun began to dip below the horizon, and the family, full of laughter from the board game championship, transitioned into the final phase of the holiday: the movie marathon. They were deep into "Holiday House-Sitter 2: Lost in the Big City," roaring with laughter as the smart-aleck kid on screen hurled a series of bricks from a rooftop at the bumbling burglars below.

In the middle of the scene, the incredible aroma of the feast being prepared upstairs seemed to intensify, and Laura Brooks appeared at the bottom of the stairs. "Dinner's ready!" she called out over the sound of a brick hitting a bad guy's face. "Come and get it before these boys," she gestured to her sons, "eat the whole turkey!"

With a scramble for the remote, the movie was paused. As everyone began to stretch and head for the stairs, one of Zoe's younger brothers asked, "Can we watch number three after dinner?"

"NO!" a chorus of eight voices from the visiting friends yelled in perfect, mock-serious unison, a response that made the Brooks siblings howl with laughter.

"There is 'Holiday House-Sitter,' and there is 'Holiday House-Sitter 2: Lost in the Big City,'" Madison declared with the feigned authority of a seasoned film critic. "The series ends there. That is the rule. We do not speak of the others."

Upstairs, the dining room table had been extended with a folding table to create one massive, continuous surface, laden with a magnificent Christmas feast. Jordan sat at the head of the table, a place of honor, with Aubrie and Madison on either side of him.

Before anyone could dig in, Daniel Brooks stood up, raising his glass of sparkling cider. The room quieted. He looked around the table at the seventeen faces—his wife, his seven children, and the eight incredible young adults who had become a part of their lives.

"I just want to say," he began, his voice thick with emotion, "looking around this table... this is what it's all about. To family." He raised his glass higher. "All of it. Merry Christmas, everyone."

"Merry Christmas!" echoed around the table.

As platters of roasted turkey, honey-glazed ham, stuffing, and a dozen other dishes were passed around, Jordan looked at the scene unfolding around him—the laughter, the shared stories, the easy affection. This was Christmas. It wasn't a quiet, tense day to be endured. It was this. This noise. This joyous chaos. This community. This was love, in its loudest, most beautiful, and most welcoming form. It was the greatest gift he had ever received.
