# Part 1: The Plan and the Journey

On the morning of December 26th, after a wonderful Christmas, Jordan, feeling brave and armed with new gift cards, approached the group. "I'd like to try the mall again," he said, his voice resolute. "I want to show I can do it."

Madison immediately had a solution. "Let's not go to New River Mall. It's too big. We should go to the Bluewater Town Plaza in my hometown," she suggested. "It's smaller, enclosed, and way less chaotic. It might be better for your autistic needs."

Jordan agreed instantly. He also insisted on taking the bus, a clear statement of his growing desire for independence. Daniel, hearing the plan, nodded in approval but added a layer of security. Caleb and Owen quickly offered to tag along. "We'd love to hang out with Zoe and see where Madison's from," Caleb said casually, the official excuse covering their unofficial role as protective older brothers, a fact only they knew. Hannah and Mark, meanwhile, decided to have a quiet couple's day at the house.

After a big breakfast with the whole Group of 17, the smaller Bluewater-bound party—Jordan, Aubrie, Madison, Zoe, Lauren, Caleb, and Owen—said their goodbyes. Madison, after checking the bus schedules and seeing they were running on a Saturday schedule, sent a quick text to her mother.

Madison: Hey Mom! We're bringing the crew to Bluewater! Can you, Dad, Bob & Em meet us at the Town Plaza for lunch around 11:15? Diane Scott: Of course, honey! We'd love to! See you then!

The seven of them made the short walk to the Falling Branch Park & Ride.

Jordan Comms: \[LOGISTICS\] Zoe signing on for the Bluewater 7. At Falling Branch, waiting for the 9:45 AM Maroon Express.

The large coach bus arrived on time. Caleb and Owen purchased their Express Day Passes while the others showed their CPI IDs. They settled in for the comfortable hour-long ride, arriving at the Bluewater Transit Center at 10:45 AM, and made a seamless transfer to the waiting Orange Line bus for the final leg of their trip to the mall.
