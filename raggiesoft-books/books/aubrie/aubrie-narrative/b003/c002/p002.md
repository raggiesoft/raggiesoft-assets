# Part 2: Operation "File First"

That Saturday, Daniel Brooks drove Jordan and Aubrie to a quiet classroom building on the CPI campus that was serving as a site for the Volunteer Income Tax Assistance (VITA) program. Before they went inside, Daniel knelt in front of Jordan's chair, his expression serious but kind.

"Jordan, listen to me for a second," he said. "The person in there is an IRS-certified volunteer. For them to file this correctly, they have to use the name and social security number that are on your official documents. That means they are going to say the name 'Aubrey.' I need you to be prepared for that. It's just a name on a form to them. It doesn't change who you are. Can you handle that?"

Jordan took a deep, steeling breath. He looked at Aubrie, who gave his hand a supportive squeeze. He nodded at Daniel. "I can handle it."

Inside, a trained student volunteer greeted them. Daniel handed over a thick, perfectly organized folder. "We have everything you should need," he said. "W-2 from his campus job, 1098-T, all his financial aid award letters, and his bank statements."

The volunteer sat down with them and began looking through the paperwork. "Okay, Mr. Fuller... Aubrey J. Fuller?" she asked, looking at her screen.

Jordan tensed, a flicker of the meltdown from Kelsey's office threatening at the edges of his mind, but Daniel's warning had prepared him. He just gave a firm nod.

The volunteer continued, impressed by the thorough documentation. "Based on this financial aid and your W-2," she said, "you absolutely meet the criteria to claim your own exemption. You provide more than half of your own support. We can file this for you today, electronically."

She quickly entered the data into the tax software and turned the screen toward him. "I just need you to verify this information and then sign the electronic authorization forms."

Jordan reviewed the screen, took the stylus, and signed his name. With a final click from the preparer, the return was sent. A moment later, a small box popped up on the screen: RETURN ACCEPTED BY IRS.

It was a quiet, administrative moment, but it felt like a massive victory. They thanked the volunteer and left the room. As they walked out into the cool February air, Daniel clapped Jordan on the back.

"Well done, son," he said with a proud grin. "Checkmate. She can't claim you if you've already claimed yourself."
