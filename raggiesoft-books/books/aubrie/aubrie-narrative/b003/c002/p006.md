# Part 6: The Briefing

While Daniel, Laura, Kelsey, Jordan, and Aubrie were in the office, the phones of the other five members of the Group of 8 buzzed in unison with a notification from the "Jordan Comms" chat. The message made their blood run cold.

Daniel B: \[URGENT\] Group of 8, I need everyone to meet me at the gazebo on the main quad in 15 minutes. Immediate meeting.

A jolt of pure fear shot through them. Daniel *never* used the chat, let alone the \[URGENT\] tag. Something was seriously wrong. They dropped everything and hurried to the rendezvous point.

They arrived at the gazebo to find Daniel and Laura Brooks waiting with Jordan and Aubrie, their faces grim. A moment later, the rest of the group started to show up, their faces pale and tear-streaked. The group's fear intensified.

"Thank you all for coming so quickly," Daniel began, his voice low and serious. "There's been a development."

Laura took a deep breath, her voice tight with anger. "Jordan's mother has filed a petition with the court in Norfolk to have him declared incompetent. She's trying to get guardianship over him."

A collective gasp went through the group, their shock quickly turning to protective fury.

"Our lawyer is confident it's a meritless case," Daniel continued, "but we have to treat the threat seriously. We also have to assume she knows where our house is. The sanctuary is no longer secure. So, we're moving to a new plan, effective immediately."

He looked at the three original occupants of Room 214. "Jordan will be staying at the Hotel at Poly. For his safety, he can't be alone. Aubrie, you will be with him." Aubrie nodded, her hand already finding Jordan's. "Madison," Daniel continued, "they have requested you come along, to keep the 'Room 214' family together."

Madison didn't hesitate. "Of course," she said firmly. "Whatever he needs."

"Understood," Zoe said, already processing the logistics. "The three of us in 216 will stay in the dorm. We'll keep an eye on their room, make it look occupied." Mark and Sarah nodded in agreement.

"Your mother and I are so grateful for how you all set up your class schedules," Laura said, her eyes full of appreciation. "That foresight, and especially you two having identical schedules," she nodded at Jordan and Aubrie, "is our biggest advantage right now."

Aubrie looked at Jordan, her voice fierce with promise. "Never alone means never alone," she said. "That includes waiting outside every class. And if you need the restroom, we're using the family restroom, even if I'm the only one who has to go."

"And if there isn't one nearby, you know I've got your back, man," Mark added, looking at Jordan with steady reassurance. "Just like at the mall."

The promise of their protection was absolute. For Daniel and Laura, no price was too high to keep Jordan safe.

"Okay," Daniel said, his tone all business now. "The plan is set. Let's go get them packed."

The entire, determined group walked together back toward Bennett Hall to execute the first phase of their new security protocol.
