# Part 8: The Promise of Academic Help

That evening, the trio from Room 214 was settled into their new, sterile fortress at the Hotel at Poly. The initial adrenaline of the move had worn off, replaced by a heavy quiet. Jordan sat at the small desk, staring blankly at the syllabus for his calculus class, the words blurring together. The weight of the impending court battle felt like a physical presence in the room, threatening to suffocate him.

Laura Brooks, who had stayed with them while Daniel and Owen went to grab some dinner, noticed his distress. She pulled a chair up beside him. "What are you thinking about, honey?" she asked gently.

"My classes," Jordan whispered, his voice still hoarse. "Astrophysics, Calculus... they're hard. How am I supposed to focus on any of it with... with this happening? She's going to say I failed. She'll tell the judge I couldn't handle it."

Just then, Daniel returned with bags of takeout. He overheard Jordan's last sentence, and his expression hardened with resolve. He set the food down and came to stand behind Jordan's chair, his hands resting firmly on his shoulders.

"You are not going to fail," Daniel said, his voice a low, steady promise. "We are not going to let you."

He looked at Laura, then back at Jordan and Aubrie. "First thing tomorrow morning, we are going back to the Office of Educational Accessibility. We're getting you a university-provided note-taker for every single one of your classes. Then we are going to the Student Success Center, and we are signing you up for their free tutoring sessions. And if that's not enough," he said, his voice leaving no room for doubt, "we will hire the best private tutors in this town to work with you one-on-one. We will move mountains to make sure you have every single resource this university offers. We want you to pass this semester, Jordan. We want you to prove to that court, and to that woman, that you are the capable adult we all know you are."

Laura reached out and took Jordan's hand, her own expression serious but kind. "But we do this the right way," she added. "We will never cross the line of academic honesty. We won't do the work *for* you. We are going to give you all the tools so *you* can do the work. Proving you're capable means actually *being* capable."

Jordan looked from Daniel's fierce, determined face to Laura's warm, steady one. He had spent his entire life being told he was a burden, his needs an inconvenience. Now, these two people—his real parents—were promising to marshal an entire academic arsenal on his behalf, not to enable him, but to empower him. He nodded, fresh tears of pure, overwhelming gratitude spilling down his cheeks. The fight had just begun, but he already had the best generals in the world on his side.
