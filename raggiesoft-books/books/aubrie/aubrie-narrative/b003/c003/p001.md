# Part 1: The Guardian’s Assessment

The day of the Guardian ad Litem's visit was cold and gray. Jordan, Aubrie, and his lawyer, Ms. Gable, sat on a bench in the hallway outside a reserved library study room, waiting. Jordan's leg was bouncing uncontrollably, and Aubrie held his hand tightly in both of hers. His phone buzzed on his lap.

Jordan Comms: Mark: \[LEGAL\] You got this, J. We're all thinking about you. Madison: \[LEGAL\] Squeeze Aubrie's hand if you need anything. She'll text us. Aubrie: \[LEGAL\] He's terrified but he's being so brave. Ms. Gable is with us. GAL should be here any minute.

A professional woman with a kind but serious face, Ms. Anya Sharma, approached them and introduced herself. Ms. Gable explained that Aubrie would be waiting outside. Aubrie gave Jordan's hand one last, firm squeeze. "I'll be right here. The second you're done."

Inside the small, quiet room, Ms. Sharma began formally. "Thank you for meeting with me. For the record, I need to confirm your identity. Are you Aubrey Jordan Fuller?"

The name, spoken in that official, legal tone, was the key that unlocked the hurricane. Jordan's entire body went rigid. A low, distressed hum vibrated from his chest as he began to rock back and forth in his chair, his hands flying up to grip his own hair. He was gone, lost in a storm of pure, overwhelming terror.

Ms. Gable immediately put a hand on his arm and spoke to the startled GAL. "Ms. Sharma," she said, her voice calm but steel-sharp. "My client's legal name is a documented trauma trigger due to a history of abuse from the petitioner. His name is Jordan. Please refer to him as Jordan."

Ms. Sharma, observing the very real and severe reaction, nodded, making a quick, decisive note on her legal pad. This was powerful evidence. "Of course. My apologies," she said. She waited a moment before speaking again, her voice now much softer. "Jordan? It's Ms. Sharma. We can take as long as you need. You are safe in this room."

It took several minutes for the worst of the meltdown to pass. Once Jordan was still and his breathing had steadied, though he was pale and trembling, the interview began. It proceeded as planned, with Jordan answering every question honestly, his competence and intelligence shining through his exhaustion.

When it was over, Ms. Gable opened the door. Jordan wheeled himself out into the hallway, completely drained.

Aubrie was on her feet instantly. She didn't say a word. She just rushed to him, wrapping him in a fierce, grounding hug, and refused to let go. He buried his face in her shoulder, holding on for dear life.

Aubrie: \[LEGAL\] It's over. He's okay. We're with him.

In the quiet library hallway, surrounded by the love of the one person he trusted most in the world, Jordan slowly began to feel the ground beneath his wheels again.
