# Part 1: The Victory Lap

Wednesday morning in Suite 214 was a world away from the tense, fearful mornings that had preceded the trip to Norfolk. Jordan woke up to the sight of Madison already getting dressed for the day, and Aubrie still laying at his side on Room 214 Bed A. The two of them were still in their birthday suits without a care in the world. The guardianship and conservatorship hearing was over. He won. Jordan was FREE of that woman.

The air was light, filled with the easy chatter of Aubrie, Jordan, and Madison getting ready for class. The shadow of the court hearing had been replaced by the warm glow of victory, and nowhere was that change more evident than in Jordan. The anxious, withdrawn boy was gone; in his place was a young man with a quiet, confident smile on his face, eager to get back to the life he had fought so hard to keep. He got himself fully dressed, only needing help with shoes and socks because he can’t bend down all the way, which Madison (who was already dressed) helped him with because Aubrie was still getting dressed herself.

As the three of them gathered their backpacks, Jordan looked at Aubrie, his eyes full of happy anticipation.

"So..." he said, his voice soft but excited. "The bus ride? Do we have time before class?”

Aubrie’s face broke into a brilliant grin. "A promise is a promise," she declared. "We have time for a short trip before our first class. Let's go."

Hannah beamed. "Keep us updated in the chat! We want to hear all about it!"

The occupants of Room 216 (Lauren, Hannah, and Zoe) joined the occupants of Room 214 for the walk to the dorm lobby where the entire Group of 8 joined up in the Dorm Lobby and walked to the CPI Transit Center together. When they arrived at the bustling hub, Aubrie gestured to the lineup of buses. "Your chariot awaits, my good sir. You pick."

Jordan scanned the options and pointed as a large coach bus pulled into an adjacent bay, its sign cycling through MAROON EXPRESS. "That one," he said with a genuine thrill in his voice. The rest of the group said their goodbyes as Aubrie and Jordan prepared to get on a bus together.

As the others peeled off toward the library, Madison sent the first celebratory text.

Jordan Comms: Madison: \[LOGISTICS\] The eagles are leaving the nest! J&A heading into the transit center for the maiden voyage of the S.S. Freedom! 🚌🎉

Boarding the long-haul coach was a more involved procedure than getting on a standard city bus. The driver, friendly and practiced, operated a noisy, mechanical lift that unfolded from the center door. The process was slow and always drew a few casual stares, but today, Jordan didn't care. Once on board, he transferred to a comfortable coach seat, and the driver stowed his folded wheelchair in the luggage bay below.

As the bus pulled away from the curb and merged smoothly onto the highway, Jordan stared out at the campus rolling by, not with fear, but with a profound sense of peace.

Jordan Comms: Aubrie: \[LOGISTICS\] We're on the Maroon Express! He chose the big coach. Heading to New River Mall Christiansburg.

Mark: Attaboy, Jordan! Enjoy the ride!

Lauren: YAY! So happy for you guys! ❤️

Sarah: Send pictures!

Zoe: Data indicates a positive emotional outcome. The system is working.

Their first stop was the New River Mall Christiansburg transit hub. After the reverse process of retrieving his chair and disembarking, they walked from the express bay over to the clearly marked bay for the local routes.

Jordan Comms: Aubrie: \[LOGISTICS\] Handoff at New River Mall complete. Waiting for Route 1 back to CPI.

Aubrie: \[Image Attached: A selfie of a beaming Jordan and Aubrie in front of the bus bay\]

Hannah: Look at that smile! My heart! 😍

Madison: HE LOOKS SO HAPPY! MISSION ACCOMPLISHED!

A few minutes later, a standard Blue Ridge Transit city bus arrived, its sign reading 1 NORTHBOUND to CPI. The boarding process was a stark and welcome contrast. The driver deployed a simple, quiet ramp, and Jordan rolled right on, parking in the designated accessible spot without having to leave his chair. It was quick and seamless.

The ride back to campus was slower, meandering along the local roads of Business U.S. 460. They passed gas stations, fast-food restaurants, and small businesses—the wonderfully mundane scenery of everyday life.

The bus arrived back at the CPI Transit Center with just enough time for them to walk to their first class, and Aubrie sent one last update.

Jordan Comms: Aubrie: \[LOGISTICS\] And we're back at CPI. Victory lap complete. Heading to class now. He did great!

This trip wasn't just a joyride; it was a victory lap celebrated in real-time by his entire family. He had successfully navigated a multi-leg, multi-agency transit journey, cheered on every step of the way. The fear was gone, replaced by the quiet confidence of a fully-fledged adult, supported by an unshakable digital and emotional fortress.
