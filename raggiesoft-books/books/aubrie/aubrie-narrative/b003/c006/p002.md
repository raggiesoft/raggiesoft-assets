# Part 2: An Unexpected Ally

Their triumphant bus ride left Jordan and Aubrie with just enough time to meet the rest of their friends for their shared Math 111 class. The eight of them took up their usual block of seats in the large lecture hall, a quiet, supportive fortress.

After the lecture on algebraic functions ended, the Group of 8 gathered their things and approached the professor's lectern at the front of the hall.

"Excuse me, Mr. Evans?" Hannah began politely. "We were hoping to speak with you for a moment about the material we all missed on Monday."

Mr. Evans, a kind-faced man in his forties with a weary look in his eyes, turned to the group with an expression of genuine empathy. "Of course," he said warmly. "I received the notice from the Office of Student Support. Please, none of you worry about it at all. What you went through for your friend was more important than any single lecture."

He leaned against the lectern, his voice dropping slightly. "I've been through a few... difficult family court situations myself over the years," he confided, seeing the stress still evident on Jordan's face. "I know how draining they can be. And I understand completely why you all needed to be there to support him. You're good friends."

As they spoke, Jordan, still feeling the lingering tremors of the past week, instinctively reached out his hands to either side. Aubrie, on his left, and Madison, on his right, both took his hands without a second's hesitation. Their hands laced through his, their grips firm and grounding—two steady anchors in the lingering storm of his anxiety. Aubrie felt the connection pass between the three of them and was not jealous in the slightest. She was just glad that her boyfriend was being comforted by people he trusted completely.

"Here's what we'll do," Mr. Evans continued, outlining a simple plan. "My lecture notes are already posted online. I want you all to go over them together this evening. If any of you have questions after that, you can all come to my office hours tomorrow, and I'll walk you through any problems you're struggling with."

A wave of profound relief washed over the group. They thanked their professor profusely. They had expected a simple academic accommodation; what they received was a moment of true human compassion from an unexpected ally.
