# Part 6: A Trip to the Mall

When Jordan and Aubrie's makeup lab ended, they were met in the hallway by the smiling faces of their entire family.

"Alright, victory lap part two!" Madison announced. "Let's actually *go* somewhere this time!"

The entire Group of 8 headed to the CPI Transit Center. As they arrived, a local Blue Ridge Transit bus pulled into a bay, its destination sign brightly lit: 1 FALLING BRANCH. Jordan pointed. "That one," he said. "I want to take the local bus. I want to see the town."

The group boarded, their mood light and celebratory. The slow, meandering ride through the streets of Blacksburg and Christiansburg was wonderfully normal.

They got off at the New River Mall Christiansburg transit hub. For Jordan, this was his first trip back since the overwhelming "Mall Gauntlet" before Christmas. They headed straight for the food court, and after they had all gotten their food, Jordan quietly signaled to Mark.

"I need to find a bathroom."

Mark nodded, understanding instantly. A quick check of the directory confirmed there was no gender-neutral restroom nearby. "I've got you, man. Let's go." With the same quiet trust as before, Mark took his friend into the men's restroom and assisted him in the handicap stall.

When they reunited with the group, Jordan was filled with a profound sense of gratitude and confidence. He looked at the five women of his pact, and one by one, offered them each a happy, affectionate kiss on the cheek. Aubrie, Madison, Zoe, Lauren, and Sarah all returned the gesture with warm smiles. Hannah then stepped forward and wrapped him in a firm, sisterly hug. She loved him dearly, but out of deep respect for her relationship with Mark, a polite kiss on the cheek was a line she would not cross.

As they started to walk through the mall, they passed a branch of a large, national bank. Jordan stopped for a second, recognizing the logo. It was the bank his mother used. The sight triggered a memory, but not a meltdown. It triggered a thought.

He turned to Hannah. "Hannah... I get paid from my library job soon. And... I don't really know what to do with money. Would you... would you help me manage it?"

Hannah's face lit up with a warm, gentle smile. She was careful with her words, wanting to avoid any terminology that might echo the recent court battle. "Of course, Jordan. I would be honored to help you with your budget. We can sit down this weekend and figure it all out."

The group, sensing the painful memory associated with the bank, gently steered their path toward a different wing of the mall. Jordan looked around at the bustling, everyday scene. The holiday shopping season was long over, and the noise and lights that had been an unbearable assault just a few months ago were now just pleasant background noise. He was happy. He wasn't being overstimulated. This, for him, was a monumental victory.
