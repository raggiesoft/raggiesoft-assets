# Part 7: The Food Court Affirmation

The Group of 8 found a large table in the bustling food court, their trays laden with a chaotic mix of pizza, tacos, and Chinese food. As they ate, Zoe, who had been quietly observing the easy affection between Jordan, Aubrie, and Madison, spoke up in her typically analytical way.

"The data is conclusive," she stated matter-of-factly. "The new relational dynamic between Jordan, Aubrie, and Madison has been successfully integrated. All subsidiary units," she gestured with her fork to the rest of the table, "indicate full approval."

The group laughed at her clinical but accurate summary. In response, Aubrie, on Jordan's left, and Madison, on his right, both leaned in at the same time and gave him a happy, simultaneous kiss on each cheek.

"Hold that! That's too cute!" Sarah said, whipping out her phone and snapping a picture. A moment later, a notification buzzed on all their phones.

Jordan Comms: Sarah: \[Image Attached: A photo of Jordan blushing happily as Aubrie and Madison kiss his cheeks from either side.\] Sarah: Look at our boy! So happy and so loved. 🥰

Miles away in Christiansburg, Laura Brooks's phone buzzed. She looked at the picture, and her heart melted. She showed the screen to her husband. Daniel Brooks looked at the image of the young man he now called his son, surrounded by love, and a deep, proud smile spread across his face.

Back at the mall, Mark shook his head in amazement. "You know, it's wild to think back to that first month," he said to the group. "The shy boy who was just trying to survive. Now you're a man who makes his own decisions."

"He still asks for help when he needs to," Hannah added, her voice full of pride. "That's the best part. He knows he can trust us enough to ask. That's real strength."

"Remember how he was after... after Tom?" Lauren said softly. "How he just... was there for me? He's always been strong. He just needed people to see it."

Jordan listened to them, a happy blush creeping up his neck. He looked around the table at his entire family. "I couldn't have done any of it without you guys," he said, his voice full of an emotion that went deeper than words.

"No, man, *you* did it," Mark said, leaning forward with a serious, proud expression. "Seriously. Remember that first week? You were so quiet. Now look at you."

"The data supports this conclusion," Zoe added, ticking points off on her fingers in her usual, logical fashion. "You proactively engaged with multiple university systems. You updated your preferred name and contact information. You filed a formal no-contact request with Housing."

"And you went to the police!" Sarah chimed in, her own voice full of admiration. "By yourself! You knew what she was going to try, and you cut her off at the pass before she could even start. That was brilliant."

"You stood up to her in the dorm," Lauren said softly, remembering the terrifying confrontation. "And then you stood up to her in court, in front of a judge, and told them exactly who you are and what you wanted. That takes so much courage."

Jordan listened, overwhelmed, as they listed his victories. He had never seen his own actions as courageous; he had just been trying to survive. Hearing them reframe his journey as one of strength and self-advocacy was a revelation.

Aubrie took his hand, her thumb gently tracing patterns on his knuckles. "We are so proud of you, Jordan," she said, her voice full of a love that encompassed everything. "Not just because you won. We're proud because you *fought*."

He looked at the faces of his chosen family, all beaming with pride *for him*, and for the first time, he started to believe it himself.
