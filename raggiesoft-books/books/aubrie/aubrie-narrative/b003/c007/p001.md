# Part 1: The War Council (Redux)

The victory in the Norfolk courthouse was profound, but the five-hour drive back to Christiansburg was a somber, angry affair. The adrenaline had faded, leaving behind the raw emotional and financial cost of the battle they had just won. Jordan was free, but the fight had taken its toll.

That evening, after the Group of 8 had been safely delivered back to their dorm, Daniel and Laura Brooks sat in their kitchen with their lawyer, Ms. Gable, who had ridden back with them. The mood was not celebratory; it was strategic.

"She can't be allowed to get away with this," Laura said, her voice a low, furious hum. "What she did to Jordan... forcing him to relive his trauma, deliberately scheduling the hearing to interfere with his studies... it's malicious."

Ms. Gable nodded, her expression grim. "I agree. The judge's dismissal 'with prejudice' prevents her from ever filing for guardianship again. But it doesn't address the damages she's already caused." She looked directly at Daniel. "You have a very strong case for a civil suit. Intentional Infliction of Emotional Distress, slander, malicious prosecution... we can go after her for everything she's worth."

Daniel looked at his wife. He saw the fierce, protective fire in her eyes. "We're not doing this for the money," he said, his voice firm. "We're doing this for justice. For Jordan. She needs to understand that there are consequences for her actions." He paused, a grim resolve settling over him. "File it. I want every single dollar we spent on lawyers, on hotels, on travel, and on therapy for Jordan paid back. And then I want a number so large for his pain and suffering that she will never have the resources to bother him, or anyone else, ever again."

"I'll draft the complaint tomorrow morning," Ms. Gable said, a small, determined smile on her face. The battle for Jordan's freedom was won. The battle for his justice was just beginning.
