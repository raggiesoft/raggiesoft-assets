# Part 2: The Ocean and the Outer Banks

That evening, a feeling of peaceful, domestic contentment had settled over them in the Brooks' backyard.

Jordan, sitting in his chair with Aubrie at his side, watched the clouds drift by. Bolstered by the confidence from his new home and his successful planning day, he finally gave voice to a desire he had held silently for years.

"I've never seen the Atlantic Ocean," he said, his voice quiet.

Madison looked over from her lounge chair. "What? Really? Not even growing up in Hampton Roads?"

Jordan flinched slightly at the mention of his hometown. "My mother... she never took me. We were close, but... it was never a priority." He looked at the faces of his friends. "I'd really like to see it. The real ocean." He paused, his next words full of a quiet, firm boundary. "But... not there. Not Virginia Beach. I never want to go back."

An immediate, profound understanding passed through the group.

"Okay," Zoe said, her laptop already open on her lap. "So, new mission. Find an ocean that isn't Virginia Beach." She started typing. "The most direct route to the coast from here is U.S. 460 to I-64, which goes right through Hampton Roads..."

"No," Jordan said, a hint of panic in his voice. "I can't. I don't want to even drive through it."

"We won't," Zoe said immediately, already mapping a new course. "Okay, new plan. We can bypass it completely. We'll take I-81 south to I-77 south into North Carolina, then cut over on I-74 east to U.S. 52, then I-40, and finally U.S. 64 east all the way to the Outer Banks. It avoids the entire Hampton Roads area and the Chesapeake Expressway toll, and it only adds about a minute to the drive."

"The Outer Banks are perfect," Lauren said. "Kill Devil Hills is supposed to be really nice and family-friendly."

Jordan's eyes lit up. The idea was perfect. It wasn't just "not Virginia Beach"; it was a deliberate, safe passage to something new, something theirs.

"Can we?" he asked, looking at his friends, then instinctively toward the back door of the Brooks' house, where Daniel had just stepped out.

Daniel had overheard the last part of their conversation. He walked over, a warm smile on his face. "You want to go see the ocean, Jordan?" he asked. Jordan nodded.

Daniel's smile widened. "Then you're going to see the ocean. We'll rent a beach house in Kill Devil Hills for a couple of nights. We can all leave tomorrow morning, spend Friday and Saturday at the beach, and drive home on Sunday. That gives you one last weekend of freedom before classes start."

It was that simple. Jordan had voiced a desire, and his new family, without hesitation, re-routed their entire world to make it happen safely. They weren't just going to the beach. They were taking him to a new ocean, one that he could claim as his own.
