# Part 6: A New System Variable

As the group finished their breakfast, the logistical problem hung in the air: Zoe’s 5:45 AM plan was efficient for beating the "standing load", but it was a failure in terms of Jordan's health and safety.

"Okay," Aubrie said, taking Jordan's hand. "So the 5:45 AM express is out. We can't have him waiting in the snow for 45 minutes. It's not an option."

"Agreed," Mark chimed in. "So what's the next bus?"

Zoe, who had been silently processing this new "medical vulnerability" variable, pulled up the schedules on her mPhone. Her original calculation was flawed because it had prioritized *capacity* over *environment*. She now had to find the optimal balance.

"The library opens at 7:00 AM," Zoe stated, analyzing the data. "We must arrive *after* that time, but *before* the 6:45 AM peak begins. This is a logical contradiction. The two events do not overlap."

"So, what does that mean?" Lauren asked.

"It means," Zoe continued, "we must accept a minimal, controlled wait. The 5:45 AM express arrives at 6:15 AM, leaving a 45-minute environmental exposure time. This is inefficient and dangerous."

She scrolled to the next departure. "The 6:00 AM Route 1 local departs 15 minutes later and arrives on campus at 6:45 AM. This reduces the outdoor wait time to only 15 minutes—a 66.7% reduction in exposure."

Jordan looked at the group. "I can do 15 minutes," he said, his voice quiet but firm. "I can't do 45."

"But won't that bus be crowded?" Madison asked. "It's closer to the rush."

"No," Zoe said, shaking her head. "The data shows the peak *starts* at 6:45 AM. The 6:00 AM local bus departs *before* the 'parking panic' wave truly begins. It is the most logical compromise."

A wave of relief passed through the group. They had a new plan.

"It's a longer ride," Mark noted. "45 minutes on a local bus instead of 30 on the express."

"It's a worthwhile trade-off," Aubrie said immediately, looking at Jordan. "His health is more important than 15 minutes."

The decision was unanimous. Their new wake-up call would be 5:15 AM.
