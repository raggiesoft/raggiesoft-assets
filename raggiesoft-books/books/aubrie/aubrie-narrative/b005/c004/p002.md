# Part 2: The Overlook

Inside the "Rocket Burger," the group found a quiet booth in the back. They ate their food in relative silence, the normal, easy chatter replaced by a protective quiet. Sarah sat beside Jordan, her arm a constant, reassuring presence around his shoulders, hugging him for as long as he needed before they got back on the road.

When it was time to leave, they rearranged the seating. Jordan moved to the minivan to sit next to Aubrie, while Sarah now rode with Mark. The minivan followed the SUV as they took the new, safe route: State Route 285 to U.S. 250, through the small towns of Fishersville and Waynesboro. Zoe held Jordan tightly the entire way as they climbed the mountain toward the Blue Ridge Parkway.

Their first stop was the Afton Overlook. Mark pulled the SUV into a parking spot, and the view was breathtaking—a panoramic vista of the Shenandoah Valley, ablaze with the colors of autumn.

They got out of the vehicles, but Jordan was still tense, the residual adrenaline from the meltdown leaving him shaky. As he sat in his wheelchair, taking in the view, Zoe and Sarah stood on either side of him, their hands on his shoulders, holding him tightly.

A moment later, the rest of the group walked over from the other vehicle, their faces etched with concern. Mark quietly explained to them what had happened, how the very short, one-exit ride on I-64 had triggered such a severe meltdown.

As Mark finished his explanation, Aubrie and Madison moved in, taking over the comforting hugs from Zoe and Sarah. They knelt on either side of his chair, their presence a warm, protective shield.

"We don't have to stay, J," Aubrie whispered. "We can go home right now if you want."

Jordan took a deep, shaky breath, the crisp mountain air filling his lungs. He looked at the vast, beautiful valley below, then at the faces of his seven friends, his family, all surrounding him.

"No," he said, his voice quiet but firm. "No. I want to see the leaves." He looked at Aubrie. "I'll ride with you."

The plan was adjusted once more. They would continue south on the Blue Ridge Parkway as intended. Then, to get home, they would take U.S. Route 60 west through Buena Vista to Lexington. This route would allow them to get back on I-81 south of the 30-mile gauntlet, ensuring Jordan never had to be on I-64 again that day.

He was still fragile, still recovering, but he was not broken. Surrounded by his wall of love and support, he was ready to reclaim the rest of his day.
