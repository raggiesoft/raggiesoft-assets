# Part 4: The Sickness

The quiet sanctuary in the Brooks’ basement, where Jordan, Aubrie, and Madison had been lost in a comforting discussion of astrophysics, was interrupted by a cheerful call from the top of the internal stairs.

"Dinner's ready!" Laura Brooks’s voice echoed down. "Come and get it!"

Aubrie and Madison stretched, smiling as they sat up. "Ready to face the world, J?" Madison asked gently.

Jordan nodded, but as he pushed himself up to sit on the edge of the queen bed, a wave of dizziness washed over him. The room tilted, and he had to brace himself with his arms. He’d felt "off" since the drive, but he had assumed it was just the lingering exhaustion from the meltdown on the highway. This felt different. This was a physical, disorienting wooziness.

"Whoa," he said, his voice quiet. "Just... give me a second."

Aubrie, who was already moving to bring his wheelchair closer, stopped instantly. "Jordan? Are you okay?"

He looked pale, and not just the "post-meltdown" pale she was used to. There was an unhealthy, faint flush high on his cheeks. Madison, who had been lying beside him, pressed the back of her hand to his forehead.

"J, you're burning up," she said, her playful tone gone, replaced by immediate, sharp concern.

The quiet shift in the basement quickly drew the others. Hannah, the group's quintessential "mom friend", came over from the other side of the room. "What's going on?"

"He feels really warm," Aubrie said, her own hand now on his neck.

Hannah’s expression turned serious. "Zoe, can you grab the digital thermometer from the bathroom cabinet?"

A moment later, Zoe returned. Hannah unwrapped the thermometer, placed it under Jordan's tongue, and they waited in silence. The device finally let out a small *beep*. Hannah pulled it out and read the display. Her eyes widened slightly.

"100.1," she announced to the room.

A different kind of anxiety settled over the group. This wasn't the aftermath of a psychological trigger; this was a new, physical threat.

"Oh, Jordan," Laura Brooks said, having come downstairs to see what the delay was. She saw the thermometer in Hannah's hand and the miserable look on his face. Her mind went straight to the immunization schedule she and Daniel were so carefully helping him manage. This was, after all, the same young man who had nearly been hospitalized by the flu just last year because his mother had refused to vaccinate him.

"This isn't the meltdown, is it, honey?" she asked gently.

Jordan shook his head, looking exhausted. "I just feel... dizzy. And hot."

Laura nodded, her face full of a new, grim understanding. "Okay," she said, her voice full of calm authority. "This is just one last ghost from your mother's past we have to get through. You're on the catch-up schedule, but you're not fully protected yet. It looks like you caught one more bug before we could finish."

The dinner plan was instantly revised.

"You are not going anywhere," Aubrie said firmly. "Madison, can you help me get him comfortable?"

The two of them, his "safest people", helped him settle back down, propping him up against the pillows.

"I'll bring you some soup and toast from upstairs," Laura said, already heading for the stairs.

Jordan lay back against the pillows, exhausted from the day and now feeling the heavy, aching onset of the fever. The terror of I-64 felt a million miles away, replaced by the simple, miserable reality of being sick. But as he looked at Aubrie and Madison sitting on either side of his bed, he knew he was safe. The meltdown had passed, and now, his family would simply weather this new storm with him, just as they always did.
