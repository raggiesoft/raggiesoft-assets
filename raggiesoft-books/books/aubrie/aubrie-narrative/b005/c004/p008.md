# Part 8: The Leviathan Unveiled

The epic, synthesizer-driven anthem of "A Sister's Love (Main Theme)" finally faded, and the opening chapter title appeared on the screen: Book I: The Sundering of Sunstead. A quiet, reverent hush settled over the basement. This was the first time any of them had seen the legendary Leviathan Cut, and the atmosphere was one of shared discovery.

Jordan, still feverish and miserable, was anchored firmly between Aubrie and Madison on the sofa. The movie's vast, logical world was the perfect medicine, his mind focusing on the intricate new details rather than his own anxiety.

For the veteran fans of the theatrical release—Hannah, Sarah, and Madison—the experience was a revelation.

"Wait, *this* is how it starts?" Hannah whispered, her eyes wide with awe as they watched the opening scenes in the gleaming Royal Court of Old Aethel. "I've never seen any of this!"

"They just... cut out Malakor's entire origin story?" Madison whispered back, scandalized. "And the prophecy? That explains so much!"

They watched, completely engrossed, as the film delved deep into the twins' childhood. They saw Kaelan and Kaela as infants, arriving in Sunstead, and their cramped, loving upbringing in the shepherd's hut. When the film showed the 18-year-old twins in the hut, forced to share a single bed, a new, profound understanding settled over the Room 214 trio. Jordan, Aubrie, and Madison exchanged a quiet, knowing look. They understood that feeling of a "cage of comfort," of a shared, cramped space forging an unbreakable, complex bond.

The hours of the morning melted away. They watched Kaelan's guilt-ridden vow and his first trials in the Whispering Woods of Whispers. The light, whispered movie talk was mostly from the veteran fans.

"See, that's new," Sarah would murmur, pointing at the screen. "In the theatrical cut, he just finds the old hermit. This whole sequence with the voices... it makes so much more sense now."

Jordan remained quiet, his fever a dull, hot thrum in the background. He was grateful for the distraction and even more grateful for the steady, warm presence of Aubrie on his left and Madison on his right. He was safe.

Finally, the screen faded to black, and a title card appeared: *End of Book I*. Mark paused the movie, and the group stirred, blinking in the low light of the basement as if waking from a dream.

"Wow," Hannah said, stretching. "That was... a lot. And that's just the first part?"

Right on cue, Daniel Brooks's voice called down the stairs. "How's the Gauntlet going, team? It's lunchtime. I can bring some food down for everyone if you're ready to pause for a bit."
