# Part 9: The Mid-Gauntlet Intermission

"Yes, please, Mr. Brooks!" Madison called up the stairs, her voice cheerful. "We're starving!"

Mark hit the pause button on the Blu-ray remote, freezing the screen on the *End of Book I* title card. The marathon was officially on intermission. As the group stirred, stretching after hours of being engrossed, the first priority was a collective trip to the basement's shared bathroom.

Jordan, however, remained on the sofa, the effort to even sit up sending a wave of dizziness through him. The fever was making his limbs feel like lead. He looked over at Hannah, who immediately understood.

"We've got you, J," she said, nodding to Lauren. As the two strongest women in the group, they instinctively took the lead in helping him when he was physically at his lowest.

Mark watched as his girlfriend, Hannah, and her suitemate, Lauren, moved to Jordan's side. They helped him transfer from the sofa to his wheelchair and then wheeled him toward the bathroom, their movements a seamless, practiced dance of support as they prepared to assist him with his toileting needs. Mark felt a familiar surge of pride. He saw Hannah step up to care for their friend in a deeply personal way, and his only thought was how lucky he was to be with someone so boundlessly compassionate.

When Hannah emerged a few minutes later, she caught his eye and gave him a warm, private smile. She felt a corresponding wave of gratitude for his unwavering trust, a stark and welcome contrast to the toxic, insecure jealousy that had defined Lauren's relationship with Tom.

Soon, Daniel and Laura came down with trays of food. For the seven healthy movie-watchers, it was a perfect, easy lunch of sandwiches, chips, and various sodas. For Jordan, Laura brought a separate tray: another steaming bowl of chicken noodle soup, a fresh sleeve of saltine crackers, and a cold glass of dry ginger ale.

The group, still in their pajamas, settled back into their comfortable nest of blankets. Jordan was propped up on the sofa again, with Aubrie and Madison on either side, as he slowly ate his soup.

"I still can't believe they cut Malakor's entire origin story from the theatrical release," Madison said, taking a large bite of her sandwich. "It makes his villain song so much more impactful!"

"And the way they introduced the twins' parents fleeing the capital," Hannah added. "It completely changes Kaelan's journey. He's not just some kid; he's royalty in hiding".

Jordan listened, his fever a dull thrum in the background, but his mind was calm. He was safe, he was fed, and his family was dissecting the "absolute 1980s cheesy fantasy bliss" of a 12-hour movie. It was, he decided, the best sick day he could have ever imagined.
