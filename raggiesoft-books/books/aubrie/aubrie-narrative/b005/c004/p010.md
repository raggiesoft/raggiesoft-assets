# Part 10: The Leviathan's Heart

With their intermission lunch complete and Jordan comfortably resettled on the sofa between Aubrie and Madison, Mark hit "play" once more. The screen lit up with the next title card: Book II: The Shadow's Heart.

The afternoon was consumed by the epic, sprawling narrative. For Hannah, Sarah, and Madison, who only knew the 100-minute theatrical version, this was like watching a completely different film.

"Wait, Kaela has a whole *subplot*?" Sarah whispered, astonished, as they watched Kaela begin her subtle psychological war against Lord Malakor, planting seeds of rebellion within the Shadowspire.

"This makes her so much better than just the damsel in the tower!" Hannah agreed, completely engrossed.

They watched in awe as the *Leviathan Cut* delved into Malakor's internal conflict, his chilling "Sacrifice or Queen?" monologue, and the full, unabridged rock opera performance of "The Tyrant's Throne (Shadow's Heart)". When the famous interruption scene played out—where Malakor implodes his own minion for praising "chaos"—Mark let out a low whistle of appreciation.

Jordan, despite his lingering fever, was completely captivated. The cheesy 80s fantasy bliss was the perfect antidote to his real-world anxieties. He found himself mesmerized not just by the story, but by the music. The soaring synth score by Klaus Richter, the motivational rock of "One Step at a Time (The Gauntlet's Path)," and the dark, theatrical power of Jareth Blade's villain song... it was all so different from anything his mother had ever let him listen to. This 1980s music, with its high drama and electric guitar solos, was starting to spark a genuine interest.

The film reached its emotional low point as Kaelan, having crossed the Bridge of a Thousand Truths, collapsed in the mud, finally understanding his love for his sister. The classic power ballad "I Can't Live Without You (Kaela's Theme)" swelled, and the entire room was silent.

"They cut this down to, like, thirty seconds in the theatrical release," Madison murmured, her eyes misty. "This... this is just beautiful."

As *Book II* drew to a close, Mark paused the film. The group sat in a comfortable, stunned silence. Hours had passed in what felt like minutes.

"Okay," Mark said, his voice a little hoarse. "That's the end of Book II. I think that's a perfect stopping point before dinner."

Right on cue, as if sensing the epic narrative beat, Laura's voice called down from the kitchen. "Dinner, everyone! I hope you're hungry!"
