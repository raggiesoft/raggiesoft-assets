# Part 14: The System Crash

Jordan’s meltdown escalated, his sobs turning into raw, panicked gasps as he was held securely by Zoe. He was drowning in a logical impossibility.

From their bed, Aubrie and Madison saw the crash in real time. They saw his terror and knew exactly what it meant. He wasn't afraid *of* Zoe; he was terrified *for* himself, expecting the inevitable punishment for breaking a rule he didn't know how to follow. They knew, instinctively, that observation wasn't enough. They had to provide overwhelming, contradictory data.

Silently, Madison got up from her bed. She climbed onto the queen-sized portable mattress and pressed herself firmly against Jordan's back, wrapping her arms around both him and Zoe, adding her warmth to the cuddle. "We've got you, J," she whispered. "You're okay."

A moment later, Aubrie joined, settling in beside Zoe, effectively cocooning Jordan between the three of them.

This was the final proof. His two absolute safest people were not angry, were not jealous; they were actively *joining in*. The System.IO.FileNotFoundException() exploded, taking his entire operating system down. The positive input was so overwhelming that his brain, lacking any script for this scenario, interpreted it as a critical threat. His meltdown worsened, his whole body shaking violently.

Zoe, still holding him, calmly processed the new data streams.

Upstairs, Daniel and Laura had breakfast ready, but they heard the muffled, raw sounds of Jordan's distress and knew to wait.

Downstairs, the three women—Aubrie, Madison, and Zoe—formed a living fortress around him. They held him tightly, grounding him against the storm his own mind had created. "Shh, you're safe," Aubrie whispered, her voice a low anchor. "This is real. We're not mad. We love you." Madison added, "We're all here. This is good, Jordan. This is family." They didn't try to stop the meltdown; they simply held him securely, offering steady, loving input until his terrified, overloaded system could finally, slowly, reboot.
