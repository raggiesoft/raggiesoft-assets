# Part 19: The Stable System

With the file of evidence now a tangible, comforting weight on the coffee table, the strategic tension in the basement finally dissipated. Mark stood up and put the Silver Gauntlet of Aethel Blu-ray case back on the shelf, his movements slow and satisfied after their 12-hour marathon. The long, emotionally draining day—which had started with Jordan's meltdown over Zoe, progressed through his fever, and ended with a cinematic epic—was finally winding down.

Jordan sat on the sofa, feeling weak but clear-headed for the first time all weekend. He looked at Lauren and Sarah, who were quietly discussing the movie's ending. He had processed the safety of cuddling with Aubrie and Madison (his safest people), Zoe (his gratitude), and Hannah (platonic/sisterly). As Jordan sat there, held securely by Lauren and Sarah, he finally felt the last of his old, broken programming delete itself. He was loved, unconditionally, by everyone in this room.

"Okay," Laura's voice called from the top of the stairs, "how is our patient? One last temperature check before dinner."

Hannah retrieved the thermometer. She placed it under Jordan's tongue and waited for the beep. She pulled it out, looked at the screen, and broke into a massive grin.

"98.6!" she announced. "He's back to normal!"

A small, happy cheer went up from the group. "That settles it," Daniel's voice boomed. "A full recovery and a successful mission call for a proper meal. No more soup!. Laura's got a roasted chicken, mashed potatoes, and green beans ready. Everyone upstairs. Dinner is served!".

Jordan, nestled between his friends, let out a long, happy sigh. He was hungry, he was healthy, and he was home.
