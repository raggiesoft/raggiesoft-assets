# Part 22: The Pact's First Night

The bathroom door opened, and Aubrie wheeled a visibly calmer, happier Jordan back into the main basement area. He was freshly dressed in clean pajamas, his skin scrubbed, and the last of the I-64 "gauntlet's" grime and anxiety had been washed away, replaced by the profound, grounding peace of his family's unconditional love.

The rest of the group, also in pajamas, was relaxing. Mark and Hannah, seeing Jordan's calm state, smiled at each other and headed to their designated queen bed in the corner to read. Sarah, sensing the "core" group needed space after the intense emotional event, gave Jordan a warm, knowing smile and quietly took one of the other queen beds for herself.

This left Jordan with Aubrie, Madison, Lauren, and Zoe. It was time for bed, but the thought of splitting up felt wrong, especially after the day's events. Jordan looked at the two remaining queen beds.

"I have an idea," he said, his voice still a little hoarse but steady. "It's not perfect, but... can we try pushing the last two beds together? I... I want to sleep with all of you tonight".

The women exchanged a look, and then smiled. "Logistically, it'll be awkward," Zoe noted, "but it's feasible."

Working as a team, they shoved the two heavy portable bed frames side-by-side, creating a massive, makeshift sleeping platform. It wasn't an Alaskan King bed, but it was their first attempt.

Jordan transferred onto the bed, and the four women piled in with him. It was a comfortable, happy "puppy pile". Jordan settled in the middle, his mind abuzz with a new, profound sense of security.

Madison, the other "backbone" of Room 214, settled in by his legs, and Lauren, the first person he had ever been able to offer unconditional compassion to, curled up on the other side of Aubrie, completing the circle of warmth.

He was cocooned. He was safe. He turned his head and looked at Zoe, his heart overflowing with gratitude. He leaned in and kissed her. This was not the brief, data-gathering kiss from the bathroom; this was a deep, passionate, romantic kiss, full of tongue and the unspoken promises of their pact.

Jordan let out a shaky breath, his mind finally, totally at peace. He was happier than he had ever been in his life. He closed his eyes, his mind already drifting to the future, imagining a night in their new house, on that massive Alaskan King bed... maybe with all five women of the pact....

Aubrie, holding him, had a similar thought. She felt the rightness of the moment. She'd love to have his children someday, she realized. And as she glanced at Madison, Zoe, and Lauren, all cuddled around the man they loved, she knew she wouldn't mind one bit if they did, too.
