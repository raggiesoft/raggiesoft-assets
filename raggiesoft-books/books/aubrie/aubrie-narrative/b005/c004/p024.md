# Part 24: Breakfast and the Victory Lap

On Tuesday morning, the last official day of Fall Break, the atmosphere in the Brooks' basement was transformed. The heavy, anxious quiet of the weekend was gone, replaced by a light, cheerful energy. When Jordan woke up—in his own bed this time, after a full night of restful sleep—he felt clear-headed, strong, and hungry.

Hannah, true to form, performed one last temperature check before breakfast. "98.6!" she announced with a triumphant smile. "He's officially back to normal!".

"That," Daniel's voice boomed from the stairs, "calls for a proper celebration meal!"

Daniel and Laura came down with trays, and for the first time in days, Jordan wasn't given a "sick tray." He received a full, hot plate of scrambled eggs with American cheese, crispy bacon, sausage links, and a short stack of pancakes. The rest of the family received similar celebratory meals, and Jordan, to his immense satisfaction, was handed a mug of hot chocolate made with milk and topped with whipped cream.

As he dug in, he felt a profound sense of relief. The fever was gone, his voice was back, and the IRS file was ready. He was free.

"I want to celebrate today," Jordan announced to the group. "Since I'm recovered. I'd like to go for a bus ride".

"Great idea," Madison said. "We could take the local route up to the New River Mall Christiansburg mall?. Or," she added, thinking, "if you want something smaller and less crowded after being sick, we could head to the Bluewater Town Plaza in my hometown".

Jordan's eyes lit up at the second option, but not for the reason she suggested. "Bluewater?" he asked. "How long is that bus ride?".

"It's about an hour on the Maroon Express from Falling Branch," Zoe answered, already pulling up the schedule on her mPhone.

"That one," Jordan said instantly, a grin spreading across his face. "Let's do that one".

Mark laughed. "You don't care about the mall at all, do you? You just want the longest possible bus ride".

"Pretty much," Jordan admitted, his good mood infectious. He then turned his gaze to Zoe, his expression becoming more serious. "Zoe," he said, his voice full of the genuine gratitude he'd felt since the bathroom revelation. "I'm so glad your family lives here. Thank you for... for all this."

He looked over at Daniel and Laura. "You guys," he said, his voice thick with emotion, "you took me in. You didn't even question it. You're the first... the first *adults* I've ever really trusted. You gave me a reason to trust authority figures again".

"So, what's this Bluewater Town Plaza actually like?" Jordan asked Madison, turning back to the plan.

"Oh, you know," she shrugged, "it's a typical small-city mall. Got a department store, a bookstore, a shoe place. It's... a mall. But," she added with a wink, "it's *my* mall."

"Perfect," Jordan said, his mind clearly on the long bus ride.

Zoe finalized the logistics. "Okay, the mall opens at 10 AM. To get there, we'll need to catch the Maroon Express from Falling Branch at 8:45 AM. That will arrive at the Bluewater Transit Center at 9:45 AM. "It's settled then," Daniel said, standing to collect the empty plates. "Everyone get ready. We've got a bus to catch."
