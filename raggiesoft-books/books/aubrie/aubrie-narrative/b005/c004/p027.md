# Part 27: Stork & Cradle

The Bluewater Transit Orange Line bus arrived at the Bluewater Town Plaza at 10:12 AM, a few minutes ahead of its 10:15 AM schedule. The group of ten disembarked, the atmosphere light and cheerful after the long, relaxing bus ride.

They walked from the bus stop to the main entrance of the enclosed, smaller mall. As they entered, they naturally fell into a new formation. Lauren, with her easy, capable strength, took the handles of Jordan's wheelchair. Zoe, his newest "safe person," walked on his left, and he reached out to take her hand. On his right, he did the same with Sarah, and she laced her fingers through his.

This was the first time Sarah had so openly held his hand in front of the entire family. Walking just behind them, Aubrie and Madison watched this scene, their hearts full. Aubrie's smile was brilliant. She saw the man she loved being surrounded by love. Madison, seeing Sarah holding Jordan's hand, felt a surge of pure joy. She knew what Sarah (and Lauren) had endured with Tom—the betrayal, the cheating, the public humiliation. To see Sarah now, so clearly happy, safe, and integrated into their pact, holding Jordan's hand without a trace of fear or jealousy in the air, felt like the ultimate victory.

The mall was quiet, a stark contrast to the overwhelming chaos of New River Mall Christiansburg during the holidays. As they wandered down the main corridor, they passed a large, brightly lit department store. The sign above it, in a soft, cursive script, read "Stork & Cradle".

The window display was a pristine, idealized image of infancy—a white wooden crib draped in a soft yellow blanket, a delicate mobile of felted stars turning slowly, and a tiny mannequin dressed in an impossibly small set of overalls.

Jordan slowed, his gaze fixed on the display. He had never, in his entire life, allowed himself to think about this. But now, surrounded by a loving, stable family—with Daniel and Laura walking ahead, and five incredible women around him—a new, fragile thought took root. *Someday... I'd really love to have kids*.

The thought was warm and bright for a single second before a cold, familiar dread washed over him. His grip tightened on Zoe's and Sarah's hands. He thought of his own childhood. He had no template for this. His own father was a ghost, killed before he was even born, leaving a void he could never understand. And his mother... his mother, Aubrie Sinclair, had been his only example of a parent. She had failed him in every way that mattered, replacing love with control, support with abuse, and safety with fear.

A wave of pure terror, cold and sharp, cut through him. *What if I'm like her? What if I don't know how? What if I fail them?* He stared at the perfect, peaceful crib, and all he could see was his own potential to break something so innocent.
