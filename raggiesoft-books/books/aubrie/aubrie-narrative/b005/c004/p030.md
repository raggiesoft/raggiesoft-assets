# Part 30: The Jealousy Rootkit

After the empowering visit to the bookstore, the group left, Jordan’s new book, *The First-Time Dad's Handbook*, tucked safely in a bag on his lap. As they re-entered the main corridor of the Bluewater Town Plaza, Mark proposed the next step in Jordan's recovery.

"Okay, J," Mark said quietly, his voice full of brotherly support. "We've all proven we're not jealous of *you*. Now let's work on that old rootkit your mom installed. Let's prove you don't have to be jealous of *us*."

Jordan took a deep, steadying breath and nodded. "Okay. I'll try."

The group re-formed. Sarah took the handles of Jordan's wheelchair, giving him a supportive shoulder squeeze before she started pushing. Lauren, a confirmed member of his pact, took his right hand, her grip warm and familiar. Hannah, his "sister”, took his left hand, and he held it with the easy, platonic comfort they had established.

Then came the hard part. Mark stepped up next to Aubrie, his "safe person", and offered his hand. Aubrie, understanding the experiment, took it with a warm, reassuring smile.

They began to walk, a strange but deeply loving procession. Mark was beaming, his face a picture of pure, uncomplicated happiness. He was proud of his girlfriend, Hannah, for being the "sister" Jordan needed, and he was proud of his "brother," Jordan, for being brave enough to face this demon. He was simply walking with his family, holding the hand of another friend.
