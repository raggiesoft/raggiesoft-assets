# Part 32: The Food Court Pact

The group of ten found a cluster of tables in the Bluewater Town Plaza's food court. The energy was light and relaxed. Jordan, now fully recovered from his fever, sat with his "safe person" trio—Aubrie, Madison, and Zoe—while Daniel, Laura, Mark, Hannah, Lauren, and Sarah filled the adjacent tables. They dug into a simple, satisfying meal of burgers, fries, and sodas from the local "Rocket Burger" joint.

The conversation was easy, mostly rehashing the epic 12-hour *Leviathan Cut* of *The Silver Gauntlet of Aethel*. But the underlying, unspoken topic of the day was the future, sparked by Jordan's trip to the parenting bookstore.

Finally, Aubrie put her burger down and reached across the table to take Jordan's hand.

"Jordan," she said, her voice quiet but clear enough for their whole family to hear. "About that book you bought... and what we all talked about in the bathroom. We need you to know we're serious."

Madison leaned in, her expression full of a love that was absolute. "We're all in, J. This is our family".

"We've talked about it," Lauren added, looking at Sarah, who nodded in confirmation. "The pact. It's not just a college thing. It's... everything. And we're all exclusively *yours*. We're not interested in any other partners. You're it for us".

Mark and Hannah, sitting at the next table, had been listening quietly. They looked at each other, and a silent, profound understanding passed between them. They were witnessing the foundation of a "forever" pact. Mark reached across the table and took Hannah's hand.

"That's... the most incredible thing I've ever heard," Mark said, his voice full of awe. He looked at Hannah, his heart full. "We should do that," he murmured, just for her. "The forever part".

Hannah’s eyes shone with tears as she smiled and squeezed his hand. "Yes," she whispered back. "Yes, we really should".

Jordan looked at the five women of his pact—Aubrie, Madison, Zoe, Lauren, and Sarah. He was in love with all of them. He finally felt, for the first time, an absolute, unshakeable security in their love. But his logical mind, now healed and running clearly, immediately hit a new, complex problem.

"But... how?" he asked, looking from Aubrie to Madison, his two "safest people". "I... I love all of you. But... legal marriage... the law... you can only have *one* partner".

Before the anxiety of this new, impossible question could build, Daniel Brooks, who had been watching the entire exchange with a proud, fatherly smile, spoke up.

"Jordan," he said, his voice a calm, steady anchor. "You're trying to solve a problem for a decade from now. Don't. You've just figured out the 'what.' The 'what' is that you love each other and you're a family. That's the most important part. The 'how'—the legal paperwork, the marriage licenses—that's just logistics. It's a problem for another day. And like every other problem we've faced, we will figure it out together."

Laura nodded in agreement. "He's right, honey. For today, just... be happy. You've earned it."

Jordan looked at Daniel, then at Laura, then back to the five women. He let out a long breath, his "CPU load" dropping back to nominal. They were right. The problem was real, but it wasn't for today. Today was just for being happy.
