# Part 34: The Bluewater Transit Center

The group of ten, full from their food court lunch and satisfied with their purchases, began walking toward the mall exit. The mood was light, but there was a new, palpable undercurrent of anticipation for the evening ahead.

As they walked, Zoe fell into step beside Mark and Hannah.

"Mark, Hannah," she began, her voice low and analytical, "I've been processing the logistics for tonight. My old childhood bedroom at the house is empty. Logistically, it makes the most sense for you two to take it".

Mark and Hannah both looked at her, a little surprised.

Zoe continued, her reasoning clear and practical, as always. "It offers you, as a committed couple, a higher degree of privacy than the basement affords. And," she added, with a subtle, knowing glance toward Jordan, "it will, in turn, provide the basement group with the privacy they need to... explore the new dynamics of their pact".

Hannah's face softened, and she squeezed Mark's arm. She understood completely. Zoe wasn't just offering them a room; she was thoughtfully and logistically engineering a safe, private space for *everyone*—for her and Mark to be a couple, and for Jordan to have his "trial run" with the women of his pact, free from any audience.

"Zoe, that's..." Hannah started, "that's incredibly thoughtful. Thank you."

"Of course," Mark said, genuinely grateful. "We accept. Thank you, Zoe."

With the plans for the evening now set, the family exited the Bluewater Town Plaza into the bright afternoon sun. They walked to the bus stop just as the 1:15 PM Bluewater Transit Orange Line bus pulled up, its sign indicating it was heading back to the downtown transit center.

The group piled on. Jordan wheeled his chair into the designated securement area, and the driver locked him in. The rest of the family—Aubrie, Madison, Zoe, Lauren, Sarah, Hannah, Mark, Daniel, and Laura—all found seats in the front section of the bus, surrounding him.

The bus pulled away from the curb, and the roughly 30-minute ride began, taking them through the familiar streets of Madison's hometown. Jordan sat, his bag with the new book and the items from "Stork & Cradle" and the adult novelty shop at his feet. He was quiet, not with anxiety, but with a deep, contented peace. He looked at his family, all chatting happily, and felt a profound sense of *rightness*.

Soon, the bus pulled into the Bluewater Transit Center, where they all disembarked. Their next step: wait for the Maroon Express that would take them on the long ride home to Falling Branch Park & Ride.
