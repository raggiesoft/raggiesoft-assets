# Part 4: A Brother's Warning, A Family's Vow

"Mark..." Jordan's voice was a raw whisper.

Mark came over, his own face pale. "Yeah, J? What's up?"

"Your uncle..." Jordan said, his breathing shallow. "You said... you said he had this. The CABG".

Mark’s face, which had been pale with worry, tightened. He pulled a chair over, sitting close to Jordan. "Yeah, man. He did," Mark said, his voice low. He glanced at the others. This was not a pretty talk.

"It's... it's rough, J. I'm not gonna lie," Mark said, his gaze unflinching. "My uncle had a quintuple bypass. They... they stop your heart. Put you on a machine. They break your chest open to get to it."

At the words *break your chest open*, a fresh, horrified sob tore from Aubrie. The clinical term CABG was one thing; open-heart surgery, as the doctor had called it, was terrifying, and this was worse. She was crying again, her knuckles white again as she gripped Jordan's hand.

Jordan was frightened. He stared at Mark, his own chest aching, imagining what he was describing.

Seeing the terror on both their faces, Mark immediately reached out and took Jordan's *other* hand. "Hey. But listen to me," he said, his voice firm. "It's rough. And the recovery is... it's *slow*, and it had *high points and low points*. But my uncle? He made a *full recovery*. He's still here. He's okay. It *worked*. You're 19, man. You're strong. You've got this."

Mark gave his hand a final, firm squeeze and then, sensing his part was done, released Jordan's hand.

The moment his hand was free, Zoe, who had been standing by, took Jordan's hand. Aubrie, on the other side, *still* had not let go.

Jordan looked at Zoe, his eyes still wide with fear. She met his gaze, her analytical mind processing the data: Jordan was terrified of the medical procedure *and* the long-term implication of being a "burden" during recovery.

"He's right, Jordan," Zoe said, her voice a steady, logical anchor in the room's emotional storm. "It will be a long recovery. Which is why you will not be doing it alone."

She squeezed his hand. "You are a part of my family now. My parents see you as their son. You *will* be looked after. No matter what happens in that operating room, whether it's two days or two months, you have a place in Christiansburg. Do you understand me?"

She leaned in, her gaze intense. "You will *never* have to go back to Hampton Roads ever again. That life is over. We are your family. We will handle this."

As she spoke, she leaned in and gave him a firm, loving kiss on the cheek. On his other side, Aubrie, still crying but bolstered by Zoe's words, leaned in and kissed him on the cheek too.

He was anchored between them, his future secured, his old life banished.
