# Part 6: The Failed Angioplasty

Jordan's consciousness returned in painful, confusing waves. The world was a blurry, sterile-white void, and the bright operating room lights were a dull, hazy smear. He heard voices, but they sounded distant and distorted, like he was underwater. They sounded concerned.

"...can't get through... it's like a rock... call Roanoke..."

He tried to turn his head, and a wave of nausea washed over him. A masked face swam into his vision. "He's waking up." The voice was clearer now.

"Jordan? The procedure is over. We're taking you back to your recovery room now".

The fog in his brain was too thick. His chest ached with a deep, profound soreness. His first, panicked thought was for his anchor.

"Aubrie..." he slurred, his throat raw. "I want Aubrie".

"She's waiting for you in your room," the nurse said, her voice a sudden, sharp point of clarity. "Let's get you moved".

The gurney ride back was a blur. He was rolled into his inpatient room, which was mercifully dim. But the scene that greeted him was not one of relief.

Aubrie was there, just as she'd promised. But she wasn't sitting calmly by his bed. She was standing by the window, crying her eyes out, her shoulders shaking as she stared at the wall. She looked scared and completely overwhelmed.

Jordan's heart, which was already aching, seized with a new, cold dread. *It didn't work.* He knew it instantly. If the surgery had been a success, she wouldn't look like this. Something must have gone wrong.

"Aubrie? What... what's wrong?" he asked, his voice cracking.

Aubrie let out a choked, wounded sound, unable to even turn around.

"Jordan..." a different voice said, soft and strained. Hannah, who had clearly swapped with the others, stepped forward from the corner. She was also upset, her own eyes red-rimmed. She gently put a hand on his arm.

"The doctor... the doctor will be here in just a minute".

"Hannah, tell me. What happened?"

"He'll explain everything," she said, her gaze not quite meeting his. "It's better if you hear it straight from the doctor".
