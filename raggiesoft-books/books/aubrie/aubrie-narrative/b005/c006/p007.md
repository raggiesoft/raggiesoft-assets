# Part 7: The Verdict

The doctor who had confirmed the heart attack stepped back into the room. The entire Group of 10 fell silent. He looked at Jordan, his face set in a grave, professional mask.

"Jordan, I'm afraid the procedure was not successful," he said, his voice quiet but direct.

Jordan’s blood ran cold. He gripped the sheets.

"It... it failed?" he whispered.

"It did," the doctor confirmed. "The blockage is 99%, just as we suspected. But it's not soft plaque. It's calcified, hard as a rock. We couldn't get the balloon to inflate.

He paused, taking a heavy breath. "We tried to force it, and... Jordan, your vitals became highly unstable. You went into distress on the table."

Jordan's eyes darted to Aubrie, who was still by the window, her body shaking.

"The lead surgeon," the doctor continued, "made the decision to abort the procedure immediately. It was the right call. If he had pushed even a few seconds longer, you... you would have died on that table".

The words hit the room like a physical blow. *Died on the table.*

Jordan finally understood. He looked at Aubrie, who was now openly sobbing, her hand over her mouth. He looked at Hannah, who was pale and trembling beside him. This was why they were so upset. They hadn't just been waiting; they had been waiting to hear if he was alive.

"Because the angioplasty failed," the doctor went on, "your only remaining option is a CABG. Open-heart surgery. We can't perform that here. We are arranging your transfer to the specialists at Star City in Roanoke as soon as possible. As I mentioned, the recovery from this will be a very long, slow process."

The doctor gave a sympathetic nod and left the room to make the arrangements.

The group was in shock. Jordan's mind was reeling. Open-heart surgery. Break your chest open. He remembered Mark's words. He looked at Mark, who was standing by the door, looking just as devastated as he felt.

"Mark..." Jordan said, his voice barely audible. "Your uncle. The one who had the bypass... can I talk to him? Can I... can I meet him?".

Mark’s face fell, a look of profound regret replacing his shock. He walked over to the bed. "Oh, man. J, I... I'm so sorry. He's not local. He's not even in Roanoke". He ran a hand through his hair, frustrated. "He lives up in Northern Virginia. Near D.C."

Jordan nodded, a fresh wave of cold fear washing over him. He had to face this completely blind.
