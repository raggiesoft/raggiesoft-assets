# Part 9: The First Watch

The evening dragged on, marked by the steady, quiet beep of the heart monitor. Daniel and Laura returned with an overnight bag for Aubrie, their faces etched with a forced calm for Jordan's sake. As 9:00 PM approached, it was clear there was nothing more to do but wait for the morning.

"Alright, team," Daniel said, his voice heavy with exhaustion. "We're going to head back to the house. We need to get some rest so we can be here for you tomorrow."

One by one, the rest of the Group of 10 came to Jordan's bedside to say goodnight. It was a ritual of quiet, desperate love. He got hugs from Daniel, Laura, Mark, and Hannah. Then came the pact: Madison, Zoe, Lauren, and Sarah each leaned down, giving him a soft, terrified kiss on the cheek or forehead.

Finally, the room was empty. It was just Jordan and Aubrie.

Aubrie let out a long, shaky breath and looked at the small, uncomfortable vinyl sofa by the window. She grabbed the pillows and blankets Daniel had brought. This was the part that upset her—she couldn't be *in* the bed with him, holding him, anchoring him through the night. But as she spread the blanket over the sofa, she felt a grim resolve. She was, at least, in the room with him. She wouldn't be anywhere else.

She grabbed her pajamas from the bag and, with the practiced no privacy normalcy of their dorm life, she began to change right in front of Jordan. It was a small, defiant act of their normal life in this sterile, terrifying place.

Just as she was pulling a t-shirt over her head, the door opened, and the evening nurse came in.

"Just here for my 9 o'clock vitals," the nurse said warmly.

Jordan let out an audible groan. He was so tired of being poked and prodded. "I know, I know," the nurse chuckled, wrapping the blood pressure cuff around his arm. "But we've got to keep a close eye on you. The doctor wants to know the second anything changes." Jordan just nodded, understanding but annoyed.

The nurse jotted down his numbers, then looked over at Aubrie, who was perched on the edge of her makeshift bed. "Are you going to be alright on that thing, honey?" she asked, her voice kind. "It's not exactly a five-star hotel".

"I'll be fine," Aubrie said, forcing a small smile. "As long as I'm here."

"Okay." The nurse dimmed the main lights. "You two try and get some sleep".

She left, plunging the room into a quiet darkness, lit only by the glowing numbers on Jordan's monitor. Aubrie laid down on the sofa, her back to the window, her eyes fixed on the man she loved. Jordan lay in the hospital bed, staring at the ceiling, listening to the steady, electronic beep that was the only proof he was still alive. Neither of them would sleep.
