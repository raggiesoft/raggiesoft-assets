# Part 12: The 6 AM Vitals Check

The 4:00 AM vitals check had come and gone, another jarring interruption of light and noise. Jordan and Aubrie had collapsed back into their respective states of misery—Jordan in the stiff hospital bed, Aubrie on the unforgiving vinyl sofa. They had managed perhaps an hour of thin, fitful sleep when the door *clicked* open again, this time with a new, brighter energy.

"Good morning!" a new nurse said, flipping on the main overhead lights.

Jordan groaned and threw an arm over his eyes. Aubrie jolted awake, her neck stiff and her back aching from the sofa, her mood instantly, deeply grumpy.

"I'm Beth, your day nurse," the woman continued, unfazed. "Just here for the 6:00 AM vitals check".

"You have got to be kidding me," Jordan mumbled into his pillow. He was so tired of being poked.

"I know, I know," Beth said sympathetically as the blood pressure cuff whirred to life on his arm. "We're almost done."

As she jotted down the numbers, Jordan’s stomach let out a loud, hollow growl. He was *starving*. He hadn't eaten anything since before the SOC 101 class yesterday morning, and the enforced NPO status felt like its own special kind of torture.

"Nurse?" he asked, his voice raspy. "Am I... am I still NPO? I'm really, really hungry."

Beth finished her charting and gave him a kind, apologetic look. "I'm so sorry, Jordan. I checked your chart as soon as I came on shift. You're NPO until the surgeons in Roanoke give the all-clear. With a CABG surgery looming, we can't risk putting anything in your stomach."

Jordan’s head fell back onto the pillow with a frustrated sigh.

Aubrie, watching from the sofa, felt her *own* stomach twist with hunger. She was exhausted, grumpy from a night of zero sleep, and desperate for a cup of coffee and a hot breakfast. But she looked at Jordan, who was miserable and being denied even a sip of water, and her own needs vanished.

"The cafeteria opens in thirty minutes," Nurse Beth said, turning to Aubrie. "You should really go get something to eat, honey. You look exhausted."

Aubrie just shook her head, her loyalty absolute. "I'm not leaving him," she said, her voice flat and final. She would not eat a bite in front of Jordan until he was allowed to eat, too.

The nurse, seeing the stubborn, terrified devotion in the young woman's eyes, didn't push. "Alright," she said softly. "I'll be back in a little while to check in. You two try and get some rest."

The door *clicked* shut, leaving them alone once more. Aubrie was hungry, grumpy, and terrified, but she dragged her chair back to his bedside and took his hand, resuming her watch.
