# Part 13: The Saturday Shift Change

The 6:00 AM vitals check ended, and Nurse Beth left the room, leaving Jordan and Aubrie in the harsh, sterile light of the new day. It was now Saturday, November 14th, 2015. Aubrie, having spent the entire night bolt-upright in the vinyl sofa, was a wreck. Her eyes were red-rimmed and puffy, her back ached, and she was dizzy with hunger, having refused to eat while Jordan was NPO.

She was running on pure, terrified adrenaline, and it was beginning to run out.

Just before 7:00 AM, the door opened. It wasn't a nurse this time, but Daniel and Laura Brooks, their faces etched with worry. With them was Madison.

"Oh, honey," Laura said, her voice full of compassion as she looked at Aubrie's exhausted state. She immediately went and wrapped her in a warm, motherly hug. "You look exhausted. You need to rest."

"I can't," Aubrie whispered, her voice cracking as she clung to Laura, but her eyes never left Jordan. "I can't leave him."

Daniel, meanwhile, had gone to Jordan's bedside. "How are you holding up, son?"

"Hungry," Jordan grumbled, the word raspy. He looked past Daniel to the doctor, who had entered behind them. "Doctor, am I still NPO? Please, I'm starving".

The doctor gave him a grim, apologetic look. "I'm afraid so, Jordan. You're stable, but you're waiting for a major surgery. We can't have anything in your system when that bed in Roanoke opens up. It could be any minute, or it could be tomorrow. Until then, it's IV fluids only."

The news was a crushing blow. Jordan let his head fall back onto the pillow in defeat.

"Aubrie," Daniel said gently, "Madison is here to take over. You've done more than enough. You need to come home with Laura, get a hot shower, and sleep in a real bed. You won't be any good to him if you collapse."

Aubrie looked at Jordan, her resolve warring with her exhaustion.

"Go," Jordan whispered, forcing a weak smile. "I'll be okay. Madi's here." He needed her to be strong for what was coming, and she couldn't do that if she was running on empty.

With a final, tearful kiss on his forehead, Aubrie finally relented. She let Laura guide her from the room. Madison immediately took the now-vacant chair by the bed, taking Jordan's hand in hers.

"Hey, J," she said softly, her "safe person" energy a familiar comfort. "I've got the watch".
