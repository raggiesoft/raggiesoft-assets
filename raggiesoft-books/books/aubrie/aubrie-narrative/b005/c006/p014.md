# Part 14: The Longest Day

Saturday was, by far, the most agonizing day of Jordan's life. The decision for the CABG had been made, but the call from Star City Roanoke General never came. There was no bed available.

He was trapped. He was still NPO, and the hunger had graduated from an annoyance to a constant, gnawing pain. His chest ached, a dull, heavy reminder of the failed procedure. And he was terrified.

The Group of 8, true to their pact, rotated in and out of the room all day, keeping Madison company. Mark and Hannah, Lauren and Sarah, and Zoe all came, bringing shifts of fresh coffee and cafeteria food *for Madison*. They were careful to eat in the hallway or the main waiting room, their respect for Jordan's NPO status absolute.

They tried to keep his spirits up, talking about classes, telling stupid jokes, and discussing the upcoming blizzard, but their cheerfulness was brittle. Every time the door opened, they all flinched, hoping it was the transport team, and sagged when it was just a nurse for another vitals check.

By evening, Jordan was miserable, grumpy, and overwhelmed by the sheer, agonizing *boredom* of waiting for a life-or-death surgery.
