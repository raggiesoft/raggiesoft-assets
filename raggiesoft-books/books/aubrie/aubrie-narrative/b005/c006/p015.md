# Part 15: The Second Watch

As night fell, the rest of the group said their goodnights. Daniel and Laura returned, bringing a fresh overnight bag, this time for Madison.

"No change?" Daniel asked.

Madison just shook her head, her own expression weary. "No change. Still waiting".

The Brooks parents left, and Madison settled in for the second watch. She changed into pajamas in the en-suite bathroom, a small, defiant act of normalcy, and set up her pillows and blankets on the dreaded vinyl sofa.

"You going to be okay on that thing?" Jordan asked, his voice weak.

"Nope," Madison said, flopping onto it with a grimace. "It's awful. But I'm here. Try to sleep, J."

But sleep was impossible. The relentless 2-hour vitals checks continued, a jarring torture of light and whirring cuffs. Between the checks, Jordan and Madison talked quietly in the dark, the beep of the heart monitor a steady rhythm beneath their words. They talked about Bluewater, about their shared classes, about how much they missed their stupid, demolished dorm.

It was an intimacy born of shared crisis, a deepening of their "safe person" bond that went beyond the pact, forged in the sterile quiet of a hospital night.
