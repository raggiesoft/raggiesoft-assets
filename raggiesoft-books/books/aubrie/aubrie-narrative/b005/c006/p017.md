# Part 17: The Call

Sunday, November 15th, dragged on, a near-perfect, agonizing copy of the day before. Lauren sat the long day shift, her presence a quiet, steady comfort. She and Jordan didn't talk much; there was nothing left to say. They listened to the relentless, steady beep of the heart monitor, each passing hour of waiting ratcheting up the tension.

Jordan was weak, miserable, and still starving, his NPO status a constant, grinding misery.

Just after 2:00 PM, the door to his room opened. It wasn't a nurse. It was the cardiologist, and for the first time in two days, he was smiling.

"We got it," he announced, his voice full of energy. "A CVICU bed just opened up at Star City in Roanoke. They're expecting you. I've got a critical care transport team on the way right now. We're moving you."

The relief in the room was so powerful it was a physical force. It was finally happening.

"I'm calling Daniel," Lauren said, her hands shaking as she pulled out her mPhone. She quickly relayed the news to the "Jordan Comms" chat.

Jordan Comms:

Lauren: \[URGENT\] \[MEDICAL\] They're moving him! A bed opened up at Star City in Roanoke! Transport is on the way to New River Valley NOW. Daniel B: \[ACK\] We're mobilizing. The convoy will be at the ER ambulance bay in 10 minutes to follow you. Aubrie: \[ACK\] On our way! See you there!
