# Part 1: Arrival at Star City

The grueling, two-hour crawl on U.S. 11 finally ended as the ambulance, followed closely by the "Rubber Duck" convoy, navigated the streets of Roanoke. The ambulance bypassed the main ER, pulling directly up to the specialized entrance of the Star City Heart Institute.

Daniel parked the minivan in the visitor's garage, and the Group of 10 hurried inside, their nerves frayed from the agonizingly slow journey. They found the CVICU (Cardiovascular Intensive Care Unit) waiting room, a large, quiet space, and were met by the rest of the Brooks clan. Zoe’s six siblings and Ben Carter, having driven directly from Christiansburg, were already there, their faces etched with worry. The full Group of 17 was now assembled.

A nurse emerged, her scrubs a calm blue. "Family for Jordan Fuller?"

Daniel and Laura stepped forward. "We are. We're his emergency contacts. How is he?"

"He's stable," the nurse said, her voice kind. "The delay wasn't ideal, but he's a strong young man. We've just gotten him settled in his prep room. The surgery is scheduled for 6:00 AM tomorrow morning". She looked at the large, anxious crowd. "He's heavily sedated and needs to rest. I can only allow one overnight visitor."

The group looked at each other. It was Lauren's turn in the rotation. She stepped forward, her overnight bag already in hand. "I'll stay," she said quietly. "I'm with him".

"Okay," Daniel said, placing a hand on her shoulder. "We'll head back to Christiansburg and get some sleep. We'll be back here before the sun comes up".

One by one, the family went in to see Jordan for a minute. He was in a private CVICU prep room, an IV line already in place. He was hooked up to a new, more complex set of monitors, including a remote heart monitor. This telemetry unit would broadcast his heart data to the nurses' station at all times, ensuring the CVICU team could respond immediately if anything changed while he waited for his surgery. He looked small, pale, and terrified.

Aubrie was the last to say goodnight, leaning down to give him a long, desperate kiss. "I love you. You're going to be okay," she whispered, her voice breaking. "We'll be right here in the morning."

Finally, the room was empty. It was just Jordan and Lauren.

Lauren pulled the visitor's chair close to the bed. It was a slightly more comfortable, reclining model than the vinyl torture-sofa at New River Valley. She settled in, pulling her blanket up.

"Hey, J," she said softly, taking his hand. "You're not alone. I'm right here. Just try to rest".

Jordan looked at her, his eyes wide with fear in the dim light of the room. He couldn't speak, so he just squeezed her hand back, a silent, grateful plea. Lauren squeezed back, anchoring him. She was on watch.
