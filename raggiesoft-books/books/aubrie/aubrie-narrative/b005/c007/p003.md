# Part 3: The Theatrical Cut

The long hours of Sunday evening began to stretch into an agonizing, silent dread. The only sounds in the CVICU prep room were the steady, electronic beep of Jordan's remote heart monitor and the quiet hiss of his IV pump. Jordan, still NPO and terrified, knew he wouldn't sleep. He needed a distraction, something to pull his mind away from the 6:00 AM surgery and the doctor's grim warnings.

"Lauren," he whispered, his voice raspy. "My backpack... can you get my laptop? I... I should at least try to look at my calculus homework".

Lauren, grateful for a task, quickly retrieved his new Pinnacle Precisionist 5000 from the bag they'd packed. She helped him settle the laptop on the rolling bedside table and pushed it over his lap. Jordan stared at the screen, but the simple act of trying to work was immediately impossible. His left arm was tethered by the IV line, and his right hand had the pulse oximeter clipped to his finger. The wires from the heart monitor leads snaked across his chest, restricting his movement. He couldn't get a comfortable angle to type, and his mind, clouded by fear and hunger, couldn't focus on the complex equations.

"It's no use," he sighed, pushing the laptop away in frustration. "I can't... I can't even type. All these wires... this thing is only good for watching movies".

Lauren’s face lit up with a small, sad smile. "A movie, huh?" she said, trying to inject some normalcy into the sterile room. "You know, after that 12-hour marathon, I never got to show you the original. The one we all grew up with."

She took the laptop. "How about we watch the 1987 theatrical cut of *The Silver Gauntlet of Aethel*?" "It’s only two hours. It’ll help pass the time".

"Okay," Jordan whispered, grateful for any distraction.

Lauren found the movie on a streaming service and dimmed the room's lights. They watched as the familiar, synthesized score of "A Sister's Love (Main Theme)" filled the quiet room, a strange comfort against the beep of the monitor.

Jordan was immediately captivated. He saw the core story he loved, but it was truncated, rushed. Malakor's origin was gone, Kaela's entire rebellion subplot was missing, and the "Bridge of a Thousand Truths" sequence was cut to a brief musical montage.

When the credits rolled, Jordan was quiet for a long moment.

"It's good," he said finally, "but... I prefer the 12-hour version". He looked at Lauren, his mind clear on this one subject. "So much of the *story* was cut out just to fit it into a 2-hour window. Malakor's 'Tyrant's Throne' song... Kaela's whole plan... that's what made it make sense".

He squeezed her hand. "From now on, I only want to watch the 12-hour *Leviathan Cut*".

Lauren smiled, a genuine, warm smile. "You're 100% right," she agreed. "The 12-hour version is so much better". "It's the only one that matters."

The movie had successfully killed two hours. It was now late, and the 6:00 AM surgery loomed closer.
