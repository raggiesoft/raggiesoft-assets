# Part 4: The Virtual Fortress

The credits for *The Silver Gauntlet of Aethel* finished rolling, plunging the room back into a heavy, apprehensive silence. It was late, and the 6:00 AM surgery loomed. Jordan, still NPO, let out a low, miserable groan. His hunger was a constant, dull ache, but it was nothing compared to the cold dread knotting in his stomach.

Lauren, sitting in the recliner, immediately leaned forward. "J? You okay? Chest hurt?"

"No," Jordan whispered, his voice trembling. "I'm just... I'm scared. What if *she* finds out I'm here?". His mind, no longer distracted by the movie, had gone back to his mother, Aubrie Sinclair. "This is a big hospital. What if she shows up? What if she tries to... to take me?"

Lauren's expression softened. She stood up, perched on the edge of his bed, and took his hand, her grip firm and grounding.

"Hey. Look at me," she said gently. "Your mother, in every single way that matters, is Laura Brooks". "She and Daniel are your legal emergency contacts on every form in this hospital. Aubrie Sinclair has no rights here". "She can't get past the front desk. You are safe".

His breathing steadied, but the fear lingered. He needed more. He needed the architect of that safety.

"I... I want to see Zoe," he whispered.

"That's a great idea," Lauren said, immediately grabbing his laptop. She opened the "InfoLink Messenger" app and quickly initiated a video call to the "Jordan Comms" group chat.

One by one, their faces popped onto the screen, filling a grid with the tired, worried, loving expressions of his family, all huddled together back in the Brooks' living room. He saw Daniel, Laura, Mark, Hannah, Sarah, and Madison.

"Hey, man!" Mark's face was the first to fill the main window. "We're all here. We're all thinking about you."

"Zoe?" Jordan asked, his voice cracking. "Are you there?"

Zoe's face replaced Mark's. She was in her pajamas, her purple hair slightly messy, her analytical eyes soft with a concern that went deeper than data.

"I'm here, Jordan," she said, her voice a calm, steady signal in his chaos.

"I... I was just thinking," Jordan began, his voice thick with emotion. "This is all because of you. Your family... you guys took me in. You unlocked... everything. You're the one who gave me a family I could belong to".

Zoe’s gaze softened. "We're the lucky ones, Jordan. We're just glad you're part of our system." She took a small breath. "I... the data is conclusive, Jordan. My feelings for you are... significant. Romantically".

Before Jordan could even process her words, Aubrie's face, beaming and full of love, cut in.

"She's right, J!" Aubrie said, her voice enthusiastic and clear. "And it's okay! It's *more* than okay!".

She looked right at him through the camera, her blessing absolute. "I need you to hear this before you go in tomorrow. You are welcome to be romantic with all five of us. With me, with Madi, with Lauren, with Sarah, and absolutely with Zoe". "There is no jealousy here. There is no exclusivity. We all love you, and we just want you to be happy and safe".

Jordan looked from Aubrie's smiling face to Zoe's, who was blushing but nodding.

"She's right, Jordan," Zoe affirmed. "The pact is stable. We're all in this together. Now, you get some rest. We have a big day tomorrow."

The call ended. Jordan let his head fall back onto the pillow, his heart still hammering with fear of the surgery, but now also overflowing with a profound, staggering love. He looked at Lauren, who was smiling at him, her eyes shining with tears. He squeezed her hand. He was ready.
