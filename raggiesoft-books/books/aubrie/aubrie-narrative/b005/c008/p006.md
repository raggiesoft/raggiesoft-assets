# Part 6: A Dinner for Two (Plus Seven)

Zoe returned to the room a short time later, balancing a hospital tray in one hand and a cafeteria to-go bag in the other.

"The kitchen just sent this up," she said, placing the tray with Jordan's cup of broth and lime-green ClearSet on his bedside table.

She pulled her own container from the bag. It was a simple piece of grilled chicken and steamed vegetables. "I did some quick research on the way down," she explained, her voice practical as she arranged her meal. "I figured I should start learning what a 'Heart Healthy' diet looks like".

The two of them began to eat. It was a strange, quiet moment. Jordan realized it was the first time he'd ever had dinner alone with just one female member of his family; meals were always a chaotic, communal event with the whole group.

"This is inefficient," Zoe stated, looking at the two of them. "We're missing the rest of the system."

She quickly grabbed her laptop, set it on the rolling table facing them both, and opened InfoLink Messenger. She initiated a group video call to the "Jordan Comms" chat.

One by one, their faces filled the screen from the safety of the Brooks' house: Aubrie, Madison, Lauren, Sarah, Hannah, and Mark.

"Hey! Look at them!" Madison's voice cheered from the speaker. "They're having a date night without us!"

Aubrie, watching from the living room in Geneva Street, felt a wave of warmth wash over her. She watched the way Zoe looked at Jordan, her usual analytical gaze softened with a deep, undeniable affection. She saw the way Jordan looked back, his gratitude and trust in Zoe clear on his pale face. They were, she realized, falling in love.

Aubrie's heart felt happy, not jealous. This was the promise of their pact made real. Jordan was welcome to be romantic with all five women, and seeing him find this new, profound connection with Zoe felt like a victory for their whole family.
