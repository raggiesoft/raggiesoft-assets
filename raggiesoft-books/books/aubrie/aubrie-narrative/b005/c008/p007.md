# Part 7: A CVICU Romance

The video call ended, plunging the CVICU room back into a quiet, intimate silence. Jordan, still weak, looked at Zoe, his heart overflowing with a gratitude and affection that went beyond just the pact. She was his protector, his fellow analyst, and the person who had been by his side when he was at his most vulnerable.

"Zoe," he whispered, his voice raspy. "I... I'm really glad it's you who's stuck here with me."

Zoe, who had been sitting in the recliner, stood up and moved to sit carefully on the edge of his hospital bed, a small, rare smile on her lips. She took his hand—the one without the IV line or the pulse oximeter.

"My feelings for you are... significant, Jordan," she said, her voice soft, confirming what she had said on the call. "The data is conclusive."

Jordan looked at her, then down at his own chest, at the web of EKG wires and the thick, hidden bandage beneath his gown. A wave of frustration and pain passed over him. "I... I wish..." he started, his voice cracking.

He didn't need to finish. Zoe understood instantly. They both wanted to act on the pact, to have the "wild night" they had all joked about, but they were trapped by a new, absolute rule.

"I know," Zoe whispered, her logical mind immediately supplying the constraints. "But we can't." She gently tapped the area near his chest, but not on it. "Sternal precautions. Your system is in a critical recovery state. Any strenuous physical activity is not just forbidden; it's dangerous."

Jordan let out a frustrated, shaky breath. "I know. It just... it hurts. Everything hurts."

"This won't," Zoe said softly.

She leaned in, her gaze fixed on his. This was not a data-gathering kiss; this was a deliberate, romantic act. She pressed her lips to his, a slow, gentle, and deeply loving kiss that conveyed all the longing she felt without making a single physical demand on his recovering body.

When she pulled back, she kept her hand on his, using her other hand to gently brush the hair back from his forehead. "This," she said, "is permitted. And this." She gently, tracing a pattern on the back of his hand with her thumb. "And this." She leaned in and gave him another soft kiss on his cheek.

Jordan closed his eyes, a single tear of relief escaping. This was safe. This was care. This was a new kind of intimacy, one defined by the absolute trust that she would protect him, even from himself.
