# Part 6: The Bed Scale

Later that evening, the nurse, Beth, returned for another check. "Alright, Jordan," she said, "one last task for the night. The doctor wants an updated weight to see how well those diuretics are working."

Zoe looked up from her laptop, her curiosity piqued. "But he can't stand on a scale," she stated, "so how will you check his weight?".

"Magic," Beth smiled. "Or, more accurately, technology. This bed has a scale built right into it".

She began the process. "Okay, Jordan, I'm going to need you to hold still for a second. Zoe, can you make sure his IV lines and the catheter tube are free of the bed frame? I need to zero it out."

Zoe stood and helped clear the lines, watching as the nurse pressed a button on a control panel built into the bed's footboard. The screen on it flashed ZEROING....

"Alright," Beth said. "Now, I'm just going to weigh you with all your bedding on, and then I'll weigh the bedding separately after you're up in the chair tomorrow and subtract the difference. It's the most accurate way."

She pressed another button. The bed whirred almost imperceptibly as the load cells under the mattress calibrated. A moment later, a number appeared on the display.

Beth checked her chart, did a quick calculation, and then looked at Jordan with a bright, encouraging smile.

"That's exactly what we wanted to see," she announced. "Your admission weight when you came in from New River Valley was 342 pounds. You're already down four pounds. That means the diuretics are doing their job perfectly, and we're getting all that extra, dangerous fluid off your system. You are, quite literally, shedding pounds as we speak".

A wave of relief washed over Jordan. For the first time, "losing weight" wasn't about Mark's workout plans or his mother's vague sense of disapproval; it was a clear, positive, medical victory.

Zoe logged the new data, a small, proud smile on her face.
