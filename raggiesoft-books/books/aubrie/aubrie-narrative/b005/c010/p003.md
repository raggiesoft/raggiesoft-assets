# Part 3: The Recovery Protocol

Jordan’s sleep was deep and restorative, a full system reboot after the emotional crash. When he woke hours later, the basement was quiet. He was still on the sectional sofa, his head resting on Aubrie's lap. She was gently stroking his hair, talking in a low voice with Madison, who sat in the armchair nearby, her legs curled under her. The rest of the family was scattered around the room, reading or talking quietly. He felt calm. The storm had passed.

Zoe, who had been at her desk sketching out Fin# architecture, noticed his eyes were open. She walked over, her expression one of calm, analytical focus. This was her childhood home, her sanctuary, and Jordan was a member of her family recovering within it. The logistics of his care were now her responsibility.

"How are you feeling?" she asked, her voice soft but clear.

"Tired," Jordan rasped, his voice still hoarse from the meltdown. "But... okay. I'm okay."

"Good." Zoe nodded, her mind immediately shifting into project manager mode. "You're stable, but we need to adhere to the medical protocol. You need to recover, and I'm taking the lead on logistics while we're here."

She addressed the rest of the group, who were all now paying attention. "Okay, team. Here's the plan. He needs his respiratory exercises every hour. He's on strict sternal precautions. He needs his pain medication in forty-five minutes. And he needs to stay hydrated."

She looked at Lauren. "Lauren, can you be on water and snack duty?"

"Got it," Lauren said, happy to have a task.

"Madi, Aubrie," Zoe continued, "you two are primary comfort detail. He's stable, but he's still post-op. He stays on the sofa for now".

Aubrie and Madison both nodded, resuming their positions as his primary anchors.

Jordan watched this, a profound sense of security washing over him. He wasn't a burden; he was a *project*, and Zoe was the most competent engineer he'd ever met.

"I don't want to just lie here," Jordan said, pushing himself up slightly on Aubrie's lap. "I want to... I don't know. Be part of the day".

"You are," Mark said from across the room. "Your job is to sit right there and heal. Our job is to make sure you're not bored while you do it."

"He's right," Zoe said. She retrieved the incentive spirometer from the coffee table. "First task: breathing exercises. We'll do them together."

Zoe sat on the floor in front of him. "Okay. Ten deep breaths. I'll count for you. One..."

Jordan braced himself, his hand instinctively moving to his sore chest. He took a slow, painful breath, hissing as he pushed the small ball up the chamber.

"Good," Zoe said. "Nine more."

The group watched, their support silent but absolute. Aubrie rubbed his back, and Madison squeezed his hand. He got through all ten.

"See?" Zoe said, logging the completion in her mental file. "You're participating."

He was exhausted from the simple effort, but he felt good. He felt *involved*. He settled back onto Aubrie's lap, one of his safe people, and looked at Madison, another of his safe people, who smiled at him. Zoe, his third safe person, was already checking the clock for his next round of meds. He was in his fortress, surrounded by his generals. The recovery had truly begun.
