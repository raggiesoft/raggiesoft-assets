# Part 6: The Recovery Rotation

The sun rose on Monday, November 23rd, 2015. It was Jordan’s first full day home, and the reality of his situation began to set in. He woke on the sectional sofa, Aubrie having stayed with him on the cushions all night, while Madison slept in the nearby recliner. The rest of the group was stirring, but the mood was different. It wasn't the relaxed, sleepy vibe of a weekend. It was the hurried energy of a class day.

Jordan watched as Mark and Hannah, Zoe and Lauren, all began packing their backpacks for their 6:00 AM bus. A cold, sharp panic, separate from his physical pain, began to rise in his throat.

"You're... you're going to class?" he asked, his voice small. "You're leaving?"

The entire room froze. "J, we have to," Hannah said gently, her face full of guilt. "We have midterms... we'll be back as soon as they're over."

"But... you can't leave me alone," he whispered, the fear of abandonment, his oldest and deepest trauma, rising up. "I... I can't..."

"We're not," a calm, authoritative voice said.

Daniel Brooks, already dressed and holding a cup of coffee, came down the stairs. Laura was right behind him, carrying a tray.

"Good morning, son," Daniel said, sitting in the armchair. "The group *is* going to class. You're right, you can't be left alone. That's why your mother," he nodded at Laura, "and I are staying home with you."

Jordan looked at them, confused. "But... what about your jobs?"

"This is the lucky part," Laura explained, her voice a wave of calm reassurance as she set the tray down. "As it happens, Daniel and I *both* put in for this entire week off, months ago, for the Thanksgiving holiday". "We were just going to be doing some projects around the house."

Daniel grinned. "Then you had to go and have a major medical event. It turned our simple vacation request into a 'medically necessary, non-negotiable' leave of absence". "Our bosses were more than understanding. So, you're stuck with us, kid."

A profound, system-wide relief washed over Jordan. He wasn't a burden. He wasn't forcing anyone to fail their classes. His recovery had, by a lucky coincidence, fit perfectly into his new parents' pre-existing plans.

"Okay," Zoe said, her logistical mind immediately processing the new, efficient protocol. "This is optimal. The Parent-Node will handle daytime care, and the Pact-Node will take over all evening and night support. This ensures Jordan has 24/7 coverage, and our academic progress remains stable."

"Exactly," Daniel said.

Aubrie, Madison, and Zoe, his three autistic safe people, came over. "We'll be on the bus," Aubrie whispered, "but we'll be in the chat. We can video call you between classes. We'll be home for dinner."

"We've got you, J," Madison added, squeezing his arm. "Always."

Jordan looked at his family. He had a 24/7, two-tiered support system. Daniel and Laura would be his primary caregivers during the day, and the entire Group of 8 would be his support system at night.

"Okay," he whispered, a wave of profound relief making him feel weak. "Okay."

The group said their goodbyes and headed out for the early bus. Jordan settled back onto the sofa, his laptop and textbooks on the coffee table. He was home, he was safe, and Daniel Brooks was already in the kitchen, making him a real, heart-healthy breakfast.
