# Part 8: The War Plan

Jordan’s declaration hung in the quiet basement, a definitive statement of his new resolve. Daniel and Laura looked at each other, a shared, proud smile passing between them. Their son was a fighter.

"Okay, Jordan," Daniel said, his voice shifting immediately from comfort to practical, paternal support. "If we're going to pass this semester, we need a war plan."

He and Laura pulled chairs up to the coffee table, transforming the cozy living room into a command center.

"The first problem is documentation," Laura said, opening her own laptop. "We can't just wait for the hospital discharge papers to be mailed. I'll call Star City's records department right now and have them email us the official admission and discharge summaries. We'll forward those to the university registrar and all your professors today".

"Good," Daniel said, picking up the thread. "Next, the work. You're on pain medication and you're going to be exhausted. You can't do it alone. But," he added, his voice firm, "we will *not* do it for you. There will be no academic dishonesty in this house".

He leaned forward. "Here's the new protocol: When you're tired, we'll read your textbooks to you. When your hands are shaky from the meds, you will dictate your English papers, and we will type exactly what you say. When you're working on that complex calculus, you'll solve the problems on a whiteboard, and we'll act as your scribes. We will be your hands and your eyes, but the *work* will be yours."

Jordan nodded, a profound sense of relief washing over him. He wasn't being coddled; he was being enabled.

His mind, now focused on the logistics of the fight, immediately went to the other battlefront.

"The IRS," he said, his voice quiet but steady. "The CP87A. That's still coming".

"Yes, it is," Daniel affirmed, his expression hardening.

"A year ago, that letter would have destroyed me," Jordan said, thinking of the shy, scared boy who didn't know how to dress himself. He looked at Daniel, his eyes clear and cold with a confidence forged in trauma. "She tried to break me. She tried to prove I was incompetent. And I just survived *open-heart surgery*."

He let out a small, humorless laugh. "I just survived a heart attack," he repeated. "I think I can survive a fight with the United States government".

"Damn right you can," Daniel said, his voice thick with pride.

"I'm ready," Jordan said, his gaze shifting to the file folder on the desk—the one they had so carefully prepared after Fall Break. "I have the facts. I have my case. And I have all of you. She's not going to snatch this victory away from me".
