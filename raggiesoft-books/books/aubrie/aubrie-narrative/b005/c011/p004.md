# Part 4: The Academic Counter-Attack

The Black Friday road trip, as simple as it was, had worked. After returning to the basement sanctuary, Jordan felt a renewed sense of purpose. He was still weak, still bound by his sternal precautions, but seeing the world outside had reminded him of the life he was fighting to get back to.

On Saturday morning, he was all business.

"Okay," he announced to Daniel and Laura, who were working quietly in the basement with him. "No more movies. I want to do homework. I need to catch up".

Daniel looked up from his laptop, a proud smile on his face. "I was waiting for you to say that. Let's get to work."

The university, having received the official documentation from Star City, was processing his request for accommodations, but Jordan wasn't waiting. He was driven by his new resolve to pass.

They initiated the "war plan" they had developed earlier in the week. Laura retrieved his Pinnacle laptop and his calculus textbook, setting them up on a rolling table in front of the sofa.

"Alright," Daniel said, pulling up a chair with a small whiteboard. "I'll be your hands. You be the brain. Talk me through this problem set."

It was a slow, grueling process. Jordan, his mind still fuzzy from the lingering effects of anesthesia and pain medication, would read a problem, think for a long moment, and then begin.

"Okay... we need to find the derivative of that function," he'd murmur. "So, we have to use the chain rule. You write..."

Daniel would write the equations on the whiteboard exactly as Jordan dictated, acting as his scribe. When Jordan got stuck, Daniel would simply wait, offering quiet encouragement but never the answer.

It took nearly two hours to get through a single problem set that would have normally taken Jordan thirty minutes. He was exhausted, his chest ached, and he was frustrated, but he had done it.

"It's done," he finally said, letting his head fall back against the cushions.

"See?" Laura said, bringing him a glass of water and his next dose of pain medication. "You're doing it, Jordan. You're passing."

He had survived the heart attack, and now, with his family acting as his hands, he was surviving his final exams. He was winning.
