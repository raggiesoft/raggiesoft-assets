# Part 1: The New Rotation

The 5:15 AM alarms were a harsh, digital intrusion into the quiet darkness of the basement. Jordan woke on the new king-sized bed, Aubrie's steady breathing a comfort beside him. Across the room, he heard the muffled groans of Zoe, Lauren, Madison, and Sarah as they began to stir from their two queen beds. It was Monday, November 30th, and the Thanksgiving break was officially over.

As the women began to get dressed for class, Mark and Hannah came down the internal stairs, already dressed and carrying their backpacks from Zoe's old room upstairs.

"Morning, team," Mark whispered.

"Morning," Jordan rasped, carefully pushing himself into a sitting position. He was sore, but the anxiety of being left alone was gone.

The group's goodbye ritual began. One by one, the five women of the pact came to him: Aubrie, Madison, Zoe, Lauren, and Sarah. Their goodbyes were a mix of soft, romantic kisses and tight, loving hugs, underscored by the quiet promise that they would be home soon. Mark came next, giving his brother a firm, supportive hug.

Hannah was the last. "Alright, J, let's get you set for the day," she said, her voice full of sisterly warmth. She wheeled his chair over and helped him with the careful pivot transfer from the bed. She assisted him in the accessible basement bathroom with his morning toileting, her movements practiced and respectful.

Afterward, she brought him back into the main living area. "Sofa, J?"

"Yeah," he said, wanting to be in the center of the house.

Hannah carefully helped him transfer to the sectional sofa, making sure he was comfortable before helping him lie down so he could rest.

Just as she was arranging his blankets, Megan, Zoe's older sister, came down the internal stairs, her laptop bag over one shoulder. She smiled warmly at Jordan. "Morning, guys. I've got the watch."

The Group of 8 was now ready, backpacks on. They gathered around the sofa for one last group hug goodbye. Jordan held onto them, dreading the sudden quiet.

"We'll be back as soon as classes are done," Aubrie promised, giving him one last kiss.

"Try not to miss us too much," Madison teased.

"We'll be in the chat," Zoe confirmed.

Then, they were gone, heading out into the cold morning to catch the 6:00 AM bus to CPI.

The basement suddenly felt vast and silent. Megan, sensing his unease, moved to fill the quiet. She placed her laptop on the dining table, knowing her workday didn't start until 8:00 AM.

"What can I get you to drink?" she asked, her voice kind.

Jordan’s first thought was a craving for hot chocolate, but he quickly shook his head. He knew he had to be careful with his heart. "Just ice water, please."

"Good choice," she said. She returned a moment later with two glasses of ice water. She handed one to him and then, instead of going to her desk, she paused. She remembered Zoe's briefing: *He needs grounding. He likes hugs. A lot*. She also remembered seeing him lying so comfortably on Aubrie's lap the night before.

She sat on the edge of the sofa near his head. "You look like you're missing your anchors," she said gently. "Want to move? You can rest your head on my lap, if you'd like. I don't mind a bit."

Jordan looked up at her, a wave of gratitude washing over him. He trusted her; she was Zoe's sister. He carefully shifted, settling his head onto her lap. It was comfortable.

"Thank you," he whispered.

Megan smiled, her hand coming up to gently rest in his hair. "Anytime, Jordan," she whispered back. "I'm perfectly fine with you wanting to hug. Zoe explained. You just rest".

He closed his eyes, his system stabilizing. He wasn't alone. His new sister was on watch.
