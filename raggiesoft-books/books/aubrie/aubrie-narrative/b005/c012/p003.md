# Part 3: The Daytime Watch

A comfortable, productive quiet settled over the basement. Megan was at the dining table, her laptop open, her focus entirely on her remote work job. She was in Jordan's line of sight, a constant, reassuring presence. Jordan, resting on the sofa, had his own laptop set up on the coffee table. He wore headphones, allowing him to quietly watch movies and listen to music without disturbing Megan in case she had a meeting.

He was halfway through a lecture video for his astronomy class when the familiar, urgent pressure of the diuretics returned. He paused the video and looked over at Megan.

"Megan?"

She looked up from her screen instantly, her focus shifting from work to him. "What's up, Jordan?"

"I... I'm sorry to bother you, but I need to use the toilet," he said, his face flushing slightly.

"No bother at all," Megan said, standing up without a moment's hesitation. She reminded herself that this was the deal; this was why she was here, to assist him so he was never alone. This was just family looking out for family.

She brought his wheelchair over to the sofa and helped him with the careful pivot transfer, her movements steady and confident. She wheeled him into the accessible basement bathroom.

"Alright," she said, her voice kind and practical, "what do you need? No strings attached".

Her open, judgment-free offer put him at ease. He told her what he needed, and she helped him with the parts of the transfer and clothing he couldn't yet manage on his own. Once he was done toileting, she helped him get settled back in his chair.

She wheeled him back to the sofa, and they reversed the transfer, getting him comfortably settled back on the cushions. Before she moved away, she leaned down and gave him a warm, sisterly hug.

"You good?" she asked.

"Yeah," he said, grateful. "Thank you, Megan."

"Anytime," she smiled. She then went back to the dining table and resumed her work.

A moment later, Jordan's mPhone buzzed on the coffee table. He picked it up. A new message had come through on the Jordan Comms chat.

Jordan Comms:

Madison: \[IMAGE ATTACHED: A blurry selfie of her and Aubrie in the back of a crowded bus\] Ugh, the 6:00 AM bus is the WORST. But we're on our way! See you this afternoon! Mark: Hang in there, J. We'll be back before you know it.

Jordan smiled, his world perfectly in balance. He had his sister's quiet, steady presence in the room and the chaotic, loving energy of his friends just a text message away. He put his headphones back on and returned to his movie.
