# Part 5: The Sister's Watch

At 4:30 PM, Megan closed her work laptop with a satisfied sigh. Her workday was officially over. She looked over at Jordan, who was quietly watching a movie on his own laptop, headphones on. The basement was peaceful, but now the anticipation for the group's return was a tangible thing. They were waiting for their family to come home.

Megan stood up and stretched, then walked over to the sofa. Jordan, sensing her movement, paused his movie and took his headphones off.

"Work's done," she said with a warm smile. "How are you holding up?"

"Okay," he said. "Just... tired. And sore."

Megan sat on the sofa beside him. She was, as Zoe had briefed her, the designated caregiver, but she was also discovering something new. She enjoyed this. Cuddling with Jordan earlier had felt... nice. It made her feel wanted, a feeling she hadn't had in a while as a single woman living in a small studio apartment. She wondered why she still lived in that cramped place; there was certainly no room to work, and this house felt so much more alive. Of course, it was nice to live on her own, but this feeling of connection was powerful.

She understood her role here was as the lovable older sister, and her main priority was to make Jordan feel comfortable and safe. But as she cuddled with him, she couldn't help but worry. She didn't want her sister Zoe, or any of the other women in the pact, to get angry with her or think she was trying to steal Jordan away. She knew his story. She knew he had been made to feel like a burden his whole life, and that it was Aubrie's radical idea that no one should ever hog him.

"You look like you could use another hug," she offered gently.

He smiled, a look of pure relief on his face. He shifted, and she settled in beside him, letting him rest his head on her shoulder as she wrapped an arm around him. She decided, for now, that this was the safest, best path. She would only hug and cuddle Jordan, offering the platonic comfort he so clearly craved, until she could get clear guidance from the pact of five themselves. She would not cross a line.

"So," she said, her voice a soft, comforting rumble. "Tell me about this astrophysics stuff. I'm trying to keep up with Zoe and Aubrie, but half the time I just nod and smile."

Jordan laughed, a small, raspy sound. "It's not that bad. It's just... finding patterns. Like, did you know that almost every element in your body, the iron in your blood, the calcium in your bones... it was all forged inside a star that exploded billions of years ago?"

"Really?" Megan asked, genuinely fascinated. "So I'm literally made of stardust?"

"We all are," Jordan said, a note of wonder in his own voice.

They talked for a long time, Megan asking about his classes and him asking about her job. He learned about her small apartment and her work as a remote data analyst. He was so easy to talk to, and the comfortable, platonic intimacy was exactly what he needed.

After a while, he stirred, his face flushing. "Ugh. Megan, I'm so sorry... I have to... you know." The diuretics were still, relentlessly, doing their job.

"Not a problem," she said, getting up immediately. "Transfer protocol: engaged."

With the same gentle, no-fuss efficiency as before, she helped him to his chair and into the accessible basement bathroom. She assisted him with what he needed, her actions all practical care, no awkwardness.

Once he was done and they had washed up, she helped him get settled back on the sofa. He was exhausted from the simple effort, but he was clean and comfortable. Megan sat beside him again, and he immediately leaned against her, his head finding its familiar spot on her shoulder.

"They should be home soon," she whispered, glancing at the clock.

"Good," he mumbled, closing his eyes. He was safe. His sister was on watch. And the rest of his family was on the way.
