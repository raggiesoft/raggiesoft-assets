# Part 6: The Evening Prep

As the late afternoon light began to fade in the basement, Megan glanced at the clock. It was nearly 5:00 PM. The Group of 8 would be embarking on their long, crowded commute home from CPI soon.

"Alright," she said, standing up from the sofa. "The team will be home in about an hour and a half, and I'm guessing they'll be starving. I'm going to start on dinner."

She looked toward the internal stairs. The main kitchen upstairs was large, modern, and perfectly equipped to cook a meal for nine people. But as she looked back at Jordan, who was resting peacefully on the sofa, she immediately dismissed the thought. Her promise to Zoe and her own instincts were clear: she would not leave Jordan alone, not even for a minute.

"Okay, basement kitchenette," she muttered to herself. "Let's see what we can do."

She went to the small kitchen area, which was equipped with a microwave, a small stovetop, and a half-sized refrigerator. She began pulling out ingredients her mother had stocked for them.

Her plan was simple but thoughtful: a heart-healthy meal. She found boneless, skinless chicken breasts, a bag of quinoa, and fresh broccoli. It was a perfect, compliant meal that she could easily prepare in the small space.

As she seasoned the chicken and started the quinoa, her thoughts were on the pact. She knew, with an older sister's intuition, that after a long, stressful day of classes, the first thing Aubrie, Madison, Zoe, Lauren, and Sarah would want to do was to reconnect with Jordan. They would want to pile onto the king bed with him, to hold him, and to just *be* with him.

She was making this decision for them. By having dinner ready the moment they walked in, she was giving them a gift: the freedom to go straight to him, to have their romantic cuddles and decompress without the added burden of having to cook for everyone.

Jordan watched her from the sofa, the simple, domestic sounds of someone cooking for him a profound comfort. He wasn't alone. He was being cared for, and his family would be home soon.
