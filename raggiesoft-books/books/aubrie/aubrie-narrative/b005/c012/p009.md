# Part 9: The Midnight Confession

It was late, but sleep wouldn't come. Jordan lay still in the center of the massive king-sized bed, trying to find a position that didn't pull at the deep, constant ache in his sternum. Every night was a new battle against the physical pain of his recovery.

But tonight, a new feeling was layered on top of the pain: an unfamiliar, fluttering warmth in his stomach. Butterflies. He was thinking about Megan.

He replayed the day in his mind: her cheerful arrival, her practical care, the way she'd sat with him, her arm draped protectively around his shoulders as he rested his head on her lap. He remembered the warm, strong, sisterly hug she’d given him when she helped him with the toilet.

His mind immediately tried to slot this new, warm feeling into the rules of his new life. The pact. He was starting to *like* Megan. He knew the rules: the pact of five meant there was no jealousy. He cautiously wondered if she, too, could have a place there.

The thought was immediately crushed by his own internal logic. *No, how could she?* She was 27. He was 19. The eight-year age gap felt insurmountable. It was true that he was a legal adult, a fact he had just proven in a court of law. It was true his mother had lost all power over him. And yet, he was still coming to terms with the very *idea* of the pact, that a life without jealousy could even exist. His mother's legacy programming was a deep, tenacious rootkit.

He was stuck, struggling with these new, confusing feelings. The ache in his chest and the butterflies in his stomach were a strange, restless combination. He sighed quietly, turning his head on the pillow. He couldn't sleep. He just knew he was already looking forward to the morning, when he could get another hug from Megan.

"Can't sleep?" a soft voice whispered.

Aubrie was awake beside him, watching him in the dim light from the hallway. She could see the faint glow on his face, the restless energy. She smiled, a soft, knowing smile.

"It just... hurts," he whispered, which was only half the truth.

She knew that look. She knew what he was feeling. As the pact's original architect, she wasn't sure how to tell him, or even if she should, but she was already processing the data. She was ready and willing to welcome Megan into their family, if that was what everyone wanted.
