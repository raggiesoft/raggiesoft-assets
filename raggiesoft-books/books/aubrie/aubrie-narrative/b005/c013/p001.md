# Part 1: The New Routine

The 5:15 AM alarms were a harsh, digital intrusion, but they were now a familiar part of the day. Jordan woke on the king-sized bed, Aubrie's warmth beside him. The basement stirred to life as the Group of 8 began their morning "get ready for class" montage.

The care protocol was now a seamless, practiced dance. Hannah and Lauren, his "strong" team, helped him with the initial transfer to his wheelchair. Aubrie and Madison, his "safe people," helped him with toileting and washing up in the accessible bathroom. He was getting stronger, but he still needed their assistance.

As Aubrie wheeled him out of the bathroom, she started to head toward the comfortable sectional sofa.

"Wait," Jordan said, his voice firm. "Stop."

She stopped the wheelchair, confused. "What's wrong?"

"I don't want the sofa today," he said, a new resolve in his voice. "I want to work. I'm tired of lying down. Can you help me get my laptop set up at the dining table?".

Aubrie stared at him for a second, then a huge, proud smile spread across her face. This was the fighter she knew. "Absolutely," she said.

Just as they were getting him settled at the table, Megan came down the internal stairs, her laptop bag over her shoulder, ready for the handoff.

"Morning, team," she said cheerfully.

Jordan looked up, and his face lit up in a way that was becoming increasingly familiar. "Morning, Megan."

She came over and leaned down, giving him his now-customary morning hug. Jordan's arms wrapped around her, and this time, he held on, his face buried in her shoulder. The hug lingered, lasting just a little bit longer than a simple platonic greeting.

Aubrie watched them, a soft, knowing smile on her face. She saw the butterflies. She saw the conflict in his eyes and the gentle, sisterly care in Megan's. She wasn't sure what words to use, but she knew, as the pact's architect, that she was 100% ready to approve this merger. She was willing to welcome Megan into their family, if that's what they both wanted.

The rest of the Group of 8, now ready to leave, came over for their goodbyes. The pact of five gave him their loving, romantic kisses, and Hannah and Mark gave him their strong, platonic hugs. With a final wave, they headed up the stairs and out into the cold morning to catch their bus.

The basement was quiet again, but this time, Jordan felt a new energy. He was sitting at a table, ready to work, just like a normal student.

Megan, seeing his setup, smiled and set her own laptop on the dining table right next to his.

"Good plan," she said. "We can be work buddies."

This new arrangement was perfect. They were close enough that they could pause their work whenever he needed to, and he could easily reach over and get the hugs he craved. He was ready to face the day.
