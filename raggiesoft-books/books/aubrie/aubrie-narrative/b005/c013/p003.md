# Part 3: The Work Buddies

The morning settled into a new, productive rhythm. Jordan, propped up in his wheelchair at the dining table, focused on his laptop, trying to catch up on the work he had missed. Beside him, Megan was focused on her own screen, her fingers tapping away as she did her remote work. They were seated side-by-side, a quiet team, which gave Jordan a profound sense of security. He knew that if something were to go wrong, Megan was right there to help.

His recovery was a constant, physical presence. He was still on a heavy regimen of pain medication, and a wave of exhaustion made it difficult to focus, his eyes feeling heavy. But he was determined. He would pass this semester; he would not take an Incomplete. He had come too far and fought too hard to let this surgery stop him.

Every so often, he would need to shift his weight in the chair, a simple act that was now a careful, deliberate process. He had to be VERY careful when moving, to avoid jostling the healing bones of his chest. As he would adjust himself, he could sometimes feel the faint, unnerving creak of his sternum, a grim reminder of how close he was to the edge.

He paused, a wave of fatigue washing over him, and just sat, listening to the quiet, rhythmic tapping of Megan's keyboard. He was so happy she was there keeping him company. The quiet of the day before, with just Daniel and Laura, had been nice, but this was better. This was the comfortable, companionable silence he had grown used to.

He looked forward to Winter Break, when the rest of the Group of 8 would be home from CPI every day, and the basement would be full of their chaotic, wonderful energy again. He smiled, closed his eyes for just a minute, and then, with a deep, determined breath, he turned his attention back to his calculus homework.
