# Part 4: A Week of a New Normal

The rest of the week settled into a predictable, healing rhythm. The 5:15 AM alarms signaled the start of the daily handoff. The Group of 8 would get dressed for class while Hannah or Lauren helped Jordan with his morning transfer and toileting, settling him on the sofa. Then, the ritual of goodbyes would begin: one by one, the pact of five would come to him for their romantic kisses, followed by hugs from Mark and Hannah.

Just as they were heading up the stairs, Megan would come down, her arrival timed perfectly for the handoff. The house would fall silent, and Jordan’s workday would begin.

He and Megan spent the days as quiet work buddies. They sat at the dining table, laptops open, a few feet apart. He worked relentlessly on his assignments, determined to pass his classes. He was still on a regimen of pain medication that left him exhausted, and he had to be constantly, agonizingly careful of his sternum. But he was not alone. Megan was there for every bathroom break, every dose of medication, and every time he just needed a grounding, sisterly hug.

At 11:30 AM, she would call her lunch break, and they would eat their heart-healthy sandwiches together. The highlight of his day was the group video call from the CPI cafeteria, a blast of happy, chaotic energy that patched him into his family. Then, the long, quiet afternoon of work would resume until the glorious sound of the basement door opening announced the group’s return.

On Friday evening, they burst in, but this time with a new, excited energy.

"We have the final project for English 102!" Aubrie announced, dropping her bag by the sofa.

"Dr. Evans assigned the groups today," Zoe explained, "and he decided to honor the spirit of your medical accommodations".

Mark grinned. "He assigned all eight of us as one single group. It's us against the world, J."

Jordan felt a surge of gratitude. Dr. Evans understood. "That's... that's amazing. What's the project?"

"It's a research and advocacy proposal. We identify a major issue on campus and present a detailed, written solution. It's in place of a final exam".

"A proposal?" Jordan said, his mind already turning. "I know what we should do. Accessibility. We should do a full proposal on making the older campus buildings truly compliant."

"That's a brilliant idea, J!" Hannah said.

"And," Jordan added, his voice quiet but firm, "I want to present it. With you".

The group looked at him, surprised.

"I know, I know," he said, holding up a hand. "I can't take the bus. And I know I can't be on campus all day. But just... for this one class. Daniel can drive me. I can go in, give my part of the presentation, and then come straight home".

A slow, proud smile spread across Aubrie's face. He wasn't just recovering; he was planning his comeback.

"It's a plan," Zoe said, a rare, brilliant smile on her face. "Let's get to work."
