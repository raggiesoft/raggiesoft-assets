# Part 11: The Smokin' Anvil

The Smokin' Anvil was bustling with the late Saturday morning crowd, but the family convoy, eleven strong, managed to secure a large rectangular table near the back. Jordan was placed at the head of the table in his wheelchair, with Aubrie on his left and Madison on his right. Megan, smiling, sat directly across from him. The rest of the family filled in the sides: Daniel sat next to Aubrie, Laura next to Madison, and Mark, Hannah, Zoe, Lauren, and Sarah filled the remaining chairs, creating a loud, happy, protective bubble.

A cheerful waitress took their orders. Jordan, savoring his first meal out, ordered scrambled eggs with American cheese, bacon, wheat toast, and an orange juice. The others ordered a mix of breakfast platters—Daniel and Mark opted for the "Anvil Slam," while Megan, still in solidarity, ordered a fruit and yogurt parfait.

The family was talking and laughing, everyone proud of Jordan for how far he had come in just one short year. He was in the middle of telling Megan a story when the bistro's front bell chimed.

A woman entered, and Jordan’s blood ran cold.

He froze, his fork clattering against his plate. It was *her*. The same height, the same build, the same dark hair. It was Aubrie Sinclair. His breath hitched, and he instinctively gripped Aubrie's hand under the table, his knuckles turning white.

"Jordan? What's wrong?" Aubrie whispered, seeing the color drain from his face.

Megan, sitting across from him, saw his eyes lock on the door, and her own "big sister" defenses went on high alert.

Before Jordan's mind could fully process the perceived threat and spiral into a meltdown, the woman at the counter spoke to the cashier. "I'll just have a large coffee to go, please," she said, her voice a high, cheerful Southern drawl.

It wasn't her. The voice was totally different. It was just a random coincidence.

Jordan let out a long, shaky breath, his heart still hammering. He felt Aubrie rubbing circles on his hand. He was okay. He was safe.

He looked at the woman again as she paid for her coffee and left. She was just a stranger. He took a sip of his orange juice, forcing his hands to steady. He did not want this to defeat him. He wanted to live his life.

He knew, in that moment, that even if his mother *had* walked in, he was not afraid anymore. He looked around the table. He had this incredible family, all of them sitting right here with him, ready to defend him in every way that mattered. They had decided he was one of them. He looked at Aubrie, Madison, Zoe, Lauren, and Sarah—his pact of five. He looked at Megan. He knew for a fact that all of them were his safe people. He looked at Daniel and Laura. They *were* his parents. Not his biological mother. Not his biological father, who had been killed before he was born. He looked at Mark and Hannah. He trusted them unconditionally.

His fear, unanchored by his old programming, simply dissolved.

He finished the last bite of his scrambled eggs and turned to Mark. "Mark," he said, his voice clear. "I want to start working out again. When can we resume the exercise protocol?"

Mark smiled, but shook his head. "Not just yet, J. We're not doing *that* protocol. I've got a better idea, and it should be in that thick discharge binder Zoe's been guarding".

Jordan looked confused.

"My uncle went through the same thing, remember?" Mark explained. "You're on sternal precautions, so you can't just go to a gym. In a week or two, a home health nurse is going to start visiting to make sure your recovery is on track". "And then, you'll start Cardiac Rehab".

"Cardiac... what?"

"Cardiac Rehab," Mark repeated. "It's exactly what it sounds like. You'll go to a special facility a few times a week, and you'll do exercises under the direct supervision of nurses. They'll have you hooked up to a heart monitor, watching you the whole time to make sure you're safe. It's how you'll get your strength back, man. It's how you'll resume living your life".
