# Part 4: The Presentation

The group presented third. Zoe led, her voice clear and analytical as she laid out the project's parameters and the flaws in the university's current information system. Mark followed, briefly explaining the technical back-end solution they were proposing. Then, it was Jordan's turn.

Hannah, who had been standing by, moved to his side, holding up an mPad so he could read his notes without having to twist or strain his neck to see the main screen. This left Jordan's hands free to clutch his heart pillow, bracing his sternum as he faced his classmates.

He spoke with a quiet, steady conviction. This wasn't just an assignment; it was his life. "The current map isn't just inaccurate," he said, his voice finding its strength. "It's a barrier. When I arrived here, I spent my first weeks lost because the resources I needed weren't on any map". He spoke of his personal experiences trying to find elevators, push button doors, and accessible routes—like the one in Bennett Hall—that were completely undocumented. "Our proposal isn't about spending millions on new construction. It's about providing accurate information. It's about telling students where the tools to succeed *already* are."

When he finished, a respectful, impressed applause filled the room. The entire Group of 8, standing with him, was beaming with pride.

They took their seats as a group and stayed for the rest of the 50-minute class, listening to the other presentations. When the professor dismissed them, Dr. Evans came over to Jordan, his expression full of warmth.

"Mr. Fuller, that was exceptional," he said. "I am so proud of how far you've come. Just to confirm, your accommodation paperwork is all set. You're clear to complete the rest of the semester remotely".

"Thank you, Dr. Evans," Jordan said, feeling a huge weight lift from his shoulders. "For everything."

In the hallway, the Group of 8 huddled for a quick, emotional goodbye. His presentation was over, and they had to run to their next classes. One by one, the pact of five gave him proud, loving kisses, and Mark and Hannah gave him careful, strong hugs.

He was sad to leave. This one hour of normalcy, of being a student with his friends, was the best he'd felt in weeks. He watched them hurry down the hall. He turned his wheelchair, and Megan was there, waiting by the building's entrance, her own face full of pride. It was time to go home and rest.
