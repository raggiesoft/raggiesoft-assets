# Part 6: The New Safe Person

The ride from CPI back to Christiansburg was heavy with the aftershock of the meltdown. Megan, having calmed Jordan down to a state of exhausted, tearful quiet, drove with both hands gripping the wheel, her own adrenaline slowly ebbing, leaving her shaking. Jordan just stared out the passenger window, his heart pillow clutched to his chest, his mind a total blank. He was mortified, but also, for the first time in his life, he felt a new kind of safety. She hadn't run. She hadn't yelled *at* him. She had dived into the storm with him and pulled him out.

When they got to the Brooks' house, Megan helped him transfer from the passenger seat to his wheelchair. She didn't say a word, just wheeled him around the side of the house, down the grassy hill to the walk-out basement's sliding glass door.

Once they were inside, the silence of the empty basement felt charged. Jordan stopped his chair, his hands trembling. He looked at Megan, his eyes shining with unshed tears. He needed to be grounded again, but this time, it was different. He didn't just need a caregiver; he wanted *her*.

"Megan," he whispered, his voice hoarse. "Can we... can we cuddle? Please?"

He wanted to hold her, to show her, without words, that she was one of them now.

Megan's expression softened instantly, her own fear melting away in the face of his need. "Of course, Jordan."

She helped him transfer from his wheelchair to the sectional sofa, his body moving with a careful, pained stiffness. He lay down, and she sat at the end, letting him rest his head in her lap, just as she had done that first morning. He immediately turned, wrapping an arm around her waist, holding her lovingly, his face pressed into her side. She rested her hand in his hair, the two of them cuddling safely, his chest protected.

They sat in silence for a long time. He finally broke it, his voice muffled.

"You... you're like them," he stammered, trying to find the words. "Like Aubrie, and Madi, and Zoe".

Megan's hand stilled in his hair. Her heart gave a painful, hopeful lurch. *Will she / won't she...*

"You're... you're one of my safe people now," he continued, struggling. "I... I just mean... the pact... you fit. You could... if you wanted...".

He was trying to offer her a spot, to tell her she was welcome.

But Megan didn't pick up on it. Or, rather, she *chose* not to. Her internal barrier, the one she had been building for weeks, slammed into place. *He's 19. He's just had a meltdown. He's grateful. He's not... he can't be... asking you to be his girlfriend. You're 27. You're his sister. Don't make this weird*.

She used his fumbled words as a cover, an off-ramp for her own confusing feelings. She gently stroked his hair, her voice calm and sisterly, though her heart was hammering.

"Shh, it's okay, Jordan," she said softly, intentionally misinterpreting him. "I know. I'm part of the family. I'm so happy to be here for you. What you have with the pact is so special, and I'm just... I'm honored to be here to help".

She had defaulted to the older sister role. It was the only one that felt safe. Jordan, hearing her words, felt a small ping of rejection, but he was too exhausted to push. He just nodded, closing his eyes, and held her a little tighter. He was safe, and for now, that was enough.
