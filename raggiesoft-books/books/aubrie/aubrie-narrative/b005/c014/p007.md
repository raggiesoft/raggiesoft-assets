# Part 7: The Holiday Mixer

The energy in the basement that evening was triumphant. The Group of 8, having returned from their last class, swarmed Jordan on the sofa, all of them talking at once about the presentation.

"You killed it, J!" Mark said, giving him a careful, one-armed brotherly hug. "Dr. Evans looked genuinely impressed."

"He was," Zoe confirmed, her face showing a rare, proud smile. "Your personal testimony provided the emotional framework that the logical data needed. It was an optimal presentation."

Jordan felt a deep, satisfying warmth. He was so happy to be back, so glad he had been able to do it.

As the group's chaotic energy settled, Megan, who had been quietly preparing dinner for everyone in the kitchenette, spoke up.

"So," she said, her voice a little hesitant. "My work sent out the official invite for the big holiday mixer. It's next Friday."

"Are you going?" Hannah asked from the dining table.

"I kind of have to," Megan said. "It's the big in-person event in Roanoke. They're allowing us to bring a guest".

Her mind flashed, unbidden, to Jordan. He was the person she had spent the most time with for weeks, her work-buddy, her new confidant. A tiny, ridiculous part of her wished she could ask *him*. She immediately crushed the thought. It was absurd. He was 19, he was her sister's... *everything*... and more importantly, he was still on strict sternal precautions. He couldn't possibly go to a crowded, standing-room-only corporate party.

On top of all that, she had seen his meltdown in the car today. She knew he needed a lot of grounding to attend crowded events, and a loud, professional mixer with hundreds of strangers was the worst possible environment. She wouldn't be able to provide the kind of total focus he'd need there, not while trying to network with her coworkers.

"So," she finished, her voice a little flat, "I guess I'll just be going by myself".

"You absolutely *should* go," Laura said firmly, having come down the stairs with Daniel.

Aubrie, who was nestled on the sofa next to Jordan, nodded in vigorous agreement. "Megan, you have to go. You've been playing nurse to Jordan for weeks now, cooped up in this basement every single day".

"She's right," Jordan added, looking over at her with a grateful smile. "You deserve a night off. Go. Have fun. Be with your coworkers. I'll be fine, I've got this whole crew to keep me company".

Megan looked at all their enthusiastic, supportive faces. She felt a small pang of guilt for even *wanting* to leave, but she knew they were right. "Okay," she said, a real smile forming. "Okay. I'll go."
