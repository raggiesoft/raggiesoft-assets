# Part 8: The New Variable

Later that Friday night, the basement was full of the quiet energy of the Group of 8 settling in for the weekend. Jordan was on the sofa, his head in Aubrie's lap, watching a movie with the group when the internal stairs door opened.

Megan came down, her nice dress and heels looking out of place in their cozy, domestic space.

"Hey!" Hannah called from the floor, where she was leaning against Mark's legs. "How was it? Did you have fun?"

Megan blushed, a small, genuine smile on her face. "It was... it was actually really nice. I met someone".

The movie was immediately forgotten. "Ooh, details!" Madison said, sitting up.

"His name is Alex," Megan said, her voice a little shy. "He's in the logistics department. He just... he seemed like a perfect gentleman. He was really easy to talk to".

"And?" Lauren prompted, grinning.

"And... he asked me on a proper date for this weekend. And I said yes".

The group, led by Aubrie, let out a small, happy cheer. "Megan, that's great!" Aubrie said, beaming. "You deserve to have a nice date."

Jordan, looking up from Aubrie's lap, smiled. "Yeah, that's awesome, Megan. I'm happy for you."

He meant it, but he also felt a small, strange, and unfamiliar pang in his chest. He quickly identified it and pushed it down.

Megan looked at all their faces—Jordan's, Zoe's, Aubrie's. There was not a trace of jealousy or anger. They were all just genuinely, completely happy for her. It was the most supportive, loving reaction she could have imagined.

Which, for some reason, only made her feel more confused than ever.
