# Part 1: The Fortress Gains a Member

Christmas Day was loud, warm, and full of the chaotic, happy energy of the entire Brooks clan. Alex, Megan's new boyfriend, had been polite and charming all through dinner, though he seemed a little stiff, like an outsider observing a strange, foreign culture.

After dinner, as the family was settling in the main living room, Alex and Megan moved to the front entryway for their goodbyes.

Down in the basement, Jordan was resting on the sectional sofa, surrounded by the Group of 8. They were laughing at a movie they had put on, a comfortable, sleepy pile. The peaceful quiet was broken by the sound of muffled, raised voices from the floor above, leaking down the internal staircase.

Jordan tensed, his good mood vanishing. He looked at Zoe, his eyes wide.

"It's just Megan and Alex," Zoe said, her voice calm, though her eyes were narrowed, analyzing the new, disruptive data.

The muffled voices escalated. They couldn't make out the words, just the sharp, begging tone of Alex, and Megan's firm, clear "No."

Then, they all heard it. The unmistakable, sharp *slam* of the front door, vibrating through the house.

A moment later, Megan came down the basement stairs. Her face was pale, and she was visibly shaking, her eyes bright with angry tears.

The movie was immediately forgotten.

"Megan? What happened?" Aubrie asked, jumping up from the sofa.

Megan stood in the middle of the room, her hands clenched. "He... he knew my lease was expiring at the end of January," she said, her voice trembling. "He... he had a key made to his house. He called it my 'real' Christmas present. He said I should just... move in. Tonight."

"What?" Madison said, her voice full of disgust.

"He said *that* was my present, and he... he clearly implied it came with... *other* expectations. Tonight." Megan shook her head, her voice breaking. "I knew I wasn't ready for that. I told him no. I told him I would rather stay here with my family". "And he just... he wouldn't get the hint. So I ended it. I just... I closed the door in his face".

Zoe and Aubrie, seeing their sister was upset, immediately moved in. "Hey," Aubrie said softly, "come here." They gently guided Megan to the sofa, where Jordan was already sitting up, his own face a mask of concern.

"He's a jerk," Jordan said, his voice firm.

"He's an idiot," Aubrie agreed. "Come on, sit."

They had Megan sit on the sofa, and then, in a single, fluid motion, they had her cuddle up with Jordan. He immediately wrapped his arms around her, pulling her into a tight, grounding hug. She buried her face in his shoulder, her body still shaking, and just let herself be held by him.
