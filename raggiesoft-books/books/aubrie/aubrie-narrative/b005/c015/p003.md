# Part 3: The Pact of Six

It was late, long after midnight. The basement was dark, filled with the soft, steady breathing of the sleeping pact. But in the king-sized bed, Megan was wide awake.

She was lying on her side, and Jordan was cuddled up against her back, his arm draped over her waist, his breathing deep and even. He had been asleep for hours. She, however, couldn't sleep.

Her mind was replaying the entire, bizarre day. The confrontation with Alex, the slammed door, the subsequent, gentle "intervention" by the pact. She thought about the "real Christmas present" Alex had demanded—that cold, arrogant, transactional key. The thing *he* wanted, which he'd tried to force on her.

And now, she was getting what she *actually* wanted.

She was simply sleeping in a bed with a trusted person of the opposite gender. A person who wasn't demanding anything, who wasn't pushing, who just *wanted* her to be there for comfort. The pact, and Jordan, had offered her the one thing Alex couldn't: a safe harbor.

In that dark, quiet moment, Megan suddenly realized she no longer cared about the age difference. She didn't care that he was nineteen. She didn't care that he was post-op and still healing from a massive surgery. She just cared that she was getting warm, safe hugs from a trusted guy who was lying in bed with her.

She sighed, a long, quiet breath of contentment, and shifted slightly.

"You 'wake?" a sleepy voice mumbled from the pillow next to her. Jordan's.

"I can't sleep," Megan whispered, feeling bad for waking him.

"Me neither," a different voice whispered from one of the queen beds. Madison.

"You okay, Meg?" Aubrie's voice, thick with sleep, came from the other side of the king bed, where she had been sleeping before Megan's arrival.

Megan felt a blush creep up her neck in the dark. "Yeah. I'm... I'm really good. This is just... a lot."

"It's a good lot, though," Jordan mumbled, his arm tightening around her waist in a sleepy, reassuring hug.

"Yeah," Megan whispered, her voice thick with emotion. "It really is."

"Good," Aubrie said, her voice a warm, happy rumble. "Now go to sleep, you two."

Megan closed her eyes, a real, genuine smile on her face. She was home. She let the steady rhythm of Jordan's breathing finally pull her under.
