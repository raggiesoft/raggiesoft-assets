# Part 4: The Longest Night

Sleep wouldn't come. Megan lay still, her heart hammering, her stomach full of butterflies. She was acutely aware of Jordan's solid, warm presence cuddled against her back. She had been given *permission* to be here, in this bed, with him. She was terrified of moving, of doing something wrong, of losing this new, incredible level of trust.

Jordan was wide awake, too, though he was pretending to be asleep, listening to her breathing. He couldn't sleep. This felt new, this felt *exciting*. His mind was already racing ahead. He was ready to resume the puppy pile, the full, comforting weight of his entire family, and he was ready to officially welcome Megan into it. But he knew he couldn't. Daniel's "one person" rule was still in effect, and his sternum was still a fragile, healing thing.

He was ready for *everything* again, including the full, strenuous physical intimacy he shared with the pact, but he knew with a deep, frustrating certainty that his doctors would say no.

He felt Megan shift, a tiny, restless movement. He couldn't stand the silence.

"You really can't sleep, can you?" he whispered into the dark.

Megan let out a small, shaky laugh. "No. My brain won't shut up."

"Mine either," he admitted.

From one of the queen beds, a phone screen lit up, illuminating Zoe's face. "The auditory sensors in this room are picking up non-sleep-pattern conversation," she whispered, her voice dry. "Is the new variable causing a system instability?"

"No," Jordan and Megan whispered in unison.

"Good," Zoe's voice came back, and the screen went dark.

A moment of silence, and then Megan and Jordan both started to giggle quietly, trying to stifle the sound in their pillows.

"At least it's Saturday," Jordan whispered, his body finally starting to relax. "No one has to get up early".

"That's good," Megan whispered back, her own tension easing. "Because I have a feeling this is going to be a long night."

They both tried to go back to sleep, but they couldn't. They just lay there, a few inches apart, wide awake and buzzing with a new, shared, and very exciting reality.
