# Part 1: The Electric Alert

It was a gray, slushy Tuesday in mid-February 2016. The excitement of the holidays and the "Black Friday Road Trip" had faded into the grinding rhythm of the Spring semester. Jordan sat at the dining table in the Brooks' basement, his laptop open to his Astrophysics homework, but his mind was wandering.

He clicked over to his favorite bookmark: *TheStardustEngine.com*. He checked it every day out of habit, usually just seeing tour dates for cities in Europe or Asia.

Today, the homepage was different. The usual tour calendar was gone. In its place was a massive, pulsing graphic in neon teal and magenta.

THE STARDUST ENGINE: COMING HOME. THE ELECTRIC COLOR TOUR FINALE. LIVE RECORDING AT THE CRUCIBLE. SATURDAY, MAY 21, 2016.

Jordan stopped breathing for a second. He read it again. *Live Recording. The Crucible.*

"Guys!" he yelled, his voice cracking with excitement. "Guys, look!"

Aubrie, Madison, and Zoe, who were studying nearby, rushed over. Mark and Hannah poked their heads out of their room upstairs.

"What is it?" Aubrie asked, looking at the screen.

"They're coming," Jordan said, pointing. "The Stardust Engine. They're playing The Crucible. And they're recording a live album. Here. In Blacksburg."

The room exploded with excitement. "No way!" Mark shouted, bounding down the stairs. "A stadium show? That's going to be insane!"

"We have to go," Jordan said, his eyes wide. "We have to be there. I need to be there."

Zoe, ever the pragmatist, immediately leaned in to read the fine print. "We need to analyze the ticketing protocols immediately. A show of this magnitude will sell out instantly."

She clicked on the "TICKETS" link. The rules were strict, designed to protect the fans.

FORGER NATION PRESALE (Students & Alumni Only)

- **Opens:** Friday @ 10:00 AM.

- **Requirement:** Valid CPI Student Poly Pass or Alumni Association Member ID.

- **Limit:** 4 Tickets per ID.

Jordan’s face fell slightly. "Four tickets? But... there are nine of us. Plus Daniel and Laura. We need eleven." He looked at Megan, who was working quietly at the other end of the table. "And Megan... she's not a student anymore."

"I'm an alumna," Megan said, smiling. "I have an Alumni ID. But the alumni presale usually puts you in the upper deck. You guys want to be on the floor, right?"

"We want to be together," Jordan insisted. "All of us."

Zoe scanned the FAQ section, her eyes darting back and forth. "Found it," she announced. "There is a 'Guest Protocol' for the Student Presale."

She read aloud:

"Current CPI Students may purchase up to 4 tickets for their personal group. Guests do not need to be students, but **must enter the venue with the purchasing student**. The Student ID will serve as the digital key for the entire party."

"Okay, that's the solution," Zoe said, her mind already building the spreadsheet. "We create 'Ticket Cells.' We have enough current students to cover everyone."

She grabbed a notepad and sketched out the plan:

- **Cell Alpha (Jordan's ID):** Buys 4 Tickets. (Jordan, Aubrie, Madison, **Megan**).

  - *Logic:* Jordan uses his student priority to secure a spot for Megan as his guest. They enter together.

- **Cell Beta (Mark's ID):** Buys 4 Tickets. (Mark, Hannah, Daniel, Laura).

  - *Logic:* Mark secures his parents-in-law.

- **Cell Gamma (Zoe's ID):** Buys 3 Tickets. (Zoe, Lauren, Sarah).

"We all log in at exactly 10:00 AM," Zoe commanded. "We select the 'Accessible Field Level' section. If we coordinate our clicks, we can secure three adjacent blocks."

Jordan looked at the plan. It was perfect. He looked at Megan. "You're with me," he said firmly. "In my cell."

Megan smiled, touched by his immediate need to include her. "I wouldn't miss it for the world, Jordan."

"Okay," Jordan said, turning back to the screen, the date glowing in neon letters. "Friday at 10:00 AM. We're going to The Crucible."
