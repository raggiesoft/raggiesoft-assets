# Part 3: The Scalper Meltdown

On Friday morning, 72 hours after Jordan had secured his ticket in the student presale, the floodgates opened for the rest of the world.

At 10:00 AM sharp, TicketSphere launched the general sale for *The Stardust Engine: Live at The Crucible*. The Group of 9, still gathered around the dining table in the Brooks' basement, watched the spectacle unfold not on the ticket purchase page, but on the forums and message boards.

"Here it comes," Mark said, hitting refresh on a popular scalper forum.

Instantly, the threads began to populate with confusion and rage.

**User: TicketKing99:** WTF? Sale just opened 10 seconds ago and the entire floor is grayed out? Glitch? **User: BotMaster:** My scripts are bouncing. Everything in the 100 level and Field Level is showing 'Restricted'. Only upper deck nosebleeds are pulling up. **User: ScalpEmAll:** This is BS. How is it sold out already? There wasn't even a queue!

"They are so mad," Madison giggled, reading a furious tweet from a broker in New York who had planned to buy fifty floor seats. "They can't figure out why the good tickets are already gone."

"They're gone because Forger Nation took them," Jordan said, a profound sense of satisfaction settling in his chest. He looked at the confirmation on his own screen—Row A, Seat 1. He had beaten the bots simply by belonging.

Zoe tapped her screen. "Look. The band just posted an update to the main site. It explains everything."

The group huddled around her laptop. On the homepage of *TheStardustEngine.com*, a new letter had appeared, written in the band's signature maroon type.
