# Part 7: The Departure Loop

The lights of **The Crucible** faded behind them as the Group of 11 navigated the crush of 60,000 fans exiting the stadium. The air was cool, filled with the smell of exhaust and the buzzing, high-energy chatter of the post-concert crowd.

They reached the **Crucible Bus Loop**, which had been transformed into a massive, humming transit hub. A long line of Blue Ridge Transit buses idled in the dedicated lanes, their engines rumbling in a low unison.

As they moved toward their designated sector, they passed a knot of people standing near a pole marked **ROANOKE EXPRESS**. They were shouting at a weary-looking transit supervisor.

"What do you mean it's gone?" a man in a denim jacket yelled. "We need to get back to the Third Street Station!"

"Sir, as the signs stated," the supervisor said, pointing to a large, illuminated board, "the last Roanoke Express departed at 7:45 PM. RVT service has ended for the night."

"But the concert just ended!" a woman next to him cried. "How are we supposed to get home?"

"There is no return service," the supervisor repeated. "You will need to call a cab or a rideshare."

Jordan watched them as he rolled past, feeling a profound relief that his family had planned their logistics with military precision.

Just past the angry Roanoke crowd, however, was a very different scene. At the **Bluewater Transit** bay, a large, familiar coach bus was idling, its engine purring. It was the last **Maroon Express** of the night.

**Sign:** MAROON EXPRESS ... BLUEWATER via FALLING BRANCH **Voice:** "Bluewater Transit Maroon Express: Bluewater via Falling Branch."

"Look at that," Madison said, pointing to the happy fans boarding the coach. "My hometown bus didn't leave them stranded. 10:15 PM departure. Right on time."

Unlike the Roanoke fans, the Bluewater crew was cheering, high-fiving the driver as they boarded. The concert had finished just in time for the final scheduled run, saving them from being stuck.

They continued to **Sector 4**. The Blue Ridge Transit queue was organized chaos.

To their left, a bus idled with its doors open. **Sign:** FOXRIDGE ... FORGERS SHUTTLE **Voice:** *"Shuttle to Foxridge. GO FORGERS!"*

Ahead of them, another bus was loading a massive crowd of students. **Sign:** HETHWOOD ... FORGERS SHUTTLE **Voice:** *"Shuttle to Hethwood. GO FORGERS!"*

And right in front of them, waiting at the curb with the ramp already deploying, was their bus. **Sign:** FALLING BRANCH ... FORGERS SHUTTLE **Voice:** *"Shuttle to Falling Branch. GO FORGERS!"*

"That's us," Daniel said, cutting through the noise. "Let's go home."

Jordan wheeled himself up the ramp. The driver, looking exhausted but happy, nodded at him. "Great show, right?"

"The best," Jordan said.

He maneuvered into the securement spot. Aubrie took the seat next to him, and the rest of the family filled the rows behind them. The doors hissed shut, cutting off the noise of the crowd.

"Next stop, Falling Branch," the automated voice announced, followed by one last, triumphant, *"GO FORGERS!"*

The bus pulled away, merging onto the dedicated lane and leaving the chaos of the stadium in the rearview mirror. Jordan leaned his head on Aubrie's shoulder, his ears ringing with the phantom sound of the gong, his heart full of electric color. The night was over, but the memory was just beginning.
