# Part 1: The Pact

The conversation happened quietly one evening in the basement "Adults' Wing" of the newly completed compound.

It was the summer of 2016. They had just finished their sophomore year at CPI, and while graduation was still two years away, the air was thick with the excitement of settling into their permanent home.

The **seven** of them—Jordan, Aubrie, Madison, Zoe, Lauren, Sarah, and **Megan**—were sprawled out together in their familiar "puppy pile" in the **Sunken Sanctuary**. They lay on the massive, custom-built foam mattress that filled the pit, a sea of comfort that held them all safely.

They had been discussing the practicalities of their future, and the topic of legal marriage had come up.

"It should be one of us," Zoe said, her voice cutting through the comfortable silence with characteristic logic. "For legal reasons. Power of attorney, next of kin... we need a firewall against his mother if something goes wrong."

A gentle quiet fell over the group. Jordan looked at the faces of the women he loved, a wave of emotion making it hard to speak.

It was Madison who broke the silence. She shifted, propping herself up on an elbow to look directly at Aubrie.

"It has to be you, Aubrie," she said, her voice soft but certain.

Aubrie looked surprised, her brow furrowed. "Mads, why? We're all..." She trailed off, but her thought was clear in the eyes of everyone present: *We're all in love with him as coequals.*

"Because you were first," Madison said, her voice full of a fierce, protective love. "That very first night. When he was so broken and scared he couldn't even speak."

*Broken?* The word echoed in Jordan's mind, a strange and distant descriptor. He didn't feel broken. He felt whole, strong, surrounded by a love so complete it had rebuilt him from the ground up. He looked down at his own hands, steady and sure, and realized with a jolt that he hadn't seen that shy, terrified boy in the mirror for a very long time.

Madison continued, her voice unwavering. "You were the one who crawled into that bed and just held him. You didn't ask questions, you didn't try to fix him. You were just... there. You were the first person to show him an ounce of kindness in his entire life. That's a place none of the rest of us can ever claim. It's only right that it's you."

Megan, who was curled up on Jordan’s other side, nodded slowly. "She's right," she said softly. "I came later, and I love this family more than anything, but even I can see it. Aubrie is the foundation we all built on."

Tears welled in Aubrie's eyes as she looked at Madison and Megan, a wave of overwhelming gratitude washing over her.

Jordan reached out, his hand covering Madison’s. He looked from her clear, steady gaze to Aubrie's tear-streaked face, his heart so full it felt like it might burst.

"I love you both," he whispered, his voice thick with emotion.

He then looked around at the other four women—Zoe, Lauren, Sarah, and Megan—his gaze lingering on each of them.

"I love all **six** of you. So much. And I am so, so grateful that this... us... works. That there's no jealousy, no heartache, no confusion or competition. Just this."

"We will always be coequals," Zoe said, her voice firm with conviction. "That never changes."

"But Madison's right," Lauren added softly, and Sarah nodded in agreement. "That first night... that's the reason. It has to be."

And so, surrounded by the warmth and weight of their collective love, they all agreed. It was their path forward. They would all remain coequals in love and life, but when the time came, Aubrie would be the one to legally marry Jordan.

The reason was singular and sacred: she was the one who had climbed into bed and comforted a scared, broken boy on his very first night of freedom, an act of kindness that had planted the seed for the extraordinary family that had bloomed around them.
