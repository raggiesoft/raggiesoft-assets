# Part 1: The Morning Of

The morning of **Saturday, July 16, 2016**, dawned bright and clear over the newly completed Kelly Family Compound on U.S. 11. The usual calm of their rural sanctuary was replaced by a low, buzzing hum of excited energy.

Down in the basement "Adults' Wing"—the massive, custom-built space they had moved into just weeks before—the first to stir was Hannah. She was too keyed up to sleep, her heart a happy flutter in her chest. She slipped out of the "Anderson Suite," the private bedroom she shared with Mark, leaving him to sleep for a few more precious minutes. As she padded past the double doors of the adjacent "Pact Suite," she smiled. Behind those doors lay the "Sunken Sanctuary," where Jordan and his six partners—Aubrie, Madison, Zoe, Lauren, Sarah, and Megan—were likely still entangled in their massive, comfortable "puppy pile."

She headed up the main stairs to the great room and found she wasn't the first one awake. Daniel and Laura Brooks were already in the massive kitchen, the aroma of fresh coffee filling the air.

"Morning, sweetie," Laura said, her smile as warm as the sunrise streaming through the large windows. "Couldn't sleep either?"

"Not a wink," Hannah admitted with a grin.

One by one, the rest of the family emerged from the basement.

- **Mark**, looking surprisingly well-rested as he joined Hannah from their private suite.

- **Zoe and Lauren**, the first to emerge from the Pact Suite, already reviewing the logistical timeline on a tablet with quiet efficiency.

- **Sarah and Madison**, practically vibrating with excitement.

- **Megan**, checking her camera gear to ensure she captured every moment for the family archives.

- And finally, **Jordan and Aubrie**, holding hands, their faces glowing with a serene, profound happiness born of the deep, committed love they shared with their entire pact.

Breakfast was a deliberately simple affair, a small island of normalcy in a day that would be anything but. There was no big, fancy meal. Toast was made, fruit was passed around, and Jordan, true to his routine, sat at the massive dining table with a simple bowl of cereal and milk, a comforting and familiar start to the most important day of his life.

After breakfast, the organized chaos began. The group split off to shower and get dressed in their respective suites. The sounds of hairdryers, laughing, and last-minute steaming of dresses and suits filled the house.

Finally, it was time. Looking resplendent in their wedding attire—the women in their beautiful, coordinated dresses, Mark in his sharp suit, and Jordan looking handsome and confident in his own tuxedo—they all gathered in the great room.

Caleb Brooks, who had arrived earlier from his own place, gave his dad a nod. "I'm heading out now, Dad. I'll get the van parked near the ballroom and wait for you all there." With a wave, he was gone, their quiet backup plan in motion.

The family then walked down the long driveway together to the newly paved bus stop at the end of their property. As the **Blue Ridge Transit Route 3** bus—a standard 35-foot city bus running its regular route between Christiansburg and Radford—approached and hissed to a stop, Jordan felt a surge of pride.

He maneuvered his wheelchair onto the ramp and into the bus with a practiced ease he hadn't possessed just a few short years ago. He didn't need a special ride. He didn't need a favor. He was taking himself to his own wedding.

His family filed in behind him, their happy chatter filling the front of the bus. As the vehicle pulled away from their home and headed toward the **Christiansburg Pepper Plaza Transit Center**, Jordan looked out the window, his hand held firmly in Aubrie's.

He was independent. He was free. And he was surrounded by everyone he loved.
