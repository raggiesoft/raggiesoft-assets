# Part 3: The Gathering

With the last centerpiece adjusted and the final sound check complete, a hush of anticipation fell over The Blacksburg Ballroom. It was time.

**Daniel and Laura Brooks**, acting as the patriarch and matriarch for the entire extended clan, took their places near the entrance to greet the arriving guests. They were joined by the women of the pact—**Madison, Zoe, Lauren, Sarah, and Megan**.

While the others stood in a receiving line, **Megan** moved with a happy, purposeful energy. She balanced her role as a greeter with her role as the family archivist, pausing between hugs to snap beautiful, candid photos of guests entering the sunlit space, capturing the genuine joy on their faces.

The two couples—**Jordan and Aubrie**, and **Mark and Hannah**—remained tucked away in a small side room, out of sight until the ceremony began, holding hands and breathing in the reality of the moment.

A small, elegant sign on an easel near the guest book set the tone for the event: *"Choose a seat, not a side. We're all family here."*

The first to arrive were the extended **Brooks clan**—a happy, boisterous flood of aunts, uncles, and cousins who enveloped Daniel and Laura in warm hugs. They were followed by **Hannah’s family (the Parkers)** and **Mark’s family (the Andersons)**, who had traveled in from different parts of the state.

There was a joyful energy as the families met, many for the first time. They shook hands and introduced themselves not as separate entities or in-laws, but as the newly connected branches of one large, sprawling family tree. Caleb, Owen, Lucas, and Jacob Brooks acted as ushers, guiding everyone not to specific sides, but simply to open chairs, physically mixing the families together.

Soon, the room began to fill with a diverse mix of friends and colleagues. Classmates from **CPI**, their faces familiar from late-night study groups and shared projects, came to support their friends.

For Jordan, watching from the crack in the side room door, seeing them arrive was particularly meaningful. These were people who knew him simply as "**Jordan**," the intelligent and kind classmate from Astrophysics, not as the sheltered, terrified boy from his past. A few of his coworkers from his part-time job at the campus **library** arrived, clapping him on the shoulder in spirit, wishing him well.

The absence of certain people was a quiet, unspoken statement of the family's victory. **No one from the Sinclair or Fuller families was present.** They hadn't been invited; they didn't even know this day was happening. Jordan's past was completely and totally severed from his present.

Every person in the room was there because they loved and supported him, Hannah, Mark, and Aubrie for *who* they were now.

As the guests found their seats, filling the two curved sections of the hall, the room buzzed with happy, low chatter. There was no division, no "bride's side" or "groom's side." There was only a single, unified community, a testament to the life and the family the eight of them—plus Megan, Bob, and Emily—had built together, ready to witness the beautiful ceremony that was about to begin.
