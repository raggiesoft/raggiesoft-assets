# Part 1: Reunion in the Hallway

It was the first day of their junior year, Fall 2016. The Group of 8 was gathered in the hallway outside their Latin 101 class, a small classroom designed for around thirty students. Jordan Shawn Kelly, his legal name now reflecting his new life, was chatting with Aubrie, his wife, while Mark and Madison argued playfully about the upcoming football season.

A new student approached, looking at the room number, her face a little nervous. She glanced at the group, and then her gaze locked onto Jordan. She stopped, her expression shifting from uncertainty to a look of profound, hopeful shock.

"Aubrey?" she asked, her voice hesitant.

Jordan looked up from his conversation with Aubrie. He froze. He couldn't believe it. He was looking into those same warm, welcoming hazel eyes he hadn't seen in four years. The face was more mature, but the kindness was identical.

"Jessica?" he breathed, his voice full of shocked disbelief. "Is it really you?"

"Yes, Aubrey, it's me! It's Jessica!" she said, her own smile widening as tears welled in her eyes.

The rest of the Group of 8 went quiet, instantly recognizing that something powerful and private was happening. Aubrie, his wife, watched, her expression open and supportive.

"Oh, Jess," Jordan said, his voice thick with emotion. Jordan was the only one ever allowed to call her Jess. "I missed you so much."

He pushed his wheelchair forward slightly, his arms opening. "I go by Jordan now," he said, just as he pulled her into a hug that was four long, painful years in the making.

Jessica knelt to meet his embrace, holding him tightly. "I missed you, too," she whispered into his shoulder, her voice cracking. "I thought about you all the time. I... I'm so sorry about your mother. I never..."

"I know," he said, pulling back. He understood. He had been rescued by his mother's actions, even as she had tried to destroy his life.

The Group of 8 looked on, seeing the profound, healing weight of the moment for their friend. Jessica finally stood, wiping a tear from her eye. Jordan, his face glowing, turned to the woman at his side.

"Jess, this is... my family," he said. He started with the woman holding his hand. "This is my wife, Aubrie Jean Kelly."

Jessica’s eyes went wide, but she smiled, seeing the pure, unconditional love between them. Jordan then proceeded to introduce her to Madison, Zoe, Lauren, Sarah, Hannah, and Mark.

"Wow," Jessica said, looking at the circle of kind, smiling faces. "It's... it's amazing to meet all of you."

"You, too," Hannah said warmly, speaking for the group. "It looks like we're in the same class. You should sit with us, unless there are assigned seats".

Jessica looked back at Jordan—the boy she had tried to save, who was now a man surrounded by a fortress of love. She smiled, a real, genuine smile. "I'd like that. I'd like that a lot."
