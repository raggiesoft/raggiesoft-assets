# Part 1: Midnight at The Foundry

It was 11:55 PM on Thursday night, the eve of the album release. A line of excited students snaked out the doors of The Foundry, winding through the lobby of Newman Hall. In 2014, Jordan had felt like an invisible ghost in this building, too poor to buy a hoodie and too scared to speak.

Tonight, he was at the front of the line.

The entire Group of 9 was with him. They were all decked out in their maroon and orange gear, buzzing with caffeine and excitement. Jessica, fully integrated into the family, stood comfortably between Jordan and Aubrie, her hand resting on the handle of his wheelchair.

At midnight, the doors opened. The industrial-chic interior of The Foundry was transformed. Neon teal and magenta lights—the colors of the Electric Color tour—washed over the exposed brick walls, mixing with the university's maroon and orange branding.

Jordan wheeled himself directly to the display tower in the center of the store. There it was. The Deluxe Vinyl Box Set of Live at The Crucible. The cover art was a stunning, high-contrast shot of the stadium lit up during the finale, the confetti raining down like stardust.

He picked up the heavy box set. He didn't look at the price tag with fear this time. He pulled out his wallet, took out his debit card—linked to the bank account he managed with Hannah's help —and paid for it himself.

"Receipt in the bag?" the cashier asked.

"No," Jordan smiled, holding the box set tight against his chest. "I'll carry it."
